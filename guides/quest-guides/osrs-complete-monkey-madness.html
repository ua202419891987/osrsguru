OSRS How to Complete Monkey Madness Quest

Master the gnome quest that unlocks Ape Atoll, the Dragon Scimitar, and 35,000 combat XP

Updated: May 2026 10 min read

Monkey Madness is one of the most rewarding and challenging quests in Old School RuneScape. It is a long quest that takes you to the mysterious Ape Atoll, tasks you with infiltrating a monkey civilization, and culminates in a fight against a powerful jungle demon. The rewards are incredible: 35,000 experience in your choice of Attack or Defense, access to Ape Atoll for training, and the ability to wield the Dragon Scimitar. For many players, this quest is the gateway to the mid-game. In this comprehensive guide, I will walk you through every step, share strategies I have refined over multiple playthroughs, and help you avoid the mistakes that turn this quest into a nightmare.

Quest Requirements

Before starting, ensure you have completed the following quests:

  • The Grand Tree
  • Tree Gnome Village

Skill Requirements:

  • No hard skill requirements, but high combat stats are strongly recommended

Recommended Combat Stats:

  • 43+ Prayer for Protect from Melee
  • 60+ Hitpoints
  • 50+ Attack and Strength

You can technically do this quest at lower levels, but the final fight will be extremely difficult. I strongly recommend meeting the recommended stats before attempting it.

Required Items

  • About 1,000 coins for charter ships and gnome gliders
  • Gold bar
  • Ball of wool
  • Monkey bones or corpse (from Karamja monkeys)
  • Plenty of food: sharks or better
  • Antipoison potions
  • Energy potions or Stamina potions
  • Teleport runes or jewelry

Starting the Quest

Talk to King Narnode Shareen in the Grand Tree. He will tell you about a missing 10th squad of gnome troops and ask you to find them. This sets up the entire questline.

Your first stop is the Gnome Glider pilot, who will take you to Crash Island. From there, you need to find a way to Ape Atoll, the home of the monkeys.

Navigating Ape Atoll

Ape Atoll is one of the most dangerous areas for unprepared players. The monkeys are aggressive, hit hard, and can poison you. Here is how to survive:

Getting to the Atoll

Talk to Lumdo on Crash Island. At first, he will refuse to take you to Ape Atoll. You need to talk to Garkor, the sergeant of the 10th squad, who is wandering around Crash Island. After speaking with Garkor, Lumdo will agree to take you.

First Steps on Ape Atoll

When you arrive, you will be surrounded by aggressive monkeys. Do not try to fight them. Instead, run directly south to the grassy area and stand still. If you have 43 Prayer, turn on Protect from Melee immediately.

The key to surviving Ape Atoll is understanding the monkey patrol patterns. The monkeys have a limited aggression range. If you run far enough south, they will stop chasing you. Use this safe spot to regroup and plan your next move.

Talking to Garkor on Ape Atoll

Garkor is located on the eastern side of the atoll, near the temple. Getting to him is tricky because you need to navigate past monkey archers and guards. Here is the safest route:

  1. From the landing spot, run south to the safe grassy area.
  2. Head east along the southern edge of the atoll.
  3. When you reach the southeastern corner, head north toward the temple.
  4. Garkor is on a ledge just east of the temple.

Pro tip: Wear a greegree (which you will get later) to make the monkeys non-aggressive. But for now, you just need to survive the run.

The Monkey Greegree

To infiltrate the monkey city, you need to transform into a monkey using a greegree. Here is how to get one:

  1. Talk to Zooknock, the gn mage in the tunnel under Ape Atoll. The tunnel entrance is on the western side of the atoll.
  2. Zooknock needs monkey bones and a talisman to create the greegree.
  3. Go to the temple in the center of the atoll and kill a monkey to get bones. Be careful: the temple monkeys are low level but the guards around them are dangerous.
  4. Return to Zooknock with the bones and the gold bar.
  5. Zooknock will create a monkey greegree for you.

Once you have the greegree, equip it. You will transform into a monkey, and all the monkeys on Ape Atoll will become non-aggressive. This makes navigation trivial.

Getting the Monkey Talisman

To proceed with the quest, you need to speak with the Monkey King and obtain the monkey talisman. Here is the process:

  1. While in monkey form, enter the monkey city in the center of Ape Atoll.
  2. Talk to the Monkey King in the palace. He will tell you about his problems with the gnomes.
  3. You need to pretend to be a monkey envoy. Talk to the king's advisor and follow the dialogue options that show loyalty to the monkey cause.
  4. The king will give you a monkey talisman.

Be careful not to unequip the greegree while in the city. If you turn back into a human, every monkey in the city will attack you, and you will almost certainly die.

The Final Battle: Jungle Demon

The climax of Monkey Madness is a fight against the Jungle Demon, a level 195 demon with powerful melee and magic attacks. This fight intimidates many players, but with the right strategy, it is very manageable.

Preparation

  • Bring your best melee or ranged gear
  • Plenty of food: at least 20 sharks
  • Protect from Magic prayer (essential)
  • 1-2 prayer potions
  • Ranging potions or super combat potions

The Fight Strategy

The Jungle Demon uses both melee and magic attacks. His magic attack is the dangerous one, hitting up to 30+. His melee is also strong but manageable. Here is the proven strategy:

Method 1: Ranged Safespot (Recommended for Low Levels)

The Jungle Demon can be safespotted using the 10th squad gnomes. When the fight starts, the gnomes will attack the demon. Stand behind the gnomes and attack with ranged. The demon will be distracted by the gnomes and cannot reach you.

This is the easiest method and what I recommend for anyone below 70 combat stats. Bring a magic shortbow or better, and plenty of arrows. Let the gnomes tank while you deal damage from a distance.

Method 2: Melee with Prayer Flicking

If you have good melee stats and are comfortable with prayer flicking, you can fight the demon directly. Turn on Protect from Magic and use your best melee weapon. Eat food whenever your health drops below 40. This method is faster but riskier.

Method 3: Ring of Recoil

A Ring of Recoil helps speed up the fight by reflecting damage. It is not required but can save you a few sharks worth of health.

The demon has 150 hitpoints, so the fight can take 5-10 minutes depending on your DPS. Do not panic if it seems to be taking a while. Just keep your prayer up and eat consistently.

After the Fight

Once the Jungle Demon is defeated, talk to Garkor to complete the quest. You will be given a choice of where to allocate your 35,000 combat experience:

  • Attack
  • Strength
  • Defense
  • Hitpoints

Most players choose Attack or Strength. If you are a pure, obviously do not put it in Defense or Hitpoints unless you know what you are doing.

Quest Rewards

  • 3 Quest Points
  • 35,000 combat XP in a skill of your choice
  • Access to Ape Atoll
  • Ability to wield the Dragon Scimitar
  • Ability to buy and use Ape Atoll teleport tablets

The Dragon Scimitar is the real prize here. With its high attack speed and strong stats, it is the best training weapon for Attack levels 60 to 70, and it remains useful well beyond that. The special attack, Sever, drains the opponent's prayer and is popular in PvP.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Unequipping the greegree in the monkey city: This is the number one cause of death on Ape Atoll. Always check that your greegree is equipped before entering the city.
  • Not bringing antipoison: The spiders and scorpions on Ape Atoll poison frequently. Antipoison is essential.
  • Forgetting food for the demon fight: The Jungle Demon fight is not hard, but it is long. Bring more food than you think you need.
  • Using the wrong prayer in the demon fight: Protect from Magic is the correct prayer. His magic hits much harder than his melee.
  • Not using the gnome safespot: There is no shame in letting the 10th squad tank for you. It is the intended mechanic.

Time-Saving Tips

  • Bring a stamina potion for running around Ape Atoll. The island is large, and you will do a lot of running.
  • Set up your quick prayers before the demon fight. You do not want to be fumbling with menus while taking damage.
  • After completing the quest, talk to Daero in the Grand Tree to claim your XP reward. Do not forget this step.
  • The Ape Atoll Agility Course becomes available after the quest and is one of the best training methods in the game.
  • Keep your monkey greegree. You will need it for future visits to Ape Atoll, including Monkey Madness II.

Final Thoughts

Monkey Madness is a long quest, but it is one of the most rewarding experiences in Old School RuneScape. The 35,000 combat XP alone is worth the effort, and unlocking the Dragon Scimitar transforms your training efficiency. Follow this guide, take your time on Ape Atoll, and use the safespot for the Jungle Demon. You will have this quest completed before you know it. Welcome to the mid-game.
