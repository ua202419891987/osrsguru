OSRS How to Get Rune Pouch Guide

Every method to obtain the Rune Pouch, the most essential magic utility item in OSRS

Updated: May 2026 8 min read

The Rune Pouch is one of the most valuable and versatile items in Old School RuneScape. It allows you to store up to three different types of runes, effectively freeing up 28 inventory slots for other items. Whether you are doing slayer, bossing, runecrafting, or PvP, the Rune Pouch is practically mandatory for efficient gameplay. The problem is that obtaining one can be confusing for new players. There are multiple methods, each with its own requirements and difficulty level. In this guide, I will break down every way to get a Rune Pouch, explain which method is best for your account type, and share strategies I have used to get pouches on everything from main accounts to hardcore ironmen.

What Does the Rune Pouch Do?

Before diving into the methods, let me explain why this item is so important. The Rune Pouch holds up to 16,000 of three different rune types. This means you can carry an entire spellbook's worth of runes in a single inventory slot.

Common uses include:

  • Slayer tasks with Ice Barrage or Blood Barrage
  • Bossing with Vengeance and teleport runes
  • Runecrafting with multiple talisman runes
  • Clue scrolls with teleport spells
  • PvP with combo runes and teleport escapes

If you do not have a Rune Pouch yet, getting one should be your immediate priority. It fundamentally changes how you play the game.

Method 1: Bounty Hunter (Fastest for PvPers)

The traditional and historically most common method is purchasing the Rune Pouch from the Bounty Hunter shop for 1,200,000 Bounty Hunter points. However, Bounty Hunter has undergone multiple reworks, so here is the current state as of 2026.

How Bounty Hunter Works

Bounty Hunter is a PvP minigame in the Wilderness where you are assigned a target to kill. Each kill earns you points based on your target's combat level and your own performance. The minigame is located in the Wilderness at level 20-30.

Earning Points Efficiently

To get 1,200,000 points for the Rune Pouch, you need to consistently kill targets. Here is what I have learned from grinding Bounty Hunter:

  • Combat bracket matters: Lower combat brackets generally have faster fights and less risk. A level 60-80 account can earn points faster than a maxed main because fights end quickly.
  • Bring risk: The more wealth you risk, the more points you earn per kill. However, do not bring more than you can afford to lose.
  • Use the right gear: Dragon weapons, Rune crossbows, and mid-tier magic gear are the sweet spot for most brackets.
  • Skip difficult targets: If your target is a maxed main and you are a mid-level account, skip them. It is not worth the time.

Realistically, expect to spend 8-15 hours in Bounty Hunter to earn enough points for a Rune Pouch, depending on your win rate and combat level.

Method 2: Last Man Standing (Best for Casual Players)

Last Man Standing, commonly called LMS, is a competitive PvP minigame where 24 players fight until one survivor remains. It is a safe minigame, meaning you lose no items on death, which makes it appealing for players who do not want to risk their gear in the Wilderness.

How LMS Works

At the start of each game, you are given a random set of gear and supplies. Everyone starts on equal footing, so your success depends entirely on your PvP skill, not your bank value. Games last about 5-10 minutes.

Earning Points

LMS uses a points system based on your placement:

  • 1st place: 5 points
  • 2nd place: 4 points
  • 3rd place: 3 points
  • 4th place: 2 points
  • 5th-24th place: 1 point

The Rune Pouch costs 75 LMS points. This means you need about 15-20 wins, or a mix of placements over roughly 30-50 games.

LMS Tips for Beginners

If you are new to PvP, LMS can feel overwhelming. Here is how to improve quickly:

  • Learn the F-key switches: Inventory, prayer, and equipment switching are essential. Practice until you can switch gear sets in under two seconds.
  • Watch YouTube guides: There are excellent LMS tutorials that explain the meta strategies for each combat style.
  • Start with ranged: Ranged is the most forgiving combat style in LMS. Learn to stack dark bow specs with bolt switches.
  • Manage your supplies: Do not waste food early. Save your best food for the final fights.
  • Use the minimap: Always know where the safe zone is and plan your movements accordingly.

LMS is my recommended method for most players. It is safe, the games are short, and you actually improve at PvP while grinding for the pouch.

Method 3: Slayer Reward Points (Best for Ironmen)

For ironmen and players who hate PvP, there is an alternative: purchasing the Rune Pouch from Slayer masters for 750 Slayer reward points.

Earning Slayer Points

Slayer points are earned by completing Slayer tasks. The amount depends on which Slayer master you use:

  • Turael: 0 points (skip him for points)
  • Spria: 0 points
  • Mazchna: 2 points per task
  • Vannaka: 4 points per task
  • Chaeldar: 10 points per task
  • Nieve/Steve: 12 points per task (15 with Kourend diary)
  • Duradel: 15 points per task (18 with Kourend diary)
  • Konar: 18 points per task

Every 10th, 50th, 100th, 250th, and 1000th task gives bonus points:

  • 10th task: 5x normal points
  • 50th task: 15x normal points
  • 100th task: 25x normal points
  • 250th task: 35x normal points
  • 1000th task: 50x normal points

Point Farming Strategy

To get 750 points as fast as possible, use this strategy:

  1. Do your first 9 tasks at Mazchna for fast completions.
  2. Do your 10th task at Duradel or Konar for the 5x bonus.
  3. Repeat this pattern for the 50th and 100th tasks.

Using this method, you can expect to earn 750 points in about 50-70 tasks, depending on your luck with bonuses. At an average of 15 minutes per task, that is roughly 15-20 hours of slayer.

For ironmen, this is often the preferred method because you are training Slayer anyway, and the points come as a natural side benefit.

Method 4: Deadman Mode and Leagues

In special game modes like Deadman Mode and Leagues, the Rune Pouch is often easier to obtain. In Deadman, it can be purchased with blood money. In Leagues, it is sometimes a starting relic or easily purchasable reward.

These methods only apply if you are playing those specific modes, but they are worth mentioning for completeness.

Which Method Should You Choose?

Here is my recommendation based on account type:

  • Main account, likes PvP: Bounty Hunter is fastest if you are good at Wilderness PvP.
  • Main account, hates PvP: LMS is safe and teaches you useful skills. Expect 10-15 hours.
  • Ironman: Slayer points are the way to go. You need Slayer levels anyway.
  • Pure account: LMS is ideal because you do not risk your account in the Wilderness.
  • Hardcore Ironman: Definitely Slayer points. Never risk your status in PvP.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Spending Slayer points early: Do not waste your first Slayer points on blocking tasks or buying items. Save for the Rune Pouch first.
  • Rushing into Bounty Hunter unprepared: If you do not know how to NH fight or switch prayers, you will just feed points to other players.
  • Not using the Rune Pouch once you have it: I have seen players get the pouch and then forget to fill it. Set it up immediately with your most-used runes.
  • Bringing expensive gear to LMS: LMS is a safe minigame. You do not need to bring anything. The gear is provided.
  • Ignoring the Rune Pouch entirely: Some players put this off for months. Do not. It is the single best quality-of-life upgrade in the game.

Time-Saving Tips

  • If doing LMS, play during off-peak hours. The lobbies fill faster, and you spend less time waiting.
  • For Slayer points, always do your milestone tasks (10th, 50th, 100th) at the highest-point master available.
  • Consider getting a second Rune Pouch if you frequently switch between activities. It saves time restocking.
  • Use the Rune Pouch for teleport runes during quests. It frees up so much inventory space for quest items.
  • Combine the Rune Pouch with a Book of the Dead or Tome of Fire for even more inventory efficiency.

Final Thoughts

The Rune Pouch is not just a nice-to-have item. It is essential for efficient gameplay in almost every aspect of Old School RuneScape. Whether you choose to grind Bounty Hunter, learn LMS, or earn Slayer points, the investment is absolutely worth it. Pick the method that suits your playstyle, commit to the grind, and enjoy the massive inventory space upgrade. Once you have a Rune Pouch, you will wonder how you ever played without one.
