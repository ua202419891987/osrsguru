OSRS How to Get Graceful Outfit Full Guide

The definitive guide to obtaining the best weight-reducing gear in Old School RuneScape

Updated: May 2026 9 min read

The Graceful outfit is the most important utility gear in Old School RuneScape for any activity that involves running. With a total weight reduction of 25 kilograms and a 30% faster run energy restoration rate when wearing the full set, it is indispensable for questing, clue scrolls, runecrafting, and practically every skilling activity in the game. Getting the full set requires collecting Marks of Grace from rooftop agility courses, and many players have no idea how to do this efficiently. In this complete guide, I will break down the optimal strategy, course recommendations, and personal tips from grinding out multiple Graceful sets across different accounts.

What is the Graceful Outfit?

The Graceful outfit consists of six pieces: hood, cape, top, legs, gloves, and boots. Each piece provides a small weight reduction bonus, and wearing the full set grants an additional 30% run energy restoration bonus. This makes it the best gear in the game for any situation where you are running long distances.

Here is the breakdown of each piece:

  • Hood: -3 kg weight reduction, costs 35 Marks of Grace
  • Cape: -4 kg weight reduction, costs 40 Marks of Grace
  • Top: -5 kg weight reduction, costs 55 Marks of Grace
  • Legs: -6 kg weight reduction, costs 60 Marks of Grace
  • Gloves: -3 kg weight reduction, costs 30 Marks of Grace
  • Boots: -4 kg weight reduction, costs 40 Marks of Grace

Total cost: 260 Marks of Grace for the full set.

How Marks of Grace Work

Marks of Grace are currency items that spawn randomly while you are completing rooftop agility courses. They appear on the ground at specific spawn locations on each course, and you must pick them up before they disappear after about 10 minutes.

The spawn rate of Marks of Grace depends on two factors: your Agility level and the level requirement of the course you are running. The formula favors running courses that are close to your current level. If you run a course that is significantly below your level, the spawn rate is reduced by 80%. This is why it is crucial to run the highest-level course you can access.

Here is the general rule: run the highest rooftop course where your failure rate is minimal. Failing obstacles wastes time and reduces your effective Marks per hour.

Best Rooftop Courses by Level

Level 1-20: Gnome Stronghold Course

This is not a rooftop course, so Marks of Grace do not spawn here. Use this course only to get to level 20 as quickly as possible. It takes about 20 minutes.

Level 20-30: Draynor Village Rooftop

The first rooftop course. Marks spawn here, but the rate is low. Expect about 8-12 Marks per hour. It is not worth grinding Graceful here. Just get to level 30 and move on.

Level 30-40: Varrock Rooftop

Better spawn rates than Draynor, but still not great. You might see 10-15 Marks per hour. Use this course to get to 40, but do not plan on getting many Marks here.

Level 40-50: Canifis Rooftop

This is where things get interesting. Canifis is the shortest rooftop course in the game, with a lap time of about 45-50 seconds. Because Marks spawn based on lap completion, the short lap time makes Canifis one of the best courses for Marks per hour, even at higher levels.

At level 40-50, you can expect 15-20 Marks per hour at Canifis. Many players intentionally stay here until they have enough Marks for the full set because the lap time is so fast. I personally got about 60 Marks at Canifis before moving on.

Level 50-60: Falador Rooftop

Falador is a longer course with a higher failure rate at level 50. The Marks spawn rate is decent, but the lap time is around 55-60 seconds. I recommend staying at Canifis if you are specifically grinding Marks, but Falador is better for Agility XP.

Level 60-70: Seers' Village Rooftop

Seers' becomes significantly better after completing the hard Kandarin Diary, which increases the XP per lap. However, for Marks grinding, it is comparable to Falador. The lap time is longer, but the spawn rate is slightly higher.

Level 70-80: Pollnivneach Rooftop

This is a solid mid-tier course. The lap time is around 60 seconds, and the failure rate at 70 is manageable. Expect 12-16 Marks per hour here.

Level 80-90: Rellekka Rooftop

Rellekka is one of the best courses for both XP and Marks. The lap time is about 50 seconds, and the spawn rate is good. At 80+ Agility, you can expect 15-18 Marks per hour.

Level 90-99: Ardougne Rooftop

The highest-level rooftop course and the best for Marks of Grace. The Ardougne course has the highest spawn rate in the game, and with the elite Ardougne Diary completed, the spawn rate increases by 25%. At 90+ Agility, expect 18-22 Marks per hour.

Optimal Graceful Grinding Strategy

Based on my experience grinding multiple sets, here is the most efficient path:

  1. Train Agility to 40 at the Gnome Stronghold, Draynor, and Varrock courses.
  2. At level 40, move to Canifis and stay there until you have at least 100 Marks of Grace.
  3. Continue training Agility at your preferred course while picking up Marks.
  4. Buy the Graceful pieces in this order: legs, top, hood, cape, boots, gloves.

I recommend buying legs and top first because they provide the largest weight reduction per Mark spent. The hood and cape are next, followed by boots and gloves. However, if you want the full set bonus as soon as possible, buy the cheapest pieces first to complete the set faster.

Canifis vs. Ardougne: The Debate

A common question is whether to grind Marks at Canifis or wait until Ardougne. Here is my take:

Canifis is better for pure Mark grinding because the lap is so short. Even though the per-lap spawn chance is lower than Ardougne, the sheer number of laps you complete per hour makes up for it. If your only goal is Graceful and you do not care about Agility XP, stay at Canifis as long as possible.

Ardougne is better if you are training to 99 anyway. The XP is much better, and the Marks spawn rate is the highest in the game. If you plan on going for 99 Agility, you will naturally get more than enough Marks at Ardougne.

On my main account, I got my full Graceful set by level 62 Agility, mostly from Canifis. On my ironman, I waited until Ardougne and got it by level 85. Both approaches work. Choose based on your goals.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Running courses too low for your level: If you are 60 Agility and still at Draynor, your Marks spawn rate is reduced by 80%. Move to a higher course immediately.
  • Missing Marks on the ground: Marks despawn after about 10 minutes. Keep your camera angled to spot them, especially on courses with multiple spawn locations.
  • Buying pieces in the wrong order: Legs and top give the most weight reduction. Buy them first unless you need the full set bonus urgently.
  • Not using summer pies: If you are close to the next course requirement, a summer pie can boost your Agility by 5 levels. This is great for accessing better courses early.
  • Ignoring the full set bonus: The 30% run energy restoration only applies when wearing all six pieces. Do not mix Graceful with other gear unless necessary.

Time-Saving Tips

  • Stamina potions: These restore run energy and are invaluable for long agility sessions. Bring one dose per inventory of food.
  • Graceful before heavy questing: If you plan on doing a lot of quests, get Graceful first. The time saved running around is massive.
  • Ardougne Diary: Complete the elite Ardougne Diary for a 25% increased Marks spawn rate. It is a long-term goal worth pursuing.
  • Canifis is AFK-friendly: The short lap at Canifis means you can watch videos or do other tasks while grinding. It is one of the more relaxed training methods.
  • Bank your Marks: Marks of Grace stack in your inventory. You do not need to bank them, but if you die, you lose them. Be careful in the Wilderness.

Recoloring Your Graceful

Once you have the full set, you can recolor it by spending additional Marks of Grace and completing various content:

  • Brimhaven Agility Arena: Dark blue recolor
  • Hallowed Sepulchre: Dark purple recolor
  • Trailblazer League: Various colors (if available)

These are purely cosmetic but look fantastic. The Brimhaven recolor is my personal favorite.

Final Thoughts

The Graceful outfit is one of the best time investments you can make in Old School RuneScape. The hours spent grinding rooftop agility pay off every single time you run across the map without stopping to rest. Whether you are a new player working on your first set or a veteran creating a new account, follow this guide, pick the right course for your level, and you will have your full Graceful set in no time. Happy grinding.
