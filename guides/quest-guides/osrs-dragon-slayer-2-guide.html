OSRS How to Finish Dragon Slayer 2 Guide

The ultimate grandmaster quest guide to defeating Galvek and unlocking Vorkath

Updated: May 2026 12 min read

Dragon Slayer II is the sequel to one of the most iconic quests in RuneScape history, and it lives up to the legacy. As a grandmaster quest, it demands significant preparation, high-level stats, and mastery of multiple boss fights. The reward, however, is well worth the effort: access to Vorkath, one of the best money-making bosses in the game, plus the Ava's Assembler upgrade and Mythical Cape. I have completed this quest on three separate accounts, and each time I discover something new. In this complete guide, I will walk you through every requirement, every puzzle, and every boss fight, with strategies that work for both experienced players and those attempting their first grandmaster quest.

Quest and Skill Requirements

Dragon Slayer II has some of the highest requirements in the game. Make sure you meet all of these before starting:

Quest Requirements:

  • Dragon Slayer I
  • Legends' Quest
  • Dream Mentor
  • A Tail of Two Cats
  • Animal Magnetism
  • Ghosts Ahoy
  • Bone Voyage
  • Client of Kourend

Skill Requirements:

  • 200 Quest Points
  • 75 Magic
  • 70 Smithing
  • 68 Mining
  • 62 Crafting
  • 60 Agility
  • 60 Thieving
  • 50 Construction
  • 50 Hitpoints

The 200 Quest Points requirement is the real gatekeeper here. If you are not there yet, focus on completing shorter quests to reach the threshold. The skill requirements are high but achievable with dedicated training.

Required Items for the Quest

  • About 50,000 coins for various fees
  • A pickaxe
  • Dragon bones
  • Molten glass
  • 2 mithril bars
  • Anti-dragon shield or Dragonfire shield
  • Plenty of food: sharks or better
  • Super combat potions or ranging potions
  • Antidote++ or antivenom
  • Teleport runes and jewelry

Starting the Quest

Begin by talking to Alec Kincade outside the Myths' Guild, located on the southwestern coast of Feldip Hills. You need a Quest Cape or 200 Quest Points to enter. Alec will tell you about a new threat involving dragons and direct you to investigate ancient dragonkin ruins.

The early part of the quest involves a lot of running around and talking to NPCs. You will visit the Varrock Museum, the Wizards' Tower, and several other locations to gather information. This section is straightforward, so I will focus on the challenging parts.

The Key Boss Fights

Dragon Slayer II features multiple boss fights, but three are particularly challenging: the three dragon bosses in the ancient cavern, Vorkath, and the final fight against Galvek. Let me break down each one.

The Three Dragon Bosses

During the quest, you must defeat three powerful dragons in the ancient cavern: a green dragon, a blue dragon, and a red dragon. Each has unique mechanics.

Green Dragon: This dragon uses ranged and melee attacks. Use Protect from Missiles and attack with melee or ranged. It is the easiest of the three.

Blue Dragon: This dragon uses magic and melee. Use Protect from Magic and bring an anti-dragon shield. Its magic attack can freeze you in place, so keep your health high.

Red Dragon: The hardest of the three. It uses all three combat styles and has a special fire attack that deals massive damage. Use Protect from Magic, wear your best dragonfire protection, and eat whenever your health drops below 50. Bring a super antifire potion if you have the Herblore level.

These fights are essentially gear checks. If you have decent combat stats and proper protection, they are manageable. The red dragon is the only one that might give you trouble.

Vorkath

Vorkath is a blue dragon boss that you fight on the island of Ungael. This fight is a preview of the post-quest Vorkath you will be farming for money, but the quest version is slightly easier.

Vorkath Mechanics

  • Standard attacks: Magic, ranged, and melee. He randomly switches between them.
  • Dragonfire: Standard dragonfire attack. Use anti-dragon protection.
  • Venom: Vorkath can venom you. Bring antivenom or serpentine helm.
  • Acid phase: Vorkath spits acid pools. Stand on the acid to avoid his rapid-fire attack, but move off quickly to minimize damage.
  • Zombified spawn: Vorkath summons a zombified spawn that walks toward you and explodes. You must kill it with a crumble undead spell or it will deal massive damage.
  • Freeze attack: Vorkath freezes you and launches a fireball. Click away immediately when unfrozen to dodge the fireball.

Vorkath Strategy

The most efficient way to kill Vorkath is with a Dragon Hunter Crossbow or a Toxic Blowpipe. Here is the setup I use:

  • Weapon: Dragon Hunter Crossbow with ruby dragon bolts (e) for most of the fight, switch to diamond dragon bolts (e) under 30% health.
  • Armor: Elite Void with ranged helm, or your best ranged gear.
  • Shield: Anti-dragon shield or Dragonfire ward.
  • Special attack: Dragon warhammer or Bandos godsword to lower defense, then switch back to crossbow.

During the acid phase, walk on the acid tiles to avoid the rapid-fire attack. During the zombified spawn phase, immediately cast crumble undead. The spawn dies in one hit. During the freeze attack, click on the ground to move as soon as you are unfrozen.

The quest version of Vorkath has 640 hitpoints, compared to 750 for the post-quest version. It is a good practice run for the real thing.

The Final Fight: Galvek

Galvek is the final boss of Dragon Slayer II and one of the most mechanically complex fights in the game. He is a level 608 dragon with multiple phases, each with different attack patterns.

Galvek's Phases

Phase 1 (100-80% health): Galvek uses standard dragonfire, ranged, and melee attacks. He also launches fire bombs that deal area-of-effect damage. Move away from the fire markers on the ground.

Phase 2 (80-60% health): Galvek adds a special attack where he pulls you toward him with a wind attack, then launches a fireball. When you see the wind animation, run away from him immediately.

Phase 3 (60-40% health): Galvek starts using a tsunami attack. He summons waves of water that push you back. Stand behind the rocks in the arena to avoid being pushed into the water, which deals heavy damage.

Phase 4 (40-20% health): Galvek uses a cage attack that traps you in place, then launches a massive fireball. When trapped, eat to full health and pray. The fireball deals damage based on your current health, so being at full health is essential.

Phase 5 (20-0% health): Galvek combines all previous mechanics. This is the most chaotic phase. Stay calm, focus on dodging mechanics, and attack when safe.

Galvek Strategy

Here is the setup I recommend:

  • Ranged method (recommended): Dragon Hunter Crossbow with ruby and diamond bolts, elite void or best ranged armor, archers ring.
  • Melee method: Dragon Hunter Lance with bandos armor and berserker ring. Higher risk but faster kills if you know the mechanics.
  • Food: At least 20 sharks or better. Manta rays or dark crabs are ideal.
  • Potions: 4-5 ranging potions or super combat potions, 2-3 prayer potions, 1 antivenom.
  • Shield: Anti-dragon shield or Dragonfire ward is mandatory.

The key to Galvek is patience. Do not greed for damage. Focus on surviving his mechanics, and the damage will come naturally. I died three times on my first Galvek attempt because I was too aggressive. On my fourth try, I focused entirely on dodging and killed him with food to spare.

Common Galvek Mistakes

  • Standing in fire: The fire bomb markers are clearly visible. Move immediately when you see them.
  • Not running from the wind pull: When Galvek pulls you, run in the opposite direction. The fireball that follows hits hard.
  • Getting pushed into water: During the tsunami phase, always position yourself near a rock.
  • Not eating before the cage: The cage fireball can one-shot you if you are not at full health.
  • Panicking in phase 5: Phase 5 is chaotic, but the mechanics are the same. Stay calm and execute.

Quest Rewards

Upon completing Dragon Slayer II, you receive:

  • 5 Quest Points
  • Access to the Myths' Guild
  • Access to Vorkath as a repeatable boss
  • Ava's Assembler (upgrade from Ava's Accumulator)
  • Mythical Cape
  • Ability to craft Super Antifire potions
  • Ability to use the Corsair Cove bank
  • 25,000 Smithing XP
  • 18,000 Mining XP
  • 15,000 Agility XP
  • 15,000 Thieving XP

The Myths' Guild is a massive reward on its own. It contains a bank, an altar, a furnace, a wrath altar for runecrafting, and a portal to the Corsair Cove Resource Area. It is one of the most convenient hubs in the game.

Vorkath is the real moneymaker, though. With proper gear, you can earn 2-4 million gold per hour at Vorkath, making it one of the best consistent money-making methods in OSRS.

Time-Saving Tips

  • Complete all prerequisite quests before starting. Nothing is worse than being gated mid-quest.
  • Bring a stamina potion for the running sections. There is a lot of back-and-forth travel.
  • Watch a video of the Galvek fight before attempting it. Visual learning helps immensely.
  • Use the Mythical Cape teleport after the quest for quick access to the Myths' Guild.
  • Invest in a Dragon Hunter Crossbow before farming Vorkath. It makes the fight significantly faster and more profitable.

Final Thoughts

Dragon Slayer II is a challenging but incredibly rewarding quest. It represents the pinnacle of quest design in Old School RuneScape, combining an engaging story with difficult but fair boss fights. The rewards permanently upgrade your account, from the Myths' Guild hub to the Vorkath money printer. Take your time with the preparation, learn the boss mechanics, and do not get discouraged by a few deaths. Galvek is tough, but he is beatable. Good luck, and enjoy your new Mythical Cape.
