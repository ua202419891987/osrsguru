OSRS Barrows Gloves Guide 2026 — Recipe for Disaster Complete Walkthrough

The definitive guide to unlocking Barrows Gloves — the best-in-slot gloves that carry you through the entire mid-game and beyond

Updated: May 2026 14 min read

Barrows Gloves are the single most important untradeable upgrade in Old School RuneScape. They offer the best all-around offensive and defensive stats for gloves across all three combat styles, and they remain competitive even against late-game options like Ferocious Gloves and Tormented Bracelet. Unlocking them requires completing Recipe for Disaster — the longest and most quest-heavy challenge in the game. But trust me, it is worth every minute. This guide will walk you through every subquest, every requirement, and every fight so you can claim your Barrows Gloves without missing a step.

Why Barrows Gloves Are Worth It

Let me put this in perspective: Barrows Gloves provide +12 to all attack bonuses (Stab, Slash, Crush, Range, Magic) and +12 Strength bonus. For comparison, a Combat Bracelet gives +7 attack and +7 Strength. That is a massive gap, and it affects nearly every piece of PvM content in the game. Barrows Gloves are not just "good for their cost" — they are best-in-slot for most accounts until extreme endgame. Ferocious Gloves beat them for melee by a small margin, and Tormented Bracelet wins for magic, but Barrows Gloves are the hybrid king. You will wear these gloves for hundreds of hours.

Ironman Tip: Barrows Gloves are achievable at a relatively low combat level on an Ironman. The hardest gate is the 175 QP requirement, but by the time you have that many points, your stats will be solid. I got mine at combat level 85 on my Ironman. It is one of the biggest milestones you will hit.

Subquest Order: Easiest to Hardest

Here is the optimal order to tackle the subquests. Start with the easy ones to build momentum, then tackle the challenging ones once you are warmed up.

1. Dwarf Subquest

The easiest subquest by far. Talk to the Dwarf in the Lumbridge dining hall, then head to the Ice Mountain dwarf colony. You need to cook a rock cake for him — just use a bucket of milk, flour, and an egg on the dwarf cook's range. No combat, no danger. Rewards: 1,000 Cooking XP and access to the Dwarf ammo shop in the dining hall.

2. Goblin Generals Subquest

Talk to the Goblin generals in the dining hall, then head to the Goblin Village north of Falador. You need to dye their goblin mail orange, blue, and then combine them. Dyes are obtained from Aggie in Draynor Village (bring onions, redberries, and woad leaves). No combat required. Rewards: 1,000 Cooking and Crafting XP.

3. Pirate Pete Subquest

Head to Port Sarim and talk to Pirate Pete. You need to learn to fish and cook specific crab meat underwater. Bring a fishbowl helmet and diving apparatus (buy from Murphy in Port Sarim for about 200gp). You will dive underwater and catch five different types of crab. Each must be cooked perfectly — if you burn one, you need to catch another. Watch the cooking animation to time it right. Rewards: 1,000 Cooking XP.

4. Lumbridge Guide Subquest

Talk to the Lumbridge Guide in the dining hall. He wants a cake that represents different areas of Gielinor. You need an egg (Saradomin), bucket of milk (Guthix), and flour (Zamorak). Combine them and cook the cake. Then you need a Magic Whistle from the ice area north of Burthorpe (bring runes for the ice path puzzle). Rewards: 2,500 Cooking XP.

5. Evil Dave Subquest

The first subquest with real combat. Talk to Evil Dave in the dining hall, then head to his basement in Edgeville. You need to capture hell-rats with a cat (complete Gertrude's Cat first) and use spice shakers to determine the exact spice combination for his stew. This is the most annoying RNG-based subquest. Bring a cat (overgrown or wily preferred), multiple stews (buy from the Warriors' Guild), and prepare for 20-30 minutes of trial and error. Hell-rats are level 12 but the basement is safe. Rewards: 2,000 Cooking XP.

6. Skrach Uglogwee Subquest

Talk to Skrach in the dining hall, then head to the Feldip Hills. You need to hunt a chompy bird, cook it with toad legs, and create a special jubbly bird dish. Jubbly birds are found near the water in Feldip Hills. Bring ogre bellows (obtained during Big Chompy Bird Hunting), a bow, and arrows. Combat is minimal but the jubbly bird can be annoying to find. Rewards: 1,500 Cooking, 1,500 Range, and 1,500 Woodcutting XP.

7. Awowogei Subquest

This is where things get serious. You must have Monkey Madness I completed. Talk to Awowogei in the dining hall, then head to Ape Atoll. You need your monkey greegree from MM1 to navigate safely. You will cook a stuffed snake dish — requiring snake meat (kill snakes in Ape Atoll dungeon), a banana, and special spices. You also need to navigate the Ape Atoll agility course while carrying the ingredients. Rewards: 10,000 Cooking and 10,000 Agility XP.

8. Sir Amik Varze Subquest

The hardest subquest. This requires 107 Quest Points and the completion of Lost City, Merlin's Crystal, Heroes' Quest, and Legends' Quest. Talk to Sir Amik, then infiltrate the Black Knights' Fortress to gather intel. You will need to disguise yourself as a fortune teller and navigate the fortress. The final fight involves a level 79 Black Knight Titan — bring melee gear and Protect from Melee. This subquest is long but straightforward if you have the quest requirements done. Rewards: 4,000 Cooking, 4,000 Hitpoints XP.

Warning: Do not underestimate the Sir Amik Varze subquest requirement list. Legends' Quest alone has 107 QP requirements including Heroes' Quest, which needs Shield of Arrav and a partner. Plan your questing path early so you do not get stuck on this one last.

9. Final Fight: The Culinaromancer

After completing all eight subquests and reaching 175 QP, speak to the Gypsy in the Lumbridge dining hall. She will warn you about the Culinaromancer, the final boss. This is a multi-phase fight against summoned food monsters:

  1. Agrith-Na-Na (Level 146): A giant banana. Uses weak melee attacks. Pray Melee and this fight is free.
  2. Flambeed (Level 149): A flaming dessert. Uses both melee and magic attacks. Protect from Magic is recommended. Has high magic defence, so use melee or ranged.
  3. Karamel (Level 136): A chocolate golem. Drains your stats. Bring super restore potions. Pray Melee.
  4. Dessourt (Level 121): Uses melee and range. Pray Melee and bring food. Weaker than the previous bosses.
  5. Gelatinnoth Mother (Level 130): Changes colors every few hits — each color is weak to a specific attack style. White = air spells, Brown = earth, Blue = water, Red = fire, Orange = melee, Green = ranged. Bring all combat styles or just use magic and wait out resistance phases.
  6. Culinaromancer (Level 75): The final boss. Despite being low level, he hits hard with magic and summons food minions. Use Protect from Magic, bring ranged or magic gear, and focus the Culinaromancer — ignore the minions. He has low HP and goes down fast if you prioritize him.

Minimum Combat Level: You can complete the final fight at around combat level 70 with good supplies. Bring your best gear, 15-20 high-healing food (sharks or better), 3-4 prayer potions, and a super restore. The Gelatinnoth Mother is the real gatekeeper — you need to handle all combat styles. Many players use Iban's Blast or Fire Blast for the magic phases and a Dragon Scimitar for melee.

Rewards and Culinaromancer's Chest

After defeating the Culinaromancer, speak to the Gypsy to receive your final reward: 1 Quest Point, 20,000 Cooking XP, and access to the Culinaromancer's Chest. The chest allows you to buy the full RFD glove progression:

Ironman-Specific Advice

On an Ironman, Recipe for Disaster is genuinely one of the most satisfying milestones. Here is what you need to know:

  • Quest Point planning is everything. Use the Optimal Quest Guide on the wiki to minimize unnecessary stat training. Every quest you complete naturally builds toward 175 QP.
  • Herblore matters. The Culinaromancer fight will drain your stats. Having prayer potions and super restores ready is critical.
  • Food preparation: Sharks are ideal for the final fight. If you are mid-level, Swordfish and Karambwans are perfectly fine. The bosses do not hit that hard if you pray correctly.
  • Gear consideration: By the time you reach 175 QP on an Ironman, you should have access to Iban's Staff (u), Dragon Scimitar, and Rune Crossbow with Broad Bolts. This is enough for the entire final fight.
  • Don't rush it. Barrows Gloves are amazing, but they are not going anywhere. Focus on questing efficiently and let the QP accumulate naturally.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

  • Starting the subquests before meeting QP requirements: Each subquest has a QP gate. Check the table above before starting.
  • Undersupplying for the Gelatinnoth Mother: This boss requires ALL combat styles. Bring at least two switches — melee weapon and magic weapon at minimum.
  • Forgetting Legends' Quest partner: Heroes' Quest and Legends' Quest require another player. Coordinate early.
  • Not banking between subquests: You can bank between subquests. Do not try to do them all in one inventory.
  • Panic during Culinaromancer fight: The Culinaromancer himself is level 75 and dies fast. Focus him. The minions he summons are annoying but not lethal.

Final Thoughts

Barrows Gloves are the single most impactful upgrade you can earn in the mid-game. They are with you through the Fire Cape, through your first Zulrah kills, through God Wars Dungeon, and well into raids. Do not sleep on Recipe for Disaster — prioritize those quest points, plan your subquest order, and enjoy one of the most rewarding questlines in Old School RuneScape. Good luck.
