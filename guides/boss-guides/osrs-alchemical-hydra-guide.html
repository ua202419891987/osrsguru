OSRS How to Get to Alchemical Hydra: Complete Location & Kill Guide

The Alchemical Hydra is a relatively new addition to the OSRS slayer boss lineup — released with the Kebos Lowlands update — and it's quickly become one of the best slayer bosses for consistent profit. With a 95 Slayer requirement, it's also one of the highest-level bosses in the game, but the fight itself is one of the most learnable boss encounters OSRS has to offer. Every mechanic is clearly telegraphed, there is zero RNG in the attack pattern, and once you learn the rotation, you can practically do Hydra with your eyes closed. This guide covers how to reach the dungeon, what gear to bring, and the exact step-by-step kill flow for Alchemical Hydra in 2026.

Prerequisites: The 95 Slayer Gate

The Alchemical Hydra requires 95 Slayer to fight, and you must be assigned a Hydra slayer task by Konar quo Maten. You cannot boost to 95 with wild pies — this is a base level requirement. You also need to have completed the Kebos Lowlands introduction quest (which is a short, dialogue-heavy quest that introduces the region, no combat).

If you don't have 95 Slayer yet, the best way to train is through efficient Slayer tasks at Duradel with a block list that removes low-XP tasks (Hellhounds, Spiritual Creatures, Black Demons, etc.) and extends high-XP tasks (Nechryaels, Dust Devils, Smoke Devils). With an efficient block list and cannoning/bursting tasks where possible, you can expect 50K-70K Slayer XP per hour on the grind to 95.

Konar Tasks Only: Unlike other slayer bosses, the Alchemical Hydra can ONLY be fought on a Konar task. Other slayer masters do not assign Hydras. If you normally use Duradel or Nieve, you'll need to switch to Konar for your Hydra tasks specifically. Konar's assignment list is somewhat random, so you may need to skip several tasks before landing a Hydra task.

Getting to the Alchemical Hydra Dungeon

The Hydra is located in the Karuulm Slayer Dungeon beneath Mount Karuulm in the Kebos Lowlands. This area is in the far western part of Zeah, south of the Farming Guild. There are three main ways to get there:

Method 1: Fairy Ring (Fastest, No Requirements)

  1. Use a fairy ring with code CIS. This places you directly at the foot of Mount Karuulm, literally right next to the dungeon entrance.
  2. Walk south about 15 tiles to the large staircase leading up the mountain.
  3. Instead of climbing the mountain, enter the cave mouth at the base — this is the Karuulm Slayer Dungeon entrance.
  4. Inside the dungeon, run northeast past the Wyrm chamber and the drakes, then go north through the tunnel until you reach the Hydra chamber at the northernmost part of the dungeon.

Method 2: Skills Necklace (Most Convenient)

  1. Use a Skills Necklace and select the "Farming Guild" teleport option.
  2. From the Farming Guild, run south across the bridge, then continue south past the lizardmen until you see Mount Karuulm on your left (east side).
  3. The cave entrance is at the base of the mountain. Enter and navigate to the northernmost chamber.

Method 3: POH Portal (Ultimate Convenience)

If you have high Construction (82+), you can build a portal chamber with a Farming Guild portal. This is the fastest banking method — teleport home, hit your pool, and portal back to the Farming Guild for a 30-second bank cycle. For anyone planning to grind Hydra long-term (for the pet or the claw drop), this is worth the construction investment.

Alchemical Hydra Attack Mechanics: The Color-Coded System

The entire Alchemical Hydra fight revolves around a color system. The Hydra has four phases, and in each phase the boss changes color — and each color determines what attack patterns the boss uses and what combat style you should use. The colors and phases are:

At each phase transition (when the Hydra's HP reaches a 25% threshold), the boss will roar and turn invulnerable for a brief moment while it changes color. During this transition, two flame walls will spawn and sweep across the room. You must stand between the walls (they move toward each other) or step into a gap in the wall. If a flame wall touches you, you take 30+ damage instantly. The exact position of the walls changes each phase, so you need to pay attention to where they spawn and move accordingly.

The Poison Attack (Phase 2+ Only)

Starting in Phase 2, the Hydra will periodically spit out three pools of poison onto specific tiles. After 3 ticks, these pools explode and deal heavy poison damage to anything standing on those tiles. You'll see the tiles glow green for about 3 ticks before the explosion. Simply step onto a clean tile and you're safe. The poison pools persist on the ground and deal damage-over-time if walked through, so try to keep your movement to a clean area of the room.

The Lightning Attack (Phase 4 Only)

In the final phase, the Hydra will target a tile with a lightning marker — a yellow swirl on the floor. After 2 ticks, lightning strikes that tile and deals heavy damage. You then need to move to any different tile, because another lightning strike hits your previous position after 1 more tick. The rhythm is: lightning marks a tile, you step off it, then immediately the tile you just left gets struck. Continue this shuffle-step pattern until the lightning sequence ends (usually 6-8 strikes).

Hydra's Most Dangerous Moment: The phase transition is where most players die. The flame walls spawn while you're also trying to figure out what color the boss just turned. My first dozen deaths were all during phase transitions because I was watching the Hydra's color instead of the flame walls. Watch the walls first, change prayers second. You have about 5 ticks to get your prayer right; you have about 2 ticks before a flame wall touches you. Prioritize the wall.

Gear Setup: Ranged and Magic Hybrid

Because the Hydra alternates between Ranged and Magic weaknesses, you'll need a Ranged and Magic switch for each phase. The most efficient setup involves wearing hybrid gear (like Blessed D'hide or Armadyl) and switching only your weapon plus amulet:

Inventory Setup

  • 3-4 Prayer Potions (4) — you'll camp protection prayers the entire fight
  • 1 Antidote++ (4) — poison from Phase 2+ is persistent
  • Blowpipe with scales and darts — around 200 darts per kill
  • Trident of the Swamp with runes — death and chaos runes primarily
  • 12-14 Sharks or Manta Rays
  • 1-click teleport — always bring one; getting comboed by flame wall + lightning is instant death

Step-by-Step Kill Flow

  1. Enter and pray Ranged (Green Phase). Use Blowpipe. The Hydra attacks with Ranged and occasional magic. The Ranged attack is more frequent so protect against it.
  2. Watch for HP threshold at 75%. When the boss hits 75%, back up to the center area of the room. Two flame walls will spawn — find the gap between them and stand there. The Hydra will turn Blue.
  3. Switch to Magic and Protect from Magic (Blue Phase). Use Trident. Dodge the three poison pools that spawn every 4-5 attacks. The floor tiles will glow green — step aside immediately.
  4. Watch for HP threshold at 50%. Flame walls again. The Hydra turns Red.
  5. Switch back to Blowpipe, pray Ranged (Red Phase). Same as Green phase but with higher damage output from the boss. Poison pools continue.
  6. Watch for HP threshold at 25%. Final flame walls. Hydra turns Grey.
  7. Final phase: Magic, Protect from Missiles (Grey Phase). This is the hardest part — the Hydra uses the lightning shuffle attack where you need to continuously step off marked tiles. Stay calm, keep attacking between steps, and eat when HP drops below 40 to account for any missed lightning dodges.
  8. Kill and loot. The Hydra drops its loot on the ground in a pile. Loot it, then either climb the rope to leave the instance or stay for your next kill if you have remaining supplies.

Even without uniques, the Alchemical Hydra drops a ton of alchable items, noted bones, and rune equipment. The average non-unique kill is worth roughly 80K-100K, making this one of the highest regular-DPH bosses in the game. At 20-25 kills per hour (which is realistic with 90+ stats), you're looking at roughly 2M-2.5M per hour from regular drops alone. Add in the chance at a Hydra's Claw and the effective hourly rate jumps to 3.5M+ over the long term.

Hydra's Claw Strategy: The claw is the primary reason to farm Hydra. At 1/1,000, it's not absurdly rare, and at 55M it's a massive payday. Many players set a goal of 1,000 kills and hope for the claw. If you don't get it by 1,000 KC, you've still made roughly 80M-100M from regular drops in the process. It's one of the few bosses where going dry doesn't feel awful because the regular loot is genuinely good.
