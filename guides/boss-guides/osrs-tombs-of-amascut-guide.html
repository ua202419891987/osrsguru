OSRS Tombs of Amascut Guide 2026 — TOA Beginner Raid Walkthrough

Tombs of Amascut (TOA) is the third raid in Old School RuneScape and by far the most accessible. Unlike Chambers of Xeric and Theatre of Blood, TOA scales to your skill level through the Invocation system. You can run Entry Mode at 50 combat with minimal gear, or you can push Expert Mode at 500+ invocation for the chance at the fabled Tumeken's Shadow. For Ironmen especially, TOA is the raid you will spend the most time in — it is solo-friendly, scales beautifully, and drops the Osmumten's Fang, one of the most important melee upgrades in the game. This guide will take you from your first Entry Mode clear to comfortably farming Normal Mode.

Invocation System Explained

Invocations are optional difficulty modifiers. You select them before starting the raid. Each invocation adds to your total invocation level. Higher invocation = harder mechanics + better loot chances. Here are the essential invocations for beginners:

For your first entry mode run, turn on Softcore Run + Walk the Path + On a Diet (+ Lively Larvae). This puts you at 70 invocation — well within Entry Mode. As you improve, add invocations one at a time.

Path Leveling Strategy

TOA has four boss paths: Kephri (Scarab), Baba (Baboon), Akkha (Human), and Zebak (Crocodile). You must complete all four before facing the Wardens. The order matters because of the path leveling system. Each path has a level (0-4+), and higher levels make that boss harder but increase your overall loot potential. The first path you choose gets +0, second gets +1, third gets +2, fourth gets +3.

Recommended route for beginners: Kephri → Baba → Akkha → Zebak

  • Kephri first (Level 0): Kephri is the simplest boss mechanically. Doing her at level 0 keeps the fight short and safe. You should have full supplies going into this fight.
  • Baba second (Level 1): Baba hits hard but is predictable. The boulder skip mechanic is consistent at level 1. Use your supplies but save prayer pots.
  • Akkha third (Level 2): Akkha's memory puzzle becomes harder at higher levels. At level 2, you can still brute force through mistakes. Save your Ambrosia doses for here.
  • Zebak fourth (Level 3): Zebak's room hazards intensify at high levels, but the fight itself is the most mechanical and least RNG-dependent. By this point, you should be comfortable with movement and can focus on the fight.
Path Level Alternative: Some players prefer Zebak first because it has the most consistent damage output and they want to face it at level 0. If Zebak is the boss you struggle with most, simply swap its position with Kephri. The optimal path is whichever order you die least in.

Boss Room Mechanics

Kephri (The Scarab)

Kephri is a stationary boss that summons scarabs and fires projectiles. The fight has three phases:

  • Phase 1: Kephri fires dung balls that push you back. Dodge these by moving sideways. Swarms of agile scarabs spawn — kill them with ranged/magic before they reach Kephri (they heal her if they reach her). Use melee on Kephri when no scarabs are alive.
  • Phase 2: Kephri shields and fires larger projectiles. Stand behind the shield scarab corpses on the ground for cover. Kill the agile scarabs that spawn while dodging projectiles.
  • Phase 3: Same as Phase 1 but faster scarab spawns. At low HP, Kephri fires a massive egg that spawns elite scarabs — kill it immediately. Then finish the boss.

Baba (The Baboon)

Baba is a melee boss that throws boulders and creates shockwaves. Key mechanics:

  • Boulder Phase: Baba throws a boulder that rolls across the room. Hide behind the pillars in the arena. If you stand behind a pillar, the boulder breaks on it and you take zero damage.
  • Shockwave: Baba slams the ground, creating a shockwave ring that expands outward. Time your jump — click the ground, then click Baba as the ring reaches you. You will jump over the shockwave and take no damage.
  • Baboon Minions: Baba occasionally summons baboons. Kill them before they reach you — they can combo with Baba's attacks if ignored.
  • Red-X Method (Optional): Use ranged or magic from a distance. Baba has no ranged attack, so maintaining distance reduces damage taken. Red-X walking can kite Baba indefinitely.
Baba Boulder Trick: If you mis-time the pillar hide and the boulder hits you, it deals 40-60 damage. This is the single most common death at Baba. Watch the boulder carefully and position yourself behind a pillar the moment you see the animation start. You have about 3-4 ticks to react.

Akkha (The Human)

Akkha is the most mechanically complex of the four demi-bosses. Three main mechanics:

  • Memory Puzzle: Akkha teleports to the center and shows a sequence of floor tiles lighting up. Repeat the sequence by standing on the tiles in the correct order. At higher invocation levels, the sequence is longer and faster. If you fail, Akkha hits you for 30+ damage.
  • Orb Phase: Akkha becomes invulnerable and orbs circle the room. Destroy the orbs with ranged or magic while dodging Akkha's shadow attacks. Each destroyed orb damages Akkha when the phase ends. This phase is time-limited — destroy as many orbs as possible.
  • Cum Phase (Shadow Attack): Akkha drops a shadow clone that mimics your movements with a delay. It drops dangerous puddles where it walks. Move in deliberate, wide circles to keep the puddles in safe patterns. Stay away from the clone at all times.

During normal fighting, Akkha switches combat styles (melee, range, mage) every few attacks. Use your best DPS — Akkha has no protection prayers, so all styles work. The Fang excels here due to its accuracy.

Zebak (The Crocodile)

Zebak is the most consistent and rhythmic fight. Mechanics:

  • Blood Barrage: Zebak fires a volley of blood projectiles. Stand on marked tiles to avoid them. The safe tiles change each volley.
  • Roar (Wave Attack): Zebak roars and sends a water wave across the arena. Run to the opposite side of the room. If you get hit, you take 30-40 damage and get dragged toward Zebak.
  • Jug Attack: Zebak throws jugs of blood that create damaging puddles. These are easy to dodge but limit your movement space over time.
  • Rock Fall: Rocks drop from the ceiling. Watch for shadows on the ground and move away.

Zebak's attacks happen in a fixed rotation. After a few kills, you will internalize the rhythm. This is the easiest boss to "no-hit" once you learn the patterns.

Puzzle Rooms

Between boss rooms, you encounter four puzzle rooms:

  • Het (Crondis Path): Mine rocks and deposit them into the light beam paths. Walk through the lit path to reach the exit. Avoid walking on dark tiles — they hit for damage. This is the simplest puzzle.
  • Scabaras (Kephri Path): Solve a tile puzzle by matching patterns. There is a reference image on the wall — click tiles to rotate them until they match. If you struggle, use the Puzzle Helper plugin.
  • Apmeken (Baba Path): Kill baboons while keeping your volatile baboon companion alive. It will attack enemies with you. If it dies, you take heavy damage. Use Protect from Melee and focus the shamans first.
  • Crondis (Zebak Path): Water three palm trees by filling water containers at the river. Avoid crocodiles patrolling the river. Bring stamina potions — this room involves a lot of running.

Warden P3/P4 Showdown

After defeating all four demi-bosses, you face the Warden of Amascut — the final boss with four distinct phases. P1 and P2 are straightforward (obelisk destruction and prayer switching against the Warden's attacks). P3 and P4 are the real challenges.

Warden P3 (Skull Phase)

The Warden floats to the center and summons skulls that fly toward you. You must destroy the skulls by using the correct attack style: melee skulls must be meleed, ranged skulls ranged, magic skulls magicked. The skulls converge on you, and if they reach you, you take 20-30 damage each. At higher invocations, skulls spawn faster and in greater numbers.

Strategy: Stand near the center. Switch weapons quickly. Prioritize the skulls closest to you. Use the sound cue — each skull type makes a distinct noise when it spawns.

Warden P4 (Insanity - Optional)

At 300+ invocation (Expert Mode), P4 becomes Insanity. The Warden moves to the center and fires lightning strikes in rapid succession. You must run in a circle around the room, dodging the lightning while dealing damage. The rhythm speeds up over time. This phase is optional at lower invocations — if you are doing Entry or Normal Mode, you can skip it entirely.

Ambrosia Usage: The raid drops supply items called Ambrosia, which heals you to full HP and Prayer instantly. Save these for the Warden fight. You can carry two doses of Ambrosia and they are a lifesaver during P3 skulls or a misplayed P4 lightning dodge. Do not waste Ambrosia on demi-bosses.

Supply Management

Your inventory for TOA should include:

  • 6-8 Saradomin Brews + 6-8 Super Restores (adjust based on your comfort level)
  • 1 Ranging Potion (or Bastion)
  • 1 Super Combat Potion
  • 1 Stamina Potion
  • Gear switches: Minimize to essential items. For Entry Mode, 4-way switches (weapon, body, legs, offhand) per style is enough. For Normal Mode, 5-6 way switches.
  • Pickaxe (for Het puzzle)
  • Rune pouch with combat runes
Supply Trick: TOA drops supplies throughout the raid — Power (restores prayer), Life (heals HP), and Chaos (restores both but with risk). The supplies the raid drops are nearly identical to your inventory supplies. Do not be afraid to use brews and restores early — every boss room has a supply chest that refills some slots.

Ironman Notes

TOA is the most Ironman-friendly raid in OSRS. Here is why:

  • Solo-friendly: You can complete the entire raid solo at any invocation level. No team required.
  • Scaling difficulty: Entry Mode is genuinely easy. You can farm it with mid-game Ironman gear (Iban's Staff, RCB, Dragon Scimitar) and learn the mechanics at your own pace.
  • Fang is transformative: The Osmumten's Fang fixes melee accuracy at high-defence bosses like Corp, Graardor, and Kree'arra. It is arguably the most impactful PvM upgrade an Ironman can get outside of Bowfa.
  • Masori components: Masori armor pieces are tradeable, but you need Armadyl components (from GWD) to fortify them. This gives you a long-term progression path that connects multiple pieces of content.
  • Lightbearer is common: At roughly 1/125, you will likely get a Lightbearer well before Fang or Masori. This ring doubles your special attack regeneration, making Dragon Warhammer and Bone Dagger specs significantly more consistent at every boss in the game.

If you are an Ironman who has completed Song of the Elves and has Bowfa + Crystal Armor, you are already overgeared for Entry and low-invocation Normal Mode TOA. Start running it as soon as you have 80+ combat stats. The Fang will change your account.

Final Tips

  • Learn one boss at a time. Do not try to master all four demi-bosses at once. Run Entry Mode repeatedly and focus on the boss you struggle with most.
  • Watch VODs. Watching experienced players run TOA teaches you movement patterns that are hard to describe in text. Pay attention to where they stand during each boss phase.
  • Use the practice mode. Rush through puzzles and focus on one boss. If you die, analyze what went wrong. Do not blame RNG — every TOA death is solvable with better positioning.
  • Turn off Insanity until you are ready. Expert Mode with Insanity invocation is a massive difficulty spike. Master 300 invocation without Insanity first, then add it when you are comfortable.

Tombs of Amascut is the best-designed content Jagex has released for OSRS. It respects your time, scales to your skill, and rewards improvement. Whether you are a main chasing the Tumeken's Shadow or an Ironman hunting your first Fang, TOA is where you will spend hundreds of hours — and you will enjoy most of them. Good luck in the tombs.
