OSRS Grotesque Guardians Guide for Low Stats: Conquer Dusk and Dawn on a Budget

The Grotesque Guardians are the slayer boss equivalent of a two-for-one deal — you fight TWO bosses simultaneously, Dusk and Dawn, and you need to manage separate mechanics for each. It sounds overwhelming, and my first few attempts were pure chaos as I forgot which color orb meant which mechanic. But here's the secret: the Grotesque Guardians have predictable attack patterns that never change, and once you learn the rhythm, they're one of the most relaxing slayer bosses in the game. This guide is specifically written for players with lower combat stats (75-85 range) who want to farm Grotesque Guardians without max gear or a 100M bank.

Prerequisites

You need 75 Slayer to be assigned Gargoyles as a task, and you need to be on a Gargoyle task to access the Grotesque Guardians boss room. You also need a Brittle Key, which drops from regular Gargoyles. The drop rate is 1/150 from regular Gargoyles in the Slayer Tower, so expect to kill a few hundred before you get one. Once you have the key, it's permanently unlocked on your key ring.

To reach the boss room, go to the Slayer Tower roof (top floor, climb the ladder at the end of the gargoyle area on the second floor) and use your Brittle Key on the roof trapdoor. The boss room is instanced, so you won't be competing with other players.

Recommended Low Stats

  • 75+ Attack and Strength — or 75+ Ranged. Both work; melee is technically more efficient but Ranged is safer
  • 70+ Defence — you'll be tanking ranged attacks from Dawn the entire fight
  • 70+ Hitpoints — the orb special attack can hit 30+ if you mess up
  • 43+ Prayer — Protect from Melee and Protect from Missiles are essential

The Dual-Boss Mechanics: Who Does What

The Grotesque Guardians fight has four phases, and Dusk and Dawn each have distinct roles in each phase. Understanding the rhythm is 80% of the fight:

Phase 1: Ranged Dawn + Dormant Dusk

When you enter the room, Dawn is active and flying around the room, hitting you with Ranged attacks. Dusk sits dormant on the ground and doesn't move or attack. Your goal is to damage Dawn while praying Protect from Missiles. Dawn hovers in the air and shoots light orbs at you — predictably, every 3-4 attacks. At 75 Ranged, a kill on this phase takes about 60-90 seconds with a Blowpipe.

Dawn's special attack occurs when she drops to roughly 55% HP: she'll start throwing lightning orbs across the arena that travel in a straight line. You need to move out of the orb's path. The orbs are telegraphed by a glowing tile roughly 2 seconds before they launch. Watch the floor, not Dawn's health bar, and you'll avoid every orb.

Phase 2: Dusk Wakes Up

After you damage Dawn to about 50% HP, she'll drop from the sky and both bosses become active. Dusk attacks with Melee and Dawn attacks with Ranged simultaneously. Pray Protect from Melee (Dusk hits harder) while tanking Dawn's Ranged attacks. At this point you're fighting both, but the priority target is still Dawn — kill her first because her Ranged damage is harder to mitigate.

During Phase 2, Dusk has a special attack: he raises both arms and slams the ground, creating a shockwave that expands outward. If it hits you, it deals 20-30 damage and stuns you for 2 ticks. To avoid it, simply run to the far corner of the room when you see Dusk raise his arms. The shockwave doesn't reach the full arena — the corners are safe. After the shockwave, run back and resume attacking.

Don't make my mistake: I tried to out-DPS the shockwave on my first attempt, reasoning that one less attack window meant a longer kill and more damage taken overall. The stun + damage from the shockwave cost me three ticks of attacking AND a shark. Just run to the corner. The 2-tick loss from running is way less than the 3-tick stun plus the damage you eat while stunned.

Phase 3: Healing Orbs

When Dawn's HP reaches zero, she doesn't drop dead — she enters a suspended state and starts launching healing orbs toward Dusk. Each orb that reaches Dusk heals him for 90 HP. You need to absorb the orbs yourself by running into them. Each orb you absorb deals 10 damage to you. With 70+ HP, you can eat about 5-6 orbs before needing food. If Dusk were already at 50% HP and he gets healed back to 80%, you're adding a full minute to the fight. Don't let orbs through.

The orb phase lasts about 20 seconds, during which Dawn is invulnerable. After this phase, Dawn finally dies and drops loot. Then it's just you and Dusk for the final phase.

Phase 4: Enraged Dusk

With Dawn dead, Dusk enters an enraged state with higher attack speed and damage. His attacks hit roughly 20% faster and deal 20% more damage. Pray Protect from Melee and finish him off. At low HP (below 30%), Dusk will use a prison attack where translucent walls close in around you, restricting movement to a 3x3 area. If the walls touch you, you take 10 damage per tick. Simply walk out of the box before it closes — the animation gives you about 3 ticks to react. After the prison, it's just a DPS race to the finish.

Which style should low stats players use? Ranged. I know melee gives better DPS at high levels, but at 75-80 combat stats, the safety of being 8 tiles away from Dusk while he throws out his shockwave is worth the slightly slower kills. You can always switch to melee once you've got 20-30 kills and are comfortable with the mechanics.

Inventory Setup

  • 1 Ranging Potion (4) — or 1 Super Combat if meleeing
  • 1 Super Defence (4) — chip damage from Dawn adds up; defence pot helps
  • 2-3 Prayer Potions (4) — you'll be camping Protect prayers the whole fight
  • 1-click teleport — the Slayer Tower is annoying to reclaim items from if you die
  • 14-16 Sharks or Manta Rays — you'll eat about 3-5 per kill at low stats
  • Blowpipe + scales + darts — about 300 scales and 200 darts per kill
  • Slayer Ring or Morytania Legs — for fast banking

Step-by-Step Kill Flow

  1. Enter the room with Protect from Missiles on. Dawn attacks immediately.
  2. Attack Dawn until 55% HP, dodging lightning orbs by watching the floor for glowing tiles. After dodging, immediately drink a Ranging Potion dose if your boost has dropped.
  3. Watch for Dawn dropping — that's Phase 2. Switch to Protect from Melee. Keep attacking Dawn while tanking Dusk's melee hits with prayer.
  4. Run to a corner when Dusk raises his arms (shockwave attack). Then run back and resume.
  5. Kill Dawn for the orb phase. Run into every healing orb you can. Eat after absorbing 4-5 orbs.
  6. Dusk is now enraged. Maintain Protect from Melee. When you see the translucent walls appear, walk out of the prison box before it closes.
  7. Finish Dusk — at this point it's just DPS. No more mechanics to worry about.
  8. Loot and teleport to bank — using Slayer Ring or Morytania Legs to return quickly.

Common Low-Stats Death Reasons

  1. Getting hit by multiple lightning orbs in Phase 1 — Dawn launches 3-4 orbs in sequence. If you dodge the first one but stop on the path of the second, you eat both. Keep moving during the orb barrage.
  2. Forgetting to switch prayer to Melee in Phase 2 — Dusk hits 30+ through no prayer. The transition from Phase 1 to 2 is the most chaotic moment and where players die most often.
  3. Letting healing orbs through to Dusk — each orb heals 90 HP. Letting through 4-5 orbs essentially resets the entire Dusk fight. At low stats with limited supplies, this means you run out of food before the kill.
  4. Getting hit by the shockwave repeatedly — it's always preceded by Dusk raising his arms very visibly. If you're tunnel-visioned on your HP bar or prayer, you'll miss this cue. Train yourself to glance at Dusk every 5 seconds.

Regular drops average about 30K-50K per kill, translating to roughly 800K-1.2M per hour with low stats. This isn't great compared to GP-focused bosses, but you're also getting Slayer XP the whole time. The real value is the slayer XP paired with the occasional big drop — treating Grotesque Guardians as a slayer task bonus rather than a primary money-maker is the right mindset.

Pro Tip for Low Stats Players: Bring a friend. The Grotesque Guardians instance supports up to 2 players, and a second person makes the lightning orb phase trivial (one person tanks orbs, the other DPSes). You both get kill credit for the slayer task. If you're struggling with solos at your stat level, duo until you get the mechanics down.
