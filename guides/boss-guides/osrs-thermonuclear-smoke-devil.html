OSRS How to Get to Thermonuclear Smoke Devil: Location, Setup & Kill Guide

The Thermonuclear Smoke Devil — affectionately called "Thermy" by the community — is one of the slayer-exclusive bosses in OSRS that many players sleep on. It's arguably the easiest slayer boss in the game, has extremely simple mechanics, and drops a pet with a 1/3,000 rate that's one of the coolest looking pets around. The hard part isn't the fight — it's figuring out how to actually get there and what gear you need. I spent a solid twenty minutes running around the Stronghold Slayer Cave my first time, and I want to save you that headache. Here's everything you need to know about reaching and fighting the Thermonuclear Smoke Devil in 2026.

Prerequisites: The Slayer Requirement

Here's the non-negotiable part: you need 93 Slayer to kill the Thermonuclear Smoke Devil. This is one of the highest slayer requirements for any boss in the game, and there's no way around it — no boosts, no wild pies, nothing. You must have a base level of 93. This is why Thermy isn't as commonly farmed as other bosses, because the slayer grind to 93 is a legitimate investment of dozens of hours.

If you ARE at 93 Slayer, you also need a Smoke Devil slayer task assigned by Duradel, Nieve (or Steve), or Konar. You cannot kill the Thermonuclear Smoke Devil off-task. The boss room simply won't let you attack it without an active task. The regular Smoke Devils in the dungeon can be killed at any time, but the Thermonuclear variant in the boss room is task-only.

Task Optimization: Use a Slayer Ring to teleport directly to the Stronghold Slayer Cave. This is the fastest way to reach the Smoke Devil chamber. If you don't have Slayer Rings unlocked (75 Crafting), the next best option is a fairy ring (code BKR), which puts you just outside the Stronghold Slayer Cave entrance near the Morytania swamp.

Getting to the Thermonuclear Smoke Devil: Step by Step

Method 1: Slayer Ring (Fastest)

  1. Craft or buy a Slayer Ring (requires 75 Crafting, or buy them on the GE untradable but craftable).
  2. Rub the ring and select "Stronghold Slayer Cave" from the teleport menu.
  3. You'll arrive at the entrance area. Run north through the cave, past the Aberrant Spectres, past the Bloodvelds, and past the Nechryaels.
  4. The Smoke Devil chamber is in the northeast corner of the cave map. Look for the chamber filled with grey/purple smoke — you can't miss it once you see the dust clouds floating around.
  5. Inside the chamber, there's a crevice on the eastern wall that leads to the Thermonuclear Smoke Devil boss room. The boss room is instanced, so you won't be competing with other players.

Method 2: Fairy Ring (No Slayer Ring)

  1. Use a fairy ring with code BKR.
  2. You'll appear at a small island in the Mort Myre swamp. Run a few steps north to the Stronghold Slayer Cave entrance.
  3. Enter the cave and follow the same route: north, then northeast through the tunnels until you reach the Smoke Devil chamber.
  4. Climb through the crevice in the eastern wall of the chamber.

Method 3: Morytania Legs 3+ (Ironman-Friendly)

  1. Wear Morytania Legs 3 or 4 (reward from the Morytania Diary).
  2. Use the legs' teleport option to go to the Slayer Tower.
  3. From the Slayer Tower, run south, cross the bridge over the River Salve, and run east until you see the Stronghold Slayer Cave entrance.
  4. This is slower than the other two methods but doesn't require any consumable items.

Thermy's Attack Mechanics (They're Simple, But Don't Get Complacent)

The Thermonuclear Smoke Devil has exactly two attack styles, and the fight is extremely straightforward compared to bosses like Zulrah or Vorkath. That said, let's not get overconfident — the attacks hit hard if you're not prepared.

Ranged Smoke Attack

Thermy launches a ball of smoke at you that deals up to 23 damage through prayer. This is the attack you'll see most often. It's Ranged-based but hits through Protect from Missiles, which is why most guides recommend praying Protect from Magic instead and tanking the Ranged hits. The ranged attack is more frequent but hits less hard than the magic blast.

Magic Smoke Blast

A larger, brighter smoke explosion that deals up to 55 damage. Yes, fifty-five. This is the attack you absolutely must pray against. Protect from Magic reduces this to zero (or near zero with some gear). If you miss a prayer flick and this hits you unprotected, you're eating a shark immediately.

Because the magic attack is the only one that poses a real threat, the meta strategy is simple: pray Protect from Magic, wear your best Ranged defence gear, and just tank the Ranged attacks while you DPS Thermy down. The fight is over in 30-60 seconds with decent gear, so supplies aren't a major concern.

Thermy is extremely weak to Magic, and using Magic gives you a massive accuracy bonus. With a budget setup using Ahrim's and a Trident of the Swamp, kills take about 45-60 seconds. With max gear (Harmonised Staff + Ancestral), kills are around 15-25 seconds. The difference in money per hour is substantial because faster kills mean more kills per task.

Don't make my mistake: I showed up to Thermy in full Bandos with a whip because I figured "boss = melee." Two magic blasts later I was in Lumbridge. Thermy has extremely high Melee and Ranged defence. You'll noodle for eternity with melee. Use Magic or don't bother.

Inventory Setup

  • 1 Magic Potion or Imbued Heart — boosted magic accuracy shortens kills noticeably
  • 1 Prayer Potion (4) — one dose per kill roughly, so one full potion covers a trip
  • 5-8 Sharks or better food — the ranged attacks add up over multiple kills
  • Runes for your Trident or Staff — don't forget these; running back to a bank mid-task is embarrassing
  • Slayer Ring — for banking between trips; one ring can teleport you 8 times
  • 1-click teleport — the magic blast can drop you from full HP to dead if you lag at the wrong moment

Kill Strategy (It's Almost Too Simple)

  1. Enter the boss room through the crevice in the Smoke Devil chamber.
  2. Immediately pray Protect from Magic and drink your Magic potion.
  3. Attack Thermy with your Magic weapon. Walk away from him after each attack if you want to safespot — Thermy's movement speed is slow and he loses interest if you move more than 8 tiles away.
  4. Eat when your HP drops below 35 to stay safe from the magic blast. The ranged attacks chip away at about 5-15 damage every few seconds, so one shark per kill is typical.
  5. Loot and repeat until your task is done.

There's really no deeper strategy than that. The boss is designed to be a straightforward DPS check with a high slayer requirement. No special attacks to dodge, no environmental hazards to avoid, no phase transitions. Just stand there and out-DPS it.

Loot Table and Profitability

Thermy's regular loot table is decent for a slayer boss but not spectacular. The real draws are the Smoke Battlestaff (1/512) and the pet (1/3,000). The pet is one of the rarest boss pets in the game and commands serious prestige among OSRS players.

Extend Your Tasks: Unlock the "Bigger and Badder" slayer reward and extend Smoke Devil tasks with Slayer Points. A regular Smoke Devil task of 180-220 kills nets you about 180-220 regular kills before the boss — but if you have the extend reward, you can push that to 270-330, giving you more Thermy attempts per task. More chances at the pet and uniques without needing a new task.

Tying It All Together

The Thermonuclear Smoke Devil is one of those bosses that seems intimidating on paper (93 Slayer! Boss room! High damage!) but turns out to be a pushover once you actually fight it. The real challenge is the slayer grind to 93, not the boss itself. My honest advice: don't specifically grind slayer just for Thermy — you'll be disappointed when you get there and the profit doesn't match the effort. Instead, view Thermy as a fun bonus that pops up during your regular slayer tasks. The pet is a nice flex, the Smoke Battlestaff is a welcome payday, and the kills are so fast you barely notice the time investment.
