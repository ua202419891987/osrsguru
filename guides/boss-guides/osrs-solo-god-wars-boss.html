OSRS How to Solo God Wars Bosses for Beginners: Your First Solo Kills

Walking into the God Wars Dungeon for the first time is genuinely terrifying. I remember standing at the rope entrance with my heart pounding, watching maxed players run past in Bandos armor while I clutched my Rune Crossbow like it was a security blanket. The GWD has a reputation for being "team content," but the truth is, three of the four bosses are extremely soloable even at mid-level stats. You just need to know the right techniques. This guide covers the three solo-friendly GWD bosses — General Graardor (Bandos), K'ril Tsutsaroth (Zamorak), and Kree'arra (Armadyl) — with exact gear setups and kill methods for each. Commander Zilyana (Saradomin) is significantly harder to solo and I recommend learning the other three first.

Getting to God Wars Dungeon

There are two main ways to reach the dungeon:

  • Burthorpe Games Room — Take the games necklace teleport to Burthorpe, run north past the Warriors' Guild to find a cave entrance, then navigate the icy path north until you see the big stone doors.
  • Ghouls teleport (Nexus or POH portal) — Much faster. Teleport to Trollheim using the standard spellbook (requires Eadgar's Ruse quest for the Trollheim teleport), run north past the thrower trolls, and climb down the agility shortcut at the northern peak. This puts you directly above the GWD entrance.

Once inside, you'll need ecumenical keys or killcount for each boss's faction to enter their rooms. Ecumenical keys drop from any monster in the Wilderness God Wars Dungeon and let you skip the killcount grind. I highly recommend farming 5-10 keys in advance if you plan on doing multiple trips, because nothing kills motivation faster than getting 40 Zamorak killcount just to die in 30 seconds and lose it all.

Ecumenical Key Farming: Low-risk, high-reward. Wear minimal gear (monk robes), bring a Dragon Scimitar, and kill imps, goblins, and icefiends in the Wilderness GWD. Each key takes about 2-3 minutes of casual killing. Stack 10 keys before heading to the main dungeon and you'll save yourself hours of frustration. They're also tradeable, so if you're flush with cash, buy them on the GE for about 40K each.

General Graardor (Bandos) — The Best Solo Starter Boss

Prerequisites

  • 70+ Strength (the Bandos door requires a warhammer smash, 70 Strength minimum)
  • 80+ Ranged (Ranged method is safest for solo learners)
  • 70+ Defence and Hitpoints
  • 44+ Prayer (Protect from Melee)

The Kiting Method

Graardor is huge, slow, and pathfinds poorly. The strategy for solo is called kiting — you run in a rectangular pattern around the room while attacking with Ranged. Graardor chases you in a straight line but can't keep up if you move diagonally. The pattern is: run to the northern wall, attack, run to the eastern wall, attack, run to the southern door, attack, run to the western wall, attack, repeat. Each time you attack, you fire one or two shots, then click the next corner before Graardor reaches you.

It sounds complicated but after 10 minutes of practice it becomes muscle memory. The critical thing is to never let Graardor get within melee distance. If he does, he'll slam the ground for a massive area-of-effect attack that hits 40+ through prayer. That's a one-way ticket to Lumbridge.

Bring a God item for Bandos faction protection — a Zamorak or Saradomin item. Without it, every Bandos minion in the room will attack you simultaneously, and your trip ends immediately. A Zamorak Blessed D'hide Body solves both the gear and god protection needs at once.

Inventory

  • 3-4 Prayer Potions — the minions after kill drain prayer fast
  • 1-click teleport (Royal Seed Pod is best, otherwise Ring of Dueling)
  • 15-16 Sharks or better food
  • 1 Antidote++ or Anti-venom (not mandatory but helpful for minion poison)

K'ril Tsutsaroth (Zamorak) — Melee with a Twist

Zamorak is the second easiest solo boss at GWD, but the approach is completely different. K'ril hits hard and fast with Melee and has a special attack that drops your prayer by half. The solo meta is a melee tank setup with Prayer being the most precious resource.

Prerequisites

  • 85+ Attack and Strength (or 85+ Ranged if using Ranged method)
  • 75+ Defence
  • 70+ Prayer (higher is better — K'ril's prayer drain is brutal)

Melee Tank Method

Wear your best melee tank gear (Barrows armor) and pray Protect from Melee. Bring an Arclight — it's the single best weapon against K'ril because demons take 70% bonus damage from it. If you don't have Arclight, use a Zamorakian Hasta. The fight is a straightforward DPS check: stand still, attack, eat when low, re-pot when you hear the prayer drain sound effect.

The key to solo Zamorak is understanding that his special attack is on a timer. Every 20 seconds, he'll slam the ground and drain roughly half your current prayer points. You need to have less than 50% prayer before the attack hits, because if your prayer drops to zero, the next attack will one-hit you. This sounds counterintuitive but here's the strategy: keep your prayer at around 30-40 points total. When the drain hits, you drop to 15-20, drink one dose of prayer potion to bring it back to 30-40, and maintain the cycle.

Don't make my mistake: I kept my prayer at 70+ for my first Zamorak attempt, thinking "more prayer = more safety." K'ril's special hit dropped me to 35 prayer, the minions each hit me once, and I was at zero prayer eating sharks while K'ril stacked me out in three hits. The trick is to keep prayer LOW, not high. The lower your prayer when the special hits, the less you lose, and the final prayer amount ends up roughly the same regardless.

Kree'arra (Armadyl) — Ranged Boss, Ranged Tank

Armadyl is the hardest of the three solo GWD bosses but drops the most valuable items. Kree'arra attacks with Ranged and Magic, and three minions also pile on damage. You'll be tanking hits with high Ranged defence while dishing out your own Ranged attacks.

Solo Armadyl Method: Chinchompa Kiting

The most efficient solo method uses Black or Red Chinchompas with full Masori or Armadyl armor. You stand in the corner of the room, let Kree'arra and his minions pile up on one tile, and throw chins for massive area damage. However, this requires 90+ Ranged, 75+ Defence, and about 80M+ in gear. For budget solos, use a Dragon Crossbow with Ruby Bolts (e) and the "hit and corner-hug" method: attack Kree'arra once, then run to the far corner of the room so the minions lose line-of-sight, wait for your attack cooldown, run back and attack again.

Pro Tip for Armadyl: The Door-Altar method is a classic solo strategy where you stand in the doorframe of the boss room entrance and pray Ranged, attacking Kree'arra while the minions can't reach you. This requires precise positioning — specifically, you stand one tile inside the doorframe so your character is technically "in the room" but the minions' pathfinding registers you as unreachable. Practice the positioning without attacking first so you don't waste supplies.

Common Deaths and How to Avoid Them

  1. Running out of prayer in the killcount area — Always bring 2 extra prayer potions specifically for the KC grind. The spiritual mages and rangers hit hard through prayer and can drain your supplies before you even reach the boss.
  2. Forgetting God protection items — Each GWD faction requires a specific god item. Without one, every minion of that faction becomes aggressive. Bring a Bandos item, Zamorak item, Armadyl item, and Saradomin item (one piece for each can overlap, like wearing a Zamorak d'hide body and holding an Armadyl crossbow).
  3. Getting stacked out during the kiting pattern transition — At Bandos, the moment where you click the next corner is the most dangerous. If you misclick or get stunned by a minion, Graardor catches up. Always have full HP before clicking a corner.
  4. Underestimating the minions after boss death — The boss's minions don't despawn when the boss dies. They keep attacking. Keep your prayer on after the boss drops, finish off the minions, then loot. I've died to a Sergeant Grimspike post-kill more times than I care to admit.
