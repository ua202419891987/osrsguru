OSRS Sarachnis Loot Guide for Ironman: Spider Boss Strategy & Rewards

Sarachnis is the mid-level spider boss introduced with the Forthos Dungeon, and it's genuinely one of the best-designed boss encounters in OSRS for ironman accounts. It doesn't have crazy stat requirements, the mechanics are simple but engaging, and the loot table is practically custom-tailored for ironmen — dragon bones, red spider eggs, grubby keys, and the Sarachnis cudgel which is a top-tier crush weapon for mid-game accounts. My ironman account sat at Sarachnis for a good 300 kills before walking away with the cudgel, and I learned quite a bit about what works and what doesn't. Here's the full breakdown for 2026.

Getting to Sarachnis

Sarachnis lives on the bottom floor of the Forthos Dungeon in Hosidius, which is in the kingdom of Great Kourend. Getting there involves navigating the dungeon and passing through several gates that require specific items or activities:

  1. Teleport to Hosidius using a Xeric's Talisman ("Xeric's Glade" option). Run south to the Forthos Dungeon entrance (a cave mouth in the cliffs).
  2. Inside the dungeon, run west past the red dragons until you reach the first locked gate. You'll need a lockpick or 57 Thieving to pass.
  3. Continue southwest and you'll reach a second gate that requires a log to repair a broken bridge (bring a regular log — any type works).
  4. Past the bridge, run west through the spider chamber. The Sarachnis boss lair entrance is a web-covered portal at the far end of the spider room.

Alternatively, if you have a fairy ring code that can be configured in your POH, the Forthos Dungeon ring location (DIR) can be set up if you've spoken to the relevant NPC. But honestly, just bringing a Xeric's Talisman and a lockpick is the most ironman-friendly approach.

Bring a Lockpick Every Time: The first gate requires passing through each trip. If you forget your lockpick, you're running all the way back to a bank. For ironmen with limited teleports, this is especially painful. Keep a stack of lockpicks in your bank and always double-check before leaving.

Recommended Stats for Ironman

  • 70+ Attack and Strength — Sarachnis is weak to crush, so you want a crush weapon specifically
  • 65+ Defence — you'll be tanking melee hits and swarm damage from spider minions
  • 60+ Prayer — for Chivalry (or 70 for Piety if you have it)
  • 70+ Hitpoints — the melee hits can stack up when minions are swarming
  • 43+ Prayer — Protect from Melee is the core prayer throughout the fight

Sarachnis is one of the most forgiving bosses for ironman stat requirements. If you have a Dragon Mace and 70 Attack/Strength, you can start farming kills. The drops will help your account progress rather than just providing GP.

Notice the emphasis on Prayer bonus (Proselyte, Ardougne Cloak). Sarachnis's fight is prayer-intensive because you'll be camping Protect from Melee. High prayer bonus means fewer prayer potions per trip, which is critical for ironmen who don't have a bottomless supply of ranarr seeds. The Dragon Mace is your best crush weapon as an ironman until you get the Cudgel — its special attack hits hard and accurately against Sarachnis.

Don't make my mistake: I brought a Dragon Scimitar to Sarachnis because it was my best melee weapon. Big mistake. Sarachnis has extremely high slash and stab defence. You'll splat 0s constantly with a scimitar. Crush weapons ONLY. The dragon mace is better here than a whip because of the crush bonus. After 3 kills with the scimitar and zero real damage, I teleported out and crafted a d mace immediately.

Inventory Setup for Ironman

  • 1 Super Attack + Super Strength (or Super Combat) — boosts dramatically increase kill speed
  • 3-4 Prayer Potions (4) — you'll burn about 1 dose per kill without high prayer bonus
  • 1-click teleport — Ring of Dueling or Games Necklace
  • 12-14 Sharks/Karambwans — Karambwans are ideal because ironmen can fish them easily
  • Lockpick — for the first dungeon gate
  • 1 regular Log — for the bridge repair

Sarachnis Attack Mechanics

Sarachnis has a straightforward but punishing attack pattern. Here are the mechanics you need to know:

Standard Attacks

Sarachnis attacks with Melee at a speed of 4 ticks (same as a whip). Pray Protect from Melee at all times. The max hit is roughly 30 damage at base, so if your prayer drops, you're going to feel it immediately. Sarachnis also attacks with Ranged occasionally — the animation is a green magical projectile. These hit up to 15 and aren't worth praying against; just tank them.

Web Wrap (Special Attack)

Roughly every 10 attacks, Sarachnis will trap you in a web, stunning you in place for 5-6 ticks. You cannot move or attack while webbed. During this time, two to four spawnling spiders (level 53) appear around you and start attacking. If you try to click away, your character will attempt to move but just shake in place. You need to wait out the web — don't spam-click. The minions deal about 2-4 damage per hit each, but with 3-4 of them attacking simultaneously, that's 10-15 damage per tick.

The moment the web breaks, Sarachnis charges at you from wherever she was on the map. You have about 2 ticks to re-establish prayer and start attacking again before she reaches you.

Minion Swarm

Every 90 seconds, Sarachnis calls a swarm of 4-6 spider minions. They all pile on you and deal rapid chip damage. This is where the real challenge of Sarachnis comes in — managing the minion swarm while dealing damage to the boss. You have two options: kill the minions (they die in one hit each) or ignore them and focus the boss. With low stats and budget gear, killing them is the safer option, but it slows down your kills. With higher stats, ignoring minions and out-DPSing Sarachnis before they overwhelm you is faster.

The key insight: each minion you kill regenerates 1 HP on Sarachnis. So killing all 4 minions heals the boss for 4 HP. It's marginal, but over a long fight it adds up. The optimal strategy is to kill minions during the web phase (since you're not attacking the boss anyway) and ignore them during active DPS phases.

Kill Strategy Step by Step

  1. Enter the lair with Protect from Melee on. Drink your combat potions immediately — the first 15 seconds are safe as Sarachnis won't web you right away.
  2. Attack Sarachnis with your crush weapon. Use Piety or Chivalry for extra DPS. Watch your prayer level — you'll be burning through it fast.
  3. When Sarachnis screams (the web sound), you're webbed. Use this time to eat food and kill the spawnling spiders that appear. This is a free inventory management window.
  4. After the web breaks, re-establish Protect from Melee (Sarachnis will be charging at you) and resume attacking.
  5. When the minion swarm appears, assess your HP. If above 50, keep attacking the boss. If below 50, kill 2-3 minions to reduce the chip damage, then return to the boss.
  6. Eat proactively, not reactively. The minion swarm plus a melee hit can combo you from 50 HP to 0 in one tick. Keep your HP above 40 at all times.
  7. Finish the kill and loot. Use your teleport to bank, then repeat.

Red spider eggs are the sleeper value from Sarachnis for ironmen. They're a secondary ingredient for Super Restore potions, and ironmen go through thousands of super restores over the life of an account (for bossing, raids, and general PVM). Each Giant Egg Sac drops 100 noted red spider eggs, which at 1/20 translates to roughly 500 eggs per hour of Sarachnis. That's massive for an ironman's herblore progression.

The Grubby Key is another great bonus. It opens the Grubby Chest in the Forthos Dungeon which drops Saradomin Brews, Super Restores, and Ranarr Weeds — all premium ironman supplies. If you stack grubby keys while farming Sarachnis, they serve as a mini-supply cache for later bossing content.

Cudgel Strategy for Ironmen: The Sarachnis Cudgel is worth grinding for because it's the best crush weapon between the Dragon Mace and the Abyssal Bludgeon/ Zamorakian Hasta. The Abyssal Bludgeon requires 85 Slayer and Abyssal Sire kills (a much harder boss), and the Hasta requires Zamorak GWD (team content). The Cudgel is obtainable entirely solo at mid-game stats, and it carries you through Nightmare Zone, Vet'ion, and even some Chambers of Xeric rooms. Set a goal of 400 kills — statistically, you have a ~65% chance of getting the cudgel by then, and you'll accumulate tons of eggs and supplies along the way.

Trip Length and Efficiency

With mid-level ironman stats (75 Attack, 70 Strength, 70 Defence) and a Dragon Mace, expect 3-5 kills per trip using sharks as food. Each kill takes about 2:00-2:30. Trip length is limited by food, not prayer — you'll run out of sharks before you run out of prayer potions with Proselyte gear. Once you get the Cudgel, kills speed up to about 1:30-1:45 and you can push to 5-7 kills per trip.

The optimal trip: bring 12 sharks, 3 prayer potions, expect 4 kills. That's about 10 minutes of combat for roughly 200K GP in alchables, 40-60 dragon bones, and ~20 red spider eggs. Plus one roll at the Cudgel per kill.
