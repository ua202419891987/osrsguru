OSRS Theatre of Blood Guide 2026 — Entry Mode & Normal TOB Walkthrough

The Theatre of Blood has a reputation as the hardest raid in OSRS. And yes, Normal mode TOB is brutal — it's the only raid where one player's mistake can wipe the entire team. But Jagex added Entry Mode in 2021, and it completely changed the accessibility of this content. If you've been avoiding TOB because every guide makes it sound impossible, this guide is for you. We're going to break down Entry Mode first (which you can solo, by the way), then walk through Normal mode mechanics, and cover the gear progression that makes TOB worth learning.

Requirements and Getting Started

There are two mandatory quest requirements to enter the Theatre of Blood:

  • Priest in Peril — unlocks access to Morytania. This is a novice quest with no combat requirements, taking about 15 minutes.
  • A Night at the Theatre — this quest is literally a run through Entry Mode TOB. You can complete it solo in Entry Mode, which is exactly how most players experience the Theatre for the first time. The quest version is even easier than standard Entry Mode because you get checkpoints after each room that resurrect you if you die.

Once you complete A Night at the Theatre, you unlock the full Theatre of Blood. You can enter via the boat in the Meiyerditch caverns (east of Slepe, north of Ver Sinhaza). The quickest route is the Drakan's Medallion teleport to Ver Sinhaza (reward from A Taste of Hope) and running north, or using the Morytania Legs 3/4 teleport to the Slime Pit and running east.

Learn in Entry Mode before touching Normal mode. The rooms in Entry Mode have the same layout and core mechanics as Normal mode — they just hit softer and forgive more mistakes. Spend 5-10 Entry Mode runs learning the flow of each room, the timing of Verzik's attacks, and the Nylo room color matching. Entry Mode also counts toward your TOB KC for Combat Achievement tasks, so it's never wasted time. If you can clear Entry Mode solo without dying, you're ready for Normal mode with a team.

Room-by-Room Mechanics Walkthrough

Room 1: The Maiden of Sugadinti

The Maiden is the first boss and sets the tone for TOB's emphasis on coordinated damage. She sits in the center of the room with 2,500 HP (Normal). The fight has two main mechanics:

  • Blood Spawns (Crabs): Every ~25% of Maiden's HP, she spawns Crablings (Nylocas Matemenos) that crawl toward her. If they reach her, they heal her for the crab's remaining HP. In Normal mode, you must freeze the crabs with Ice Barrage or Entangle and kill them before they unfreeze. In Entry Mode, the crabs have lower HP and getting one to the Maiden isn't run-ending.
  • Blood Spit: Periodically, the Maiden launches blood projectiles at random players. These deal damage on impact and leave blood pools on the floor (which heal the Maiden if she walks over them). In Normal mode, the designated "freezer" (usually the mage) stands in specific tiles to bait the blood pools to the edges of the room.

The Maiden fight is a DPS check. In Normal mode, if your team can't kill the crabs fast enough, the Maiden will out-heal your damage and the room becomes unwinnable. A common strategy is to lure all crabs into a stack (the mage freezes them grouped up), then use chinchompas or burst spells to kill them simultaneously. In Entry Mode, just kill the crabs as they spawn — stacking isn't necessary.

Room 2: The Pestilent Bloat

The Bloat is a massive, slow-moving abomination that patrols a U-shaped hallway. This room is about movement, not DPS. The Bloat has two forms:

  • Walking Phase: The Bloat slowly walks around the room. If you stand in the tiles directly in front of or behind it (its line of sight), it charges forward, dealing massive damage and potentially one-shotting you in Normal mode. Stay on the sides — run around the Bloat using the curved hallway. Attack from the sides only. The Bloat's walking pattern is predictable: it walks clockwise around the room, pausing occasionally.
  • Stationary Phase (Mirror Mechanic): After the Bloat takes enough damage or time passes, it drops to the ground and becomes stationary. A large shadow appears on the floor — this is the Bloat's AoE stomp. Every few ticks, the Bloat stomps and sends out a damage wave to all players in the room. You need to stand behind the pillars (meat hooks along the walls) to block the stomp damage. The stomp timing is telegraphed by the Bloat's animation and a screeching sound — one tick before the stomp, you move behind the pillar, then immediately step out to attack.
Mirror mechanic breakdown: In Normal mode, the stomp damage is applied to everyone who isn't behind a pillar, and it hits for 30-50 damage through prayer. If two players stand behind the same pillar, only one is protected — the other takes full damage. Each player needs their own pillar. The stomp timing is every 3 ticks during the stationary phase. Count "1-2-step out, attack, 1-2-step out, attack" — this rhythm becomes muscle memory after a few runs. In Entry Mode, the stomp damage is reduced significantly and you have more pillars to share.

Room 3: The Nylocas

The Nylocas room is a wave-based encounter where different colored Nylocas spawn and must be killed with their matching combat style. There are three pillars at the back of the room that you must protect — if any pillar falls, the room is failed in Normal mode.

The Nylocas come in three colors, each weak to a specific attack style:

  • Green (Melee Nylocas): Only damaged by crush attacks. They attack the pillars with a rapid attack speed. High priority — kill them first.
  • White (Range Nylocas): Only damaged by ranged attacks. Attack pillars from a distance.
  • Blue (Magic Nylocas): Only damaged by magic attacks. Also attack pillars.

In a 4-man team, the standard role split is: 1 melee player handles greens, 1 ranger handles whites, 1 mage handles blues, and 1 flex player assists whichever lane is struggling. The room has 20 waves total. Wave 20 spawns two "big Nylocas" (Nylocas Vasilias) with higher HP that must be killed to complete the room. Between waves, you can repair pillars with a hammer (bring one!).

In Entry Mode, the spawn rate is slower and Nylocas have less HP. You can solo the room by switching weapons constantly or simply using a Crystal Halberd (which works on all colors at reduced effectiveness). Entry Mode solo Nylo strategy: bring a whip for greens, blowpipe for whites, trident for blues, focus on the color that's attacking the most damaged pillar, and don't panic — you have much more time than you think.

Room 4: Sotetseg

Sotetseg is a large dark beast fought in a square arena with a grid of 4x4 tiles. The mechanics:

  • Auto Attacks: Sotetseg alternates between magic and ranged attacks. The magic attack hits the entire team through prayer. The ranged attack hits one player hard. Protect from Magic should be on by default, and swap to Protect from Missiles when the ranged attack animation plays (Sotetseg rears its head back).
  • Death Ball: Sotetseg launches a large, slow-moving projectile at one player. If it hits, it deals 50+ damage and bounces to nearby players. The targeted player must run away from the team — ideally toward a corner of the room that's far from everyone else. In Entry Mode, the ball damage is reduced and it only hits the targeted player.
  • Maze Mechanic: At 66% and 33% HP, Sotetseg teleports players to a shadow realm maze. One player (randomly chosen) can see the safe path through the maze — this player is marked with a red overlay on their screen. They must lead the team through the maze by standing on the correct tiles. Everyone else follows directly behind the marked player. The maze has lightning that strikes tiles periodically for heavy damage. In Normal mode, touching the shadow (wrong tile) deals 50+ damage and bounces you back. In Entry Mode, the shadow deals ~25 damage.
Maze Callout Tip: If you're the marked player in the maze, call out which direction you're moving (e.g., "North 2, East 3, North 1"). The team follows you single-file. Don't run — walk one tile at a time. If the marked player dies in the Normal mode maze, the team is effectively wiped because nobody else can see the path. The maze is the most team-wipe-prone mechanic in TOB for learner teams. Practice following a caller's movement in Entry Mode until the 3-2-1 rhythm feels natural.

Room 5: Xarpus

Xarpus is a large, insectoid boss in a square room. The fight has two distinct phases:

  • Phase 1 — Screech (Exhume): Xarpus is stationary and cannot be attacked directly. It periodically "screeches," dealing damage to all players and exhuming skeletons from piles on the floor. After the screech, you need to look away from Xarpus (click on the ground away from the boss) to avoid taking heavy damage. The skeletons rise from the floor piles and walk toward Xarpus — if they reach it, they heal Xarpus. Kill skeletons before they reach the boss. The screech timing is every ~8 ticks. In Normal mode, the screech one-shots anyone who doesn't look away. DO NOT look at Xarpus during the screech.
  • Phase 2 — Exposed: After enough skeletons are killed, Xarpus rotates to face the team and becomes vulnerable. It attacks with ranged projectiles and a poison spit AoE. The ranged attacks can be flicked with Protect from Missiles. Xarpus periodically spits poison pools on the floor that deal rapid damage — simply move away from the pools. In this phase, it's a straightforward DPS burn. In Normal mode, Xarpus has a final desperation mechanic at low HP where it shoots poison rapidly — stay calm and finish the kill.

Room 6: Verzik Vitur — The Final Boss

Verzik is the final boss and the most mechanically complex fight in TOB. She has three phases:

Verzik Phase 1 (P1) — Pillar Phase

Verzik is on a raised platform and cannot be directly attacked. You need to use the pillars in the room — attack them with a pickaxe (bring a bronze pickaxe!), and when a pillar is destroyed, Verzik takes damage. During P1, Verzik launches magic attacks at players and occasionally does a lightning attack that strikes random tiles in the room. Move away from highlighted tiles (they glow white 1 tick before lightning strikes). In Normal mode, Verzik also casts a bind on players. P1 ends when all pillars are destroyed.

Verzik Phase 2 (P2) — Webs and Tornados

Verzik descends and becomes attackable. This is the longest phase. Key mechanics:

  • Auto Attacks: Verzik alternates between magic (hits entire room, protect from Magic) and ranged (single target, protect from Missiles). She attacks every 4 ticks.
  • Web Bomb: Verzik throws a web projectile at one player. If it hits, the player is snared and loses 50% of their current HP. The web can bounce to nearby players. The targeted player must step away from the team to prevent the bounce.
  • Purple Nylocas (Nylos): Verzik periodically spawns purple Nylocas that run at her. If they reach her, they heal her. Kill them before they reach Verzik. Purple Nylos are weak to ranged attacks.
  • Yellow Projectile: Verzik launches a yellow orb at one player that deals high damage and applies venom. If you're targeted, eat to full HP before it lands. The yellow attack can be tick-eaten (eat on the exact tick the projectile lands) to mitigate damage.
  • Green Ball (Phase transition): At ~35% HP, Verzik charges a green ball. All players must stack on one tile to split the damage — if fewer than 3 players stack, the ball one-shots whoever it hits. In Entry Mode, the ball damage is massively reduced.

Verzik Phase 3 (P3) — Tornados

At 20% HP, Verzik enters her final phase. She walks toward the nearest player and attacks with powerful melee hits (up to 70 damage in Normal mode). She also summons tornados that circle the room — getting hit by a tornado deals 30+ damage and bounces you across the room. Key mechanics:

  • Melee Attacks: Verzik runs toward a player. When she reaches them, she attacks and pauses briefly. The team moves in a rectangle pattern: one player tanks a hit, runs away, another player tanks the next hit. This is the "Verzik walk" or "tile-dancing" — the tank takes a hit, steps back, the next player steps in.
  • Tornados: Two tornados circle the room. You must walk around the room in a large circle, staying ahead of the tornados while tanking Verzik's hits. Touching a tornado is often fatal in Normal mode due to the bounce causing you to land on another tornado (chain KO).
  • Yellow Pools: Verzik leaves pools of acid on the floor as she walks. Stepping in them deals rapid damage and can stack with tornado hits for a quick KO. Walk around the pools.
P3 is where most learner wipes happen. The tornado + melee dance is genuinely difficult to learn because it requires synchronized movement from the entire team. In Entry Mode, Verzik walks slower, tornados move slower, and melee hits deal less damage — use this to practice the walking rhythm. The key to P3 is staying calm. When you hear the tornado sound cue, don't panic. Walk methodically in a rectangle pattern. In Normal mode, a Verzik melee hit deals 60-75 damage — you need to be above 80 HP at all times in P3 unless you're the designated tank.

Ironman TOB Progression

TOB is critical for ironmen. The Scythe is the BIS melee weapon for nearly all endgame content, and the Rapier, Sang Staff, and Avernic are all account-defining upgrades. The ironman path to TOB:

  1. Complete A Night at the Theatre in Entry Mode. Solo it with whip/blowpipe/trident. The quest version has checkpoints so you literally cannot fail — just keep throwing yourself at each room until you clear it.
  2. Grind Chambers of Xeric (CoX) for Rigour/Augury. Rigour is non-negotiable for Normal mode TOB. It's a ~23% DPS increase for ranged, and you use ranged heavily at Nylos and Verzik. Augury is less critical but nice to have for Maiden freezes and Nylos mage.
  3. Get your gear upgrades: Fang from Tombs of Amascut (great all-purpose melee weapon, lower DPS than Rapier but works until you get Rapier), Blowpipe from Zulrah (mandatory for Nylos), Trident of the Swamp (you'll use this until Sang Staff).
  4. Learn Normal mode with learner-friendly teams. The We Do Raids (WDR) Discord runs learner TOB sessions. Be upfront that you're learning. Most Normal mode learner teams expect you to have completed at least 5-10 Entry Mode runs and know the mechanics of each room.
  5. Target the Rapier first. As an ironman, the Rapier fills the gap between Whip and Scythe and has zero operating cost (no charges). The Avernic is also a strong early target because it's a permanent upgrade.
