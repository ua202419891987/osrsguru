OSRS Fire Cape Guide 2026 — Defeat Jad in the Fight Caves

The Fire Cape is the undisputed rite of passage for every OSRS player. It is the second-best melee cape in the game (only beaten by the Infernal Cape), provides +4 Strength bonus and +1 Prayer, and unlocks the ability to use the Mor Ul Rek inner city. More importantly, earning a Fire Cape means you have conquered the Fight Caves — 63 waves of increasingly difficult TzHaar monsters, culminating in a battle against TzTok-Jad, a level 702 monster that can one-shot you if you misclick a prayer. This guide will get you your first Fire Cape in 2026, whether you are on a main account or an Ironman.

Prerequisites and Requirements

The Fight Caves have no hard quest or skill requirements — you just need to be able to survive. Here are the minimum recommended stats:

  • 75+ Ranged — This is the most important stat. Every level increases DPS and reduces fight time.
  • 70+ Defence — Higher Defence means less food used during waves.
  • 70+ Hitpoints — You need to survive Jad's max hit off-prayer (97 from Ranged, variable from Magic).
  • 44+ Prayer — Eagle Eye is extremely helpful. 60+ for Chivalry if you have the stats. Rigour (74 Prayer) is the holy grail but expensive.

You can technically do this at 61 Ranged with a Rune Crossbow if you are patient, but I strongly recommend getting 75+ Ranged first. A typical first attempt takes 60-90 minutes — you do not want to fail at Jad because your DPS was too low and you ran out of supplies.

Blowpipe is king: The Toxic Blowpipe with Amethyst darts or better makes the Fight Caves dramatically easier. The special attack heals you, and the attack speed melts through magers and rangers. If you can afford a Blowpipe, bring it. On an Ironman without Zulrah access, the Rune Crossbow with Broad Bolts is perfectly viable — your runs will just be longer (90-120 minutes).

Inventory Setup

Here is the standard inventory for a Blowpipe setup. Adjust if you are using a crossbow (less prayer, more food).

  • 2 Ranging Potions (or Bastion Potions) — Bastion potions also boost Defence, which is helpful. Use 2 doses if bringing Bastion.
  • 8-10 Saradomin Brews — Brews are the meta because they heal 15+ HP per dose and boost Defence. Pair these with Super Restores.
  • 16-18 Super Restores — Restores prayer and counteracts brew stat drain. A 3:1 ratio of restores to brews is standard.
  • Optional: 1 Bastion Potion — If not using brews for ranged boost, bring an extra.
  • Fill remaining slots with Saradomin Brews or Sharks — If you are uncomfortable with brew mechanics, bring 4-6 brews and the rest as Sharks/Anglers.
Brew-Restore Combo Rule: Drink 3 doses of Saradomin Brew, then 1 dose of Super Restore. This resets your drained stats and restores your prayer. Do NOT drink Super Restore first — it wastes the prayer restoration. This is the most common supply mistake first-timers make.

Wave-by-Wave Strategy

The Fight Caves have 63 waves. Each wave spawns a specific combination of monsters. The key to survival is understanding the Italy Rock safespot — a rock formation in the southeast corner of the arena shaped like Italy. You can trap melee monsters behind this rock while dealing with rangers and magers.

Monster Priority (Kill Order)

  1. Tz-Kih (Level 22 bat): Always kill first. Their melee attacks drain your prayer points. Pray Melee and one-shot them.
  2. Tz-Kek (Level 45 blob): Kill at range. They explode on death, dealing damage to anything in melee range. Do NOT melee them unless you have a halberd.
  3. Tok-Xil (Level 90 ranger): Hits hard with Ranged. If a mager is also alive, pray Protect from Magic and tank the ranger. Kill ranger after mager.
  4. Yt-MejKot (Level 180 melee): Heals itself and other monsters. Lure it behind Italy Rock and kill it while it is safespotted. If you cannot safespot, kill it before the mager.
  5. Ket-Zek (Level 360 mager): Always pray Protect from Magic when one is alive. Their max hit is 49. Kill after everything else is dead.

Italy Rock Safespotting

The Italy Rock is your best friend. Position yourself so the rock is between you and melee monsters. Melee monsters cannot path around the rock if you stand on the correct tile. The key tiles are the southernmost tile of the rock's east side. Practice this on early waves: if a melee monster walks up to you, you are on the wrong tile. If it stands still confused, you found the safespot.

Critical Waves

  • Wave 31: First mager appears. This is your first real test. Pray Mage immediately and check your position. If a melee monster is also alive, get to Italy Rock.
  • Wave 45: Mager + Ranger combo. Pray Mage and kill the ranger first. Rangers hit up to 17 with ranged, which is manageable.
  • Wave 53: Mager + Melee (Yt-MejKot). Safespot the melee behind Italy Rock while praying Mage. Kill the mager, then the trapped melee.
  • Wave 62: Two magers. This is the hardest wave before Jad. Kill one mager while praying Mage. Once one is dead, the other is easy. Find the orange 360 that spawns first and kill it — the second spawns seconds later. Stay calm.

Jad Fight: The Main Event

After wave 62, a brief pause. Jad spawns where the orange 360 on wave 62 spawned. This is your moment. Breathe. You have trained for this.

The key distinction: Stomp = Range, Rear up = Mage. The ranged attack has a fast projectile — you must switch prayer the moment you see the stomp. The magic attack has a delayed fireball from above — you have slightly more time to react.

Audio Cue Trick: The Magic attack makes a distinct charging sound before the fireball drops. If you hear the charging noise, switch to Protect from Magic. Many players find audio cues easier to react to than visual ones. Try keeping Protect from Missiles as your default and only switching to Mage when you hear the charge sound.

Healer Strategy

When Jad reaches 50% HP (125 HP remaining), four Yt-HurKot healers will spawn and start healing Jad. This is where most first-timers panic and die. Here is the plan:

  1. Keep your prayer up. Do not forget to pray switch while dealing with healers. This is priority one.
  2. Tag one healer at a time. Attack each healer once to aggro it onto you. After tagging, immediately go back to pray switching against Jad.
  3. Line them up. Once all four healers are on you, run to a position where only one healer can attack you at a time (use Italy Rock if needed). The healers hit 10-14, so if all four attack you at once you will take heavy damage.
  4. Two options from here:
  • Option A (Tank and Kill Jad): If your Defence is 80+, you can tank the healers and continue attacking Jad. Pray switch correctly and Jad will die before the healers kill you.
  • Option B (Kill Healers): Kill all four healers one by one while still pray switching. They have 80 HP each but low defence. If Jad is healed to full, the healers will respawn at 50% again — you must kill them first.

Unlocking Jad as a Slayer Task

You can unlock "TzHaar" as a Slayer task by spending 100 Slayer points or by talking to a Slayer Master. When you have a TzHaar task, you can choose to fight Jad for bonus Slayer XP — 25,000 Slayer XP per kill. You still get the Fire Cape, and you gain the Slayer helm damage bonus (15%) throughout the entire Fight Caves. This makes the caves significantly faster. Highly recommended for your first attempt.

Common Mistakes and How to Recover

  1. Praying wrong on Jad: If you misclick and get hit, do not panic. If you survive (and you likely will at 70+ HP with brews), chug a brew dose, restore, and get back into the rhythm. One mistake does not end the run — giving up does.
  2. Getting comboed by healers: If four healers pile you at once, run behind Italy Rock. Only one healer can attack you at a time from that angle. Brew up and regroup.
  3. Running out of prayer during Jad: You should have 3-4 super restores left for Jad. If you are low, drink a dose between Jad attacks (after switching your prayer).
  4. Over-attacking the wrong monster: On wave 62, make sure you identify which mager spawned first. If you attack the wrong one, you will pull the second mager early and get double-blasted.
  5. Blowpipe running out of scales or darts: Check your blowpipe charges before starting. 80-100 minutes of combat uses ~2,000 scales and ~1,500 darts.
  6. Nerves at Jad: Log out after wave 61 to take a 5-minute break. The game saves your wave progress. Come back calm. This single trick improves first-time success rate dramatically.

After the Cape: What Now?

Once you equip your Fire Cape, the game changes. Here is what to do next:

  • Do not risk it in the Wilderness. The Fire Cape is lost on death in the Wilderness. Use a Trimmed Skill Cape or Ardy Cloak instead.
  • Access Mor Ul Rek. The inner city has a bank, a furnace, and the Inferno minigame (your next challenge).
  • Sacrifice for the Inferno attempt. To attempt the Inferno, you must sacrifice a Fire Cape. Keep an extra if you plan to do Inferno.
  • Unlock Jad tasks. If you enjoyed the fight, farm Jad on Slayer tasks for 25K bonus XP per kill.

Getting your first Fire Cape is one of the most satisfying moments in OSRS. The Fight Caves test your prayer switching, your positioning, your supply management, and most importantly — your composure under pressure. Take your time, use the safespots, and remember: Stomp = Range, Rear Up = Mage. You will have the cape before you know it.
