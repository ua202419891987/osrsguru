OSRS How to Beat Zulrah for Beginners: The Full Rotation Guide

Zulrah is hands-down one of the most intimidating bosses for new players in Old School RuneScape. You've probably watched streamers casually dance through rotations while you sit there wondering how they even know what's coming next. I was the same way. My first ten attempts ended with me teleporting out at 15 HP before I'd done a single point of damage to the snake. But here's the thing — once you understand the rotation system, Zulrah becomes less of a panic-fest and more of a rhythm game. This guide is going to walk you through exactly what you need to know to get your first Zulrah kill in 2026, from gear setups all the way down to rotation charts you can keep on a second monitor.

Prerequisites and Requirements

Before you even set foot on Zul-andra's dock, you need to have a few things squared away. The quest Regicide is mandatory — you can't access Zulrah without finishing it up to the point where you reach Port Tyras. I'd also highly recommend having at least 76 Agility for the fairy ring shortcut (code BJS then run south), because the alternative involves running through a bunch of aggressive elf warriors and that gets old fast after your twentieth death. If you don't have 76 Agility, use the Zul-andra teleport scrolls. They cost about 25k each on the GE but save you literal minutes per attempt.

Recommended Stats

  • 75+ Ranged — Blowpipe is your best friend here
  • 75+ Magic — Trident of the swamp or better
  • 70+ Defence — You'll tank some hits, and higher defence means fewer supplies used
  • 70+ Hitpoints — Zulrah can stack you out if you're not careful
  • 45+ Prayer — For Protect from Magic and Protect from Missiles

Real talk: you can do Zulrah at lower stats. I got my first kill at 78 Ranged and 75 Magic, and it was sweaty. If you're just learning, give yourself the best chance and grind those levels first.

Understanding Zulrah's Rotations

This is the single most important section of this guide. Zulrah has four distinct rotations (sometimes called "rotations 1 through 4" or named after colors: Green/Red/Blue). Each rotation is a fixed sequence of forms Zulrah cycles through, and where Zulrah pops up on the island is entirely predictable once you know which rotation you're in.

At the start of every fight, Zulrah always spawns in the middle of the island as the Green (Ranged) form. The first few seconds are free — Zulrah doesn't attack immediately, so use that window to drink a ranging potion and start dealing damage. After the first form, Zulrah dives underwater and reappears in a different position with a different color. The color tells you what you're dealing with:

  • Green (Ranged form) — Attacks with Ranged. Pray Protect from Missiles. Zulrah can also fire poison clouds that float around the island — DO NOT stand in those clouds, they deal up to 15 damage per tick and that's how most beginners die.
  • Blue (Magic form) — Attacks with Magic. Pray Protect from Magic. This form is relatively safe unless you get caught off-prayer.
  • Red (Melee form) — Zulrah moves her head side to side and lunges at you. DO NOT pray anything — Zulrah doesn't attack during Red phase. If you stand on Zulrah's tile, she won't attack you. But if you run away, she will follow and can hit 40+. Just stand still on her tile or one step off it.
  • Jad phase — Zulrah alternates between Ranged and Magic attacks every single tick. You need to prayer-switch in sync with the attacks. Watch Zulrah's head: if she rears back and flicks her head up, it's Ranged. If she lowers her head and a green ball shoots out, it's Magic.
Don't make my mistake: I spent my first 30 attempts trying to memorize all four rotation charts at once. That's a recipe for frustration. Instead, learn one rotation at a time. If you log in and Zulrah's second form doesn't match the rotation you're practicing, just log out or teleport out. The rotation is random each fight, so keep resetting until you get the one you're learning.

Inventory Setup

Here's what your inventory should look like for a typical Zulrah trip:

  • 1 Ranging Potion — for boosted Ranged DPS
  • 1 Magic Potion or Imbued Heart — Magic accuracy matters a lot
  • 2 Prayer Potions — you'll be praying the entire fight
  • 1 Anti-venom+ — venom damage stacks quickly; regular antipoison won't cut it
  • 1-click teleport (Ring of Dueling, Ectophial, etc.) — for emergency escapes
  • 1 Zul-andra teleport scroll — to get back fast after dying
  • 12-14 Sharks or better food — Manta rays or Anglerfish are better
  • Ranged switch: Blowpipe + Ava's + Armadyl helm (or D'hide body if voiding)
  • Mage switch: Trident + Occult + Ancestral hat (or Mage hood if voiding)

Step-by-Step Kill Strategy

Step 1: Identify the Rotation

After Zulrah's initial Green middle spawn, Zulrah will dive and reappear. The next position tells you which rotation you're on. If Zulrah moves to the east side and turns Blue (Magic), you're in Rotation 1 or 2. If it moves to the west and turns either Blue or Green, you're in Rotation 3 or 4. There are detailed flowcharts online for each rotation — I recommend having a rotation chart pulled up on your phone or second monitor. After about 50 kills, you'll start recognizing the patterns without the chart.

Step 2: Damage During Green Phase

Pray Protect from Missiles. Use your Ranged weapon (Blowpipe with Addy darts is totally fine). Watch for poison clouds — Zulrah will spawn 3-5 clouds that drift across the arena. Move to the tile where the next form will spawn while still attacking. The clouds take about 3 ticks to drift to their destination, so keep your eyes on the minimap markers.

Step 3: Switch During Blue Phase

Pray Protect from Magic. Switch to Magic gear and use your Trident. The Blue phase is the easiest to deal damage during — Zulrah stands still and the attacks are predictable. This is where you want to unload your best DPS. If you have a spec weapon like a Dragon Warhammer, now is a good time to drop specs.

Step 4: Navigate the Red (Melee) Phase

Drop your prayer entirely during Red phase. Stand still on Zulrah's spawn tile and use either Ranged or Magic — both work fine here. If you're running low on HP, the Red phase is the perfect time to eat up, since Zulrah doesn't attack and you have a breathing window of about 10-15 seconds.

Step 5: Handle the Jad Phase

The Jad phase always comes toward the end of each rotation. Zulrah will alternate Ranged and Magic attacks every single tick. The trick is to start with Protect from Missiles as the default, then switch to Protect from Magic the moment you see the Ranged attack animation finish, then back to Missiles, and so on. Don't try to predict — react. The animations are fast but consistent. If you mess up one switch, don't panic — stay calm, eat up, and get back into the rhythm.

Common Death Reasons for Beginners

  1. Standing in poison clouds — These deal massive damage per tick. Always be aware of where the clouds are and position yourself on safe tiles between phases.
  2. Not bringing Anti-venom+ — Regular antipoison reduces venom to poison, but poison still ticks. Anti-venom+ is the real deal and pays for itself in saved food.
  3. Forgetting Ring of Recoil / Suffering — The snakelings will drain your supplies faster than Zulrah itself. Don't leave home without recoil effects.
  4. Running during Red phase — I cannot stress this enough: stand still on Zulrah's tile. If you run, Zulrah chases and hits for 40+. It's the most common way new players die, and it's 100% avoidable.
  5. Panic-eating during Jad phase — Eating costs ticks where you should be prayer-switching. If you need to eat during Jad, eat after switching your prayer, not before.
  6. Low Agility path frustration — Running through elf warriors lowers your supplies before you even reach Zulrah. Either get 76 Agility or buy teleport scrolls.

After Your First Kill

Landing your first Zulrah kill is a legitimately exciting moment. The profit from Zulrah scales directly with how comfortable you become with the rotations. Start tracking which rotation gave you the most trouble and focus on practicing that one specifically. After about 100 kills you'll be switching prayers and gear without even thinking about it. That's when the real money starts flowing.

Good luck out there, and remember — everyone dies to Zulrah their first dozen attempts. It's practically a rite of passage. Stick with it.
