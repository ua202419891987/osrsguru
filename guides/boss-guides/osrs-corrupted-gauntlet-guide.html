OSRS Corrupted Gauntlet Guide 2026 — Complete T2/T1 Prep & Hunllef Strategy

The Corrupted Gauntlet is arguably the single most important piece of PvM content for Ironman accounts in Old School RuneScape. It is the only source of the Enhanced Crystal Weapon Seed, which creates the Bow of Faerdhinen (Bowfa) — the most versatile ranged weapon in the game. For mains, it is a consistent 3-5M GP per hour. For Ironmen, it is a mandatory grind that changes everything. This guide covers everything from your first normal Gauntlet kill to speed-running Corrupted Gauntlet with T1 armor for maximum kills per hour.

Prerequisites

Before you can enter the Gauntlet, you must complete Song of the Elves, the grandmaster quest at the end of the Elf questline. This is a high-level quest with significant requirements:

  • 70 Agility, Construction, Farming, Herblore, Hunter, Mining, Smithing, Woodcutting
  • Completion of Mourning's End Part II, Making History, and several other elf quests
  • A boss fight against Arianwyn's fragment, which teaches you basic movement and prayer-switching

Prep Strategy: T1 vs T2 Armor

T2 Armor Prep (Recommended for Beginners)

T2 armor provides significantly better defence and reduces Hunllef's max hit when prayed correctly. This is the survival setup — you will take less damage from tornadoes and floor tiles, and Hunllef hits you less often through prayer. The downside: gathering enough resources for T2 armor leaves you with roughly 30-60 seconds of spare prep time, which requires efficient room scouting.

T2 Prep Resource Targets:

  • 7 of each resource (Bark, Ore, Linum) — this gives you T2 helm, body, legs
  • 2 T3 weapon frames (from demi-bosses: Dark Beast, Dragon, Bear)
  • At least 500 crystal shards
  • 16-20 cooked paddlefish
  • 2 potions (3-dose each) — combine into 4-dose

T1 Armor Prep (Speed Runs)

T1 armor is the speed-running meta. You gather only 3 of each resource and focus on getting T3 weapons and food. Prep time drops to 3-4 minutes, which means more kills per hour. The catch: Hunllef hits harder and more frequently, so your movement and prayer switching must be nearly perfect.

T1 Prep Resource Targets:

  • 3 of each resource (Bark, Ore, Linum)
  • 2 T3 weapon frames
  • At least 380 crystal shards
  • 16-20 cooked paddlefish
  • 2 potions
Room Scouting Method: When you enter the Gauntlet, run a C-shape around the starting room. Scout the five rooms directly adjacent to the start. If you see at least 3 resource nodes and a fishing spot, you have a good layout. If not, you may need to extend your loop. The key to consistent prep is memorizing how many resources each room can have and prioritizing rooms with 3+ resource nodes. Always scout toward the outer ring — demi-bosses spawn in the rooms directly touching the outer edge.

Weapon Preference: Bow + Staff Meta

The optimal weapon combination for Corrupted Gauntlet is Perfected Bow (Ranged) + Perfected Staff (Magic). Here is why:

  • Bow + Staff both attack from a distance. This lets you stay mobile during tornado phases while still dealing damage. The Halberd requires melee range, which forces you close during dangerous phases.
  • Hunllef's protection prayer switches are predictable. Hunllef changes prayer every 6 attacks. With dual ranged/magic, you never have to wait — just swap weapons when you are on the wrong style.
  • Both styles scale with Rigour and Augury. The DPS gap between Halberd and Bow/Staff shrinks at high levels, and the mobility advantage of ranged attacks is enormous.

The Halberd (melee) has higher max hits, but it is harder to use during tornado phases. If you are comfortable with 5:1 or tick-perfect melee movement, Halberd + Staff is also viable. For 95% of players, Bow + Staff is the way to go.

Hunllef Boss Fight Mechanics

The Hunllef fight is one of the most mechanically demanding encounters in OSRS. Here is everything you need to know:

Prayer Switching

Hunllef attacks with Ranged and Magic. It switches attack styles every 4 attacks (NOT every 6 like some older guides say — this was changed). You must switch your protection prayer to match. Count Hunllef's attacks in your head: 1, 2, 3, 4, switch. A plugin like Hunllef Helper makes this much easier by showing a countdown.

Prayer Disable

When Hunllef reaches 666 HP and 333 HP (66% and 33%), it will roar and disable all protection prayers for a few ticks. Run away from Hunllef during these moments. You cannot pray, so any attack will hit at full damage. Eat up to full HP before these thresholds.

Tornado Phase

At certain HP thresholds, Hunllef summons tornadoes that chase you around the room. These deal massive damage (up to 25/tick in Corrupted) and are the number one killer. Here is how to survive:

  • Keep moving. Never stand still during tornadoes. Run in a wide circle around the room.
  • Stay out of corners. Tornadoes will trap you if you stop in a corner. Always run along the walls or through the middle.
  • Use true tile indicators. Your character's true position is one tile ahead of where the animation shows. Running in a predictable path confuses the tornado pathing.
  • Eat and attack simultaneously. Only eat during tornadoes. Attack Hunllef between tornado spawns. If you eat while there are no tornadoes, you are wasting DPS time.
  • Ignore weapon switches during tornadoes. Survival comes first. If Hunllef is praying against your current weapon, just wait out the tornadoes then switch.

Floor Tiles

Throughout the fight, the floor will light up with dangerous tiles. These hit 20-40 damage when stepped on. The pattern changes with each phase. Train your eyes to see the safe tiles and path around them even during tornado phases. This is the hardest skill in the Gauntlet — combining tornado dodging with floor tile avoidance while prayer switching.

Stomp Attack

If you stand directly under Hunllef, it will stomp you for heavy typeless damage. Stay at least one tile away and do not run through Hunllef. This is especially important during tornado phases when you are frantically running.

Plugin Recommendations

Use RuneLite. These plugins make the Gauntlet significantly easier:

  • Hunllef Helper: Adds a visual countdown showing when Hunllef will switch attack styles. Changes color when you need to switch prayer. This is the single most helpful plugin for the Gauntlet.
  • The Gauntlet: Tracks your resources, highlights gathering nodes, and shows your prep progress. Essential for consistent T2 prep.
  • True Tile Indicator: Shows your character's actual position. Critical for tornado dodging.
  • NPC Indicators: Highlight demi-bosses on the minimap so you do not waste time searching.
  • Tile Markers: Mark the 8 safe tiles around Hunllef for consistent positioning during the fight.

Ironman Significance: The Bow of Faerdhinen (Bowfa) created from the Enhanced Crystal Weapon Seed is the single most important upgrade for Ironman accounts. It is your primary ranged weapon for God Wars Dungeon (Zilyana, Bandos), Chambers of Xeric, Tombs of Amascut, and countless slayer tasks. Combined with full Crystal Armor, it rivals the Toxic Blowpipe with Dragon Darts at most content. The Crystal Armor Seeds you accumulate on the way to the Enhanced Seed give you the armor set for free. There is a reason every Ironman progression guide says "rush Song of the Elves, then camp Corrupted Gauntlet."

Common Mistakes

  1. Greeding DPS during tornadoes: If you try to attack Hunllef while dodging tornadoes, you will eat floor damage. Focus on survival during tornadoes. DPS comes between tornado phases.
  2. Standing still to eat: Never stop moving during tornadoes. Eat while running. The tick loss from standing still is what kills you.
  3. Mis-prepping resources: Running out of time because you gathered too much or too little. Follow the resource targets above exactly.
  4. Not using Steel Skin: After Protect from Missiles/Magic, the 15% Defence boost from Steel Skin significantly reduces Hunllef's accuracy. It is worth the prayer drain.
  5. Going for T3 weapons but T1 armor: T3 weapons with T1 armor is worse than T2 weapons with T2 armor for learners. Prioritize T2 armor when starting out.
  6. Forgetting to make potions: You need 2 four-dose potions for the Hunllef fight. Make them during prep. Running out of prayer mid-fight is a guaranteed death.

Kill Progression Goals

Here is what your Corrupted Gauntlet journey should look like:

  1. 5 Regular Gauntlet kills: Learn the basics. Master prep, understand Hunllef attacks, get comfortable dodging tornadoes.
  2. First Corrupted Gauntlet kill: Expect to die 10-20 times before your first completion. This is normal. Every death teaches you something.
  3. Consistent T2 completions: Once you can clear CG 80%+ of the time with T2 prep, you are ready to grind.
  4. T1 speed runs (optional): If you want faster kills per hour, learn T1 prep. This adds another 10-20 deaths to your learning curve but pays off in the long run.

The Corrupted Gauntlet is hard. It is supposed to be. But it is the fairest kind of hard — every death is your fault, which means every death is a lesson. Stick with it, use the plugins, and remember that Bowfa is waiting at the end. Good luck.
