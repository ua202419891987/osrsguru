OSRS Ironman Crafting Guide 1-99 2026 — From Buckets to Zenytes

Crafting on an ironman account is one of the most resource-intensive skills. Unlike main accounts that can simply buy supplies from the Grand Exchange, ironmen must collect every bucket of sand, every giant seaweed, and every piece of dragonhide themselves. The payoff is worth it: Crafting gates access to zenyte jewelry, the amulet of fury, black dragonhide armor, and at level 99, the crafting cape — the closest bank teleport in the game. This guide covers the complete ironman Crafting journey from level 1 to 99, including every viable training method and resource gathering strategy.

Level 1-20: Quest Experience & Early Skips

Start your Crafting journey with free quest XP to skip the low-level grind entirely:

Quest XP Reward Requirements Notes
Sheep Shearer 150 XP None Takes 3 minutes. Level 2 from level 1.
Goblin Diplomacy 200 XP None Also unlocks goblin mail. Very quick.
Misthalin Mystery 600 XP None Halloween quest, takes 5 minutes.
Elemental Workshop I 5,000 XP 20 Mining, 20 Smithing, 20 Crafting The big one — jump to level 21+ instantly.
Elemental Workshop II 7,500 XP 30 Magic, 20 Crafting Another huge boost after EW I.
In Aid of the Myreque 2,000 XP 25 Crafting, 15 Mining, 7 Magic, In Search of the Myreque Required for Morytania content anyway.
The Fremennik Trials 2,812 XP Various, 40 Crafting needed for one section (boostable) Needed for Fremennik Isles (neitiznot helm).

With these quests, you can reach approximately level 30 Crafting without gathering a single resource. After quests, you'll be ready to transition into production training.

Level 20-61: Charter Ships & Early Glass

For the early mid-levels, charter ships are the most accessible source of crafting materials. Buy buckets of sand (2-3 gp each) and soda ash (3-5 gp each) from charter ship traders, then use a furnace to make molten glass. Each molten glass requires 1 bucket of sand and 1 soda ash, giving 20 XP per piece.

Best charter locations:

  • Port Khazard: Closest to a bank deposit box. Buy from Trader Stan, run north to deposit.
  • Catherby: Bank chest is close by but the shop is often bought out on popular worlds.
  • Port Phasmatys: Less popular, good availability but slower bank access.

Progression:

  • Level 1-4: Craft molten glass (20 XP each) using the furnace
  • Level 4-33: Blow the molten glass into empty light orbs (70 XP each), but note this is slow without the glassblowing pipe
  • Level 33+: Blow vials (35 XP each) — you'll need thousands for Herblore anyway
  • Level 49+: Blow lantern lenses (55 XP each) — good XP and useful for later content

Charter ships are tedious but efficient for early levels. Expect 30K-60K XP per hour depending on shop stock. Total cost from 20-61 is roughly 300K-500K gp for sand and soda ash.

Warning: Charter ship stock is shared across all worlds and resets slowly. You may need to world-hop extensively to find stocked shops. The Catherby and Port Khazard shops are heavily camped. Consider using less popular worlds or less busy hours for efficient shopping.

Level 61+ (with 77 Magic): Superglass Make & Giant Seaweed

At level 77 Magic (boostable to 77 from 72 with a wizard mind bomb), unlock the Lunar spell Superglass Make via Dream Mentor. This spell converts all buckets of sand and seaweed/soda ash in your inventory into molten glass in a single cast, granting 78 XP per cast (10 XP per sand bucket used, up to 13 buckets per cast). When used with giant seaweed, the conversion efficiency is greatly improved.

Giant Seaweed Farming

Giant seaweed is farmed underwater on Fossil Island at the seaweed patch. Each patch holds two giant seaweed spores. Seeds are obtained from lobstrosities, spitting wyverns, wyverns, or as a random drop while diving. Each giant seaweed (when used with Superglass Make) effectively replaces 6 buckets of sand worth of glass output, making it vastly more efficient than using regular soda ash.

Giant Seaweed Mechanics:

  • Grows in approximately 40 minutes (same as regular farming cycles)
  • Use ultracompost for maximum yield (average 30-50 seaweed per run with 2 patches)
  • Each giant seaweed counts as 6 soda ash equivalent for Superglass Make ratios
  • At 99 Farming, the farming cape provides a 5% increased yield on seaweed patches

Sandstone Mining

Buckets of sand are mined from sandstone in the Al Kharid quarry (requires completion of the Desert Diary, or more precisely, access behind the gate). Mine 3-tick granite: 3 sandstone per successful mine at 35+ Mining. Alternatively, the Sandstorm mine (located in the same quarry) provides an AFK alternative.

Sand collecting options:

  • 3-tick sandstone: ~4,000-5,000 buckets per hour at high Mining levels. Bring a water tiara/tome of water for the Camelot or Humidify spell to fill buckets in the bank.
  • Sandstorm mine: ~2,500-3,000 buckets per hour, fully AFK (60 seconds per click). Less efficient but manageable while playing other accounts.
  • Bert (Hand in the Sand quest): After completing the quest, Bert delivers 84 buckets of sand to your bank daily. Free passive sand — check after reset.

Superglass Make Efficiency

The optimal inventory for Superglass Make: 3 giant seaweed + 18 buckets of sand. Each cast yields approximately 1.6 molten glass per bucket of sand and 9.6 per giant seaweed when accounting for the spell's bonus conversion rate. A full inventory produces roughly 30-35 molten glass per cast.

Setup Seaweed per Inv Sand per Inv Average Molten Glass
Max Efficiency 3 Giant Seaweed 18 Buckets of Sand ~30-35
Regular Seaweed (early) 13 Seaweed 13 Buckets of Sand ~16-18

Level 49-87: Blowing Glass (AFK Training)

Once you have a stockpile of molten glass, blowing it into items is the primary Crafting XP method. This is completely AFK — each inventory takes roughly 60-70 seconds to blow, making it ideal for training while doing other activities.

Item Level XP per Item XP per Hour Notes
Vial 33 35 ~35K Needed for Herblore. Make 10K-20K early.
Lantern Lens 49 55 ~90K Best AFK XP at this range. Drop or keep for later.
Empty Light Orb 87 70 ~110K-130K Best AFK XP for high levels.
Tip: Blowing glass is the second-best AFK activity in OSRS (after NMZ). You can do it while working, watching videos, or playing another account. Always train at a bank — bring a full inventory of molten glass, blow it all, bank, and repeat. Lantern lenses from 49-87 and light orbs from 87-99 is the standard path.

Battlestaves (Medium-Active Method)

Crafting battlestaves provides significantly more XP per item than blowing glass, but requires more preparation. Battlestaves are purchased from Zaff in Varrock — the stock increases with Varrock diary completion:

  • No diary: 15 staves per day (8K gp each → 120K gp/day)
  • Varrock Medium: 30 staves per day (7,980 gp each → 240K gp/day)
  • Varrock Hard: 60 staves per day (7,960 gp each → 480K gp/day)
  • Varrock Elite: 120 staves per day (7,940 gp each → 950K gp/day)

Each battlestaff must be charged with an orb (air/water/earth/fire), which are crafted from unpowered orbs at the corresponding obelisk. Unpowered orbs are blown from molten glass at level 46 (52.5 XP each). Each charged battlestaff gives a further 100 XP plus 125 XP when crafting the staff (225 XP total per staff).

Staff Type Crafting Level XP per Staff Charging Location
Water Battlestaff 54 100 Water Obelisk (Taverley Dungeon / Catherby)
Earth Battlestaff 58 112.5 Earth Obelisk (Edgeville Dungeon / Wilderness)
Fire Battlestaff 62 125 Fire Obelisk (Taverley Dungeon / Catherby)
Air Battlestaff 66 137.5 Air Obelisk (Edgeville Dungeon / Wilderness)

The most efficient method is air battlestaves (level 66+) using the air obelisk in the Edgeville Dungeon. Bring cosmic runes, unpowered orbs, staff of air, and a charged inventory of air orbs. High alch the completed staves for 9,300 gp each, covering the cost of the battlestaff purchase and cosmic runes plus a modest profit.

Dragonhide (Passive from Slayer/Bossing)

Dragonhide is a secondary Crafting XP source that accumulates passively through Slayer and bossing. The primary sources are:

  • Vorkath (Dragon Slayer II): Massive quantities of blue and black dragonhide. Each kill drops 2 noted black d'hide and 2 noted blue d'hide on average. Crafting black d'hide bodies gives 258 XP each at level 84.
  • Green Dragons (Myths' Guild / Wilderness): Green dragonhide bodies (258 XP at 63 Crafting). Kill green dragons for bones (Prayer XP) and bank the hides for Crafting.
  • Blue Dragons (Taverley / Myths' Guild): Blue d'hide bodies give 210 XP at 71 Crafting.
  • Red Dragons (Forthos Dungeon): Red d'hide bodies give 234 XP at 77 Crafting.
  • Brutal Black Dragons: Drop 2 noted black dragonhide per kill. 250 XP per body at 84 Crafting.

For efficiency, save all dragonhide gained through Slayer and bossing, then batch-craft them in one session. Black dragonhide bodies at level 84 are the best XP per hide.

Zenytes & Onyx Jewelry (Endgame Goals)

The ultimate goal for ironman Crafting is the ability to make zenyte and onyx jewelry. These are best-in-slot items that require both the rare gemstone and a high Crafting level:

Item Crafting Level Gem Required Source Priority
Amulet of Fury 87 (boostable from 82) Onyx (cut) Zulrah, Skilling, Tokkul purchase Mid-Game
Ring of Suffering 84 (boostable from 79) Zenyte Shard Demonic Gorillas (MM2) Top Priority
Necklace of Anguish 90 (boostable from 85) Zenyte Shard Demonic Gorillas (MM2) 2nd Priority
Tormented Bracelet 93 (boostable from 88) Zenyte Shard Demonic Gorillas (MM2) 3rd Priority
Amulet of Torture 93 (boostable from 88) Zenyte Shard Demonic Gorillas (MM2) 4th Priority

Boosting Crafting: Use a mushroom pie (gives +4 Crafting boost) or spicy stew (+5 boost, unreliable) to reach zenyte crafting levels early. With 85 Crafting (boosted +5 to 90), you can craft the Necklace of Anguish. With 88 Crafting (boosted +5 to 93), you can craft the Tormented Bracelet and Amulet of Torture.

Onyx for Fury: The amulet of fury requires a cut onyx. Sources on an ironman include: Zulrah (1/512), skilling activities at the Myths' Guild or Prifddinas (Fishing, Woodcutting, Mining), or purchasing an uncut onyx for 300K tokkul from the TzHaar gem shop (sell chaos runes for tokkul with Karamja gloves equipped).

Pro Tip: Plan your Crafting level around your zenyte drops. There's nothing worse than getting an early zenyte shard from demonic gorillas and being 10+ levels away from crafting it. Aim for 84 Crafting at minimum before starting the demonic gorilla grind. If you get a shard early, grind out the remaining Crafting levels as a priority — the ring of suffering and anguish are massive upgrades.

Crafting Cape Perks

At level 99 Crafting, the Crafting Skillcape provides unlimited teleports to the Crafting Guild. The guild is located immediately next to a bank chest, making this the closest teleport-to-bank in the entire game (tied with the Farming cape). This is incredibly valuable for all ironman activities requiring frequent banking.

The Crafting Guild also contains a furnace, pottery wheel, spinning wheel, silver and gold ore rocks, and clay rocks — all within a few tiles of the bank. This makes it the optimal location for future Crafting activities like making jewelry or blowing glass.

Recommended Progression Path Summary

Level Range Method Resources Needed
1-30 Quests (Elemental Workshop I/II, Goblin Diplomacy, etc.) None — quests provide instant XP
30-49 Charter Ships → Molten Glass → Vials / Lenses ~300K gp for sand + soda ash
49-61 Blowing Lantern Lenses (AFK) Molten glass from charter ships
61-87 Giant Seaweed + Sandstone → Superglass Make → Lantern Lenses Seaweed spores, sandstone mining, 77 Magic, cosmic + astral runes
87-99 Giant Seaweed → Superglass Make → Light Orbs (AFK) Same as above but for ~5M more molten glass

The total journey requires approximately 100K-120K molten glass from level 61 to 99. This translates to roughly 6,000-7,000 giant seaweed and 100K-120K buckets of sand. Add battlestaves and dragonhide drops for passive XP along the way.

Important: Start giant seaweed farming early — ideally by the time you reach 30-40 Crafting. The seaweed grows on 40-minute cycles, and you need thousands. Planting 2 patches every 40 minutes over weeks adds up. Similarly, mine sandstone in batches. Don't try to gather all 100K buckets of sand at once — break it into manageable 5K-10K mining sessions.
