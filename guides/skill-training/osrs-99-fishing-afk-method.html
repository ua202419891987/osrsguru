OSRS How to Get 99 Fishing AFK Method — Max Fishing While You Work/Sleep/Netflix

Fishing is the quintessential second-monitor skill in OSRS. You click a spot, wait 30-90 seconds, click again. Repeat for 200 hours. The question everyone asks is: what's the most AFK method for each level bracket? This guide answers exactly that, covering every low-attention fishing method from level 1 to 99 in 2026, ranked by AFK intensity rather than pure XP efficiency. If you want to max Fishing while watching Netflix, working from home, or playing another account, this is your roadmap.

What "AFK" Actually Means for Fishing

In OSRS fishing, "AFK" doesn't mean walking away from your keyboard for 20 minutes. The game logs you out after 5 minutes of inactivity (25 minutes with the Runelite logout timer plugin enabled, though that's borderline rule-breaking). For fishing purposes, AFK means minimal clicks per minute — ideally one click every 30-90 seconds. The holy grail is a fishing spot that doesn't move, which is why methods like anglerfish and dark crabs are so popular at high levels.

Level 1–20: Shrimp and Sardines (Lumbridge / Draynor)

This is the least AFK bracket in the entire skill. Shrimp and sardine spots move every 2-3 catches at very low levels. Just click the net fishing spots south of Lumbridge or west of Draynor Village and power through. It'll take about 20 minutes. Alternatively, complete the Sea Slug quest for 7,175 Fishing XP — that skips straight to level 20 and completely bypasses this bracket. Do the quest.

Level 20–58: Fly Fishing (Barbarian Village)

Fly fishing for trout and salmon at Barbarian Village (the river just west of the village, south of Edgeville) is the fastest XP in the game from 20 to 58 — and it's surprisingly AFK-adjacent. The fishing spots last for about 2-5 minutes each, and you have a roughly 30-60 second window between full inventories. Drop the fish (shift-click drop with Runelite is non-negotiable here) and keep clicking. XP rate: 35K-55K/hour depending on level.

The river spots are more AFK than lakes because the banks replenish faster and the spots move less often. Stand on the east bank of the river for the best spot visibility.

If you have access to OSRS Mobile, fly fishing on mobile while doing something else (watching TV, commuting) is genuinely one of the easiest ways to knock out the early levels. The tap-to-drop interface is surprisingly smooth for power-fishing.

Level 58–82: Barbarian Fishing (The Efficiency Play)

At level 48 Fishing and 15 Agility and Strength, barbarian fishing (leaping fish) unlocks at Otto's Grotto near Baxtorian Falls. Heavy rod fishing here gives leaping trout (level 48), leaping salmon (level 58), and leaping sturgeon (level 70). Each fish gives Fishing XP + Strength XP + Agility XP, making this the most efficient method in terms of total XP gained.

Fish at the easternmost fishing spot on the lake — it has the least movement and the best spawn pattern. With the barbarian rod, feathers as bait, and a knife for cutting fish (you'll get roe and caviar for Herblore), you'll average 50K-70K Fishing XP/hour plus passive Strength and Agility gains. Spots last 3-5 minutes, and you'll empty your inventory in about 90 seconds of dropping.

Level 62–99: Monkfish (True AFK Starts Here)

At level 62 Fishing with Swan Song quest complete, monkfish enter the chat. Monkfish are caught at the Piscatoris Fishing Colony using a small fishing net. This is the first genuinely AFK fishing method because the spots have a long despawn timer — often 3-8 minutes — and the fish stack neatly in your inventory without needing to be dropped. One inventory takes 2-4 minutes to fill, then you bank at Piscatoris (30-second round trip).

XP rate: 35K-45K/hour at 62, scaling to 50K/hour at 90+. Profit: roughly 150K-200K GP/hour in 2026 (monkfish sell for about 450 GP each). This is where thousands of players park their accounts while doing real-life work. Click a spot, look away for 3 minutes, click again — repeat for 100 hours.

Level 65–99: Karambwans (The Underrated AFK King)

Karambwan fishing is arguably the most AFK method in the entire skill, and it's available as early as level 65 Fishing with Tai Bwo Wannai Trio quest complete. Here's why it's amazing:

  • The fishing spot never moves. It's a singular, permanent spot.
  • Karambwan vessels stack, so no need to drop anything.
  • With the fairy ring (D-K-P) to the spot and a bank teleport (Karamja gloves 3 from the medium diary, or a ring of dueling to Castle Wars/Ferox Enclave), the banking loop takes 30 seconds.
  • Each raw karambwan sells for about 480 GP in 2026, making this a ~200K GP/hour method at 50K XP/hour.

The catch: you need to catch karambwanji (the bait) first, which are caught at the same spot with a small net. A 30-minute bait-farming session yields enough karambwanji for 5-8 hours of karambwan fishing. That's a one-time investment for a week of AFK bliss.

Level 82–99: Anglerfish (Maximum AFK, Minimum XP)

At level 82 with 100% Port Piscarilius favor, anglerfish unlock. This is the most AFK fishing in the entire game — the spots last 5-10 minutes per location, and the fish are caught one at a time with a sandworm. You can click a spot, walk away, come back 5 minutes later, click again, walk away. XP is abysmal (15K-22K/hour) but if your goal is "I want 99 Fishing without thinking about it," anglerfish are the answer.

In 2026, anglerfish sell for about 1,200 GP each. At 22K XP/hour, the 2.8M XP from 82 to 99 will take about 127 hours. That's five solid days of Netflix — but you'll only click a few times per episode. The profit ends up around 12-15M GP.

The fish barrel from Tempoross is a game-changer for AFK fishing. It holds up to 28 raw fish (banked separately), effectively doubling your inventory space. With a fish barrel at karambwans or monkfish, you can fish for 6-8 minutes without banking. This single item turns already-AFK methods into borderline hands-off training. Grind Tempoross early — it's worth every minute.

Dark Crabs (85 Fishing, Wilderness)

Dark crabs at the Resource Area in level 34-35 Wilderness (with a Wilderness sword 2+ for fast teleports) offer great XP (35K-40K/hour) and excellent profit (300K-400K GP/hour). The spots move but are reasonably static. The Wilderness risk is real — you'll occasionally see a PKer, but in 2026 the deep Wildy fishing area is much less trafficked than the Chaos Altar or Revenant Caves. Bring minimal risk (just the lobster pot and bait), keep the Wilderness sword for a one-click teleport under level 30, and you'll escape 90% of PK attempts.

Tempoross: The Minigame Option

Tempoross (spirit angler's minigame) at the Ruins of Unkah in the desert gives about 60K-80K Fishing XP/hour at level 70+ with concentrated play. It's less AFK than the pure fishing methods, but you earn the fish barrel, tackle box, and spirit angler outfit. The outfit gives a 2.5% XP boost — small, but it adds up over 13 million XP. If you plan to fish for hundreds of hours (and you will, to get 99), prioritize getting the full spirit angler set and fish barrel early.

The AFK Fishing Roadmap to 99

Here's the optimal path if your only priority is minimizing attention: complete Sea Slug quest to skip 1-20, then fly fish at Barbarian Village to 62 (or barbarian fish if you want passive Strength/Agility). At 62, switch to monkfish and call it a day for 150 hours. If you want even more AFK, grind the fish barrel from Tempoross at 70+ Fishing, then hit karambwans at 65 for the never-moving spot. At 82, park yourself at anglerfish and click once every 5 minutes until the cape appears. Fishing at 99 takes about 250 hours total following this path — more than the efficient methods, but with a fraction of the attention. In 2026, that trade-off has never been more worth it.
