OSRS Construction Guide 1-99 2026 — Cheapest & Fastest Training

Construction is one of OSRS's most rewarding skills, unlocking the Player-Owned House (POH) and its incredible teleportation and restoration perks. From the ornate rejuvenation pool to the portal nexus and jewelry box, a maxed POH is the ultimate quality-of-life upgrade. This guide covers every viable training method from levels 1 to 99, including the Mahogany Homes minigame, cost breakdowns, and ironman strategies.

Why Train Construction?

Before diving into methods, understand what you're working toward. At level 83 Construction (boostable from 80 with a spicy stew or 78 with the crystal saw and tea), you can build every major POH upgrade:

  • Portal Nexus (72) — Unlimited teleports to dozens of locations in a single click
  • Ornate Jewellery Box (91, boostable) — Unlimited ring of dueling, games necklace, combat bracelet, skills necklace, glory, and ring of wealth teleports
  • Ornate Rejuvenation Pool (90, boostable) — Restores HP, run energy, prayer, special attack, and all stats, while curing poison and venom
  • Spirit Tree (75) & Fairy Ring (85) — Combined in one superior garden for infinite transportation
  • Occult Altar (90, boostable) — Switch to any spellbook without changing at an altar

Level 1-8: Crude Chairs

Start by building crude wooden chairs in your POH parlour. Each chair requires 2 planks and gives 58 XP. With regular planks costing roughly 300-350 gp each, this is extremely cheap. You only need about 26 chairs to reach level 8, costing less than 20K gp total.

Setup: Buy 55 regular planks from the sawmill or Grand Exchange. Bring a hammer and saw. Enter building mode and build crude chairs until level 8. Use the butler to unnote planks from level 1 if you have one, though at this level you may prefer to bank manually.

Level 8-33: Oak Chairs

At level 8, switch to oak chairs in the parlour. Each uses 2 oak planks and grants 120 XP. Oak planks cost approximately 450-500 gp each. You'll need roughly 150 oak chairs (300 planks) to reach 33. Expect to spend about 150K-200K gp.

This is also a great time to hire a butler. The normal butler (costs 5K gp to hire, 2.5K per 8 trips) will unnote planks from your inventory while you build. Use him to keep your inventory stocked.

Level 33-52: Oak Larders

Oak larders are built in the kitchen and require 8 oak planks each for 480 XP. This is roughly 2.5x more efficient than chairs. You'll build about 380 larders to 52, costing around 500K-600K gp with a demon butler.

Butler strategy: At this point, hire the demon butler (10K hire, 5K per 8 trips) for maximum speed. Have him fetch 24 planks per trip. Build two larders, send butler to bank, build a third with remaining planks, and repeat.

Tip: Use the "Build" and "Remove" right-click options instead of building through the furniture menu for faster training. Place your kitchen near the entrance portal to minimize running distance.

Level 47+: Mounted Mythical Cape (Ironmen)

For ironmen, the mounted mythical cape in the quest hall is an excellent training method from level 47. Each cape rack requires 3 teak planks and a mythical cape (purchased from the Myths' Guild after Dragon Slayer II). The cape costs 10K from the guild and the rack gives 370 XP. At only 30-40 gp/XP, this is one of the cheapest and most viable ironman methods.

Regular accounts can use this too, but the need for completed DS2 and the cape purchase requirement makes it less convenient than teak tables.

Level 52+: Teak & Mahogany Tables

Tables are built in the dining room and are the classic fast construction method. Teak tables require 4 teak planks for 270 XP at level 52. Mahogany tables require 6 mahogany planks for 840 XP at level 52.

Demon butler table method:

  1. Have the demon butler fetch 24 planks from the bank
  2. Build tables until planks run out
  3. Send butler back to bank
  4. Repeat

With mahogany tables, this yields approximately 480K XP per hour but costs around 7 gp/XP — roughly 70M gp from 52 to 99. Teak tables provide about 350K XP/hour at roughly half the cost.

Warning: Make sure to use a demon butler and have plenty of cash in your bank. Construction is a buyable skill — you cannot train effectively without gp backing. The butler's wages add up to several million gp over a full 99 grind.

Level 55+: Mahogany Homes (New Minigame)

Mahogany Homes is a construction minigame introduced in 2020, located in Falador. Speak to Amy in the Falador park to start. You'll receive contracts to repair furniture across Varrock, Falador, Ardougne, and Hosidius.

How it works:

  • You're assigned 3-4 houses to repair per contract
  • Each repair uses 1-2 planks plus steel bars for some tasks
  • Completing a contract gives bonus XP based on the tier (Beginner, Adept, Expert)
  • Earn Carpenter Points to purchase the Carpenter's outfit (2.5% bonus XP), plank sack (28 extra planks), and Amy's saw (speeds up repair animations)

Contract tiers:

  • Beginner (1+): Uses regular and oak planks, ~35K XP/hr
  • Adept (50+): Adds teak planks, ~80K XP/hr
  • Expert (70+): Adds mahogany planks, ~130K-200K XP/hr (with teak/mahogany)

With expert contracts using mahogany planks, you can achieve roughly 200K XP/hour while spending significantly less gp than traditional table training. You'll also earn the Carpenter's outfit for a permanent 2.5% XP boost. The plank sack is essential for any future construction training, letting you carry 28 extra planks.

Level 74+: Dungeon Doors

Oak and mahogany dungeon doors in the dungeon room offer some of the fastest XP rates. Oak doors require 10 oak planks for 600 XP at level 74, giving roughly 450K XP/hour at 4 gp/XP. Mahogany doors give even more XP but are pricier.

The dungeon room should be built as close to the entrance as possible, similar to the dining room for tables. The demon butler is mandatory for maintaining rates.

Pro Tip: For the absolute fastest XP rates, use mahogany dungeon doors with a demon butler. Build two doors, send butler to bank, build a third, and repeat. This can push past 550K XP/hour but will cost you over 8 gp/XP.

POH Layout for Efficient Training

Optimize your house layout to minimize running distance between the portal and your building spot:

  • Place the dining room (for tables) or kitchen (for larders) directly adjacent to the entrance portal
  • Rotate the room so the door faces the portal — this reduces a few tiles of running per cycle
  • Keep your house in Rimmington if using the house party world (330), or use your own butler for banking
  • For tablet training, use a butler and keep your house wherever your portal is
Important: Construction is the most click-intensive skill in OSRS. Mahogany tables with a demon butler requires clicking every ~2 seconds. Consider using Mahogany Homes if you want a less intense alternative, even though it's slower XP.
