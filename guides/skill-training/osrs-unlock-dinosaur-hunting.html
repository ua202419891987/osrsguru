OSRS How to Unlock Dinosaur Hunting — Your Complete Access Guide for 2026

Dinosaur hunting is one of the coolest activities added to OSRS in recent years. It combines Hunter with Slayer, offers some of the best XP in the skill, and drops valuable materials like dinosaur claws and hides used for upgrading the Slayer helmet. But unlocking it isn't obvious — there are multiple quests, level requirements, and location prerequisites you need to knock out first. This OSRS how to unlock dinosaur hunting guide walks you through every step, from your first quest to your first raptor-like catch in 2026.

What Is Dinosaur Hunting in OSRS?

Dinosaur hunting takes place primarily on Anachronia (also referred to as Fossil Island's northern section, though technically Anachronia is distinct from the base Fossil Island). Using Hunter traps, you catch various species of dinosaurs — from the humble ferret-sized creatures up to the massive brutish dinosaurs. The activity gives Hunter XP, and when combined with Slayer tasks (assigned by Laniakea, the level 90 Slayer master on Anachronia), you gain Slayer XP simultaneously.

The main draws of dinosaur hunting are: high Hunter XP rates (100K-160K/hour depending on the species), Slayer XP on-task, and valuable drops including dinosaur claws (used to upgrade Slayer helmets at 98 Crafting) and dinosaur bones for Prayer training.

Step 1: Complete Bone Voyage (Fossil Island Access)

Before anything else, you need access to Fossil Island. The quest Bone Voyage is the gatekeeper. Requirements:

  • 100 Kudos with the Varrock Museum (earned by completing the Natural History Quiz on the basement floor, cleaning finds, and completing quest-related exhibits)
  • Completion of Dig Site quest (10 Agility, 10 Herblore, 25 Thieving)

Start Bone Voyage by speaking to the barge guard just north of the Dig Site, east of Varrock. The quest itself is quick — about 15-20 minutes — and you'll sail to Fossil Island at the end. Once on Fossil Island, you're technically in the right region, but you're not done yet.

While unlocking Fossil Island, knock out the Natural History Quiz in the Varrock Museum basement if you haven't already. It takes 10-15 minutes and gives 1,000 XP lamps (Hunter and Slayer XP included), which directly helps with the level requirements for dinosaur hunting. Free XP is free XP.

Step 3: Access Anachronia (Northern Fossil Island)

Anachronia is the northern landmass connected to Fossil Island. To reach it, you need to build the rowboat at the northernmost point of Mushroom Forest on Fossil Island. This requires:

  • 500 numulite (obtained from fossil island activities: mining volcanic ash, chopping sulliuscep mushrooms, or killing the ammonite crabs and other creatures on the island)
  • Oak, willow, or maple logs (depending on which boat tier you build — maple is the best because it reduces travel time from 5 to 2 minutes for the return trip)
  • A hammer and a saw

Once the boat is built, you can sail to Anachronia. The first area you land on is the Base Camp, a construction-focused hub where you build and upgrade facilities. For dinosaur hunting, you don't need to heavily invest in base camp construction — just having the boat is enough.

Step 4: Unlock the Slayer Master Laniakea (Optional but Recommended)

While not strictly required to hunt dinosaurs, unlocking Laniakea as your Slayer master gives you the ability to receive dinosaur Slayer tasks. This turns dinosaur hunting from a pure Hunter activity into a combined Hunter + Slayer training method — far more efficient for your account.

To unlock Laniakea:

  • Reach 90 Slayer (this is a hard requirement)
  • Reach 120 Combat (Laniakea's combat requirement)
  • Speak to Laniakea at the Anachronia base camp after the above requirements are met

At 90+ Slayer, Laniakea has the best Slayer task list in the game — dinosaurs, hydras, dark beasts, abyssal demons, and more. Getting dinosaur assignments from her is a massive XP and GP upgrade.

Step 5: Gear Up for Dinosaur Hunting

Dinosaur hunting uses standard Hunter gear but with some specific optimizations:

  • Hunter's outfit (full set): 2.5% XP boost, obtained from the Hunter kit minigame or directly purchased from the GE (but earn it yourself for the ironman flex)
  • Hunter's cape (99 Hunter): Act as a butterfly net and rope, saving inventory slots
  • Ring of pursuit (optional): From the Hunter skill, this increases the catch rate on tracked prey (only works on dinosaur species that are tracked rather than trapped)
  • Bonecrusher + Dragonbone necklace: For passive Prayer XP while hunting dinosaurs that drop bones (dinosaurs drop large quantities)

The Hunter's potion (made at 53 Herblore with avantoe + kebbit teeth dust) boosts your Hunter level by 3. At level 72 Hunter, this potion lets you catch the 75-level dinosaurs early. Brewing a few hundred Hunter potions before your first Anachronia trip is worth the Herblore XP investment.

Trapping Methods: How Dinosaur Hunting Actually Works

Unlike traditional Hunter (bird snares, box traps), dinosaur hunting uses a pitfall trapping method. Here's the loop:

  1. Find a dinosaur spawn on Anachronia. Each species has designated hunting grounds marked on the mini-map with a Hunter icon.
  2. Set up a pitfall trap using a teasing stick, logs, and a knife. This creates a pit covered by foliage.
  3. Use the teasing stick on the dinosaur to provoke it into charging you. As it charges over the pit, it falls in.
  4. Check the trap to deal damage to the trapped dinosaur and collect your Hunter XP + loot.
  5. Repeat until the trap breaks (random chance), then rebuild it.

The process is more interactive than traditional box trapping. Each successful catch requires about 5-6 clicks (tease, dodge, check pit, loot, rebuild), creating a rhythm that's engaging without being exhausting.

Dinosaurs + Slayer: The Synergy

Once you have Laniakea unlocked at 90 Slayer, you'll regularly receive dinosaur Slayer tasks. Each task assigns 120-200 dinosaurs of a specific species. On-task, you gain:

  • Full Slayer XP for each kill (the dinosaur's HP in XP: ~250-500 XP per dinosaur)
  • Hunter XP from trapping
  • Slayer helm bonuses (15% damage and accuracy boost on-task if you have the imbued Slayer helmet)

A single task of 180 Bagrada rexes at 90+ Slayer and 90+ Hunter yields roughly: 50K Slayer XP + 170K Hunter XP + 500K-1M GP in drops. Over the course of 99 Hunter, you'll passively gain millions of Slayer XP — efficiency at its finest.

Unlock Summary: Your Checklist

To start dinosaur hunting in OSRS (2026), complete these steps in order: (1) Dig Site quest (10 Agility/Herblore, 25 Thieving), (2) earn 100 Kudos at the Varrock Museum, (3) complete Bone Voyage to reach Fossil Island, (4) train Hunter to at least 75 (bird houses or salamanders), (5) build the maple rowboat to Anachronia (500 numulite + maple logs), (6) optionally unlock Laniakea at 90 Slayer for combined Slayer + Hunter tasks. Grab your teasing stick, set those pitfalls, and start hunting. The dinosaurs are waiting.
