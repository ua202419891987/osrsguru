OSRS Fletching Guide 1-99 2026 — Fastest Methods & Profit

Fletching is one of the fastest and most straightforward 99s in OSRS. With methods ranging from zero-cost bow fletching to GP-devouring dragon dart making, the skill offers something for every account type and budget. This OSRS Fletching guide 1-99 2026 covers every viable training method with XP rates, profit calculations, and the all-important ironman meta using broad ammunition.

The Basics: Bow Fletching Progression

The core loop of Fletching is simple: use a knife on logs to cut unstrung bows, then attach bowstrings for additional XP. Both steps give the same XP, but cutting is faster. Most players only cut bows and drop or sell them, which is faster overall. Stringing is only worth it if you want more total XP per log or if the strung bow has value.

Here's the standard bow fletching path — cut the highest-level shortbow or longbow unlocked, string only if you want additional XP.

Level 1-10: Arrow Shafts

Use a knife on regular logs to make 15 arrow shafts per log (5 XP each). You'll need to make approximately 2,300 arrow shafts (154 regular logs) to reach level 10. This takes about 10 minutes and costs nothing — normal logs are free from any tree. Alternatively, attach feathers to bronze arrow tips for faster XP (1.8 XP per dart, ~15K XP/hour), but this costs more and arrow shafts are simpler to start.

Level 10-55: Oak to Maple Longbows

From 10-55, follow the progression table above: oak longbows (10-25), willow longbows (25-40), and maple longbows (40-55). At each tier, buying logs from the GE and cutting bows at a bank gives consistent XP:

  • Oak longbows: ~25K-30K XP/hour cutting only, ~50K XP/hour cutting + stringing
  • Willow longbows: ~40K-50K XP/hour cutting only, ~80K XP/hour cutting + stringing
  • Maple longbows: ~60K-80K XP/hour cutting only, ~120K XP/hour cutting + stringing

Oak and willow logs are cheap (~50-100 GP each), and maple logs are roughly 20-30 GP each. The total cost from 1-55 via bow cutting is only 20K-50K GP — practically free. If you string the bows and sell them on the GE (strung bows have alch value), you can break even or make a small profit.

Pro Tip: For maximum AFK, only cut bows (don't string). Cutting is a single action repeated — click "use knife → log" then wait. Stringing requires two actions (click bowstring → unstrung bow) and the bank run. Cutting alone gets you 99 Fletching faster than cutting + stringing, since the time spent stringing could be used cutting more bows. Only string if you want profit or broader XP.

Level 55-70: Yew Longbows

At level 55, yew longbows become your best friend. Cut yew logs into yew longbow (u) for 75 XP each at about 100K-120K XP/hour. Yew logs cost ~250-350 GP each, and unstrung yew longbows can be sold back to the GE for ~200-300 GP — meaning each one costs you only 50-100 GP. That's roughly 1 GP per XP at this tier. Total cost from 55-70: about 500K-700K GP.

Level 70-99: Yew Longbows to Magic Longbows

Yew longbows remain competitive from 70-85 (the XP rate plateaus at about 120K-130K/hour with full attention). At level 85, upgrade to magic longbows — 91.5 XP per cut, yielding 150K-180K XP/hour. Magic logs cost ~900-1,100 GP each, and unstrung magic longbows sell for ~700-900 GP. Net cost: 200-300 GP per bow, or about 2-3 GP per XP. Total cost from 55-99 using yew (55-85) and magic (85-99): roughly 15M-20M GP.

The bow-fletching path from 1-99 takes roughly 80-100 hours if cutting only, or 120-150 hours if cutting + stringing. It's consistent, low-intensity, and easy to do while watching videos or playing an alt.

Darts: Extremely Fast XP at a Cost

Darts are the fastest Fletching XP in the game by a massive margin. Each dart (dart tip + feather) gives XP per tip tier, and you fletch 10 sets of darts per action (1 action = 15 ticks = 9 seconds to fletch 10 darts, then another set). This means you can process hundreds of darts per minute.

Broad Bolts & Broad Arrows (The Ironman Meta)

Broad ammunition is the single most important Fletching method for ironmen and a solid profitable option for mains. Here's how it works:

  • Broad bolts: Level 55, requires unlocking the ability to fletch broad bolts from a Slayer Master for 300 Slayer points. Each broad bolt (adding feathers to unfinished broad bolts) gives 30 XP. Unfinished broad bolts cost 50 GP each from Slayer Masters. XP rate: ~70K-90K XP/hour, extremely cheap.
  • Broad arrows: Level 52, requires unlocking from a Slayer Master (300 Slayer points). Each broad arrow (adding headless arrows to broad arrowheads) gives 10 XP per arrow + 5 XP for the headless arrow creation. XP rate: ~90K-120K XP/hour. Broad arrowheads cost 50 GP each from Slayer Masters.

For ironmen, broad bolts are the meta because:

  1. Slayer tasks provide the GP to buy broad bolt packs
  2. Broad bolts are your primary ranged ammunition from level 55 onward (used with the rune crossbow)
  3. Making broad bolts while doing Slayer or bank-standing between tasks is zero-time Fletching XP
  4. Feathers are easily obtained from birdhouse runs and the fishing shop in Port Sarim

Main accounts can also profit: buy unfinished broad bolts from GE, add feathers, and sell the finished bolts. Profit margins are small (5-15 GP per bolt) but consistent, and the XP is decent.

Warning: You must spend 300 Slayer points to unlock broad bolt/arrow fletching for EACH type. Prioritize broad bolts first (they're the more useful ammunition), then unlock broad arrows later if you need them. If you're rushing Slayer unlocks (slayer helm, rings, task extensions), delay this unlock — Slayer points are precious early on.

Magic Longbows (Level 85+): The Balanced Path

From level 85, magic longbows offer the best balance of XP, cost, and intensity. Cutting magic logs into magic longbow (u) gives 91.5 XP each at 150K-180K XP/hour. The cost is reasonable: each magic log costs ~1,000 GP, the unstrung bow sells for ~800 GP, so you lose ~200 GP per bow. That's roughly 2.5 GP per XP — one of the cheapest high-level methods.

If you string the bows (requires bowstrings, ~150 GP each), each completed magic longbow alchs for 1,536 GP (or 1,920 GP with the high alchemy spell). With nature runes at ~200 GP, the alch profit is about 300-400 GP per bow — enough to cover the magic log cost and earn a small profit. Stringing takes about twice as long, so your XP/hour drops but your GP goes positive. Decide based on your priorities.

Fastest 99 Fletching Route

If speed is everything and cost is no concern, here's the fastest route to 99:

  1. 1-10: Make arrow shafts (10 minutes)
  2. 10-45: Fletch the best darts available (iron → steel → mithril → adamant)
  3. 45-95: Adamant darts (~180K XP/hour)
  4. 95-99: Dragon darts (~700K XP/hour)

Total time: ~35-45 hours. Total cost: 60M-80M GP. This is the speedrunner approach — you'll get 99 Fletching in a week playing casually, or a weekend if you grind hard.

Cheapest 99 Fletching Route

For budget-conscious players who don't mind a slower grind:

  1. 1-55: Cut and string longbows (oak → willow → maple), selling strung bows on GE. Breakeven.
  2. 55-85: Cut yew longbows, string and alch for profit (~200K GP/hour profit, 60K-80K XP/hour)
  3. 85-99: Cut magic longbows, string and alch for profit (~300K GP/hour profit, 100K-120K XP/hour)

Total time: 100-130 hours. Total result: 10M-15M GP profit. Yes, you read that right — you can earn money getting 99 Fletching if you're willing to string and alch every bow. Fletching is one of the rare skills where the slowest method puts GP in your pocket.

Ironman Fletching Strategy

Fletching as an ironman is fundamentally about feeding supplies from other skills:

  • Woodcutting provides all your logs. Cut oaks at Draynor, willows at Draynor or Barbarian Outpost (fastest), maples at Seers' Village, yews at the Woodcutting Guild, and magics at the Woodcutting Guild or Prifddinas. Birdhouse runs on Fossil Island also yield nests with seeds and rings.
  • Farming for yew and magic tree seeds (from bird nests and boss drops). Plant them in the Woodcutting Guild patch for consistent high-level log supplies.
  • Slayer provides the GP for broad bolt packs (50 GP each from Slayer Masters). Unlock broad bolt fletching for 300 Slayer points — this alone carries you to 99. You'll also get bowstring from Temple Trekking (bowstring packs) or spinning flax from Zulrah/Kurask drops.
  • Feathers: Buy from the fishing shop in Port Sarim (1,000 stock per world at 2 GP each). World-hop to restock. Also comes from birdhouse runs.
  • Kurasks (70 Slayer): Drop noted flax (spin into bowstrings), nature runes, and broad bolts — an incredible mid-game slayer task for ironmen fletching.
Pro Tip: If you have 80 Agility and the Kandarin Hard Diary, you can use the Seers' Village agility course for marks of grace, then spin flax at the nearby spinning wheel between laps for free Crafting + Fletching stacking. It's not efficient, but it's a zero-cost way to make incremental progress in two skills at once.

Fletching is forgiving. You can rush it in a weekend with darts, coast through it with bows while watching Netflix, or let it passively happen through Slayer on an ironman. The Fletching cape's perk (a free knife and the ability to search for mithril grapples) isn't game-changing, but the cape itself is a solid flex and one of the faster trim completions for max cape hunters.
