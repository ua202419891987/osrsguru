OSRS Magic Guide 1-99 2026 — Fastest Training & Money Making

Magic is one of the most versatile skills in Old School RuneScape. Whether you want the fastest XP rates via burst/barrage at maniacal monkeys, passive profit while alching during Agility, or the unique utility of the Lunar spellbook, Magic offers training paths for every playstyle and budget. This guide covers every viable method from level 1 to 99, with detailed XP rates, profit calculations, and ironman-specific strategies.

Level 1-55: Splashing (AFK Method)

Splashing is the simplest and most AFK method for early Magic training. You intentionally wear gear that gives you -65 or lower magic attack bonus, ensuring your spells always splash (miss) on the target. Since splashed spells don't deal damage, the monster never dies and you can cast indefinitely without interaction.

Requirements: Full metal armor (rune), a metal shield, vambraces, and a staff that gives negative magic attack (like the mud battlestaff or smoke battlestaff). Combined, these give -65 magic attack, guaranteeing splashes.

Setup: Stand in a safe spot. Popular locations include the Lumbridge basement rats or the seagulls in Port Sarim. Activate autocast on your chosen spell. You can click away for up to 20 minutes before auto-logout kicks in — simply interact with the game once every 15-19 minutes to stay logged in.

Spell progression for splashing:

  • 1-13: Wind Strike (2 XP, ~5K/hr) — cheapest
  • 13-35: Fire Strike (11.5 XP, ~13K/hr) — best value
  • 35-55: Fire Bolt (22.5 XP, ~22K/hr) — upgrade at 35

From level 1 to 55, you'll splash approximately 12,000 times, costing roughly 1.2M gp with fire strike and 2.5M with fire bolt.

Warning: Jagex periodically changes NPC aggression mechanics in certain areas. Always test that your splashing spot works before committing to a long AFK session. The Lumbridge rats and Port Sarim seagulls are consistently reliable.

Level 55+: High Alchemy (Profit Method)

At level 55, unlock High Level Alchemy. Each alch grants 65 Magic XP, and you can alch approximately 1,200 items per hour for roughly 78K XP/hr. The key is finding items where the alchemy value exceeds the Grand Exchange purchase price, creating a profit.

Level 43+: Superheat Item (Smithing Synergy)

Superheat Item requires level 43 Magic and provides 53 XP per cast. The real benefit is the Smithing XP synergy — you can smelt bars without a furnace at the same speed as regular smelting, earning both Magic and Smithing XP simultaneously.

Popular superheat uses:

  • Gold ore: 56.2 Smithing XP + 53 Magic XP (requires goldsmith gauntlets from Family Crest)
  • Iron ore: 35 Smithing XP + 53 Magic XP, with a 50% success rate unless wearing a ring of forging
  • Steel/Mithril/Adamant/Rune: Standard Smithing XP + 53 Magic XP

Superheating gold ore with goldsmith gauntlets at the Blast Furnace bank chest yields roughly 120K Magic XP and 130K Smithing XP per hour, at a loss of about 150K gp/hr.

Level 80+: Stun-Alching (Fast Speed Method)

Stun-alching combines casting Stun (80+ Magic required) and High Alchemy in the same game tick. This exploits the fact that Stun has a short cooldown and Alchemy doesn't share the same cooldown timer. The result is roughly 180K Magic XP per hour at a cost of 1.3-1.5M gp/hr.

Requirements: 80 Magic, a mud battlestaff (or smoke battlestaff), full armor with -65 magic attack (to guarantee Stun splashes), and a large stack of nature runes and soul runes.

How to stun-alch: Equip gear giving -65 magic attack, fill inventory with noted alchable items and runes, and stand in a safe spot. Cast Stun on a target, then immediately cast High Alchemey on the same tick. With practice, you'll hit both actions every 3 ticks (1.8 seconds). Use a staff that provides both water and earth runes (mud staff) to reduce soul rune costs for Stun.

Warning: Stun-alching is one of the most expensive Magic training methods. From level 80 to 99 you'll need approximately 60,000 casts, costing 80-90M gp. It's extremely click-intensive — not suitable for AFK training. Consider it only if gp is not a concern.

Level 70/94+: Burst & Barrage (Multi-Target AOE)

Ancient Magicks burst and barrage spells hit up to 9 targets in a 3x3 area, making them the most efficient Magic training method in terms of XP per damage dealt. The key is finding monsters that can be stacked tightly together.

Maniacal Monkeys (70+ / 94+)

Located in Kruk's Dungeon beneath Ape Atoll (requires Monkey Madness II completion), maniacal monkeys are aggressive, respawn quickly, and can be stacked in a 3x3 area for consistent multi-hit bursts.

  • Ice Burst (70+): ~180K-220K XP/hr, ~400K gp/hr loss
  • Ice Barrage (94+): ~300K-400K XP/hr, ~1.2M gp/hr loss

Use bonecrusher necklace (or bonecrusher + prayer bonus gear) with Protect from Melee for infinite prayer. Bring divine magic potions and an imbued heart for maximum damage.

Dust Devils (65+ Slayer, 70+ Magic)

Located in the Smoke Dungeon or Catacombs of Kourend. Dust devils drop valuable alchables and dust battlestaves, making burst tasks profitable. Approximately 100K-150K Magic XP/hr with 500K-1M gp/hr profit.

Nechryaels (80+ Slayer, 94+ Magic)

In the Catacombs of Kourend, nechryaels are a slayer task that yields excellent profit from alchable drops and rune items. With ice barrage, expect 200K-250K Magic XP/hr and 800K-1.5M gp/hr profit.

Pro Tip: Equip a tormented bracelet, occult necklace, imbued god cape, and elidinis' ward for maximum magic damage bonus when bursting. With max mage gear, you'll significantly increase kill speed and XP rates. The imbued heart (magic level boost) is also essential for maximizing barrage damage.

Lunar Spellbook Methods

Plank Make (86+)

The Plank Make spell converts logs into planks for a fixed coin cost (70% of the sawmill fee), granting 90 Magic XP per cast. It's semi-AFK — each cast has a 3-tick animation. Cast approximately 1,000 times per hour for 90K XP. Profit margins depend on plank vs log prices but typically yield 100K-250K gp/hr with mahogany logs. The spell autocasts, making it viable while watching other content.

Tan Leather (78+)

Tan Leather tans up to 5 hides per cast at a cost of 5 fire runes, 2 astral runes, and 1 nature rune. It grants 81 XP per cast (regardless of hide count), but since you can tan 5 at a time, effective XP rates reach 130K/hr with dragonhide. Dhide tanning margins are profitable: black d'hide bodies typically offer 300K-800K gp/hr profit alongside the XP.

String Jewellery (80+)

Strings one unstrung amulet per cast for 83 XP. With manual clicking, you can string about 1,800 amulets per hour for 150K XP/hr. The spell can autocast, making it semi-AFK at slightly lower rates. Profit margins vary — some amulets break even or yield small profits.

Ironman-Specific Magic Training

Mage Training Arena (MTA)

The Mage Training Arena is essential for ironmen. Located north of the Duel Arena, MTA offers four minigame rooms where you earn Pizazz Points redeemable for critical untradeable magic items:

  • Bones to Peaches (B2P): Essential for GWD and many PVM scenarios. Costs 200 Telekinetic, 300 Alchemist, 2000 Enchantment, and 200 Graveyard points.
  • Infinity Boots: Best-in-slot magic boots until eternal boots. 120 Telekinetic, 120 Alchemist, 1200 Enchantment, and 12 Graveyard points.
  • Master Wand: Required for the Kodai wand upgrade from Chambers of Xeric. 240 points in each room.
  • Mage's Book: Best magic offhand (until elidinis' ward/arcane), 500 points in each room.

Completing MTA for B2P, master wand, mage's book, and infinity boots will grant approximately 2M Magic XP from the minigame itself.

Burst Tasks for Ironmen

Slayer is the primary Magic training method for ironmen. Prioritize burst/barrage tasks: dust devils (65+ Slayer), nechryaels (80+), smoke devils (93+), and abyssal demons (85+). You'll simultaneously train Magic, Slayer, and Hitpoints while collecting resources. Runes are sourced from runecrafting, the Mage Bank, or buying from shops with GP from alchables.
