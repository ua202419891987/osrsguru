OSRS Crafting Guide 1-99 2026 — Cheapest & Fastest Methods

Crafting is one of the few skills in OSRS where you can earn a profit on the road to 99 — or spend 100M+ GP racing through it in a weekend. The variety of training methods means you can tailor the grind to your budget and playstyle, from zero-cost glassblowing to high-intensity gem cutting. This OSRS Crafting guide 1-99 2026 breaks down every viable method with XP rates, GP costs, and ironman strategies so you can pick the path that works for you.

Level 1-30: Leather Crafting (Nearly Free)

The fastest way to start Crafting is with leather. Buy cowhide from the Grand Exchange (~150 GP each), tan them into leather at Al Kharid or the GE tanner (~1 GP per hide for regular leather), and craft the highest-level leather item available. The tanning process is the bottleneck — use the Al Kharid tanner (closest to a bank, use ring of dueling to bank at Castle Wars and run east) for maximum speed.

Progression path:

  • Level 1-7: Leather gloves (1 XP each, buy from GE completed if lazy)
  • Level 7-9: Leather boots (16.25 XP each)
  • Level 9-11: Leather cowl (18.5 XP each)
  • Level 11-14: Leather vambraces (22 XP each)
  • Level 14-18: Leather body (25 XP each)
  • Level 18-28: Leather chaps (27 XP each)
  • Level 28-30: Hardleather body (35 XP each)

Expect roughly 30K-40K XP/hour doing leather. Total cost to level 30: about 50K-80K GP — nearly free. If you want to skip this entirely, complete Sheep Shearer (150 Crafting XP), Goblin Diplomacy (200 Crafting XP), and use any lamps from other early quests.

Level 1-99: Glassblowing via Superglass Make (The Meta)

Glassblowing is the most cost-effective path to 99 Crafting and the ironman meta. The process has two phases: creating molten glass (via Superglass Make or furnace), then blowing that glass into items with a glassblowing pipe. The key spell is Superglass Make (77 Magic, Lunar spellbook, requires Lunars Diplomacy quest) which converts all buckets of sand and seaweed/soda ash in your inventory into 1.3x molten glass per material. This means 13 sand + 13 seaweed yields ~16.9 molten glass per cast — a massive efficiency gain over the furnace.

Phase 1: Obtaining Molten Glass

  • Main accounts (buying): Buy buckets of sand (~80 GP each) and soda ash (~40 GP each) from the GE. Each Superglass Make cast with a full inventory of 13 sand + 13 seaweed costs ~1,560 GP and produces ~17 molten glass. Cost per molten glass: ~92 GP.
  • Ironmen (giant seaweed + sandstone): Grow giant seaweed on the two underwater patches north of Fossil Island (requires 23 Farming, can be boosted). Each giant seaweed counts as 6 soda ash. Mine sandstone at the Desert Quarry (south of Bandit Camp), grind it into buckets of sand at the sandstorm grinder. One giant seaweed + 6 buckets of sand = ~9 molten glass via Superglass Make when using 3 giant seaweed + 18 sand per inventory (producing ~23-25 molten glass per cast).
  • Charter ships (alternative): Buy soda ash and buckets of sand from charter ship traders (Port Khazard, Catherby, etc.). World-hop to restock. About 5K-6K buckets of sand + soda ash per hour. Slower than growing/mixing but requires no mining or farming.

Phase 2: Blowing Glass

Equip a glassblowing pipe and blow molten glass into the highest-level item available:

  • Level 1-4: Beer glass (17.5 XP)
  • Level 4-12: Empty candle lantern (19 XP)
  • Level 12-33: Oil lamp (25 XP)
  • Level 33-42: Vial (35 XP, profitable — sell on GE)
  • Level 42-46: Fishbowl (42.5 XP)
  • Level 46-87: Unpowered orb (52.5 XP each, ~60K-70K XP/hour). These are always in demand for battlestaff crafting — you can sell them or use them yourself.
  • Level 87-99: Light orb (70 XP each, ~80K-100K XP/hour). Slightly profitable (~50-80 GP profit per orb) and excellent XP.

Blowing glass at a bank (GE, Castle Wars, or Edgeville bank) gives consistent 70K-100K XP/hour from level 46 onwards. Use the deposit-all button to rapidly bank between inventories. The entire 1-99 via glassblowing costs 5-15M GP for main accounts and is effectively free for ironmen who gather their own materials.

Level 54-99: Battlestaves (Balanced XP + Profit)

Attaching charged orbs to battlestaves creates elemental battlestaves, yielding excellent Crafting XP and consistent profit. You need charged orbs (air, water, earth, fire — from charging unpowered orbs at obelisks) and battlestaves (buy from Zaff in Varrock, the Mage Guild, or GE).

Progression:

  • Level 54: Water battlestaff (100 XP each)
  • Level 58: Earth battlestaff (112.5 XP each)
  • Level 62: Fire battlestaff (125 XP each)
  • Level 66: Air battlestaff (137.5 XP each) — the most popular, since air orbs are fastest to charge (Edgeville Dungeon obelisk, Wilderness with a looting bag for extra profit).

Battlestaves are ~100K-130K XP/hour and yield 200K-400K GP/hour profit, making them one of the only methods where you earn money while training. The trade-off? You need to spend time charging orbs, which is slow Magic XP (Air Orb charging: ~30K Magic XP/hour). Many players buy charged orbs from the GE instead, which reduces profit but preserves XP rates.

Pro Tip: If charging orbs yourself, the air obelisk in Edgeville Dungeon (requires level 60 Magic, air staff, cosmic runes) is the fastest. Use a glory to teleport to Edgeville, run through the dungeon, charge, and glory back to bank. With 80+ Agility (shortcut in the dungeon) you can charge 550-600 orbs per hour.

Level 63-99: Dragonhide Bodies (Fastest XP)

Crafting dragonhide bodies is the fastest Crafting XP in the game, reaching 250K-350K XP/hour depending on the tier. The progression:

  • Level 63: Green dragonhide body (186 XP each) — ~250K XP/hour
  • Level 71: Blue dragonhide body (210 XP each) — ~280K XP/hour
  • Level 77: Red dragonhide body (234 XP each) — ~310K XP/hour
  • Level 84: Black dragonhide body (258 XP each) — ~350K XP/hour

But here's the catch: dragonhide bodies are expensive. Each body consumes 3 dragon leathers, and the finished body alchs for significantly less than the material cost. Expect to lose 5-15M GP per hour depending on the leather type. This method is for players who prioritize speed over cost. For most accounts, combining glassblowing with battlestaves or amethyst is better financial sense.

Level 83-99: Amethyst Arrowtips (AFK Profit)

Cutting amethyst into arrowtips is a low-intensity, profitable Crafting method that becomes available at level 83 (with boosts usable from 80+). Each amethyst cuts into 15 arrowtips, yielding 60 Crafting XP and 15 Fletching XP per amethyst. The arrowtips stack in your inventory, so you can cut a full inventory of amethyst (27 slots, each cutting 15 tips = 405 arrowtips per invent) before banking.

XP rate: ~55K-65K Crafting XP/hour. Profit: varies with amethyst prices, but typically 100K-200K GP/hour (amethyst arrowtips are always in demand). The AFK window is 20-30 seconds per inventory — not as AFK as amethyst mining, but still relaxed.

Ironman Crafting Strategy

The ironman Crafting meta revolves around the giant seaweed + sandstone grinding + Superglass Make pipeline:

  1. Grow giant seaweed on Fossil Island (two underwater patches). Each patch yields 30-50 seaweed per harvest, growing in ~40 minutes. Harvest regularly — you'll need roughly 6,000 giant seaweed for 99 via glassblowing.
  2. Mine sandstone at the Desert Quarry. At 3,000-4,000 buckets of sand per hour (with dragon pickaxe and 70+ Mining), you'll need roughly 50,000-60,000 buckets of sand total. The sandstorm grinder next to the quarry converts sandstone into buckets of sand (costs 50 GP per bucket, or use the camulet/desert amulet for teleports).
  3. Cast Superglass Make (77 Magic) for mass molten glass production. With 3 giant seaweed + 18 sand per inventory, you produce 23-30 molten glass per 6-tick cast. One hour of Superglass Make can bank 3-4 hours of glassblowing.
  4. Blow into unpowered orbs (46-87) then light orbs (87-99) at a bank. Light orbs can be alched or dropped — they have no practical use beyond XP.

The grind is time-consuming but self-sufficient. Expect 80-120 hours of total effort for 99 Crafting as an ironman, split across farming, mining, and blowing.

Warning: Do not blow molten glass at low levels expecting profit. Vials (level 33) are the first profitable item, and unpowered orbs (46) are where the XP gets good. Blowing beer glasses or oil lamps to 46 is a necessary slog — bank your glass efficiently and power through it.

The Crafting cape is one of the most useful in the game (unlimited teleport to the Crafting Guild, closest bank to a teleport location), so the grind pays off in convenience as well as levels.
