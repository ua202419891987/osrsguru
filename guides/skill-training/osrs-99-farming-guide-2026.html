OSRS Farming Guide 1-99 2026 — Tree Runs, Herbs & Tithe Farm

Farming is unique among OSRS skills: you don't stand at a bank grinding the same action for hours. Instead, you plant crops, go do something else, and return when they're grown. This makes Farming one of the fastest skills by active time — you can reach 99 in 30-40 hours of actual playtime spread across weeks or months. This OSRS Farming guide 1-99 2026 covers every patch, every tree, every herb, and the optimal daily routine that turns Farming into your most efficient skill.

How Farming Works

The core mechanic: you use seeds on patches, optionally apply compost, wait real-world time for them to grow, and harvest for XP. Growth happens in background cycles (ticks) — you don't need to be logged in. A yew tree takes ~6.5 hours to grow from seed to fully grown; a ranarr herb takes ~80 minutes. Once a plant is fully grown, check its health for the bulk of the XP. Herbs, allotments, and hops are harvested repeatedly (each harvest gives XP); trees, fruit trees, and hardwood are a single check for massive XP then optionally cut down.

Key tools and upgrades to prioritize:

  • Magic secateurs: Reward from Fairy Tale Part I (no Farming requirement). When worn while harvesting herbs, allotments, and hops, yield increases by 10%. Get these immediately.
  • Bottomless compost bucket: Drops from Hespori (65 Farming). Holds up to 10,000 compost doses and doubles the effect of compost placed inside. A permanent account upgrade.
  • Seed dibber, spade, rake: Basic tools from any farming shop. Always keep them in your inventory or tool leprechaun storage.
  • Ultracompost: Made by combining supercompost with volcanic ash (Fossil Island). Reduces disease chance to ~8% and significantly increases herb yield. Always use ultracompost on herb patches — the increased yield more than pays for the cost.

Tree Runs: The Backbone of 99 Farming

Tree runs are the primary XP engine for Farming. A tree run takes 5-7 minutes and gives 50K-150K+ XP depending on the trees planted. Do one tree run per day (normal trees) or two per day (fruit trees, which grow in ~16 hours) and your Farming level climbs passively.

Normal Trees (6 patches)

Plant saplings in tree patches, wait for them to grow, check health for massive XP, then optionally chop down (requires an axe, gives roots for herblore). Patches: Varrock Castle, Falador Park, Taverley (south of the tree patch), Lumbridge (behind the castle), Tree Gnome Stronghold, and the Farming Guild (45+ Farming).

Most players use willows to 45, maples to 60, yews to 75, then magics to 99. If you're on a budget, stick with yews — they're half the cost for half the XP, so the GP/XP ratio is similar.

Fruit Trees (7 patches)

Fruit tree patches grow faster (~16 hours) and give more XP than equivalent-tier normal trees — but seeds are more expensive. Patches: Tree Gnome Stronghold (west of the spirit tree), Catherby (east of the bank), Brimhaven (south of the dock, 40 coins per boat ride), Tree Gnome Village (follow Elkoy out of the maze), Lletya (requires Mourning's End Part I quest), Farming Guild (85+ Farming for the high-tier patch), and Prifddinas (northwest of the bank, requires Song of the Elves).

Palm trees are popular from 68 because the coconuts harvested (6 per tree, worth ~2,000 GP each) partially offset the seed cost.

Hardwood Trees (Fossil Island, 3 patches)

Hardwood trees on Fossil Island take ~3 days to grow but give enormous XP per check. There are 3 hardwood patches: two on the east side of Fossil Island (accessed via the east-west agility shortcut or the boat from the Digsite barge) and one unlocked after completing the Fossil Island quests.

Three mahogany trees give 47,160 XP every 3 days. Plant them, forget about them, and collect a massive XP drop twice a week. Teaks and mahogany are extremely cost-efficient — the GP/XP is among the best in Farming. Always have hardwood patches planted.

Special Tree Patches

  • Calquat (72 Farming): Single patch in Tai Bwo Wannai, grows in ~21.5 hours. Checking health gives 12,096 XP — enormous for a daily. The fruit can be used to make Calquat kegs (brewing), but most people just replant. Seed cost: ~1,000 GP. One of the best GP/XP ratios in the game.
  • Spirit Tree (83 Farming): Three spirit tree patches (Port Sarim, Etceteria, Brimhaven, plus the Farming Guild at 85+). Each planted spirit tree gives 19,501 XP for checking health and acts as a permanent teleport network. More a utility than XP source, but the XP is nice.
  • Celastrus (85 Farming): Single patch in the Farming Guild high tier. Grows in ~13 hours, check health for ~14,000 XP, then harvest celastrus bark which can be fletched into battlestaves. A great daily for both XP and profit.
  • Redwood (90 Farming): Single patch in the Farming Guild high tier. Grows in ~4 days, check health for 22,450 XP. The highest single XP drop in Farming. Worth the wait.

Herb Runs: Farming's Profit Engine

Herb runs are the most profitable recurring activity in OSRS. With 8-9 herb patches (depending on quest/diary completion) and ultracompost, a single herb run takes 5-7 minutes and earns 200K-500K GP. You can do a herb run every 80 minutes — the growth time for herbs. Do 5-6 herb runs per day alongside tree runs, and you'll pay for your tree seeds while earning Farming XP passively.

The most profitable herbs in 2026: ranarrs (consistent demand for prayer potions) and snapdragons (super restores, always in demand). Torstols give the most XP but are volatile — the profit depends entirely on whether the market is saturated.

Herb Yield Boosts (Stack Them All)

  • Ultracompost: Increases minimum yield to 6 herbs per patch (from the default 5) and reduces disease chance to ~8%. Always use ultracompost on herbs — the cost (~3K GP per bucket) is covered by one extra herb.
  • Magic secateurs: +10% yield when worn during harvesting.
  • Farming cape perk (99 Farming): +5% yield from herb patches.
  • Attas anima plant (Farming Guild): Planted in the anima patch (76 Farming to unlock, seed from Hespori). Increases yield of ALL crops across Gielinor by ~5%.

With all boosts, the minimum herb yield is 6 and the maximum is over 15 per patch. A "bad" snapdragon run is still 48 herbs (6 per patch × 8). A good run is 80+. This consistency is what makes herb runs so profitable.

Optimal Herb Run Route

The fastest herb run route (with all teleports unlocked):

  1. Ardougne cloak teleport → Ardougne herb patch (north of the farm)
  2. Explorer's ring teleport (Cabbage Port) → Falador herb patch (south of the park)
  3. Camelot teleport (or Catherby teleport tab) → Catherby herb patch (north of the bank)
  4. Ectophial teleport → Port Phasmatys herb patch (northwest, need ghostspeak amulet or Morytania Hard Diary for free access)
  5. Trollheim teleport (standard magic, requires My Arm's Big Adventure) → Troll Stronghold herb patch (never diseased if My Arm's patch)
  6. Xeric's talisman (Xeric's Glade) → Hosidius herb patch (south of the vinery, disease-free with 50% Hosidius favor)
  7. Harmony Island teleport tab (Morytania Elite Diary) → Harmony Island herb patch
  8. Farming Guild teleport (Jewellery Box/Farming cape/Skills necklace) → Farming Guild herb patch (third tier, 65+ Farming)
  9. Weiss teleport (icy basalt, requires Making Friends with My Arm) → Weiss herb patch (disease-free)

The first 6 patches are accessible to most players. The Harmony, Weiss, and Farming Guild patches require questing/diary completion but add significant profit to every run.

Warning: Herb seeds can die if disease hits (unless the patch is disease-free like Hosidius with 50% favor, Troll Stronghold after My Arm's Big Adventure, or Weiss). Always use ultracompost on non-protected patches. The ~8% disease chance means the occasional dead seed, but the vastly increased yield more than compensates. If you're paranoid, use the Resurrect Crops spell (78 Magic, Arceuus spellbook) to revive dead herbs — it costs nature runes and is usually worth it for snapdragons/snapdragons and above.

Farming Guild & Contracts

The Farming Guild at Kourend's Kebos Lowlands is the central hub for post-45 Farming:

  • Tier 1 (45 Farming): Tree patch, anima patch, herb patch, allotment/flower/seaweed patches, Farming contracts (easy). Also has a bank chest, seed vault for extra seed storage, and a tool leprechaun.
  • Tier 2 (65 Farming): Cactus patch, bush patch, fruit tree patch, Farming contracts (medium). Hespori boss (bottomless compost bucket, anima seeds, white lily seeds).
  • Tier 3 (85 Farming): Redwood patch, celastrus patch, spirit tree patch, herb patch, Farming contracts (hard).

Farming contracts are the most important feature. Each contract asks you to grow a specific crop in the guild, and upon harvest/check-health, you receive a seed pack with seeds of that contract tier and higher. This is the best passive seed income for ironmen and a solid maintenance source for mains. Rotate contracts by up/downgrading: if you get a hard contract you don't have the seed for, ask for an easier one (with a point penalty). Pre-plant common contract crops (watermelons, snape grass, potato cacti, white lilies) so you can instantly complete contracts when they appear.

Tithe Farm (Active Farming XP)

Tithe Farm (Hosidius, 34 Farming, requires 100% Hosidius favor) is the only active, repeatable Farming training method. You plant seeds, water them with the Gricoller's watering can, and harvest them — all in real time with no growth waiting period. It's not the most efficient way to 99 (tree runs are), but it provides useful unlocks and fills gaps between tree runs.

XP rates: ~30K XP/hour at 34-54, ~60K-70K at 54-74 with the Gricoller's can, and ~100K+ at 74+ with the full setup (farmer's outfit, seed box, etc.). The rewards:

  • Seed box (250 points): Stores 6 types of seeds in one inventory slot. Essential for extended herb runs and inventory management.
  • Gricoller's can (200 points): Waters all plants in one action instead of individually. Saves significant time.
  • Farmer's outfit: 4-piece set (800 points total) giving +2.5% Farming XP. Usually acquired alongside other unlocks.
  • Grape seeds (2 points each): Plant in the vinery for grapes → wines of Zamorak (profit or herblore secondaries).

Most players spend 6-10 hours at Tithe Farm for the seed box and Gricoller's can, then never return — tree runs are simply far better XP per active hour. Tithe Farm is for the unlocks, not 99.

Hespori (65 Farming)

Hespori is a solo Farming boss in the Farming Guild's second tier, fought once every ~32 hours (you get a Hespori seed on average every 1-2 tree checks). Defeating Hespori rewards:

  • Bottomless compost bucket (1/35): The best composting tool. Holds 10K charges and doubles compost effectiveness. An account-changing upgrade.
  • Anima seeds: Attas (+yield to all crops), Iasor (disease resistance), Kronos (faster growth cycles). Each lasts ~3 days once planted. Attas is the most useful for herb runs.
  • White lily seeds: Protects allotment patches from disease without compost. Plant these in the Farming Guild flower patch for instant contract completions.
  • Tangleroot pet (1/7,000 from Hespori, also 1/5,000 from herbs/trees/seaweed): One of the rarest and most prestigious skilling pets.

Fight Hespori whenever the seed is ready — the bottomless bucket is too important to delay.

Ironman Farming Strategy

Farming is the ironman's best friend. Without the Grand Exchange, tree seeds and herbs come exclusively from drops and contracts:

  • Early seeds: Birdhouse runs (Fossil Island) are the #1 source of tree seeds. Each birdhouse run takes 2 minutes and yields nests with rings, eggs, and seeds — including yew and magic seeds at higher Hunter levels. Do birdhouses religiously.
  • Farming contracts: Unlock these at 45 Farming and complete every one. Hard contracts (85+) shower you with palm, yew, magic, and dragonfruit seeds.
  • Slayer/PvM: Later-game slayer tasks (aberrant spectres, nechryael, kurasks) drop herb and tree seeds. Bosses like Zulrah and Vorkath are also excellent herb sources.
  • Giant seaweed: The two underwater patches north of Fossil Island grow giant seaweed (23 Farming). Harvest every 40 minutes when you're actively playing — you'll need thousands for crafting (superglass make).
  • Herb seeds: Pickpocketing master farmers (38 Thieving at Draynor, 94+ recommended for best rates) with the rogue's outfit gives a steady stream of ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol seeds. A few hours of master farmer thieving can fund weeks of herb runs.

Ironmen should plant whatever seeds they have, not save them. A willow seed planted today is better than a magic seed saved in the bank for a level you haven't reached yet. The XP compounds.

Pro Tip: Use the Kronos anima seed (from Hespori) on days when you have multiple tree runs to check — it skips growth cycles, meaning your trees mature faster. A Kronos-boosted magic tree can grow in 4-5 hours instead of 8. This is the most efficient way to push through late levels (90-99). Switch to Attas on normal days for herb yield.
