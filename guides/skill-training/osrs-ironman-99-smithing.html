OSRS Ironman 1-99 Smithing Guide — Forge Your Own Path

Smithing on an ironman is a marathon that touches nearly every other skill. You need ores from Mining, coal from Mining or Kingdom of Miscellania, gold ore from blast mining or Motherlode Mine, and a reliable source of GP to fund the blast furnace. This OSRS ironman 1-99 smithing guide covers the complete self-sufficient path from level 1 to 99 in 2026, with special attention to the Giants' Foundry update that revolutionized ironman Smithing. If you're wondering whether to smelt gold at the blast furnace or go the Foundry route — the answer has shifted dramatically in the last year.

Giants' Foundry vs Blast Furnace: The 2026 Ironman Meta

Since Giants' Foundry released in 2022, the ironman smithing meta has been a two-headed beast. Here's the 2026 breakdown:

  • Blast Furnace (gold ore with goldsmith gauntlets): 250K-350K XP/hour, requires buying gold ore from the Blast Furnace shop at 150-200 GP each. You need GP and an efficient world for the BF pumps. More XP per hour but costs gold.
  • Giants' Foundry (mixed metal bars): 180K-220K XP/hour, consumes bars you've already made (or buy armor/weps from shops, melt them down). Gives Foundry reputation for the Smithing outfit (5% XP boost total with full set + the cannonball mold). Free method once you have bars, but slower.

The general ironman consensus for 2026: do Giants' Foundry to 99 if you have an excess of bars from Slayer, PVM, and Kingdom. Supplement with Blast Furnace gold when you have a GP surplus from CG, Hydra, or Vorkath. Neither method is "wrong" — the optimal path mixes both based on your current resources.

Level 1–40: The Knight's Sword and Early Smelting

Start with The Knight's Sword quest in Falador. It requires 10 Mining (mine blurite in the ice dungeon south of Port Sarim) and gives 12,725 Smithing XP, which jumps you from 1 to 29 instantly. This is non-negotiable for every ironman — do this quest immediately after tutorial island.

From 29 to 40, smelt iron bars from iron ore. Mine iron at the Al Kharid scorpion mine (3 rocks, close to the Al Kharid bank) or if you've done the Ardougne cloak 1 achievement, the mining spot just northeast of East Ardougne has a bank even closer. With a ring of forging (made at 20 Crafting with a ruby and gold bar), each iron ore has a 100% success rate at smelting, so you don't waste any. Smelt iron bars, then make iron platebodies (5 bars each, 125 XP each) at the Varrock anvil until 40. This takes about 3-4 hours of combined mining and smithing.

Level 40–70: Gold Ore at Blast Furnace (If You Have GP)

At 40 Smithing with the Family Crest quest completed (goldsmith gauntlets multiply gold smelting XP by 2.5x), the blast furnace gold method becomes available. Each gold ore smelted gives 56.2 XP (22.5 base × 2.5 gauntlets). At the blast furnace on an official world with the dwarven machines running, you can smelt 5,000-6,000 gold ore per hour.

The question is: where do ironmen get gold ore?

  • Buy from the Blast Furnace ore shop (Ordan in Keldagrim) for 150-200 GP per ore. Stock of 100, resets every few seconds on official worlds. At 200 GP per ore and 56.2 XP per ore, that's about 3.5 GP/XP. To go from 40 to 70 (roughly 700K XP), you'd spend about 2.5M GP on gold ore — expensive for early ironmen but manageable once you have a decent moneymaker.
  • Mine gold ore directly at the Crafting Guild (40 Crafting for entry), Motherlode Mine, or blast mine in Lovakengj. This is self-sufficient but incredibly slow.
  • Volcanic Mine (50 Mining, access from Fossil Island) — the best group minigame for Mining XP, and it drops a ton of gold/dragon/rune ores on the side. Hit 70+ Mining at Volcanic Mine and you'll passively bank thousands of gold ore.

If you're doing the Corrupted Gauntlet for your BowFa (and you should be), every CG completion drops 70-100K GP worth of alchables. After 400+ CG clears for the enhanced seed, you'll have accumulated 30M+ in raw GP — more than enough to fund gold ore for 99 Smithing. In other words: CG literally pays for your Smithing cape.

Level 70–99: Giants' Foundry (The Ironman MVP)

Giants' Foundry is tailor-made for ironmen. It converts bars (or melted-down armor/weapons from drops or shops) into Smithing XP at an excellent rate, and you earn the Smithing outfit along the way. Here's the optimal Foundry strategy:

  • Alloy ratio: Use 14 steel bars + 14 mithril bars (or the equivalent in melted items) per sword. This gives the best balance of XP per commission and material efficiency.
  • Material sourcing: Steel bars from iron + coal (both abundant from Miscellania: get coal from Kingdom, mine iron). Mithril bars from mithril + coal (mithril ore from Miscellania, Zalcano, or various PVM drops). If you have an excess of addy/rune bars from PVM, use 14 mithril + 14 adamant for faster rep but higher cost.
  • Commission optimization: Always pick the commission with the highest quality requirement you can reliably hit. At 80+ Smithing, you can comfortably hit the "Master" tier commission for bonus XP and more rep points.

XP rate at Giants' Foundry (14 steel + 14 mithril, 80+ Smithing): 180K-220K XP/hour. You'll earn enough Foundry reputation to buy the full Smithing outfit (2.5% XP boost × 4 pieces = 10% total) within the first 20 hours. After the outfit, the effective XP rate jumps another 10%.

Ironman Smithing Roadmap Summary

1-29: The Knight's Sword quest. 29-40: Iron bars and iron platebodies. 40-70: Gold ore at Blast Furnace (buy with GP or mine at Volcanic Mine). 70-99: Giants' Foundry with steel + mithril alloys, using bars from Miscellania (coal) + Mining Guild (iron) + PVM/Zalcano (mithril+). Supplement with BF gold when cash is plentiful. The total time to 99 varies based on how you source ores, but the Smithing cape is one of the more satisfying ironman 99s — every bar you smelted was mined or earned, not bought on the GE. That's the ironman way.
