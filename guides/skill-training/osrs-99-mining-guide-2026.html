OSRS Mining Guide 1-99 2026 — Fastest & AFK Training Methods

Mining is one of the most rewarding gathering skills in OSRS, with multiple viable training paths that range from high-intensity click-heavy methods to completely AFK alternatives. Whether you're chasing the fastest 99 with 3-tick granite or want to stack up ores at Motherlode Mine for smithing, this OSRS Mining guide 1-99 2026 covers every method, every XP rate, and the best gear to wear along the way.

Mining Gear & Boosters

Before you start swinging your pickaxe, get the right gear. These items directly affect your XP rates and should be acquired as early as possible:

  • Prospector Outfit: Purchased from the Motherlode Mine for 180 golden nuggets. Grants +2.5% Mining XP when wearing the full set — essential for any serious miner. Expect to spend 10-15 hours at MLM to earn the full outfit.
  • Varrock Armor: Reward from the Varrock Achievement Diary. At tier 1, you have a 10% chance to mine two ores at once (up to adamantite). This effectively increases XP and ore yield per hour — get at least Varrock Armor 1 (easy diary) immediately. Varrock Armor 4 from the elite diary gives the best rates.
  • Celestial Ring: Purchased with stardust from Shooting Stars (requires 2,000 stardust). Provides an invisible +4 Mining boost that does not stack with visible potion boosts. Worth getting once you can mine stars at 10+.
  • Dragon Pickaxe: The best pickaxe in the game for most players (+6 Mining boost special attack). The crystal pickaxe (upgraded with 120 crystal shards and a dragon pickaxe) is marginally better but the difference is small.
Pro Tip: Always use the best pickaxe you can equip. Pickaxe progression: Bronze/Iron (1) → Steel (6) → Black (11) → Mithril (21) → Adamant (31) → Rune (41) → Dragon (61, requires 60 Attack). The dragon pickaxe costs around 1.5M GP and is absolutely worth the investment — the faster mining speed pays for itself.

Level 1-15: Questing & Copper/Tin

Skip the early grind entirely with quests. Complete Doric's Quest (1,300 Mining XP, no requirements) and Plague City (2,425 Mining XP) to jump from level 1 to level 15 in under 30 minutes. If you're an ironman or prefer not to quest, mine copper and tin in the Lumbridge swamp mine (southwest of Lumbridge) to 15 — expect about 10K XP/hour.

At level 10, you can also choose to mine at the Shooting Stars for early mining XP and stardust toward a celestial ring, but the rates are slow at low levels. Stars are an excellent long-term AFK option.

Level 15-45/75: Power Mining Iron Ore

Power mining iron ore is the fastest Mining XP in the game until you unlock 3-tick granite at level 45. The concept is simple: mine iron rocks, drop the ore, repeat. Since iron rocks respawn in roughly 4.8 seconds (1-tick after depletion in most spots), you can establish a rhythm and sustain 45K-65K XP/hour depending on your Mining level and attention.

The best locations for power mining iron:

  • Mining Guild (Falador, 60+ Mining recommended for the underground area) — 4 iron rocks close together with a deposit box nearby if you want to bank instead of drop.
  • Al Kharid Mine (scorpion mine) — Classic spot with 3 iron rocks, close to the bank.
  • Legends' Guild (southwest of the guild) — 4 iron rocks in a compact area. Great if you have access.
  • Piscatoris Mining Site — 3 iron rocks, often quiet on most worlds.

Use varrock armor to double your ore yield when banking. If you're dropping, the XP is identical — what matters is pure click speed. At maximum efficiency with a dragon pickaxe, expect ~65K XP/hour at level 60+ doing power mining. This method takes you from 15 to 75 in about 15-18 hours of power mining.

Level 30-99: Motherlode Mine (AFK & Profitable)

The Motherlode Mine (Falador, accessed through the cave on the east side of the Dwarven Mine) is the best AFK Mining method in OSRS and a staple for both main accounts and ironmen. You mine pay-dirt from ore veins, deposit it in the hopper, and collect cleaned ores from the sack — gold nuggets earned throughout can be spent on the prospector outfit and coal/gem bags.

MLM XP rates:

  • Level 30-55 (lower level, no upper access): ~20K-25K XP/hour
  • Level 55-72 (upper level unlocked): ~30K-40K XP/hour
  • Level 72-85: ~40K-50K XP/hour
  • Level 85-99: ~45K-55K XP/hour

The upper level (unlocked at 72 Mining, accessed via the ladder on the north wall of the mine) dramatically improves rates because the veins are closer together and deplete more slowly. Unlock the upper level as soon as you hit 72 — 100 golden nuggets well spent.

For ironmen, MLM provides a steady supply of coal, gold, mithril, adamantite, and runite ores for smithing. Combined with the blast furnace, MLM is the most efficient way to feed smithing and crafting (through gold ores with goldsmith gauntlets). Prospector outfit from 180 nuggets is the priority purchase — then coal bag (100 nuggets), then gem bag (100 nuggets).

Warning: The lower level at MLM (levels 30-72) is painfully slow. If you can handle power mining iron, do it to at least 55-60 before switching to MLM. You'll save dozens of hours. The lower level should only be used if you absolutely require the AFK time.

Level 45-99: Granite (3-Tick Mining)

3-tick granite mining is the absolute fastest Mining XP in OSRS, reaching 75K-100K+ XP/hour at higher levels. It uses a tick-manipulation technique where you interrupt your mining action after 3 ticks (every 0.6 seconds) by performing another action — typically dropping or using a herb on a tar — then clicking back on the rock immediately.

The technique: equip a pestle and mortar and have swamp tar and a clean herb (like guam) in your inventory. Click the granite rock, and after 3 ticks (the moment your character starts the mining animation), use the herb on the tar to reset the mining cycle, then click the rock again. The rock immediately responds as if the full mining cycle has elapsed. It takes practice, but once mastered it's a rhythm — and the XP is unmatched.

The Quarry (Desert, south of the Bandit Camp) is the sole location. Bring waterskins (or wear a circlet of water/desert amulet 4) and drop all granite under 5kg. At 45 Mining you mine 500g blocks; at 75 you can mine 2kg blocks for better XP. The Kamikaze method (3-tick) at the quarry in the southeast section yields ~100K-110K XP/hour at 90+ with a dragon pickaxe.

Granite is not AFK at all — it's the most click-intensive skilling method in the game. Only pursue this if you want the fastest 99 and can tolerate the carpal tunnel risk. Most players use MLM instead.

Level 92-99: Amethyst (AFK Profit)

At level 92 Mining, you unlock amethyst crystals in the Mining Guild's underground area (requiring Falador Hard Diary for the underground access). Amethyst is the most AFK profitable mining method: each amethyst crystal takes roughly 60-90 seconds to deplete, and amethyst ammunition (darts, arrows, bolts) is always in demand for endgame PvM.

XP rates: 20K-24K/hour. Profit: 400K-500K GP/hour. Each amethyst is worth roughly 3,500 GP, and you can expect 120-140 amethyst per hour with a dragon pickaxe and varrock armor. The complete AFK window (60+ seconds between clicks) makes this ideal while playing an alt, watching videos, or working on something else.

For ironmen, amethyst is the best source of ammunition for the blowpipe (amethyst darts at 90 Fletching) and crossbows (amethyst bolts at 83 Fletching). It's a long-term grind but produces ammo that competes with dragon darts/bolts without requiring constant bossing.

Other Mining Methods

Blast Mine (Level 43+)

The Blast Mine in Lovakengj (Zeah, requires 100% Lovakengj favor) uses dynamite on the cave walls to blast out ores. Expect 55K-70K XP/hour and decent profit from runite and coal ores. The downside: dynamite costs ~1,000 GP each, and you use roughly 150-200 per hour. Net profit is modest (100K-300K GP/hour depending on runite luck) but the XP is competitive for the mid-levels.

Volcanic Mine (Level 70+, Fossil Island)

Volcanic Mine is a group-based minigame on Fossil Island that yields 70K-90K Mining XP/hour in a coordinated team. You channel vents, avoid lava, and mine boulders in the central chamber. It's the second-best XP in the game after 3-tick granite but requires finding a team (use the "Volcanic Mine" friends chat or Discord). You also earn numulite and unidentified fossils. Not AFK, but less intense than granite while still providing excellent XP.

Ironman Mining Strategy

Ironmen should approach Mining differently than main accounts. The goal isn't just XP — it's gathering ores for smithing, crafting, and fletching. Here's the optimal ironman Mining path:

  • Early game (1-72): Power mine iron to 30, then switch to Motherlode Mine for coal, gold, mithril, and adamantite. Buy prospector outfit first (180 nuggets), then coal bag, then gem bag.
  • Sandstone Mining (Desert Quarry): Mine sandstone (1kg blocks) and grind it into buckets of sand using the sandstorm grinder — each bucket of sand combined with giant seaweed yields ~1.7 molten glass via superglass make. This is the meta for ironman crafting. Mining 3,000-4,000 buckets of sand per hour at the quarry with a dragon pickaxe.
  • Runite for alchables: At 85 Mining, mine runite at the Mining Guild, Myths' Guild, or Fossil Island and smith into rune items at the blast furnace for alch profit.
  • Amethyst (92+): Essential for ammunition. Amethyst darts at 90 Fletching are your blowpipe ammo forever.
Pro Tip: Use the Mining Gloves from the Mining Guild (purchased with unidentified minerals) to speed up mining in the guild. Superior mining gloves (60 minerals) give a chance to mine two ores at once from silver, coal, and gold rocks. Expert mining gloves (120 minerals) extend this to mithril, adamantite, and runite. They stack with varrock armor's double-ore effect.
