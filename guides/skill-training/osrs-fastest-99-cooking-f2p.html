OSRS Fastest 99 Cooking Guide F2P — Max Cooking Without Membership

If you're looking for the OSRS fastest 99 cooking guide f2p, you've landed in the right place. Cooking is hands-down the easiest and cheapest 99 you can get in Old School RuneScape free-to-play, and in 2026 the meta has only gotten more refined. Whether you want the cape for fashionscape, a trimmed skillcape requirement, or just a quick 99 to flex on your friends, this guide breaks down every method, every XP rate, and every gold piece you'll spend along the way.

Why Cooking Is the Easiest F2P 99

Cooking in F2P has one massive advantage over every other free-to-play skill: it's fast and profitable. At higher levels, you can pull 200,000+ XP per hour without burning a single coin — in fact, you'll often make money while training. Compare that to Runecrafting or Smithing in F2P, and the choice is obvious.

The skill works on a simple burn mechanic: the higher your Cooking level relative to the food's required level, the less you burn. Once you stop burning entirely (which happens well before 99 for most fish), every single cook is a guaranteed success.

Level 1–15: Getting Started (Shrimp & Sardines)

At level 1, you're stuck with shrimp. Head to the fishing spots south of Lumbridge or the pond in Lumbridge itself. You'll burn more than half of what you cook, but that's normal. Shrimp stop burning at level 34, but you won't be here long.

For maximum speed, just power through shrimp until level 15. It'll take about 10 minutes. Buy raw shrimp from the Grand Exchange for peanuts and cook them at the Lumbridge castle range — the range gives a lower burn rate than fires.

Level 15–30: Trout Territory

At level 15, unlock trout. This is where the XP starts getting real. Trout give 70 XP each and are dirt cheap on the GE. You'll burn a decent amount until level 50, but the XP per hour is far superior to sticking with shrimp or sardines.

Method: Buy 5,000 raw trout at the GE (~20 GP each in 2026), run to the Lumbridge castle range, and cook an inventory in about 55 seconds. You'll hit 30 Cooking in under 20 minutes.

Level 30–50: The Salmon Grind

Salmon unlocks at 30 and gives 90 XP per cook. Combined with trout — since you'll be catching both if fishing yourself — this bracket flies by. If buying from the GE, salmon costs slightly more than trout (around 50 GP raw) but the XP efficiency is worth it.

Use the Lumbridge castle range for ALL your cooking in F2P. It has a hidden burn rate reduction that beats campfires and regular ranges. The Hosidius range is members-only, but Lumbridge castle is the F2P equivalent.

Level 50–74: Lobsters — Your First Big Milestone

At level 40, lobsters unlock — but you'll burn them like crazy until about level 64. Start cooking lobsters at 50 for a good balance of XP and reduced frustration. Each lobster gives 120 XP, and by level 68 you'll stop burning them at the Lumbridge range. By level 74 (the no-burn point even on fires), you're literally printing XP with zero risk.

Raw lobsters cost about 80-100 GP each in the 2026 economy. Cooked lobsters sell for 120-140 GP. That's a 20-40 GP profit per lobster, meaning you can make 1M+ GP on the road to 99. Not bad for a "training" activity.

Level 74–84: Swordfish Power Hour

Swordfish unlock at 45 but don't stop burning until level 86 at a range (81 at Lumbridge range). Start them at level 74 when your burn rate is manageable. Each swordfish gives 140 XP. At this bracket you'll average about 150K XP/hour with concentrated play.

In 2026, raw swordfish hover around 120 GP each, and cooked sell for 160+. Profit margins are thinner than lobsters but the XP is significantly faster.

Level 84–99: The Final Stretch

This is where you have two clear paths, and the choice comes down to speed versus profit.

Method A: Swordfish to 99 (Profit)

Stick with swordfish all the way. At level 86+ you never burn them, making this 100% AFK and 100% profitable. You'll earn about 140K-160K XP per hour and walk away with roughly 2-3M GP profit from 84 to 99. This is the "chill" method.

Method B: Wine (Speed — Members Only, F2P Skip)

Wine requires membership (Dragon's Breath quest for the drunk effect removing fermenting failure). In pure F2P, swordfish is your fastest option. There's no equivalent speed method without membership, so plan accordingly.

What About Anchovies and Tuna?

Skip them. Anchovies give only 30 XP and tuna give 100 XP but burn forever and have terrible GE margins. They're a noob trap. The trout-to-lobster-to-swordfish pipeline is mathematically superior.

If you're a brand new account with zero GP, fish your own supplies. Go to Draynor Village, fish shrimp to 20 Fishing, then trout/salmon at Lumbridge to 50 Fishing and Cooking simultaneously. It's slower, but free, and you'll train two skills at once.

F2P Cooking FAQ

Can I get 99 Cooking in one day on F2P?

Absolutely. From level 1, going hard at 160K XP/hour average, you're looking at about 80-85 hours. If you no-life it across a long weekend, one 99 cape coming right up.

Does the cooking gauntlets help in F2P?

Cooking gauntlets are a members-only item from the Family Crest quest. In F2P, your best burn reduction comes from the Lumbridge castle range alone. No gauntlets, no Hosidius range — just the castle range.

What's better for profit — lobsters or swordfish?

Lobsters have the highest profit margin per cook in F2P (about 20-40 GP each). Swordfish give faster XP but lower margins. If you want to maximize GP, cook lobsters until 84, then switch to swordfish purely for the speed.

Final Verdict: Cooking Is Your First F2P 99

Cooking is the fastest and most profitable F2P 99 in OSRS, period. It funds itself, it's low-attention, and by the end you'll have a clean 3M+ GP in your pocket and a shiny orange cape on your back. Start with shrimp, push through trout and salmon, settle into lobsters, and finish strong with swordfish. No membership required, no quests needed, no RNG — just raw, consistent progress. In the 2026 meta, nothing else in free-to-play comes close.
