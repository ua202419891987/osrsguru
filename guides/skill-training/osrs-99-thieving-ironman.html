OSRS 1-99 Thieving Guide for Ironman — No GE, No Problem

Thieving on an ironman is a completely different beast compared to a main account. You can't just buy dodgy necklaces off the Grand Exchange or chug stamina potions by the dozen. Every resource you use, you have to earn or craft yourself. This OSRS 1-99 thieving guide for ironman players covers the optimal path from level 1 all the way to that purple cape, using only methods that don't rely on trading. In 2026, with Varlamore's introduction and several quality-of-life updates, the ironman thieving meta has shifted — and I've tested the new routes extensively.

Why Train Thieving on an Ironman?

Thieving is low-key one of the best early-game ironman skills because it generates raw GP, seeds for Farming, and crucial supplies like ranarr and snapdragon seeds from master farmers. It also unlocks pickpocketing elves and vyres in the late game, which are insane for ironman resource generation. Plus, the skill is completely self-sufficient — you can train it naked with zero supplies and still hit 200K+ XP/hour at higher levels.

Level 1–5: Men and Women (Lumbridge)

Start by pickpocketing men and women around the Lumbridge castle courtyard. Each successful pickpocket gives 8 XP and a handful of coins (3 GP). Don't bother banking the GP — just drop it if inventory fills up, though at these levels you won't fill your inventory before hitting level 5. This takes under 2 minutes.

At level 5, immediately move to the next target. Staying on men past level 5 is a waste of time.

Level 5–25: Tea Stall (Varrock)

The tea stall in Varrock (just east of the east bank) is the best early thieving XP in the game. Each successful steal gives 16 XP, and the stall respawns in about 5 seconds. You can click the same stall repeatedly while standing in one spot. The guard nearby will occasionally catch you, but at these levels the fail rate is low enough that it barely matters.

Drop the cups of tea — they're worthless. You're here for pure XP. From level 5 to 25, this takes about 15 minutes of steady clicking.

If you're a hardcore ironman worried about dying to the guard — don't be. The guard only stuns you for a few seconds and does negligible damage. Just bring 3-4 pieces of food (shrimp from tutorial island works) and you'll be fine.

Level 25–38: Silk Stall (Ardougne)

At level 20, silk stalls unlock, but wait until 25 when you fail less often. The silk stall in Ardougne market (north of the east bank) gives 24 XP per steal. The key advantage here is that the stall is right next to the bank, so you can bank the silk and sell it to the silk trader in Ardougne for 60 GP each later — or just keep it for Crafting (you'll need silk for quests like Ghosts Ahoy).

From 25 to 38, this will take about 30-40 minutes. You'll end up with roughly 300 silk, which is decent starter cash (18K GP) or useful for questing.

Level 38–55: Master Farmers — The Ironman MVP

At level 38, unlock master farmers. This is the point where thieving becomes genuinely valuable for your ironman. Master farmers in Draynor Village (trapped in the house north of the bank) give 43 XP per pickpocket and drop seeds of all types, including ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol. At 38 you'll fail about half the time, but by 50 the fail rate drops significantly.

The strategy here is simple: trap the master farmer in the Draynor house, click like a madman, and bank the seeds at Draynor bank 10 steps away. Even at low levels, this method generates ranarr seeds worth 25K+ each on a main — priceless on an ironman where you can't buy them.

Level 55–70: Ardougne Knights (The Classic)

At level 55 with the Ardougne medium diary complete (which gives a 10% increased pickpocket success rate in Ardougne), knights become viable. Each knight gives 84.3 XP and a consistent 50 GP per pickpocket. In 2026, the meta is to trap a knight in the Ardougne market house (northeast building, east room) by having another player or alt stand in the doorway. If you're a solo ironman without an alt, use the south bank knight — open the door, lure him inside, and close the door.

At level 55, expect to fail roughly 30% of the time. Dodge necklaces from the rogue trader minigame help, but as an ironman that's a grind in itself. Honestly, just click through the fails — the XP is still among the best in the game.

Level 70–84: Knights or Master Farmers (Your Choice)

From 70 to 84, you have two strong options:

Level 84–99: Elves and Vyres (Endgame Ironman Thieving)

At level 82 (boostable to 85 with a summer pie for elves), the endgame opens up. Pickpocketing elves in Prifddinas or Lletya gives crystal shards and enhanced teleport seeds (the eternal glory equivalent for ironmen). Vyres in Darkmeyer give blood shards, which are insanely valuable for PVM.

At level 99 with the Thieving cape perk (10% increased success rate), elves become nearly AFK with the shadow veil spell from the Arceuus spellbook. This is the peak of ironman thieving: you're generating crystal shards for armor charges, enhanced seeds for teleports, and solid XP all at once.

What About Pyramid Plunder?

Pyramid Plunder unlocks at 21 Thieving but becomes efficient around level 71 when you can access all but the final room. It offers two things knights don't: a Pharaoh's sceptre (1/1000 drop rate per golden chest/ sarcophagus) and the fastest raw XP in the game (200K+/hour at 91+). For ironmen, the sceptre is a massive quality-of-life upgrade — it teleports you to the pyramid, the ancient altar, and the agility pyramid. Absolutely worth grinding out. Do rooms 5-7, looting only the golden chest and sarcophagus in room 7 for the sceptre chance, then finish the last room's urns for XP.

Use the shadow veil spell (Arceuus spellbook, level 47 Magic) whenever pickpocketing anything valuable. It gives a 15% chance to avoid being stunned and provides double loot. For ironmen picking elves or vyres, this is non-negotiable — the extra crystal shards and blood shards add up to tens of hours saved.

Ironman-Specific Quest XP

Several quests give notable Thieving XP that can skip entire early-game grinds:

  • The Feud — 15,000 XP (requires 30 Thieving). Do this ASAP after hitting 30. The blackjack method you learn during the quest is also decent XP from 30-45.
  • Fight Arena — 1,000 XP (easy, early game)
  • Hazeel Cult — 1,500 XP
  • Contact! — 7,000 XP (requires Icthlarin's Little Helper)
  • Ratcatchers — 4,500 XP (controversial quest, but the XP is free)

Final Thoughts

Thieving on an ironman is one of the most rewarding grinds in OSRS. It funds your kingdom, supplies your herb runs, gives you crystal shards for your BowFa upkeep, and delivers a top-tier skillcape perk that makes all future thieving 10% more successful. The key milestones are 38 (master farmers for seeds), 55 (knights for GP), and 84 (elves for shards/vampyres for blood shards). Don't sleep on Pyramid Plunder for the sceptre, and always use shadow veil when pickpocketing high-value targets. In 2026, this path is battle-tested by thousands of ironmen and it works every time.
