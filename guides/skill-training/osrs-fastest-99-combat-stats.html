OSRS Fastest 99 Attack Strength Defence — Speed to Max Melee

Getting 99 Attack, Strength, and Defence is the holy trinity of OSRS combat training. Whether you're a PVM main prepping for raids, a PK build rushing stats, or just someone who wants the classic trimmed skillcape, this fastest 99 attack strength defence guide breaks down every viable training method in 2026 ranked by pure XP per hour. I'll cover the fastest methods regardless of cost, plus budget-friendly alternatives when your cash stack is thin. Melee training has gotten more varied than ever — with Scurrius, Perilous Moons gear, and the Naguas in Varlamore all shaking up the meta.

The Golden Rule of Melee Training: Prioritize Strength First

Here's a mistake I see new players make constantly: training Attack and Defence before Strength. Strength determines your max hit, which directly controls how much XP you earn per swing. Higher max hit = higher XP per hour for ALL melee stats. The optimal order is: Strength to 90+ first, then Attack to 75 (for godswords/whips), then Defence wherever you need it for gear upgrades. If you're going for all three 99s, train them in this order: Strength → Attack → Defence.

Level 1–40: Chickens, Cows, and the Low-Level Gauntlet

Starting from scratch, kill chickens (3 HP, Lumbridge) or cows (8 HP, Lumbridge east farm) with whatever weapon you have. At level 5 Attack, switch to a steel scimitar (bought from Zeke's in Al Kharid for 400 GP). At level 10 Attack, upgrade to a black scimitar. At level 20 Attack, get a mithril scimitar. At 30, adamant. At 40, rune scimitar. Stick to scimitars — they have the highest DPS for their tier due to attack speed (4 ticks, or 2.4 seconds per hit). The slow, hard-hitting weapons like battleaxes and warhammers are a trap — DPS is king for XP.

From 1-40 in all three melee stats, just grind cows or the Al Kharid warriors (in the palace, they're passive and have decent HP). Duration: about 2 hours.

At level 10 combat, do the Waterfall Quest. It requires zero combat, gives you 13,750 Attack and Strength XP, and jumps you from 1 to 30 in both stats instantly. This is the single best quest for new accounts and skips 2 hours of cow punching.

Level 40–70: Sand Crabs and Ammonite Crabs (AFK Heaven)

At level 40 melee stats, sand crabs on the southern coast of Hosidius (Zeah) become your training ground. Sand crabs have 60 HP, hit 1s, and are aggressive for 10 minutes before losing interest — meaning you can literally click once every 10 minutes to reset aggro. Each crab gives about 200 combat XP (split between the style you're training). XP rate: 30K-45K/hour.

At level 60+ melee, graduate to ammonite crabs on Fossil Island (requires Bone Voyage quest). Ammonite crabs have 100 HP, the same passive aggression mechanic, and better respawn density. With decent gear and mid-60s stats, expect 55K-65K XP/hour. Ammonite crabs will carry you to level 70-75 easily.

The 2026 crab meta is still strong, though some of the southern amonnite spots are more crowded than ever. Bring a rock crab (or any aggressive monster) to the westernmost ammonite spot near the seaweed patches — fewer players, more crabs for you.

Level 70–99: Nightmare Zone (NMZ) — The King of AFK Melee

Nightmare Zone in Yanille is arguably the best melee training method in the game for the simple reason that you can do it while completely AFK for 20 minutes at a time. Here's the standard NMZ AFK setup for 2026:

  • Gear: Full obsidian armor (helm, platebody, platelegs) + obsidian sword (berserker necklace) — this set gives a massive damage bonus with obsidian weapons. At high levels, switch to bandos + rapier/fang.
  • Bosses (Normal mode, Hard rumble): Count Draynor, Sand Snake, King Roald, The Kendall, Me (from Lunar Diplomacy), Tree Spirit, Khazard Warlord. These are the standard "easy rumble" bosses with low defense.
  • Absorption potions: Drink a full inventory (up to 1,000 absorption points) and use the rapid heal prayer flick trick to reset your HP regen to 1, keeping absorption up indefinitely.
  • Overload potions (51 Herblore): Boosts all combat stats by 5-19 levels for 5 minutes. Non-negotiable for maximum XP.

Dharok's at 1 HP is the fastest melee XP in the game, but it requires active play (flicking rapid heal every 50 seconds to stay at 1 HP while absorption tanking). For pure AFK, obsidian or bandos setups are the meta.

Alternative: Scurrius (The Rat King, Level 60+ Combat)

Scurrius, introduced in early 2024, is a mid-level boss under Varrock that's specifically designed as a training boss. You need 43 Prayer (for protection prayers) and level 60+ combat stats. Scurrius drops rat spines that can be combined into bone weapons — the bone mace, in particular, gives a massive bonus against Scurrius (up to +20 max hit).

With the bone mace, Scurrius offers 60K-80K melee XP/hour at mid-level and is significantly more engaging than crabs. It also trains your PVM fundamentals (prayer switching, movement) and drops alchables worth about 300K GP/hour. If you're bored of crabs and don't want to pay for NMZ potions, Scurrius is an excellent bridge from 60 to 85+ melee.

Level 70+ Slayer: Efficiency + Profit

If you're going to max your account eventually, training melee through Slayer from 70+ is the most efficient long-term approach. You'll train combat, Slayer, and earn GP simultaneously. The key is blocking slow XP tasks (metal dragons, spiritual creatures) and skipping them with Slayer points. At 85 Slayer with a whip/rapier and high Strength, you'll average 60K-80K melee XP/hour while gaining 25K-35K Slayer XP/hour and earning 500K-1M GP/hour depending on the task.

The best Slayer master for melee XP is Duradel (100 combat, Shilo Village). His task list heavily favors burstable/barrage tasks (dust devils, nechryaels, smoke devils) and high-HP melee tasks (gargoyles, abyssal demons, dark beasts).

When training Slayer, always use the expeditious bracelet on bad tasks and the bracelet of slaughter on good ones. Expeditious makes a task count for half the kills (30 kills instead of 60, less time wasted). Slaughter makes a task last 25% longer (75 kills instead of 60 on good tasks like nechryaels). These bracelets radically increase your average XP/hour over time.

The Verdict: NMZ for Speed, Slayer for Value

If you want nothing but raw speed to 99 in Attack, Strength, and Defence, NMZ with Dharok's at 1 HP is the fastest method in the game, period. You'll max all three melee 99s in roughly 200-250 hours of NMZ. If you care about your account's overall progression, train through Slayer instead — it takes about 50% longer but you'll end up with 99 Slayer, a fat cash stack, and all the PVM unlocks along the way. Either path works. The melee cape is yours for the taking.
