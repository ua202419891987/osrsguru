OSRS 1-99 Woodcutting Guide Early Game — Every Chop from Bronze to Redwood

Woodcutting is one of OSRS's most iconic skills and for good reason — it's simple, profitable, and highly AFK. If you're searching for an OSRS 1-99 woodcutting guide early game that covers everything from your first bronze axe swing to redwood trees, this is it. In 2026, the Woodcutting meta has evolved significantly thanks to the Forestry update, which added events, two-handed axes, and new training dynamics that fundamentally changed how you train. I've ground through the skill on multiple accounts and here's the most efficient, least painful path.

Forestry 2026: Why It Changed Everything

If you've been away from OSRS for a while, Forestry is the biggest Woodcutting update in the game's history. It introduced Forestry events that spawn randomly when multiple players are cutting the same tree type in the same area. These events give bonus XP, anima bark (used to buy Forestry items), and break up the monotony. The key items from the Forestry shop:

  • Log basket (5,000 anima bark) — Holds 28 extra logs, doubling inventory capacity
  • Lumberjack outfit — 2.5% XP boost (obtained from Forestry events or Temple Trekking)
  • Forester's ration — Auto-uses food when low HP (useful for ent encounters)
  • Two-handed axes — 10% faster chopping but no off-hand slot

Level 1–15: Normal Trees (Lumbridge)

Start with a bronze axe (buy from Bob's Axes in Lumbridge for 16 GP if you don't have one from Tutorial Island). Chop normal trees around Lumbridge castle. At level 6, upgrade to a steel axe. At level 11, upgrade to a black axe. Each log gives 25 XP. This bracket takes about 15 minutes. Keep the logs — they sell for about 45 GP each in 2026 and add up to decent starter cash.

Don't burn your normal logs for Firemaking. It might seem efficient to train two skills at once, but normal logs give terrible Firemaking XP. Sell the logs to the GE and buy oak/willow logs for Firemaking later — you'll get vastly more Firemaking XP per GP spent.

Level 15–30: Oak Trees (Draynor / Varrock West)

Oak trees give 37.5 XP per log and are plentiful just west of Draynor Village and west of Varrock (near the bank). At level 21, upgrade to a mithril axe. Chop oaks until level 30 — this will take about 40 minutes. Oak logs sell for around 60 GP each in 2026, so you'll pocket about 15K GP from this bracket.

Level 30–45: Willow Trees (Draynor / Port Sarim)

Willows unlock at level 30 and mark your first taste of fast Woodcutting. Each log gives 67.5 XP, and willow trees have a very fast respawn time. The best early-game spot is the willow patch just southeast of Draynor Village bank — the trees are 5 steps from the bank, making this one of the simplest banking loops in the game.

From 30 to 45, chop willows. At level 31, upgrade to an adamant axe. You'll average 35K-45K XP/hour at these levels. This bracket takes about 90 minutes. Willow logs are cheap (12 GP each), so don't bother banking — just drop them.

Level 45–60: Maple Trees (Seers' Village / Corsair Cove)

At level 45, maple trees open up. Maples give 100 XP per log (110 with the Kandarin medium diary perk in Seers' Village). The Seers' Village maple patch (north of the bank) is your home for this bracket. If you're a new account without Kandarin diary, the Corsair Cove maple patch (south of the bank, unlocked via Dragon Slayer I) is an alternative.

At 41 Woodcutting, upgrade to a rune axe. At 61 with 60 Attack (or buy a dragon axe on the GE for ~50K GP in 2026), switch to a dragon axe for the special attack that gives +3 Woodcutting boost.

XP rate: 40K-55K/hour. Duration from 45 to 60: about 3 hours. Maple logs sell for 8 GP each — drop them unless you're starved for GP.

Level 60–75: Yew Trees (Edgeville / Woodcutting Guild Entrance)

Yews at level 60 give 175 XP per log. The classic spot is behind Edgeville bank (east side), where two yew trees sit within 10 steps of the bank. At level 60, you'll be cutting yews alongside dozens of other players — the trees despawn and respawn on a timer, so the XP rate depends heavily on world population. In a quiet world with 2-3 other cutters, expect 30K-35K XP/hour. In a packed world with 10+ people, it drops to 15K-20K.

Yew logs sell for about 250 GP each in 2026. From 60 to 75, you'll cut roughly 7,500 yew logs, banking about 1.9M GP. Not bad for a passive grind. Grab the log basket from Forestry before this bracket — it doubles your trips between banks.

At level 60, immediately head to the Woodcutting Guild in Hosidius (requires 75% Hosidius favor, easily gained by pushing the plough for 15 minutes). The invisible +7 Woodcutting boost inside the guild is the single biggest XP accelerator in the skill. You'll cut yews and magics here significantly faster than anywhere else.

Level 75–90: Magic Trees (Woodcutting Guild)

At 75, magic trees unlock. Each log gives 250 XP and sells for about 950 GP in 2026. The Woodcutting Guild has the best magic tree cluster in the game (6 trees in the northwest corner). With the invisible +7 boost and a dragon axe, you'll get about 30K-35K XP/hour here, which is slow but highly AFK — magics take 30-90 seconds per log, giving you plenty of time to look away.

From 75 to 90, you'll cut about 22,000 magic logs with the +7 guild boost. That's roughly 20M GP in profit at 2026 prices. Magic logs pay for a bond twice over. Duration: about 120 hours (that's the Woodcutting reality — the mid-game is slow).

Level 90–99: Redwood Trees (Woodcutting Guild)

At 90, you've reached the promised land: redwood trees. Each log gives 380 XP. The redwood patch is in the northeast section of the Woodcutting Guild and features a single massive tree that never despawns. You can stand in one spot and click the same tree for hours. This is the most AFK activity in the game, period — click once, wait 2-4 minutes for a full inventory (with log basket, 4-8 minutes), bank at the deposit box 5 steps away, repeat.

XP rate: 65K-75K/hour. Redwood logs sell for about 500 GP each in 2026. From 90 to 99 (roughly 7.7M XP remaining), you'll spend about 110-120 hours at redwoods and earn roughly 10M GP in profit.

Forestry Events and the Log Basket

Since the Forestry update, every tree spot with multiple players can spawn random events that give bonus XP and anima bark. The log basket (5,000 bark) should be your first Forestry purchase — it effectively doubles your inventory, cuts banking trips in half, and is the single best quality-of-life upgrade for the entire skill. Buy it before you start yews at 60, and definitely before magics.

Axe Upgrade Path

  • Bronze (1) → Iron (1, optional) → Steel (6) → Black (11) → Mithril (21) → Adamant (31) → Rune (41) → Dragon (61 WC + 60 Attack, or buyable)

At 90 Woodcutting with 85 Firemaking (Wintertodt requirement), you can earn the crystal axe from the Elf quest series. The crystal axe is about 15% faster than the dragon axe, making it the BIS for the 90-99 redwood grind. If you're going all the way to 99, the crystal axe is absolutely worth the questing.

The Woodcutting Roadmap Summarized

Woodcutting in 2026 is a marathon, not a sprint. The path is linear: normal → oak → willow → maple → yew → magic → redwood. The Woodcutting Guild is your mid-game home from 60 onward, and redwoods at 90 are the AFK payoff you've been grinding toward. Use the log basket, upgrade your axe on every milestone, and don't sleep on Forestry events for bonus XP. Total time to 99: about 250-300 hours. Total profit: roughly 35M GP. Is it the fastest 99? No. Is it one of the most relaxing? Absolutely.
