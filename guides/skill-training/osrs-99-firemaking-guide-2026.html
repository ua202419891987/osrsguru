OSRS Firemaking Guide 1-99 2026 — Wintertodt & Fast Methods

Firemaking used to be a meme skill — hours of clicking "use tinderbox on log" on endless rows of fires, producing nothing of value. Then Wintertodt arrived. Today, Firemaking is one of the most rewarding skills for early-game accounts, pumping out valuable supplies, GP, and a highly sought-after pet. This OSRS Firemaking guide 1-99 2026 covers everything from the first log you light to the final Wintertodt crate.

Level 1-50: Traditional Log Burning

You need 50 Firemaking to enter Wintertodt, so the first leg of the journey is old-school log burning. Buy logs from the Grand Exchange, find a clear area (Grand Exchange northwest corner is popular), and start lighting fires in a line. Each logged fire takes up one tile and burns out after about 60 seconds, so you can walk in a straight line indefinitely.

The progression:

  • Level 1-15: Normal logs (40 XP each, ~30K-35K XP/hour). Need about 60 logs — 5 minutes.
  • Level 15-30: Oak logs (60 XP each, ~45K-50K XP/hour). Need about 250 logs — 10-15 minutes.
  • Level 30-45: Willow logs (90 XP each, ~65K-70K XP/hour). Need about 580 logs — 15-20 minutes.
  • Level 45-50: Maple logs (135 XP each, ~80K-90K XP/hour). Need about 350 logs — 10 minutes. Alternatively, stick with willows — cheaper and only marginally slower.

Total time from 1-50: about 45 minutes to 1 hour. Total cost: 20K-40K GP (willows are 30-40 GP each, maples are 10-15 GP). It's fast, cheap, and the only pre-Wintertodt grind in Firemaking.

Pro Tip: Burn logs at the Grand Exchange. The northwest area is wide and flat, and you can buy more logs instantly when you run out. Alternatively, burn at the Duel Arena (now the Emir's Arena) or the Rogue's Den — other popular flat spaces with banks nearby. Each fire occupies one tile, and a north-south line of 26 fires covers a full inventory.

Level 50-99: Wintertodt

Wintertodt is a firemaking minigame/boss located in the Northern Tundras of Great Kourend, accessible by the Games Necklace teleport (Wintertodt Camp). At its core, it's a team activity where players work together to keep braziers lit, channel the Wintertodt's energy, and accumulate points for reward crates. The Firemaking XP is excellent, but the real appeal is the reward crates — each one contains resources that fund your entire account.

How Wintertodt Works

Inside the Wintertodt chamber (cold, requires warm clothing), four braziers stand in a diamond pattern. Players chop bruma roots from the bruma tree in the center, fletch them into kindling (optional, gives Fletching XP), and feed the kindling or roots to lit braziers. Each brazier gains energy and contributes to the Wintertodt's energy bar. When the bar hits 100%, the boss is defeated and everyone earns points based on their contribution (500 points minimum for a reward crate, 13,500 points for the maximum reward crate at 99 Firemaking).

Wintertodt periodically attacks using:

  • Cold aura: Passive damage ticks every few seconds (~10% of your HP). This is the main damage source.
  • Brazier explosions: When a brazier is extinguished, nearby players take heavy damage. Step away when you see the brazier sparking.
  • Snowfall: Delayed AoE attack — move when snow starts falling on your tile.
  • Brazier shattering: The Wintertodt can break a lit brazier, dealing damage and requiring it to be relit.

Wintertodt Damage Mechanics

Critically, Wintertodt damage scales with your Hitpoints level. The formula: standard damage = (Hitpoints level + 1) × 0.5, with an additional random component. This means:

  • At 10 HP: you take ~2-3 damage per hit. Food cost: minimal (cakes, ~100 GP each).
  • At 50 HP: you take ~5-8 damage per hit. Food cost: moderate (lobsters, monkfish).
  • At 99 HP: you take ~12-16 damage per hit. Food cost: significant (sharks, brews).

This scaling mechanic is why ironmen universally rush 50 Firemaking at 10 HP — you can sustain with a few cakes or wines for hours, saving massive food costs. Main accounts can simply buy sharks and ignore the HP issue entirely.

Solo vs. Mass Wintertodt

There are two fundamentally different Wintertodt strategies:

Mass Wintertodt (Wintertodt World — World 309)

Join the official Wintertodt world and play with 20-50 other players. The boss dies in 2-3 minutes per round. You earn 2-3 reward crates per game. XP rate: 300K-350K Firemaking XP/hour. This is the fastest Firemaking XP but gives fewer reward crates per hour. Damage is distributed across many players, so you take less damage overall. Ideal for main accounts who just want 99 as fast as possible.

Solo Wintertodt

Enter Wintertodt alone (use a non-Wintertodt world). The strategy shifts: your goal is to keep the Wintertodt between 5-10% energy, feeding it slowly while you maximize points through fletching all bruma roots into kindling. Each solo round takes 40-60 minutes and rewards 28-30 crates per game (maximum rolls). XP rate: 250K-280K Firemaking XP/hour + 20K-30K Fletching XP/hour + 30K-40K Woodcutting XP/hour. This is the most rewarding method — more crates, more loot, more total XP — and the best option for ironmen.

The solo strategy: enter, rush to light all 4 braziers, then chop bruma roots, fletch them all into kindling (gives more points than raw roots), and feed braziers one at a time. Let the Wintertodt energy hover at 10-15%. When it drops below 5%, feed a few kindling. The idea is to prolong the round for maximum points without letting the Wintertodt die too quickly or reach 0.

Pro Tip: In solo Wintertodt, always fletch bruma roots into kindling. Fletched kindling gives more points per log fed to braziers (higher contribution = more crates). You'll earn substantial Fletching and Woodcutting XP simultaneously. A single solo game to 99 can net you thousands of bruma roots converted to kindling, adding millions of bonus Fletching XP.

Warning: Wintertodt crates scale with your non-Firemaking skill levels. If you do 50-99 Wintertodt at 10 HP with level 1 in everything but Firemaking, your crates will be filled with low-level junk — iron ore instead of runite, guam instead of snapdragon, oak logs instead of magic logs. For mains, this doesn't matter (you buy supplies). For ironmen, train your base stats before committing to 99 — at minimum 30+ in Mining, Woodcutting, Farming, Fishing, and Herblore. The difference in crate value is enormous.

Ironman Wintertodt: THE Early Game Meta

For ironmen, Wintertodt isn't just Firemaking — it's the economic foundation for the entire early game. Here's why every ironman guide puts Wintertodt first:

  • Seed supply: Crates dump fruit tree seeds, herb seeds, and hop seeds into your bank. These fund 50+ Farming without needing to kill monsters.
  • Herb supply: Grimy herbs from crates combined with seeds let you train Herblore, the hardest ironman skill.
  • GP: Coins and alchable drops from crates provide 500K-1M+ GP by 99 — enough for early construction, runes, and shop purchases.
  • Ore/bars: Coal, iron, gold, and mithril for smithing and crafting. Combined with Motherlode Mine later, your smithing is set.
  • Raw food: Raw fish (lobsters, swordfish) supplement early cooking and food supplies.
  • Logs: Oak, willow, maple, yew — for fletching, construction, and birdhouse runs.
  • Tome of fire: If you get lucky (1/1,000), infinite fire runes change the entire magic training meta. You can alch, superheat, and teleport without ever crafting a fire rune.

The optimal ironman Wintertodt strategy: get 10 HP, complete the quests for base levels (Druidic Ritual for Herblore, Rune Mysteries for Runecrafting, etc.), get 30+ in the key skills, grab the Clue Hunter outfit, buy 200 cakes from Ardougne, and solo Wintertodt from 50 to 99. You'll exit with a 99, millions of GP in resources, and a bank that makes every other grind easier.

Post-99 Wintertodt

Even after 99 Firemaking, Wintertodt remains relevant. Many players continue for:

  • Pet hunt: The phoenix is a prestige pet and takes ~500 hours on drop rate at 1/5,000.
  • Tome of fire: Irons who missed it during the 50-99 grind often return to chase it.
  • Pyromancer outfit: If RNG was cruel, you might reach 99 without the full set.
  • 200M XP: Firemaking is one of the fastest 200M skills in the game. Wintertodt mass worlds make it a consistent ~350K XP/hour all the way to 200M — about 500 more hours from 99.
