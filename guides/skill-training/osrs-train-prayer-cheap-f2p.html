OSRS How to Train Prayer Cheap F2P — Budget Prayer Training That Doesn't Drain Your Bank

Prayer is the one skill that everyone hates paying for. In members, you have the Chaos Altar, gilded altars, and the Ectofuntus — all expensive but efficient. In F2P, your options are limited, and the standard method (buying big bones and burying them) costs a fortune relative to how much GP a F2P account can actually make. This OSRS how to train prayer cheap f2p guide focuses entirely on minimizing cost while still reaching meaningful Prayer levels — because in free-to-play, you don't need 99 Prayer. You need 43 for protection prayers, maybe 45 for Mystic Might, and beyond that it's purely cosmetic.

F2P Prayer Reality Check

Before diving into methods, let's be honest about what's achievable. Training Prayer past level 70 in F2P is painfully expensive — we're talking north of 30M GP from 70 to 99 using the cheapest method available. The average F2P moneymaker maxes out at 300K-400K GP/hour (high-alching rune items, making sapphire rings, etc.), which means funding 99 Prayer in F2P requires roughly 100+ hours of pure moneymaking before you even start training. Most F2P players realistically stop at 43-50 Prayer and move on.

This guide covers the cheapest path to each milestone — 43, 55, 70, and 99 — so you can pick your stopping point and save your GP for members' bonds instead.

Method 1: Buy and Bury Big Bones (Moderate Speed, Moderate Cost)

This is the standard F2P prayer method. Buy big bones at the Grand Exchange for 230 GP each. Each gives 15 XP. Burying a full inventory of 28 takes about 15 seconds. The best spot is the GE itself — stand at the GE booth, buy 28, bury all 28 by spam-clicking, repeat. You can also do this at any bank with a nearby empty ground tile.

XP rate: 35K-45K XP/hour (varies based on click speed and GE buy limit — big bones have a 12,000 buy limit per 4 hours, which is more than enough for manual burying). Cost to 43 Prayer: about 440K GP. Cost to 70 Prayer: about 11.4M GP. Cost to 99: roughly 199M GP.

Method 2: Hill Giants (Self-Sufficient, Zero Cost)

If spending GP makes you physically ill, kill hill giants for their big bone drops. Hill giants in the Edgeville Dungeon (level 28, requires brass key from the small house west of the GE to access the shortcut) have 35 HP and drop big bones 100% of the time. Each kill gives about 35-40 combat XP and a big bone worth 15 Prayer XP when buried.

With melee stats of 40+, you can kill roughly 200 hill giants per hour, yielding 3,000 Prayer XP from buried bones plus about 7,000 melee XP. It's a zero-GP method but incredibly slow — about 3K Prayer XP/hour. To get to 43 Prayer (50K XP), that's about 17 hours of hill giant grinding. To 70 Prayer (737K XP), you're looking at 245 hours. This is strictly a "I refuse to spend any GP" option.

Hill giants in the Edgeville Dungeon also drop limpwurt roots (worth ~4K GP each) and giant keys (used to fight Obor, the hill giant boss). Obor drops a rune kiteshield, rune full helm, and various alchables — about 40K GP per kill on average. If you're already grinding hill giants for big bones, save the keys and kill Obor with melee and protection prayers. It partially funds your Prayer grind.

Method 3: Boneyard (Wilderness, Free Bones)

The Forgotten Cemetery (commonly called the boneyard) in level 29-31 Wilderness northwest of Edgeville has big bone spawns on the ground that respawn every 10-15 seconds. You can pick up and bury big bones here for completely free. At about 10-12 bones per minute (picking + burying), that's roughly 9K-11K Prayer XP/hour with zero cost and zero risk (just don't bring anything into the Wilderness).

To 43 Prayer: about 5 hours of boneyard grinding. To 70 Prayer: about 70 hours. The boneyard is the best free method in F2P and decently faster than hill giants, but it's incredibly tedious. Put on a podcast and commit to the grind.

Method 4: F2P Bossing — Obor and Bryophyta

Obor (hill giant boss, Edgeville Dungeon) and Bryophyta (moss giant boss, Varrock Sewers) both drop big bones, dragon bones, and ensouled heads (members only for ensouled heads, but the normal bone drops work in F2P). Farming these bosses with mid-70s combat stats and protection prayers is a more engaging way to accumulate bones passively:

  • Obor (giant key drop from hill giants): Drops 1 dragon bone (72 XP) guaranteed per kill. The rune items from Obor (kiteshield, full helm) cover the cost of the giant keys, making the dragon bone essentially free.
  • Bryophyta (mossy key from moss giants): Drops 5 dragon bones (360 XP) and a 1/118 chance at Bryophyta's essence (worth about 6M GP in 2026). Farming moss giants for keys is a viable GP method in itself, and the prayer XP from Bryophyta kills is a nice bonus.

The Bottom Line: Go Members for Serious Prayer Training

If you're reading this as a pure F2P player, here's my honest advice: get 43 Prayer for protection prayers, then stop. Buy big bones at the GE, spend your 440K GP, and get Protect from Melee/Magic/Ranged in under 2 hours. Anything beyond 43 Prayer in F2P is exponentially more expensive for diminishing returns. Use the GP you save to buy a bond, go members, and use the Chaos Altar in level 38 Wilderness where you get 7x the XP per bone (big bones give 105 XP instead of 15, dragon bones give 504 XP instead of 72). Training Prayer on a budget in F2P is like swimming with ankle weights — you can do it, but there's a much better way.
