OSRS 1-99 Hunter Guide AFK Method — Train Hunter Without the Carpal Tunnel

Hunter has a reputation as a high-attention, click-intensive skill — and for many of the traditional training methods like salamanders and chinchompas, that reputation is earned. But in 2026, there are several genuinely AFK-friendly Hunter methods that let you train the skill passively while doing other things. This OSRS 1-99 hunter guide afk method focuses exclusively on the low-click, low-attention path. If you want speed (3-tick chinchompas at 150K+ XP/hour), there are other guides for that. This is for the player who wants to click once every 50 minutes and let Hunter train itself.

Bird House Runs: The Backbone of AFK Hunter

Bird house runs are not technically AFK in the traditional sense — they're a recurring activity that takes 90 seconds every 50 minutes. But from a total-time-spent perspective, they are the most efficient AFK Hunter method in the game. Here's how they work:

  • Build bird houses on Fossil Island (requires Bone Voyage quest and Digsite Quest)
  • Each bird house takes 4 logs (any type, use cheap maple logs for 5 GP each) and 40 hop seeds (bought from Olivia in Draynor for 4 GP each, or stockpile from Managing Miscellania)
  • Place 4 bird houses around Fossil Island. They passively trap birds for exactly 50 minutes.
  • Return after 50 minutes, dismantle each house for Hunter XP + bird nests, rebuild, repeat.

Each bird nest contains a bird nest ring (2K-5K GP), seeds (random, including valuable spirit tree and magic seeds), or a piece of the evil chicken outfit. In 2026, crushed bird nests (from crushing nests with a pestle and mortar) sell for about 3,500 GP each, making bird house runs profitable on top of the XP.

Set a 50-minute timer on your phone or use the Runelite "Bird House Timer" plugin. Do a bird house run between every Slayer task, every farming run, or every episode of whatever you're watching. Over the course of a month, 3-4 runs per day at redwood tier add up to roughly 600K-800K Hunter XP — completely passive. This is how I got 99 Hunter on my ironman without ever feeling like I was "training" the skill.

Level 1–33: Questing + Bird Houses (Skip the Early Grind)

The early Hunter levels are the worst part of the skill — you're stuck with crimson swifts and copper longtails that require constant clicking. Here's how to skip nearly all of it:

  • The Ascent of Arceuus — 500 XP (no Hunter requirement)
  • Client of Kourend — 500 XP in any skill (put it in Hunter)
  • Natural History Quiz (Varrock Museum basement) — 1,000 XP in Hunter at a specific fossil display case
  • Eagles' Peak — 2,500 XP (requires 27 Hunter)

Combined, these quests give about 4,500 Hunter XP, taking you from level 1 to roughly level 19 without catching a single creature. From 19 to 33, do regular/oak bird house runs (4 runs over 3-4 hours while questing elsewhere) and you'll hit 33 without the click-intensive low-level grind.

Level 33–60: Willow to Yew Bird Houses (Pure Passive)

From 33 to 60, continue with bird house runs using the best tier available. At level 44 you unlock maple houses, at 49 mahogany, at 59 yew. Each run takes 90 seconds and gives progressively better XP. Doing just 3-4 runs per day, you'll hit 60 Hunter in about 2-3 weeks of completely passive training. That's maybe 3 minutes of active play per day.

If you want to accelerate this bracket without giving up the AFK lifestyle, mix in maniacal monkeys at 60 (see below), but bird houses alone will get you to 60 eventually.

Level 60–80: Maniacal Monkeys (The AFK Sweet Spot)

Maniacal monkeys on Kruk's Dungeon (Ape Atoll, requires Monkey Madness II quest completion) are the most AFK active Hunter training method in the game. Here's why:

  • The monkeys are aggressive and infinitely respawn — you never need to re-bait traps or move
  • You place a deadfall trap with a banana (bought from the food shop on Ape Atoll for 2 GP each). The trap catches a monkey, you click to reset it, repeat.
  • Each catch gives 1,000 Hunter XP at a base rate of about 60K-80K XP/hour when paying moderate attention
  • When played semi-AFK (clicking every 5-10 seconds), you still get 50K-60K XP/hour

The catch: Monkey Madness II has high requirements (69 Slayer, 70 Crafting, 55 Thieving, 60 Agility, 50 Construction, and several difficult boss fights). This isn't an early-game method. But if you already have MM2 done for other reasons, monkeys are your best AFK option from 60 to 99.

Level 80–99: Herbiboar (Engaging but Low-Intensity)

Herbiboar on Fossil Island (requires 80 Hunter and 31 Herblore) is technically not AFK, but it's low-attention in a different way. You track footprints through a fixed set of paths on Fossil Island — it's like a simple, repetitive puzzle that becomes muscle memory after the first hour. Each successful track and catch gives 1,950 XP (at level 80, scaling to 2,750 XP at 99) plus herbs as loot (grimy guam through torstol, weighted by Herblore level).

XP rate: 140K-180K/hour. Profit: 300K-500K GP/hour in herbs in 2026. Herbiboar is the sweet spot between AFK and efficient — you're engaged enough not to be bored but it's repetitive enough to do on a second monitor. Combined with bird house runs between herbiboar sessions, this is how most maxed accounts train 80-99 Hunter.

Level 85–99: Butterfly Catching in Varlamore (New 2026 Method)

With the Varlamore expansion, butterfly catching at the Hunter Guild area in Aldarin became a viable high-level method. At 85+ with a butterfly net and jar, catching moonlight butterflies gives 800 XP per catch and the spots respawn instantly. XP rate: 80K-100K/hour. It's not the fastest, but it's click-once-per-catch AFK since the butterflies move slowly and respawn near the same tile.

The AFK Hunter Roadmap to 99

If you follow the pure AFK path, here's the timeline: quest to 19 Hunter (30 minutes of questing), do bird house runs 2-4 times daily for roughly 2-3 months to hit 60 (5 minutes of active play per day). At 60, grind maniacal monkeys for a few weeks if you have MM2 done, or continue bird houses passively until 99 (which takes about 6-8 months at 3-4 runs per day). It's not fast, but Hunter is one of the few skills where you genuinely can reach 99 by spending 5 minutes a day over half a year. The cape is red, the grind is patient, and your wrist will thank you.
