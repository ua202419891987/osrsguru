OSRS How to Get 99 Agility Fast 2026 — The Agility Guide That Won't Make You Quit

Agility is the most complained-about skill in OSRS. It's repetitive, the XP rates are glacial compared to combat, and the entire grind takes 250+ hours to reach 99. But Agility is also one of the most impactful skills in the game — faster run energy restoration, better shortcuts, access to the Hallowed Sepulchre, and the graceful outfit (the single best QoL upgrade in OSRS). This OSRS how to get 99 agility fast 2026 guide covers every course, every XP rate, and every efficiency trick to make the grind as quick as humanly possible.

The Fastest vs. The Optimal Path

Before we break down the courses, an important distinction: the fastest raw XP route is different from the optimal account-building route. The fastest XP route prioritizes Hallowed Sepulchre at 72+ Agility. The optimal route mixes rooftop courses for marks of grace (graceful outfit + amylase crystals) with Sepulchre for XP. This guide covers both, and you can decide how much you value marks of grace versus raw speed.

Level 1–10: Gnome Stronghold Course

Start at the Gnome Stronghold Agility Course (northwest of the Grand Tree). This is the only option from level 1 and it's perfectly fine for what it is. The course takes about 40 seconds per lap and gives 86.5 XP per completion. After roughly 13 laps, you'll hit level 10. Total time: about 10 minutes.

At level 10, you unlock the Draynor Village Rooftop Course — your first rooftop, and the start of the graceful outfit grind.

Complete the Tourist Trap quest early (requires 10 Fletching and 20 Smithing). It lets you choose two 4,650 XP lamps in Agility, which jumps you from level 1 to 26 instantly. Along with the Grand Tree quest (7,900 Agility XP) and Recruitment Drive (1,000 XP), you can quest your way past the worst agility courses without ever touching the Gnome Stronghold for more than 5 laps.

Level 10–20: Draynor Village Rooftop Course

The Draynor rooftop course is a simple 7-obstacle loop around the Draynor Village rooftops. XP per lap: 120. The marks of grace rate is low, but everything counts toward graceful. Stay here until level 20 — roughly 35-40 laps, about 30 minutes.

Level 20–30: Al Kharid Rooftop Course

The Al Kharid course gives 180 XP per lap and has a better mark of grace drop rate than Draynor. It's located on the rooftops of Al Kharid, starting near the gem stall by the south side of the palace. Use the ring of dueling to Castle Wars for banking between laps (optional, but the agi pyramid is nearby if you want to mix in GP at level 30).

Level 30–40: Varrock Rooftop Course

Varrock rooftop (start near the east bank, climbing the rough wall) gives 238 XP per lap — the first significant XP upgrade. The course is smooth with no frustrating obstacles. Stay here until 40. Marks of grace drop about 7-8 per hour at this bracket.

Level 40–50: Canifis Rooftop Course (Agility Power Spike)

At level 40, unlock Canifis (requires Priest in Peril quest to enter Morytania). The Canifis rooftop course gives 240 XP per lap — nearly identical to Varrock — but has the highest mark of grace rate of any course in the game. You'll average 12-15 marks of grace per hour here, meaning you can earn the full graceful outfit (260 marks for the complete set) in about 18-20 hours.

If you don't have graceful yet, stay at Canifis until level 60 — even though the XP rate is lower than Falador at 50. The mark of grace rate is worth the delay. Graceful is a permanent account upgrade that affects literally everything you do, and farming it at Canifis is optimal.

Level 50–60: Falador Rooftop Course

If you already have graceful from Canifis, move to Falador at 50. Falador gives 440 XP per lap (nearly double Canifis) but has a lower mark rate. The course is straightforward, with the final jump landing you right back at the start. Use a Falador teleport tab or the Falador teleport spell for quick access.

Level 60–70: Seers' Village Rooftop Course

At 60, Seers' Village becomes available — and with the Kandarin hard diary complete, this course transforms. The diary reward gives a 15% increased teleport chance when using the Camelot teleport to return to the start, making Seers' the best XP from 60 to 70 (and arguably to 80). XP rate without diary: 48K/hour. With Kandarin hard: 56K-60K/hour. The diary requirements are modest (70 Fishing, 70 Cooking, 50 Firemaking, 53 Thieving, and a few quests) and absolutely worth completing.

Level 70–80: Pollnivneach Rooftop Course

At 70, Pollnivneach (desert, requires The Feud quest) gives 540 XP per lap at about 52K-55K XP/hour. It's a slight downgrade from diary-boosted Seers' at 70-72, then overtakes it as you level. The course is annoying (lots of back-and-forth obstacles and a camel), but the XP keeps climbing.

Level 72–92: Hallowed Sepulchre (Fastest Agility XP in the Game)

The Hallowed Sepulchre (Darkmeyer, unlocked after Sins of the Father quest) is the best Agility XP in OSRS and one of the most engaging skilling activities ever designed. It's a maze-like dungeon with five floors, each containing traps, puzzles, and a chest at the end. Unlike rooftop courses, Sepulchre requires attention, timing, and skill — but rewards you with:

  • 82K-95K Agility XP/hour at 82+ (floors 1-4)
  • Floor 5 (92+ Agility): 95K-105K+ XP/hour — the highest in the game by a wide margin
  • Hallowed marks — used to buy the dark graceful recolor, the ring of endurance (stores 1,000 stamina doses, BIS for run energy), and hallowed tools
  • Loot chests with runes, alchables, and the chance at the ring of endurance (1/200 from the grand coffin on floor 5, worth ~40M GP in 2026)

The Sepulchre is not beginner-friendly. The floor 3 final obstacle (flame statues) and floor 4 traps punish mistakes harshly. Expect to fail repeatedly while learning. But once mastered, it's genuinely fun — a rhythm game disguised as an agility course. I've done 72-99 at Sepulchre on two accounts and would never go back to rooftops for high-level Agility.

Prifddinas Course (75+, Song of the Elves)

The Prifddinas agility course at 75+ gives 60K-70K XP/hour and is notably less click-intensive than Sepulchre. It's a good alternative if you tried Sepulchre and hated the difficulty. The course also gives crystal shards (used for divine potions and armor charges), which have real value. If you have SOTE done and want a middle ground between painful Sepulchre and slow rooftops, Prif is the answer.

The Fastest 99 Agility Roadmap

The absolute fastest path to 99 Agility in 2026: quest to 26+ (Tourist Trap, Grand Tree), do rooftops from 10-60 (picking up graceful at Canifis 40-50), grind Seers' with Kandarin hard diary from 60-72 (the diary is non-negotiable for speed), then commit to Hallowed Sepulchre from 72 to 99. Sepulchre floors 1-4 from 72-92, then floor 5 from 92-99. Total time: roughly 220-250 hours from level 1 — about 20% faster than the pure rooftop path. You'll earn 150M+ GP from the ring of endurance and loot chests along the way. The Agility cape is a grind, but the Sepulchre turns it into a skill challenge rather than a click-fest. Good luck.
