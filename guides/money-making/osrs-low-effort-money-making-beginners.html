OSRS Low Effort Money Making for Beginners – 2026 Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Beginner Guides

Not everyone wants to sweat through intensive grinding sessions in Old School RuneScape. If you're looking for OSRS low effort money making for beginners in 2026, you're in the right place. Whether you're watching a show, studying, or just want something chill to do while you earn gold, these methods are designed to maximize profit with minimal input. I've personally tested every method listed here, and I'm going to give you the honest truth about which ones are actually worth your time.

What Makes a Method "Low Effort"?

Before we dive in, let me define what "low effort" actually means in the context of OSRS money making. A low-effort method typically requires:

  • Minimal clicks per minute — Ideally fewer than 5-10 actions per minute
  • Low attention requirement — You can look away for 30+ seconds without dying or losing progress
  • Forgiving gameplay — Mistakes don't cost you significant time or money
  • No complex gear setups — You don't need best-in-slot equipment to be effective
  • Reasonable profit — At least 50K-100K per hour for the time invested

Keep in mind that "low effort" usually means lower profit per hour compared to intensive methods. That's the trade-off. But if you're playing casually, time spent comfortably is often more valuable than raw GP/hr.

1. Wintertodt (Firemaking 50+)

Wintertodt is arguably the most popular low-effort money-making activity in all of OSRS, and for good reason. You only need 50 Firemaking to participate (though 60+ is recommended for better rewards), and the entire activity is semi-AFK. You chop a braziers' branches, fletch them into kindling, and occasionally fix the brazier or warm up at the fire. The loot includes seeds, logs, phoenix gear, and the coveted Tome of Fire.

Profit per hour in 2026: Approximately 200-400K gold per hour, depending on your Firemaking level and how many points you've accumulated (higher point brackets give better supply crates). The value comes primarily from rare seeds like magic and yew seeds that can be worth 50K-300K each.

What makes it low effort: You click maybe once every 10-15 seconds. The activity itself handles timing — you just need to be at your keyboard enough to respond to the Wintertodt's attacks. Many players use this as their primary "watch Netflix and make money" method.

Beginner tips: Bring a hammer (to repair the brazier), a knife (to fletch logs), and warm clothing (the same gear you'd use in the Desert). The warm clothing requirement has been slightly relaxed in 2026's quality-of-life updates, so you don't need a full set — just enough to not freeze instantly. At level 50 Firemaking, expect to succeed on roughly 30% of your subduing attempts, but you'll still earn points and rewards even from failures.

2. fishing for Profit: Monkfish or Sharks

Fishing is the quintessential low-effort skill, and it doubles as a money-making method when you sell your catches. In 2026, the best low-effort fishing money makers depend on your level:

  • Monkfish (level 62 Fishing): Caught at the Piscatoris Fishing Colony. Each monkfish sells for around 300-400 GP on the Grand Exchange, and you can catch roughly 130-150 per hour. That's about 40-55K gold per hour from raw monkfish sales alone.
  • Sharks (level 76 Fishing): Caught at various locations including the Fishing Guild and Catherby. At 110-130 sharks per hour, you're looking at approximately 80-110K gold per hour from raw shark sales.
  • Anglerfish (level 82 Fishing): Caught at the Fishing Guild with bait. These sell for 300-500 GP each and you can catch around 100-120 per hour, yielding roughly 35-55K gold per hour.

Pro Tip: Use the fishing guild if you're level 68+. The close proximity to the bank makes this incredibly efficient. For sharks, Catherby is decent but the Fishing Guild is better since you can stand right next to the bank chest.

3. Woodcutting Magic Trees

Magic trees require 75 Woodcutting, and each magic log sells for around 1,200-1,500 GP on the Grand Exchange in 2026. At higher woodcutting levels (85+), you can chop roughly 80-100 magic logs per hour, generating about 100-140K gold per hour. The effort level is very low — you click a tree, wait for it to fall, click the next one. Between each tree, you have several seconds of downtime.

The best locations for magic trees are the Woodcutting Guild (level 60+ Woodcutting required to enter, bonus: +7 invisible woodcutting level) and the Resource Area in the Wilderness (risky but faster due to fewer players). For beginners who want zero risk, the magic trees south of Seers' Village are a solid choice.

What I wish I knew: In 2026, the Woodcutting Guild has been buffed slightly — there are now more magic tree spawns within the guild, reducing wait time between trees. If you have 60 WC, this is the place to be.

4. Air Orb Charging (Magic 66+)

Charging air orbs requires 66 Magic, 60 Crafting, and completion of the "Watchtower" quest. You take unpowered orbs to the Obelisk of Air in the Wilderness (level 7 Wilderness, very safe) and cast Charge Air Orb on them. Each charged orb sells for around 1,500-2,000 GP, and you can create roughly 200-250 per hour. That translates to about 250-400K gold per hour after accounting for the cost of unpowered orbs.

Why it's low effort: The process is incredibly repetitive — charge an orb, bank it, repeat. There's no combat involved (the Wilderness risk at level 7 is minimal since you can easily escape to Edgeville), and you can get into a rhythm that requires very little thought.

2026 update: Jagex added a closer bank option near the Obelisk area, cutting banking time by about 30%. This has boosted effective profit per hour by roughly 15% compared to 2024-2025.

5. Mushroom and Herb Farm Runs

Farm runs are the definition of "set it and forget it" money making. You plant seeds, wait for them to grow, and harvest them — the entire process takes maybe 5-10 minutes of active play per run. In 2026, the most profitable low-effort farm runs for beginners include:

  • Herb runs (Farming 9+): Plant ranarr, snapdragon, or torstol seeds depending on your level. Even with some deaths, herb farming can generate 100-500K gold per run depending on the herbs you grow and their market prices.
  • Mushroom runs (Farming 31+): Morchella mushrooms have been updated in 2026 with slightly improved yield rates. At higher farming levels, each mushroom patch can yield 6-12 mushrooms worth 1,000-2,000 GP each.
  • Hespori (Farming 65+): The Hespori boss requires almost no combat — you just need a spade and some seeds. It drops a unique seed worth 20K-50K and takes about 2 minutes per kill. Do this once per day for easy money.

Pro Tip: Combine your farm runs with other daily activities like bird house runs, kingdom management (if you've done Royal Trouble), and checking your player-owned house for special events. Ten minutes of dailies can easily generate 200K+ per day.

6. Amethyst Mining (Mining 92+)

Amethyst is the highest-level rock in the mining skill, requiring 92 Mining to extract. Located in the Mining Guild resource dungeon (requiring 60 Mining and Dungeoneering), amethyst can be chipped into bolt tips which sell well. In 2026, amethyst mining generates roughly 100-150K gold per hour from selling amethyst bolt tips or javelin heads.

While 92 Mining is a high requirement, this method is about as low effort as it gets. You mine a rock, it depletes, you wait for it to respawn, mine again. With the 2026 mining guild update that added additional amethyst rock spawns, wait times are shorter than ever before.

7. Crafting Battlestaves

If you have 40+ Crafting and have completed the "Family Crest" quest (or bought the battlestaves from various shops), crafting battlestaves is a reliable low-effort money maker. Buy battlestaves from NPC shops (they restock daily), attach orbs you've charged, and sell the completed staves on the Grand Exchange.

In 2026, each battlestaff (e.g., fire battlestaff) sells for around 6,000-8,000 GP. Buying the staff for roughly 7,000 GP from Zaff's shop in Varrock (with staff discount from completing the Varrock Diary), and attaching a fire orb you've charged yourself, you can profit approximately 200-400K gold per hour depending on how many shops you visit per day.

Comparing the Methods

Here's a quick comparison of all the methods discussed, sorted by effort level and profit:

  • Easiest: Farm runs (5 min/day, 200K+) and fishing (AFK, 40-110K/hr)
  • Easy: Wintertodt (semi-AFK, 200-400K/hr) and magic tree woodcutting (100-140K/hr)
  • Moderate: Air orb charging (250-400K/hr) and battlestaff crafting (200-400K/hr)
  • Higher requirement: Amethyst mining (100-150K/hr at level 92+)

Conclusion and Recommendations

If you're a beginner looking for the absolute easiest way to make money in OSRS, start with fishing and farm runs. These require almost no upfront investment, have minimal risk, and can be done while watching something else. As you progress, move into Wintertodt for the best combination of low effort and solid profit. For players willing to invest a bit more time into quest requirements, air orb charging offers the highest profit per hour among the low-effort methods listed here.

Remember that in 2026, the OSRS economy fluctuates. Check current Grand Exchange prices before committing to any method — what was profitable last month might not be this month. The methods I've listed here are generally stable, but always verify current prices on the official GE tracker or your preferred price-checking tool.
