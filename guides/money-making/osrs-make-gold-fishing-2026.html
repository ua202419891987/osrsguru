OSRS How to Make Gold with Fishing 2026 – The Ultimate Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Fishing, Skilling

Fishing is one of the oldest and most reliable money-making skills in Old School RuneScape. If you're wondering how to make gold with fishing in OSRS in 2026, you've come to the right place. This guide covers every profitable fishing method from level 1 all the way to 99, with updated profit calculations based on current Grand Exchange prices. Whether you're a fresh account or a maxed player looking for AFK money, I'll break down exactly where to fish, what to bring, and how much you can expect to earn.

Why Fishing is Great for Money Making

Fishing has several unique advantages that make it an excellent money-making skill:

  • Zero upfront cost — Most fishing methods only require a rod and bait, which are cheap or free
  • Extremely AFK-friendly — Many fishing spots let you click once and wait 10-30 seconds
  • Multi-purpose — Fish can be sold raw, cooked for food, or used as offerings
  • Consistent income — Fish prices on the Grand Exchange are relatively stable compared to other commodities
  • No risk — Unlike Wilderness or boss methods, you won't lose your gear or loot

Level 1-20: Shrimp and Anchovies

Starting out, you'll be catching shrimp and anchovies with a small net at any coastal fishing spot. Let's be honest — the money here is terrible. Raw shrimp sell for around 10-15 GP each, and anchovies for about 20-30 GP. At maybe 200 catches per hour, you're looking at less than 5K gold per hour. Don't worry about money at this stage; focus on getting your levels up as fast as possible. Al Kharid and Draynor Village are the best spots due to close bank access.

Level 20-40: Trout and Salmon (Fly Fishing)

Fly fishing at Barbarian Village is where fishing starts to become somewhat profitable. You'll catch a mix of trout (level 20) and salmon (level 30) using a fly fishing rod and feathers. In 2026, raw trout sell for about 80-100 GP and raw salmon for about 100-130 GP on the Grand Exchange.

At level 20-30, you'll catch mostly trout, generating around 15-25K gold per hour. Once you hit level 30 and salmon enter the mix, your hourly rate jumps to approximately 25-40K gold per hour. By level 40, with a good rhythm and Feather supplies, you can hit 35-50K gold per hour.

Location tip: The Fishing Guild doesn't allow fly fishing, so Barbarian Village remains your best bet. Use the Edgeville bank shortcut through the dwarf tunnel (requires completion of the "Dig Site" quest for faster access). If you haven't done that quest, the walk from Barbarian Village to Edgeville bank is still manageable.

Level 40-50: Lobsters at Catherby

Lobster fishing at Catherby (using a lobster pot) unlocks at level 40. This is a significant milestone because lobsters are in high demand as food for mid-level bossing and Slayer tasks. In 2026, raw lobsters sell for approximately 150-200 GP each on the Grand Exchange. At roughly 100-120 lobsters per hour, you'll earn about 15-25K gold per hour.

Honest assessment: Lobsters are actually less profitable per hour than trout/salmon due to the lower catch rate relative to their sell price. The advantage of lobsters is that they're more useful to you personally — cooked lobsters heal 22 HP, making them excellent food for your own combat training. If your goal is pure gold, stick with fly fishing until level 58 for monkfish.

Level 50-58: Swordfish and Tuna at Catherby

At level 50, you can catch tuna with a harpoon, and at level 60, swordfish join the catch pool. Catherby remains the best free-to-play and member spot for harpoon fishing. In 2026, raw swordfish sell for around 350-400 GP each, and raw tuna for about 100-130 GP.

The problem is that you catch a lot of tuna mixed in with swordfish, which dilutes your profit. At level 50-60, expect roughly 25-40K gold per hour. Once you hit level 60+ and your swordfish catch rate improves, this can increase to 40-60K gold per hour. The Fishing Guild (level 68+) is significantly better for harpoon fishing due to the bank proximity.

Pro Tip: If you have the "Song of the Elves" quest completed (unlikely for beginners), the Prifddinas waterfalls offer the fastest harpoon fishing XP in the game. But for pure gold, the Fishing Guild is still your best bet because banking is faster.

Level 58-76: Monkfish at Piscatoris

Monkfish are unlocked at level 58 Fishing and are caught at the Piscatoris Fishing Colony (north of Ardougne). This requires completion of the "Swan Song" quest, which has several prerequisite quests including "One Small Favour," "Royal Trouble," and "Fishing Contest." It's a significant quest chain, but monkfish are worth the effort.

In 2026, raw monkfish sell for approximately 350-450 GP each. At a catch rate of 130-150 per hour, monkfish generate about 50-65K gold per hour. The Piscatoris colony is also relatively uncrowded compared to Catherby and the Fishing Guild, meaning less competition for fishing spots.

What I wish I knew: Monkfish heal 16 HP when cooked, which is less than lobsters (22 HP). For your own food supply, lobsters are actually better. But for selling on the Grand Exchange, monkfish are more profitable because there's constant demand from players training at the Fight Caves and other mid-level PvM activities.

Level 76-90: Sharks and Anglerfish

At level 76, sharks become available, and they remain one of the most iconic money-making fish in OSRS. In 2026, raw sharks sell for approximately 800-1,000 GP each on the Grand Exchange. At the Fishing Guild or Catherby, you can catch 100-120 sharks per hour, generating about 85-115K gold per hour.

Anglerfish, unlocked at level 82 Fishing (with 100% catch rate at level 99), are caught at the Fishing Guild using sandworms as bait. In 2026, raw anglerfish sell for approximately 350-450 GP each, and you can catch about 120-140 per hour. That's roughly 45-60K gold per hour — less than sharks per fish, but comparable overall due to the slightly faster catch rate.

Why choose anglerfish over sharks? Anglerfish have a unique property — they can heal you above your maximum hitpoints. This makes them incredibly valuable for bossing and PvP. In 2026, demand for raw anglerfish has been consistently high due to meta shifts in high-level PvM content. The price stability of anglerfish makes them a safer investment than sharks, which can fluctuate more dramatically.

Level 90-99: Dark Crabs and Sacred Eels

For high-level fishers, two methods stand out in 2026:

Dark Crabs (Fishing 85+, Wilderness)

Dark crabs are caught in the Resource Area of the Wilderness (level 35+), requiring 85 Fishing and a crab pot. Raw dark crabs sell for approximately 600-800 GP each in 2026. The catch rate is good at about 120-150 per hour, yielding 75-110K gold per hour. The major drawback is the Wilderness risk — you can be PK'd by other players. Always bring teleport tabs and only risk what you can afford to lose.

Sacred Eels (Fishing 87+, Regicide quest)

Sacred eels are caught at the Zul-Andra bank area and require 87 Fishing plus 80 Cooking (to cut them for Zulrah scales). Each sacred eel yields 2-8 Zulrah scales when cut, and Zulrah scales sell for approximately 250-350 GP each in 2026. With 100-120 eels caught per hour and an average of 4 scales per eel, you're looking at roughly 100-160K gold per hour from Zulrah scales alone, plus any onyx bolt tips you receive as bonus drops.

2026 update note: The Zulrah scale drop rate from sacred eels was slightly buffed in early 2026, making this method about 10% more profitable than it was in 2025. If you have the levels and quest requirements, sacred eels are now one of the best fishing money makers in the game.

Level 99 Methods: Minnows and Harpoonfish

Minnows (Fishing 94+)

Minnows are caught in the Fishing Guild at level 94+ and are exchanged for raw sharks at the Angler NPC (Travis). The exchange rate is roughly 40 minnows per shark. This method can yield approximately 200-300K gold per hour at high fishing levels, making it one of the most profitable fishing methods in the game.

Important warning: Minnows are consumed by flying fish that randomly appear near your character. If you don't move away, you'll lose your minnows. In 2026, Jagex updated the flying fish mechanic so that they appear slightly less frequently but are faster when they do. You need to stay alert — this is NOT an AFK method despite being fishing.

Gear and Setup Recommendations

While fishing doesn't require much gear, a few items can significantly boost your efficiency:

  • Fishing cape (level 99): Grants a +5 fishing level boost, useful for catching higher-level fish sooner
  • Angler outfit: Obtained from the Fishing Trawler minigame, provides a +2.5% fishing XP boost when wearing the full set
  • Rada's Blessing 4: Provides a 100% chance to catch double fish at the Mount Karuulm fishing spot and several other locations
  • Dragon/Infernal harpoon: Provides a 20% chance to catch two fish at once; the Infernal harpoon also grants passive Cooking XP
  • Crystal harpoon: The best-in-slot fishing tool, providing a +3 invisible Fishing level and 50% chance to catch two fish at once (requires "Song of the Elves")

Profit Summary Table

  • Shrimp/Anchovies (1-20): ~5K gp/hr
  • Trout/Salmon (20-40): 15-50K gp/hr
  • Lobsters (40-58): 15-25K gp/hr
  • Monkfish (58-76): 50-65K gp/hr
  • Sharks (76+): 85-115K gp/hr
  • Anglerfish (82+): 45-60K gp/hr
  • Sacred Eels (87+): 100-160K gp/hr
  • Dark Crabs (85+): 75-110K gp/hr (Wilderness risk)
  • Minnows (94+): 200-300K gp/hr

Conclusion

Fishing remains one of the most accessible and consistent money-making skills in OSRS in 2026. For beginners, fly fishing at Barbarian Village provides the best early-game profit, while mid-level players should aim for monkfish at Piscatoris. High-level players with access to the Fishing Guild should target minnows for maximum profit, and those willing to brave the Wilderness can try dark crabs for solid income with added risk. The key to maximizing fishing profit is always banking your fish raw — cooked fish sell for less than raw fish on the Grand Exchange in most cases. Check current prices before committing to any method, and happy fishing.
