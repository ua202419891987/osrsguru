OSRS F2P Ironman Money Making Early Game – 2026 Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Ironman, F2P, Early Game

Starting a fresh F2P ironman in Old School RuneScape in 2026 is both a challenge and a thrill. With no Grand Exchange access, every resource must come from your own grinding. If you're looking for the best OSRS F2P ironman money making early game strategies, this guide will walk you through the optimal progression path from level 3 all the way to preparing for member content. I've been through this grind multiple times, and I'll share the exact route that I've found most efficient.

The F2P Ironman Mindset

Before we dive into specific methods, it's crucial to understand how F2P ironman economics work. You can't buy or sell on the Grand Exchange. Your only income sources are:

  • Selling to general stores: Most items can be sold to shops, typically at 10-40% of their GE value
  • High alchemy: Once you reach level 55 Magic, you can alch items for their high alch value (requires nature runes)
  • Shop stock arbitrage: Buying items from NPC shops and selling them to other shops for a profit (limited but viable)
  • Quest rewards: Gold and items from completing quests

The key insight is that every item has a "store price" and an "alch value," and your goal is to maximize the gold you get from each item you produce or find.

Phase 1: The First Hour (Levels 1-10)

Complete Tutorial and Easy Quests

Start by completing the tutorial island, which gives you basic tools and a few hundred gold. Then immediately complete these quests for their gold and item rewards:

  1. Cook's Assistant: 300 gold + access to the Lumbridge kitchen range
  2. Sheep Shearer: 60 gold + 150 Crafting XP
  3. Rune Mysteries: Access to rune essence mining (critical for Magic XP and income)
  4. Imp Catcher: Amulet of accuracy (sell to general store for 100-200 GP)
  5. Doric's Quest: Access to Doric's anvils + 1,300 Mining XP (instant level boost)

After completing these quests, you'll have approximately 1,000-1,500 gold and some useful unlocks. In 2026, the updated tutorial now awards 1,000 gold upon completion, giving you a head start.

Mine Clay at Varrock East

With your free bronze pickaxe from the tutorial, head to the Varrock east mine and mine clay. Each soft clay sells to the Varrock general store for about 1-5 GP. At level 1 Mining, you can mine roughly 200-300 clay per hour, generating about 5-15K gold per hour. Save this gold — you'll need it for pickaxe upgrades.

Phase 2: Building Combat Stats (Levels 10-30)

Kill Cows at Lumbridge

Cows are the bread and butter of early F2P ironman money making. At the Lumbridge cow field, you'll kill cows for cowhides, bones, and raw meat. Cowhides are your primary income source — take them to the Al Kharid tanner (costs 1 GP per hide) to turn them into soft leather, which you can then craft into leather items.

At levels 10-20, expect roughly 15-30K gold per hour from selling leather items to general stores. As your combat levels increase, your kill speed improves dramatically, potentially reaching 40-60K gold per hour by level 30.

Pro Tip: In 2026, there's a deposit box near the Lumbridge cow field (added in late 2025). Use it to bank cowhides without walking to the Lumbridge bank. This saves enormous amounts of time.

Mine Iron at Al Kharid

Once you hit 15 Mining, switch to iron ore at the Al Kharid mine. Iron ore sells to the Al Kharid general store for about 15-25 GP each. With a mithril or adamant pickaxe (buy from a shop in Lumbridge or Varrock), you can mine approximately 200-300 iron ore per hour, generating 30-60K gold per hour.

Even better, smelt the iron into bars at the nearby furnace and smith the bars into items. Iron platebodies sell to general stores for 200-400 GP each and alch for much more. This dual-approach (smithing + selling/alching) is the core money-making strategy for F2P ironmen.

Phase 3: Mid-Game Transition (Levels 30-50)

Smithing Steel Bars

At level 30 Smithing, you can smelt steel bars (2 coal + 1 iron ore). Steel bars sell to general stores for about 300-500 GP each, and steel items (especially platebodies) have high alch values. This becomes your primary money-making method once you can reliably mine coal at the Barbarian Village or Lumbridge swamp mines.

The profit comes from smithing steel items and either selling them to shops or (ideally) high-alching them once you reach level 55 Magic. A steel platebody alchs for roughly 1,200 GP, which is significantly more than its shop sell price of 650 GP. The difference between alch value and shop price is essentially lost gold on an ironman without nature runes.

Fishing for Food and Profit

At level 20 Fishing, start catching trout at Barbarian Village. You'll need every fish you catch for food (ironmen can't buy food from the GE). Sell any excess raw fish to the general store — you won't get rich, but the extra gold helps fund your smithing operations.

Phase 4: The High Alchemy Milestone (Level 55+)

Reaching level 55 Magic for High Level Alchemy is the single biggest milestone for a F2P ironman's economy. Before this point, you're limited to selling items to shops at a fraction of their value. After unlocking high alch, you can convert items into their full alch value.

The problem: You need nature runes, which are extremely scarce in F2P. The primary sources are:

  • Bryophyta drops: The F2P moss giant boss can drop nature runes (but you need the champion scroll first)
  • General store stock: Some magic shops occasionally stock nature runes (very limited)
  • Wilderness resource area: Nature rune spawns in the deep Wilderness (risky for F2P ironmen)

Strategy: Save every nature rune you find. Use high alchemy selectively on only the most valuable items (steel platebodies, mithril items). Each nature rune used should generate at least 500+ GP in profit over the shop sell price.

Phase 5: Bossing (Obor and Bryophyta)

Once you have 40+ combat stats and decent gear (mithril or adamant), start killing Obor and preparing for Bryophyta.

Obor (Hill Giant Boss)

Obor drops rune items, which are incredibly valuable to an ironman (since you can't buy them). Each rune item is worth its high alch value to you. With decent gear, you can kill Obor every 5-10 minutes (including setup time) for an effective 100-200K gold equivalent per hour.

Bryophyta (Moss Giant Boss)

Bryophyta drops Bryophyta's Essence (used for nature rune crafting supplies) and various seeds. The nature rune savings alone make Bryophyta one of the most profitable F2P ironman bosses, generating approximately 80-150K gold equivalent per hour when you factor in the value of nature runes obtained.

Complete Early Game Quest List for F2P Ironmen

Quests are free XP and gold. Complete these as early as possible:

  • The Knight's Sword: 12,725 Smithing XP (nearly instant level 29 Smithing)
  • Vampyre Slayer: 4,825 Attack XP + silver stake (useful weapon)
  • Dragon Slayer: 18,650 Strength XP + access to mithril/addy armor
  • Lost City: Access to Zanaris (member content, but unlock it early)
  • Rune Mysteries: Rune essence mining access

2026 F2P Ironman Tips

  • The 2026 tutorial awards 1,000 gold — use it wisely for basic supplies
  • The Lumbridge deposit box near the cow field saves 30+ seconds per banking trip
  • F2P worlds with ironman mode have been improved with better resource respawn rates in 2026
  • Group Ironman (GIM) mode allows sharing resources with teammates, dramatically speeding up early-game progression

Conclusion

The F2P ironman early game is a marathon, not a sprint. Follow the progression from cow killing and mining through smithing and eventually to Obor/Bryophyta bossing. Your biggest milestones are reaching level 55 Magic (high alch) and getting consistent Obor kills for rune supplies. With patience and a structured approach, you can build a bank of 500K-1M gold in F2P before transitioning to member content, where the real money-making potential explodes. The F2P ironman grind teaches you resourcefulness and game mechanics that will serve you well throughout your entire ironman journey.
