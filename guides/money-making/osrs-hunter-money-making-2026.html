OSRS Hunter Money Making Guide 2026 – Every Level Covered

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Hunter, Skilling

Hunter is one of the most versatile money-making skills in Old School RuneScape, offering profitable methods at almost every level bracket. In this comprehensive OSRS hunter money making guide for 2026, I'll cover every profitable hunter method from level 1 to 99, with updated profit calculations based on current Grand Exchange prices. Whether you're looking for AFK money, intensive grinding profits, or a mix of both, hunter has something for you.

Why Hunter is a Top Money-Making Skill

Hunter stands out among OSRS skills for several reasons. First, many hunter products are in constant demand — herbiboar slimes for herblore, chinchompas for ranged training, and bird nests for farming XP. Second, hunter methods range from extremely AFK (bird houses) to highly intensive (black chinchompa hunting), letting you choose a playstyle that fits your mood. Third, hunter XP rates and profits have been buffed multiple times since the skill's introduction, and 2026 continues that trend with quality-of-life improvements.

Level 1-27: Bird Snaring and Butterfly Catching

Bird Snaring (Levels 1-9)

At level 1, you can set up bird snares to catch birds in the Feldip Hills or south of Varrock. The caught birds (crimson swifts, golden warblers, and copper longtails) can be dropped for XP or sold. In 2026, bird drops sell for about 10-30 GP each, making this virtually zero profit. Skip this method for money and just use it to reach level 9.

Butterfly Catching with a Net (Levels 15-27)

Starting at level 15, you can catch butterflies with a butterfly net and jar. Ruby harvest butterflies (level 15) and sapphire glacialis butterflies (level 25) can be released near other players to provide skill boosts. In 2026, butterfly jars sell for about 50-100 GP each, and the butterflies themselves for 200-400 GP. This method generates roughly 10-20K gold per hour at best. It's more of a novelty than a money-maker.

Level 27-63: The Mid-Range Grind

Swamp Lizards (Levels 29-39)

Swamp lizards are caught with a net trap in the Canifis swamp area (near the Werewolf Agility Course). Each swamp lizard sells for approximately 500-800 GP on the Grand Exchange in 2026. At level 29-39, you can catch roughly 40-60 lizards per hour, generating about 25-45K gold per hour. While not amazing money, it's a decent training method that covers your supply costs.

Orange Salamanders (Levels 47-59)

Orange salamanders are caught in the desert using net traps. In 2026, each orange salamander sells for about 600-900 GP. At these levels, you can catch 50-70 per hour, yielding approximately 35-55K gold per hour. The Uzer hunter area is the most efficient location.

Red Salamanders (Levels 59-67)

Red salamanders are caught near the Chaos Temple (level 38 Wilderness). Each one sells for approximately 900-1,200 GP in 2026. The Wilderness location means you risk being PK'd, but the profits are better than orange salamanders. Expect roughly 60-90K gold per hour at level 59-67. Always bring teleport tabs and wear minimal gear to reduce risk.

Level 63-73: Black Chinchompas and Herbiboar

This level range is where hunter starts becoming a serious money-making skill. Two methods dominate:

Black Chinchompas (Level 73, Wilderness)

Black chinchompas are the most famous hunter money-making method in OSRS, and they remain profitable in 2026. Located in level 32-36 Wilderness, black chinchompas sell for approximately 600-900 GP each. At level 73, you can catch 100-150 per hour, generating about 70-120K gold per hour. As your level increases toward 99, catch rates improve to 200-300 per hour, pushing profits to 150-250K gold per hour.

The risk factor: Black chinchompas are in the Wilderness, and the area is one of the most popular PKing spots in the game. You will be killed by other players — it's not a matter of if, but when. Bring your best survival setup (teleport jewelry, ring of life, protected prayers), and never bring more than you can afford to lose.

2026 update: Jagex added a "safe zone" near the black chin area where you can bank without being attacked. This has slightly reduced PK risk but also means more competition for chinchompa spawns.

Herbiboar (Level 80, Kourend)

Herbiboar hunting requires level 80 Hunter (and benefits from higher levels) and is located on Great Kourend. You track the herbiboar through tunnels, then loot it for herbs and herbsack materials. In 2026, herbiboar loot averages approximately 20-40K gold per kill, and you can complete 40-60 kills per hour, generating 100-250K gold per hour depending on your Hunter level and the herbs you receive.

What makes herbiboar great: It's in a safe area (no PK risk), the loot includes valuable herbs (ranarr, snapdragon, torstol), and you get Herblore XP from cleaning the herbs. At level 99 Hunter, you can expect approximately 300-400K gold per hour from herbiboar, making it competitive with many bossing methods.

Pro Tip: Wearing the full Graceful outfit significantly speeds up herbiboar runs by reducing stamina drain while running between tunnels. If you have the Herbiboar slayer unlock from the Anachronia base camp, you can also store your herb sack at the herbiboar area.

Level 73-80: Grey Chinchompas (Alternative to Black Chins)

If you want chinchompa profits without the Wilderness risk, grey chinchompas are caught in the Feldip Hills (safe area). Grey chinchompas sell for approximately 300-500 GP each in 2026. At level 73-80, you can catch 150-200 per hour, yielding about 50-80K gold per hour. The profit is significantly lower than black chinchompas, but the zero-risk nature makes it appealing for players who don't want to deal with PKers.

Level 89-99: CrystalImplings and Maniacal Monkey Hunting

Crystal Impling Hunting (Level 89+)

Crystal implings are found on Prifddinas (requires "Song of the Elves" quest). They require level 89 Hunter to catch and provide extremely valuable loot including crystal shards, seeds, and rare items. In 2026, each crystal impling jar is worth approximately 20K-50K GP depending on its contents. At 20-30 implings per hour, you can generate 400K-1.5M gold per hour — the most profitable hunter method in the game.

Catch: Crystal implings spawn rarely and are competed for by other players. You need to react quickly to catch them before someone else does. This is a highly intensive method that requires full attention.

Maniacal Monkey Hunting (Level 60+ Hunter)

Maniacal monkeys are located in the Kruk's Dungeon area (requires completion of "Monkey Madness II"). You hunt them using box traps, and they drop monkey bones and monkey gp. While the direct GP from drops is modest (~50-80K per hour), the real value comes from the Prayer XP from burying monkey bones at the Maniacal Monkey bone altar, which is one of the fastest Prayer training methods in the game.

Bird Houses: The AFK Hunter Money Maker

I covered bird houses in the passive income guide, but they deserve mention here too because they're technically a hunter activity. Setting up bird houses at all five locations takes about 10 minutes and generates 50-100K gold per run primarily from bird nests. With redwood bird houses (level 44 Hunter), each run yields approximately 8-15 bird nests, each worth 3,000-5,000 GP plus the value of seeds inside.

Bird houses fill every 50 minutes. If you do them religiously, you can generate 1-2M gold per day from bird house runs alone, plus the Hunter XP gain. This is the most efficient combination of money and XP for low-to-mid-level hunters.

Hunter Gear and Boosts

To maximize your hunter profits, consider these gear options:

  • Spottier cape / Graceful cape: Reduces weight, allowing longer runs without stamina potions
  • Full Graceful outfit: Essential for herbiboar runs and general hunter movement
  • Hunter potion: Boosts your Hunter level by 1-3 levels for catching higher-level creatures
  • Stamina potions: Keep your run energy up during movement-intensive methods
  • Magic butterfly net: Slightly increased catch chance for implings
  • Hunter's gear from the Hunter Guild (2026 update): The new Hunter Guild shop offers consumable items that temporarily increase catch rates for specific creature types

Profit Summary by Level

  • Level 1-27: 0-20K gp/hr (skip for money, train to 27)
  • Level 29-39: 25-45K gp/hr (swamp lizards)
  • Level 47-59: 35-55K gp/hr (orange salamanders)
  • Level 59-67: 60-90K gp/hr (red salamanders)
  • Level 73: 50-80K gp/hr (grey chinchompas, safe)
  • Level 73: 70-250K gp/hr (black chinchompas, Wilderness)
  • Level 80: 100-400K gp/hr (herbiboar)
  • Level 89: 400K-1.5M gp/hr (crystal implings)
  • Bird houses (44+): 50-100K per 10-min run

What I Wish I Knew When Training Hunter

Hunter was one of the last skills I maxed, and looking back, I made several mistakes:

  • I ignored bird houses for too long: Bird houses are free XP and money. Start them at level 5 and never stop.
  • I went to black chins too early: At level 73, your catch rate is low, and you'll get PK'd frequently. Wait until level 80+ for significantly better profits.
  • I didn't unlock herbiboar sooner: Herbiboar at level 80 is better money than black chins at level 73, with zero PK risk. Get to 80 first.
  • I underestimated the Graceful outfit: It makes a massive difference in herbiboar and general hunter efficiency. Prioritize getting it early.

Conclusion

Hunter is one of the best money-making skills in OSRS in 2026, with methods ranging from completely AFK (bird houses) to highly intensive (crystal implings). For most players, the optimal path is to start with bird houses at level 5, progress through salamanders for training, unlock herbiboar at level 80 for safe, consistent money, and then either continue to 99 with black chinchompas (if you enjoy risk) or crystal implings (if you've completed Song of the Elves). Whatever route you choose, hunter provides excellent XP rates and profits that make every hour of training feel rewarding.
