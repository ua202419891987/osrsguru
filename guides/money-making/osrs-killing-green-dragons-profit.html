OSRS Killing Green Dragons Money Per Hour – 2026 Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, PvM, Wilderness

Killing green dragons has been a staple money-making method in Old School RuneScape for over two decades, and in 2026 it remains a solid choice for mid-level players looking for combat-based income. Green dragons drop valuable dragonhide and dragon bones, both of which are always in demand. In this guide, I'll break down the exact money per hour from killing green dragons, including the best locations, gear setups, and Wilderness survival strategies.

Why Green Dragons?

Green dragons are the lowest-level adult dragons in OSRS, with a combat level of 79. They drop two valuable items consistently:

  • Green dragonhide: Always drops 1. Used for Crafting green dragonhide armor. Sells for approximately 1,500-2,000 GP each in 2026.
  • Dragon bones: Always drops 1. Best-in-slot Prayer XP for free-to-play and low-level members. Sells for approximately 1,500-2,000 GP each in 2026.

Combined, each green dragon kill yields approximately 3,000-4,000 GP in drops. With a kill rate of 40-80 dragons per hour (depending on gear and location), your gross income ranges from 120K to 320K per hour before accounting for supply costs.

Green Dragon Locations in 2026

1. Wilderness (Level 13-26)

The most iconic green dragon location is in the Wilderness, north of the Edgeville monastery. There are 8-10 green dragon spawns scattered across levels 13-26 Wilderness. The advantage is proximity to the Edgeville bank and the "boneyard" teleport. The disadvantage is the constant threat of PKers (player killers).

Expected kills per hour: 50-70 depending on competition and PKer interruptions. At 3,500 GP average per kill, that's approximately 175-245K gold per hour.

2. Chaos Tunnels (Non-Wilderness)

The Chaos Tunnels contain green dragons in a multi-combat area with no Wilderness risk. Access requires navigating through the tunnel system. The advantage is zero PK risk, but the disadvantage is the multi-combat nature — green dragons can attack you simultaneously, and the area is often crowded.

Expected kills per hour: 40-55. At 3,500 GP average per kill, that's approximately 140-190K gold per hour.

3. Corsair Cove (Safe, Requires Quest)

After completing the "Dragon Slayer II" quest (a major quest with significant requirements), green dragons appear in the Corsair Cove dungeon. This is the safest green dragon location — no Wilderness, no multi-combat, and a nearby bank deposit box. However, "Dragon Slayer II" requires high stats and completion of many prerequisite quests.

Expected kills per hour: 45-65. At 3,500 GP average per kill, that's approximately 155-225K gold per hour.

4. Myths' Guild Basement (Safe, Requires Quest)

After completing "Dragon Slayer II," green dragons also appear in the basement of the Myths' Guild. This location is convenient with a nearby bank and completely safe. It's the most popular safe location for green dragon killing in 2026.

Expected kills per hour: 50-70. At 3,500 GP average per kill, that's approximately 175-245K gold per hour.

Gear Setups

Budget Setup (Wilderness, 50-60K Risk)

  • Weapon: Rune scimitar or dragon scimitar
  • Armor: Mithril or adamant platebody, rune platelegs
  • Shield: Anti-dragon shield (required)
  • Inventory: Food (lobsters or swordfish), teleport tabs, 1 dose antifire potion

Kill rate: 40-50 per hour. Profit after supplies: approximately 120-170K gold per hour.

Mid-Range Setup (Wilderness, 200-400K Risk)

  • Weapon: Dragon longsword, whip, or rune crossbow with broad bolts
  • Armor: Dragon platebody or Bandos chestplate, dragon platelegs
  • Shield: Anti-dragon shield or dragonfire shield
  • Inventory: Saradomin brews, super restores, antifire potions, teleport jewelry

Kill rate: 55-65 per hour. Profit after supplies: approximately 180-230K gold per hour.

Ranged Setup (Recommended for Wilderness)

  • Weapon: Rune crossbow or dragon crossbow with broad/diamond bolts
  • Armor: Green d'hide body/chaps (or better), archer helm
  • Shield: Anti-dragon shield (ranged bonus)
  • Inventory: Range potions, antifire, food, teleport

Why ranged? Green dragons are weak to ranged attacks. A ranged setup kills them faster than melee at similar gear levels, and you can safespot certain spawns. Kill rate: 55-70 per hour. Profit: 180-250K gold per hour.

Cost of Supplies

Don't forget to account for supply costs when calculating your net profit:

  • Food: 20-50K per hour (depending on food quality and deaths)
  • Antifire potions: 10-20K per hour (3-4 doses)
  • Weapon ammo (if ranging): 5-15K per hour
  • Prayer potions: 5-10K per hour (if using protect prayers)

Total supply cost: Approximately 40-95K per hour. This means your net profit is roughly 40-50% lower than your gross income from drops.

Wilderness Survival Guide

If you're killing green dragons in the Wilderness (the most accessible location), survival is key. Here are the strategies that keep you alive:

  • Always carry a teleport: Ring of life, Ectophial, or teleport tabs. The moment you see a PKer, teleport out. Don't try to fight back unless you're specifically build for PvP.
  • Use the "hop" strategy: If you see a PKer or the area is too crowded, hop worlds. Most PKers move on quickly if there's no one to kill.
  • World selection: Use low-population worlds (under 200 players) for green dragons. Fewer people means more dragons and fewer PKers.
  • Protect from Magic: PKers who hunt dragon killers typically use freezing spells (Ice Barrage). If you have Protect from Magic, use it proactively when you see a white dot approaching.
  • Watch the minimap constantly: Every 10-15 seconds, glance at the minimap for approaching white dots. The sooner you see a PKer, the sooner you can react.

2026 Changes Affecting Green Dragon Profit

  • Dragonhide price stability: In 2026, green dragonhide prices have been remarkably stable at 1,500-2,000 GP, making profit calculations reliable.
  • Myths' Guild improvements: Jagex added more green dragon spawns to the Myths' Guild basement in a 2026 update, reducing competition at the safe location.
  • Wilderness PvP updates: New Bounty Hunter changes in 2026 have shifted some PKer attention away from green dragon areas, slightly improving safety.

Net Profit Summary

  • Wilderness (budget): 120-170K gp/hr net
  • Wilderness (mid-range): 180-230K gp/hr net
  • Wilderness (ranged): 180-250K gp/hr net
  • Chaos Tunnels: 100-150K gp/hr net
  • Corsair Cove / Myths' Guild: 155-225K gp/hr net (zero risk)

What I Wish I Knew

  • I spent too long at the Wilderness location before completing Dragon Slayer II. The Myths' Guild basement is safer and equally profitable. If you have the quest done, go there.
  • Using a cannon at the Wilderness dragons (if you have one) increases kills per hour by 30-40% but draws massive PKer attention. Only use a cannon on low-pop worlds.
  • Green dragon bones have the same Prayer XP as any other dragon bone. If you need Prayer XP, burying them yourself is more valuable than selling and buying them back.

Conclusion

Green dragons remain a reliable mid-level money-making method in 2026, offering net profits of 150-250K gold per hour depending on your setup and location. For players who have completed Dragon Slayer II, the Myths' Guild basement is the best location — zero PK risk with competitive kill rates. For players who haven't, the Wilderness location is profitable but risky. Bring your best gear you're willing to lose, carry teleports, and stay alert. Green dragons won't make you rich overnight, but they provide consistent income while training combat stats and collecting valuable Crafting materials.
