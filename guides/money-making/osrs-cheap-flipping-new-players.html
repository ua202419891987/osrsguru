OSRS Cheap Flipping Methods for New Players – 2026 Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Flipping, Beginner

You don't need millions of gold to start flipping on the Grand Exchange. In fact, OSRS cheap flipping methods for new players can be just as profitable percentage-wise as flipping expensive items. The beauty of starting small is that you learn the mechanics of the Grand Exchange with minimal risk, and as your capital grows, so does your earning potential. In this 2026 guide, I'll show you exactly how to start flipping with as little as 50K gold and grow it into a significant bank over time.

Why Start with Cheap Items?

Cheap item flipping has several advantages for beginners:

  • Low risk: Even if an item's price crashes, you lose very little gold
  • High volume: Cheap items trade more frequently, meaning your offers fill faster
  • Learning opportunity: You can experiment with different items and strategies without significant financial consequences
  • Percentage returns are similar: A 5% margin on a 100 GP item is the same percentage as a 5% margin on a 100K item
  • No barrier to entry: No stats, quests, or gear requirements — just gold and access to the GE

Essential Flipping Knowledge for Beginners

Understanding Margins

A margin is the difference between what you can buy an item for and what you can sell it for. To check a margin, instant buy one unit of the item and then instant sell it. The buy price minus the sell price (after GE tax) is your margin. In 2026, the GE tax is 1% of the sell price, so always factor that in.

Example: You instant buy a bronze arrow for 12 GP and instant sell it for 10 GP. The gross margin is 2 GP, but the GE tax on the sell is 0.1 GP (1% of 10 GP), so your net margin is 1.9 GP per arrow. When flipping 1,000 arrows, your profit would be approximately 1,900 GP minus any offers that don't fill.

The GE Tax Impact on Cheap Items

Here's something that trips up many new flippers: the 1% GE tax disproportionately affects cheap items. On a 10,000 GP item, 1% is 100 GP — noticeable but manageable. On a 5 GP item, 1% is 0.05 GP, which rounds to essentially nothing. However, when margins are small (like 1-3 GP on cheap items), the percentage lost to tax can eat up a significant portion of your profit.

Rule of thumb: For items under 100 GP, you need margins of at least 3-5% to make meaningful profit after tax. For items over 1,000 GP, margins of 1-2% can still be profitable.

Best Cheap Items to Flip in 2026

1. Bones and Ashes

Bones are one of the most traded items on the Grand Exchange, making them ideal for cheap flipping. In 2026, big bones sell for approximately 80-100 GP each, and dragon bones for 1,500-2,000 GP each. The margins on bones are typically 3-8 GP per bone for big bones and 30-80 GP per dragon bone.

Why it works: Players constantly kill NPCs for combat training and need to get rid of bones quickly. Sellers often undercut the market price to sell faster, creating buying opportunities. Buyers (prayer trainers) need bones urgently and will pay above market price for instant purchase.

With 50K gold, you can flip about 500-600 big bones per cycle. At a 5 GP margin (after tax), that's roughly 2,500-3,000 GP profit per cycle. It sounds small, but when you can complete 5-10 cycles per hour, you're looking at 12-30K gold per hour on a 50K investment — a 24-60% hourly return on investment.

2. Low-Level Runes

Runes are always in demand for Magic training. The best cheap flipping targets include:

  • Chaos runes: ~150-180 GP each, margins of 3-8 GP, extremely high volume
  • Death runes: ~200-250 GP each, margins of 5-15 GP
  • Blood runes: ~300-400 GP each, margins of 8-20 GP
  • Law runes: ~180-220 GP each, margins of 3-7 GP
  • Fire runes: ~5-8 GP each, margins of 0.5-1 GP (high volume, low margin)

Chaos runes are my top recommendation for new flippers because they trade in massive quantities and the margins are relatively stable. With 100K gold, you can flip 550-650 chaos runes per cycle.

3. Arrows and Ammunition

Ranged ammunition trades in enormous volumes and is perfect for cheap flipping:

  • Bronze arrows: ~2-5 GP each, margins of 0.5-1 GP (volume in the thousands)
  • Iron arrows: ~8-12 GP each, margins of 1-2 GP
  • Steel arrows: ~20-30 GP each, margins of 2-5 GP
  • Adamant arrows: ~50-70 GP each, margins of 3-8 GP

Pro Tip: Arrow flipping works best in large quantities. Buy and sell in batches of 1,000+. At bronze arrow volumes, you can move 10,000+ arrows per cycle, which can generate surprising profits even with sub-1 GP margins.

4. Low-Level Ores and Bars

Mining and Smithing materials are always in demand:

  • Iron ore: ~80-120 GP each, margins of 2-5 GP, very high volume
  • Coal: ~150-200 GP each, margins of 3-8 GP
  • Mithril ore: ~200-300 GP each, margins of 5-15 GP
  • Iron bars: ~200-280 GP each, margins of 5-12 GP
  • Steel bars: ~500-600 GP each, margins of 10-25 GP

Iron ore is the most accessible flipping material for new players with under 100K gold. It trades in massive volumes and the margins, while small, add up quickly when you're flipping thousands per cycle.

5. Gems

Uncut and cut gems are traded by both skillers and combat players. In 2026:

  • Uncut sapphire: ~50-70 GP each, margins of 2-5 GP
  • Uncut emerald: ~80-120 GP each, margins of 3-8 GP
  • Uncut ruby: ~700-900 GP each, margins of 15-40 GP
  • Uncut diamond: ~2,000-2,500 GP each, margins of 40-100 GP

Uncut sapphires and emeralds are ideal for beginners with small banks. They come from mining, thieving, and various drops, keeping supply steady. Demand comes from Crafting training, ensuring consistent trade volume.

Step-by-Step: Your First Flip

Here's exactly how to execute your first flip with 50K gold:

  1. Choose an item: Start with chaos runes or iron ore. Both have stable prices and high volume.
  2. Check the margin: Instant buy 1 chaos rune. Note the price. Instant sell 1 chaos rune. Note the price. Calculate: (buy price - sell price - GE tax) = your margin.
  3. Place a buy offer: Set your buy price to the instant sell price (or slightly above it, like +1-2 GP). Set the quantity to fill most of your budget. For 50K on chaos runes at ~160 GP each, buy about 300 runes.
  4. Wait for the offer to fill: This can take anywhere from 1 minute to 30 minutes depending on the item.
  5. Place a sell offer: As soon as your buy fills, immediately place a sell offer at or near the instant buy price. Set the quantity to match what you bought.
  6. Collect your profit: When the sell offer fills, you've completed your first flip. Calculate your total profit and note the time it took.

Growing Your Capital: The Compounding Effect

The real power of cheap flipping comes from compounding your profits. Here's a realistic growth scenario starting with 50K gold:

  • Week 1 (50K): Flip cheap runes and ores for ~20-30% profit per day = 75-100K by end of week
  • Week 2 (100K): Move to slightly more expensive items (steel bars, mithril ore) = 150-200K
  • Week 3 (200K): Start flipping consumables (prayer potions, food) = 300-400K
  • Week 4 (400K): Diversify into multiple items = 600K-1M

By reinvesting all your profits (not spending on gear or skilling supplies), you can grow 50K into 1M in about a month of consistent daily flipping. The key is to reinvest profits rather than spending them — every GP sitting in your bank not being flipped is a wasted opportunity.

Common Mistakes New Flippers Make

  • Forgetting the GE tax: Always calculate your profit AFTER the 1% tax. Many beginners think they're profiting when they're actually losing money.
  • Flipping items they know nothing about: Stick to items you understand the market for. If you don't know why an item's price is moving, don't flip it.
  • Not tracking profits: Use a notebook, spreadsheet, or app to track every trade. You can't improve what you don't measure.
  • Being too greedy with margins: Setting your buy price too low means your offer takes forever to fill. A slightly lower margin that fills in 5 minutes is better than a higher margin that takes an hour.
  • Putting all gold in one item: Always keep at least some gold in reserve for new opportunities that arise suddenly.

Conclusion

Cheap flipping in OSRS is the perfect entry point for new players looking to build their bank from scratch. Starting with just 50K gold and flipping items like chaos runes, iron ore, and big bones, you can generate consistent profits that compound over time. The skills you learn — margin checking, patience, risk management — will serve you throughout your OSRS career, whether you're flipping 100 GP items or 10M items. Start small, stay consistent, reinvest your profits, and watch your bank grow. The Grand Exchange is the most accessible money-making tool in all of Gielinor — use it.
