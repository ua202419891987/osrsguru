OSRS Ironman Money Making F2P 2026 – The Complete Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Ironman, F2P

Playing as a free-to-play ironman in Old School RuneScape is one of the most rewarding experiences the game has to offer — but let's be real, OSRS ironman money making f2p 2026 can feel like an uphill battle if you don't know where to start. Unlike regular accounts, you can't just buy what you need off the Grand Exchange. Every coin, every resource, every piece of gear has to come from your own sweat and grinding. In this guide, I'll walk you through the most efficient money-making methods for F2P ironmen in 2026, updated with all the latest game changes and economic shifts.

Why F2P Ironman Money Making is Different in 2026

The F2P ironman economy has shifted significantly over the past couple of years. Jagex has introduced quality-of-life updates that indirectly affect ironman progression, and the community has discovered more efficient skilling routes. In 2026, the biggest difference from earlier years is the improved low-level skilling experience and the rebalanced drop tables from F2P bosses like Obor and Bryophyta. If you're coming back to the game or starting fresh, you'll find the early game smoother than before — but you still need a solid plan.

The No-GE Reality

On a regular account, making money in F2P often means buying low and selling high on the Grand Exchange. On an ironman, that option is completely off the table. Your income comes from two sources: selling items to general stores and high-alching drops. This fundamentally changes what's profitable. High-volume items like runes, arrows, and certain drops become your bread and butter rather than niche flipping opportunities.

Early Game Methods (Levels 1-20)

Mining and Selling Clay

Mining clay at the Varrock east mine is one of the very first money-making activities you can do. Each soft clay sells to the general store for a small but steady amount. While it won't make you rich, it generates enough starting capital to buy a pickaxe upgrade and some basic gear. At level 1 Mining, you can pull in roughly 15-20K gold per hour just from clay. It's not glamorous, but every ironman empire starts somewhere.

Pro Tip: If you mine clay near the Varrock west bank and use the bucket of water to soften it yourself, you can get slightly more per unit. It adds up over time, especially when you're just starting out with zero capital.

Cowhide Collection

Killing cows at the Lumbridge cow field and banking cowhides remains a timeless F2P ironman classic. Each cowhide can be tanned into soft leather (costing a few coins per hide at the tanner in Al Kharid — yes, even ironmen pay this NPC fee) and then crafted into leather gloves or body armor. In 2026, the crafting route is still viable because you need Crafting XP anyway, so you're essentially double-dipping: building a skill and generating sellable items at the same time.

Expect around 30-40K gold per hour at low combat levels, scaling up as your kill speed improves with better gear. The real value here is that you're also getting combat XP and potential cowhide deposits for Crafting training later.

Mid Game Methods (Levels 20-50)

Smithing Iron Bars at Al Kharid

Once you hit 15 Mining, you can mine iron ore, and at 30 Smithing, you can smelt it into iron bars. The Al Kharid mine-to-furnace-to-bank triangle is incredibly efficient. Each iron bar can be smithed into items that you can high-alch (once you get the Magic level) or sell to specialty shops. In 2026, iron bar production still yields about 50-80K gold per hour for F2P ironmen, depending on your Mining and Smithing levels and how organized your banking runs are.

What I wish I knew: Many ironmen skip the Ring of Forging. If you mine enough gold to purchase one early (they cost a few thousand from the grand exchange on regular accounts, but on ironman you'll need to find alternative ways — or just accept the 50% smelting rate), your iron bar output doubles. Without a GE, you're limited to what NPCs stock or what you can craft yourself, so plan your smithing route accordingly.

Fishing and Selling Raw Trout/Salmon

At level 20 Fishing, you can catch trout at the Barbarian Village river, and at level 30, salmon join the mix. For an ironman, raw fish are incredibly valuable because they're your primary food source. However, once you have a surplus, you can sell excess raw trout or salmon to general stores. In 2026, this method generates approximately 40-60K gold per hour, with the bonus of feeding your own combat training.

The key here is efficiency: use a fly fishing rod and feathers (which you can buy from fishing shops or get from killing chickens). Barbarian Village is close to the Edgeville bank via the shortcut tunnel, making banking runs fast.

Bossing for Ironmen: Obor and Bryophyta

Obor (F2P Hill Giant Boss)

Obor was a game-changer for F2P ironmen when he was introduced, and in 2026 he remains one of the most profitable F2P bosses. Located in the Hill Giant area beneath Edgeville (accessible with a Giant Champion scroll or by paying the Dwarf multicannon area fee), Obor drops the Hill Giant Club and a variety of rune items. On an ironman, every rune drop is pure gold value since you can't buy them.

With decent melee stats (40+ Attack, 40+ Strength, 30+ Defence), you can consistently kill Obor for an estimated 100-200K gold per hour factoring in the value of rune items and other drops. The Hill Giant Club itself is worth a significant amount for its combat stats. In 2026, Jagex has kept Obor's drop table stable, making him a reliable money maker.

Bryophyta (F2P Moss Giant Boss)

Bryophyta is the F2P equivalent boss found in the Moss Giant dungeon south of the Fishing Guild entrance. She drops the Bryophyta's Essence, which is crucial for ironmen looking to upgrade their Nature Rune supplies. In 2026, Bryophyta remains a solid target, yielding around 80-150K gold equivalent per hour when you account for the resource savings from her unique drops.

Requirement check: You'll need the Moss Giant Champion scroll to access Bryophyta's lair, which means killing a lot of moss giants first. Think of it as an investment — the champion scroll grind is tedious, but the payoff from Bryophyta kills makes it worthwhile.

High-Alching as an F2P Ironman Income Strategy

High Level Alchemy (level 55 Magic) is the single most important money-making spell for F2P ironmen. Once you unlock it, your entire approach to money making changes. Instead of selling items to shops at a fraction of their value, you can alch them for their high alchemy value. The catch? You need nature runes, and on an ironman, those are hard to come by in F2P.

In 2026, the primary source of nature runes for F2P ironmen is Bryophyta or crafting them yourself (if you've progressed far enough in member skills and returned). Without member access, you'll be limited to what Bryophyta drops and any you can obtain from general store stock — which is minimal. This is why many F2P ironmen focus on saving every nature rune they find and using high alchemy selectively on the most valuable items only.

Profit breakdown: Smithing steel platebodies (level 48 Smithing) and high-alching them yields approximately 150-200K gold per hour when you factor in the cost of nature runes versus the alchemy return. Without nature runes, this drops to selling to shops at around 40-60K per hour. The difference is massive, which is why getting your first batch of nature runes is such a milestone for F2P ironmen.

Runecrafting: The Hidden Goldmine

Runecrafting is often overlooked as a money-making skill for F2P ironmen, but it deserves serious attention in 2026. Crafting mind runes (level 2 RC) and body runes (level 20 RC) provides a steady stream of runes that you use for Magic training or sell to magic shops. At higher levels, crafting cosmic or chaos runes (if accessible through quests or events) can be even more profitable.

The 2026 update to the Runecrafting experience in F2P has slightly improved XP rates at low levels, making it less painful to train. Earth runes (level 9 RC) are particularly useful since they're consumed by the Earth Bolt spell, a staple of mid-level F2P combat. Each rune you craft is one you don't have to buy, effectively saving gold that would otherwise go to magic shops.

Woodcutting and Firemaking as Income

While woodcutting in F2P isn't the most exciting money-making method, it serves a dual purpose: generating gold through selling logs and providing firemaking XP. Willow logs (level 30 WC) can be sold to general stores or burned for Firemaking XP. In 2026, oak logs and willow logs still sell reasonably well to NPCs, generating around 20-35K gold per hour depending on your woodcutting level and banking efficiency.

The Draynor Village willow trees are a classic spot. They're close to the bank, the trees respawn quickly, and there's always competition — but on an ironman world, you'll often have the trees to yourself. If you're patient and want a low-intensity method to generate gold while watching YouTube or chatting, willow woodcutting is hard to beat.

2026 Changes That Affect F2P Ironmen

A few notable changes in 2026 have impacted F2P ironman money making:

  • Bank placeholders are free in F2P — This is a small but meaningful quality-of-life improvement that helps you organize drops more efficiently.
  • Updated F2P quest rewards — Several quests now grant slightly better gold or item rewards, giving new ironmen a small boost in starting capital.
  • Obor/Bryophyta drop table tweaks — Minor adjustments to rune drop rates mean slightly more consistent income per kill.
  • F2P worlds with group ironman support — If you're playing Group Ironman (GIM) in F2P, you can now share certain resources with your team, which dramatically speeds up early-game progression.

My Recommended Progression Route

Based on hundreds of hours of ironman gameplay, here's the money-making progression I recommend for F2P ironmen in 2026:

  1. Levels 1-10: Mine and sell clay at Varrock. Save every coin for gear upgrades.
  2. Levels 10-20: Kill cows for cowhides. Start tanning and crafting leather items.
  3. Levels 20-30: Begin fishing trout at Barbarian Village. Sell excess raw fish.
  4. Levels 30-40: Smith iron bars at Al Kharid. Craft steel bars once you reach 30 Smithing.
  5. Levels 40-50: Kill Obor regularly. Start preparing for Bryophyta.
  6. Level 55+: Unlock High Alchemy. Pivot to smithing and alching as your primary income.

Conclusion

F2P ironman money making in OSRS during 2026 is all about efficiency and patience. You won't become a millionaire overnight, but by following a structured progression from low-level skilling through bossing and eventually high alchemy, you can build a solid financial foundation before even stepping into the member areas. The key milestones are reaching level 55 Magic for High Alchemy and getting consistent Obor/Bryophyta kills for rune supplies. Stick with it, track your hourly profits, and remember that every coin earned on an ironman is a coin truly earned.
