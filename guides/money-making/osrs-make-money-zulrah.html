OSRS How to Make Money with Zulrah (2026 Profit Guide)

Author: OSRS Guru Team  |  Updated: May 2026  |  Category: Boss Money Making

If you are looking for one of the most consistent money-making bosses in Old School RuneScape, Zulrah should be at the top of your list. Learning OSRS how to make money with Zulrah is a rite of passage for any mid-to-high level player, and for good reason. TheShrine of theSerpent provides an instanced boss fight with essentially zero competition, fast kill times, and a drop table packed with valuable resources and rare uniques.

In this updated 2026 guide, we will break down exactly how much gold you can expect per hour at Zulrah, the best gear setups for each budget, how to learn the four rotation patterns, and the supply costs that eat into your profits. Whether you are attempting your first kill or pushing for sub-40-second kill times, this guide covers everything you need.

Quick Summary: Zulrah averages between 1.5M and 3.5M gold per hour depending on your gear, kill speed, and current market prices for resources like onyx bolts, rune items, and skilling supplies. Unique drops like the Tanzanite fang (700K-1.1M), Magma mutagen (800K-1.2M), and Uncut onyx (1.2M-1.6M) provide massive profit spikes.

Zulrah Requirements

Before you can start farming Zulrah for profit, you need to meet the following requirements:

  • Quest: Regicide (to access Port Tyras and the fairy ring network near Zulrah)
  • Quest: The Fremennik Trials (for access to the Fairy Ring system)
  • Recommended Stats: 80+ Ranged, 75+ Magic, 70+ Prayer
  • Minimum Combat Stats: 70+ Ranged, 70+ Magic, 43+ Prayer (doable but slower)
  • Prayers: Protect from Missiles and Protect from Magic are essential
  • Inventory: Antipoison+ or Anti-venom (Zulrah applies venom, not poison)
  • Additional: A Ring of recoil is recommended for the Snakeling phase to passively damage them

Gear Setups

Budget Setup (15-30M GP)

You can start learning Zulrah on a relatively modest budget. The key is having decent defensive stats and enough offensive power to keep kills under two minutes.

  • Helm: Serpentine helm (charged with Zulrah's scales)
  • Body: Karil's leathertop or Armadyl chestplate
  • Legs: Karil's leatherskirt or Armadyl chainskirt
  • Weapon (Ranged): Rune crossbow with diamond or dragon bolts (e)
  • Weapon (Magic): Trident of the swamp (e) or Iban's staff with fire surge
  • Shield: Book of law or Ancient book
  • Cape: Ava's assembler or Accumulator
  • Boots: Ranger boots or Blessed d'hide boots
  • Ring: Archers ring (i) or Ring of the gods (i)
  • Amulet: Amulet of glory or Amulet of fury
  • Gloves: Barrows gloves or Blessed d'hide vambraces

Optimal Setup (100M+ GP)

Once you are comfortable with the rotations and have some Zulrah profits banked, upgrading your gear significantly increases kills per hour.

  • Helm: Serpentine helm (charged)
  • Body: Masori body (f) or Armadyl chestplate
  • Legs: Masori chaps (f) or Armadyl chainskirt
  • Weapon (Ranged): Toxic blowpipe with rune or dragon darts
  • Weapon (Magic): Trident of the swamp (e)
  • Shield: Void knight deflector (during Zulrah magic phase) or Ancient book
  • Cape: Ava's assembler
  • Boots: Pegasian boots
  • Ring: Ring of suffering (ri) with recoil
  • Amulet: Necklace of anguish
  • Gloves: Barrows gloves or Infernal gloves

In 2026, the Masori armor from the Tombs of Amascut has become the standard for high-end Zulrah players. The fortified variant provides better defensive stats than Armadyl, and the damage bonus is noticeable when using the Toxic blowpipe. If Masori is out of budget, Armadyl remains perfectly viable for consistent profit.

Understanding the Four Rotations

Zulrah has four possible rotation patterns, and learning to identify which one you are on within the first two phases is critical for fast kills. Each rotation dictates which attack styles Zulrah uses and where you need to stand.

The Four Rotations:

1. Ranged-Magic-Melee: Start with ranged (southwest corner), then magic (northeast), then melee with Snakelings.

2. Magic-Ranged-Melee: Start with magic (northeast), then ranged (west), then melee with Snakelings.

3. Ranged-Melee-Ranged: Start ranged (southwest), melee phase, then ranged again with Snakelings.

4. Magic-Ranged-Magic: Start magic (northeast), ranged (west), then magic again.

How to Learn Rotations Quickly

  1. Watch the first two forms carefully. If Zulrah starts green (ranged) from the southwest, you are on rotation 1 or 3. If Zulrah starts blue (magic) from the northeast, you are on rotation 2 or 4.
  2. Check the second form. The second phase tells you which specific rotation you are on. If green (ranged) appears second, you are on rotation 2 or 4.
  3. Use an overlay or guide. Many players use the Zulrah helper plugin on RuneLite, which highlights the correct tile to stand on during each phase. This is legal and widely used in the community.
  4. Practice with food. During your first 50-100 kills, bring extra food so you can survive mistakes while you learn the patterns.

Supply Costs and Inventory Setup

One of the biggest mistakes new Zulrah players make is over-spending on supplies. Here is an optimal inventory setup for a full Zulrah trip.

Recommended Inventory

  • Antivenom+ (4): Each dose lasts 3 minutes of venom immunity. Four doses cover a full trip for most players.
  • Saradomin brews (4): Your primary healing. Saradomin brews restore more hitpoints per inventory slot than any other food.
  • Super restore (4): Counteracts the stat drain from Saradomin brews. Essential for maintaining your prayer and ranged/magic levels.
  • Ranged ammo: 500+ bolts or darts depending on your weapon
  • Magic runes or charges: Trident charges or runes for your spellbook
  • Prayer potions (2-3): Backup prayer restoration if your restores are not keeping up
  • Ring of recoil: Helps damage Snakelings during the melee phase
  • Teleport: A quick teleport out in case things go wrong

Supply Cost Per Hour

For an experienced player doing 30+ kills per hour, supply costs break down roughly as follows:

  • Antivenom+ doses: 40K-60K/hr
  • Saradomin brews: 25K-35K/hr
  • Super restores: 20K-30K/hr
  • Ammo/charges: 15K-30K/hr
  • Total: approximately 100K-155K per hour

This is remarkably low for a boss that generates 2M-3.5M per hour in profit. The supply cost ratio at Zulrah is one of the best in the game, which is why it remains such a popular money-making method in 2026.

Tips to Maximize Your Profit

Pro Tip: Always pick up and loot everything from Zulrah drops, including Zulrah's scales. While scales are low in value individually (100-200 GP each), they accumulate to 10K-20K per trip and are needed to charge your serpentine helm for future kills.

1. Use the RuneLite Zulrah Plugin

The Zulrah plugin is the single biggest quality-of-life improvement for learning and mastering this boss. It shows you the exact tile to stand on during each rotation phase, highlights Zulrah's current attack style, and tracks your loot in real-time. In 2026, this plugin has been refined to support all four rotations with near-perfect accuracy.

2. Learn to Dodge the Toxic Clouds

Throughout the fight, Zulrah spawns toxic clouds on the ground. Standing in these clouds deals rapid damage and can drain your supplies fast. Position yourself at the designated safe spots during each phase to avoid unnecessary damage. The RuneLite plugin marks these tiles clearly.

3. Manage Your Prayer Efficiently

Zulrah alternates between ranged and magic attacks, so you need to switch between Protect from Missiles and Protect from Magic accordingly. During the melee phase, you do not need any protection prayer active, which is a good time to sip a restore or reposition. Efficient prayer flicking saves you significant money on prayer potions over hundreds of kills.

4. Optimize Your Switches

Fast gear switches between ranged and magic are crucial for sub-50-second kills. Set up your inventory so your weapon switches are easily accessible. Some advanced players use a one-tick prayer flick and instant weapon switches to push their kill times even lower. While this takes practice, the difference between a 45-second and a 90-second kill adds up to hundreds of thousands of gold per hour.

5. Sell Drops Strategically

Not all Zulrah drops should be immediately sold on the Grand Exchange. High-alchable items like rune battleaxes, rune longswords, and rune knives can be alched for additional magic XP while generating consistent profit. However, always check the GE price versus the high alch value first. In 2026, many rune items sit near or slightly above their alch value, so selling on the GE is usually better.

What I Wish I Knew When Starting Zulrah

Looking back at my first few hundred Zulrah kills, there are several things I wish someone had told me before I started. First, do not bring too much food. New players often fill half their inventory with sharks, but Saradomin brews and super restores are far more efficient. You only need 4 brews and 4 restores for a full trip once you are comfortable with the rotations.

Second, the RuneLite Zulrah plugin is not cheating. Some players feel guilty about using it, but it is a widely accepted tool in the OSRS community. The developers at Jagex have explicitly allowed UI plugins that display publicly available information. Using the helper does not make the fight trivial; it just removes the memorization barrier so you can focus on execution.

Third, your profit will vary wildly session to session. I have gone 150 kills without a single unique drop, and I have also gotten two Tanzanite fangs in 30 kills. Do not get discouraged during dry streaks. The math works out over time, and Zulrah's common drop table alone makes it profitable even without uniques.

Finally, take breaks. Zulrah is a highly repetitive fight, and burnout is real. I personally cap my Zulrah sessions at 2-3 hours before switching to a different money-making method or skilling activity. This keeps the game enjoyable and prevents sloppy mistakes that cost you supplies.

Zulrah vs. Other Bosses for Money

How does Zulrah compare to other popular money-making bosses in 2026?

  • Zulrah: 1.5M-3.5M/hr, instanced, low supply cost, requires 70+ ranged/magic
  • Vorkath: 2M-4M/hr, similar requirements, slightly higher gear requirement, better unique drops
  • Chambers of Xeric: 3M-15M+/hr, team-based, high variance, requires multiple players
  • Corporeal Beast: 3M-8M/hr, team-based, requires top-tier gear and experience
  • Grotesque Guardians: 2M-4M/hr, requires 75+ Slayer, relatively low gear requirements

Zulrah occupies a sweet spot between accessibility and profitability. It is easier to access than Raids or Corp, yet provides consistent returns that rival or exceed many harder bosses. For solo players looking to build their bank, Zulrah remains one of the best options in 2026.

Conclusion

Learning OSRS how to make money with Zulrah is one of the best investments of your time in Old School RuneScape. With profit potential of 1.5M to 3.5M per hour, low supply costs, and an instanced fight with no competition, Zulrah provides a reliable income stream for players of all experience levels. The learning curve is manageable thanks to tools like the RuneLite Zulrah plugin, and the rewards scale well as you improve your gear and kill speed.

Whether you are saving up for your first Bandos godsword, funding a prayer altar, or building towards end-game PvM gear, Zulrah is the boss that makes it happen. Start with the budget gear setup, learn the four rotations, and watch your gold stack grow. Good luck, and may the Tanzanite fangs drop in your favor.
