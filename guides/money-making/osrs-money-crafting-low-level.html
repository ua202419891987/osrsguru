OSRS How to Make Money with Crafting Low Level – 2026 Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Crafting, Skilling

Crafting is often overlooked as a money-making skill in Old School RuneScape, especially at low levels. But if you know what to make and when to sell, making money with crafting at low levels is absolutely viable in 2026. This guide covers every profitable crafting method from level 1 to 50, including the materials you need, expected profits, and the step-by-step process for each method.

Why Low-Level Crafting Can Be Profitable

The key to profitable low-level crafting is understanding supply and demand. Many mid and high-level crafters skip low-level items entirely, which means there's often a gap in the market for basic crafted goods. Additionally, several low-level crafting products are consumed in large quantities by other players — bowstrings for Fletching, leather armor for ironmen, and jewelry for clue scrolls and PvP.

In 2026, the OSRS economy has stabilized after several years of fluctuation, and low-level crafting materials have found reliable price floors. This makes it a great time to invest in crafting as a money-making skill.

Level 1: Spinning Flax into Bowstrings

This is the single most profitable level 1 Crafting method in the entire game. Bowstrings are made by spinning flax on a spinning wheel, and they sell for approximately 100-150 GP each on the Grand Exchange in 2026. Flax itself costs about 3-5 GP each. That means each bowstring you spin has a profit margin of roughly 95-145 GP.

At level 1 Crafting, you can spin approximately 1,200-1,400 bowstrings per hour (the speed is the same at all crafting levels). That translates to about 110-200K gold per hour in profit. Yes, you read that right — level 1 Crafting can generate over 100K gold per hour.

The Best Locations

  • Seers' Village: Flax field is right next to a spinning wheel on the second floor of the house south of the bank. This is the most efficient location — pick flax, walk upstairs, spin, bank, repeat.
  • Lumbridge Castle: Spinning wheel on the second floor. Requires walking between the bank and the castle, slightly slower than Seers' Village.
  • Neitiznot (after Fremennik Trials): Flax field and spinning wheel nearby. Good alternative if Seers' Village is crowded.

Pro Tip: Buy flax in bulk from the Grand Exchange rather than picking it yourself. Picking flax is free but extremely slow (~200-300 per hour), while buying 1,400 flax at 5 GP each only costs 7,000 GP and lets you focus entirely on spinning. The time saved is worth far more than the 7K spent.

Level 1-7: Leather Gloves and Boots

If you're training Crafting through leatherworking (which is cheaper than spinning if you're collecting your own cowhides), leather gloves at level 1 and leather boots at level 7 are worth making. Each soft leather costs about 1-2 GP (cowhide tanning cost), and leather gloves sell for about 5-10 GP each. The profit per item is tiny, but if you're mass-producing them while training, the XP and small profit add up.

Profit: Approximately 5-15K gold per hour. Not exciting, but this method doubles as Crafting training, so you're not losing money while leveling — a rare thing in OSRS skilling.

Level 7-20: Hard Leather Bodies

At level 7, you can craft hard leather bodies, which are useful for ironman accounts and clue scroll completion. Hard leather is made by tanning cowhides at a tanner with hard leather mode selected. Each hard leather body requires 3 hard leathers to craft.

In 2026, hard leather bodies sell for approximately 50-80 GP each. The cost of 3 hard leathers (tanning) is about 15-30 GP. That leaves a profit of roughly 20-65 GP per body. At a crafting speed of about 200-300 bodies per hour, you're looking at 5-20K gold per hour in profit.

Honest take: The money from hard leather bodies is modest, but it's profitable Crafting training. If your goal is pure gold, stick with bowstrings. If you want to level Crafting while making some money on the side, this is a reasonable option.

Level 8-20: Gold Jewelry (Sapphires, Emeralds, Opals)

Jewelry crafting opens up at level 8 with gold rings and requires both Crafting and a mould (bought from crafting shops for a few GP). The process is: get a gold bar (from Mining 40 + Smithing 40, or buy from GE), get a gem, smelt them together at a furnace, then string the amulet.

In 2026, the most profitable low-level jewelry items are:

  • Opal rings (level 8): Low value but cheap to make. Each sells for about 200-300 GP, costs about 100-150 GP in materials. Profit: ~100-150 GP per ring.
  • Sapphire rings (level 20): Each sells for approximately 500-700 GP, costs about 300-400 GP. Profit: ~200-300 GP per ring.
  • Sapphire amulets (level 24): After stringing, sell for approximately 800-1,000 GP. Costs about 400-500 GP. Profit: ~300-500 GP per amulet.
  • Emerald rings (level 27): Each sells for about 600-900 GP, costs about 400-500 GP. Profit: ~200-400 GP per ring.

Profit: At level 20-30, jewelry crafting generates approximately 30-60K gold per hour depending on the item you choose. Sapphire amulets are the best value because the stringing process adds significant value.

Important note: Jewelry crafting has a chance to fail at lower levels, destroying the gem. Always craft slightly above the minimum required level to reduce failure rates. At 5+ levels above the requirement, failures become rare.

Level 20-38: Glassblowing

Glassblowing becomes available at level 1 (glass), but it becomes profitable at level 20+ when you can make worthwhile items. You need soda ash and buckets of sand (both obtainable from cooking seaweed and collecting sand from Yanille or Entrana). Molten glass is made by using soda ash and sand on a furnace, then blown into items with a glassblowing pipe.

In 2026, profitable glass items include:

  • Beer glasses (level 1): Sell for 1-2 GP. Not worth it.
  • Candle lanterns (level 4): Sell for 20-30 GP. Marginal profit.
  • Oil lamps (level 12): Sell for 50-80 GP. Slight profit possible.
  • Vial (level 33): The gold standard of glassblowing money. Vials sell for approximately 20-30 GP each in 2026, and the molten glass cost is about 15-20 GP per vial. With ~1,000 vials per hour, profit is roughly 10-15K gold per hour.
  • Orb (level 46): Worth 200-400 GP each, but requires level 46 Crafting. Profits of about 80-150K gold per hour at higher levels.

Pro Tip: The best way to get sand for glassblowing is using bucket of sand from the Yanille sand pit or buying seaweed (200-250 GP) and cooking it for soda ash. In 2026, the most cost-effective method is buying buckets of sand directly from the Grand Exchange, as the price per unit is competitive with collecting it yourself.

Level 38-50: Battlestaves and Mystic Staves

At level 38 Crafting, you can attach elemental orbs (fire, water, earth, air) to battlestaves. This is where crafting starts to become genuinely profitable. The process requires charged orbs (from charging them at their respective obelisks using level 66 Magic) and battlestaves (obtained from various NPC shops that restock daily).

In 2026, the most profitable battlestaves are:

  • Fire battlestaff: Sells for 6,000-8,000 GP. Cost of battlestaff + fire orb: approximately 4,000-5,500 GP. Profit: ~2,000-3,500 GP per staff.
  • Water battlestaff: Sells for 4,000-6,000 GP. Similar margins to fire staves.
  • Earth battlestaff: Sells for 5,000-7,000 GP. Profit margins vary based on orb prices.

Profit: With battlestaff crafting, you can generate approximately 200-500K gold per hour depending on how many staves you can obtain per day and how efficiently you craft them. The bottleneck is usually the battlestaff supply from NPC shops, which limits you to about 100-200 staves per day from all available shops combined.

What I wish I knew: Complete the Varrock Hard diary for increased battlestaff stock at Zaff's shop in Varrock. This dramatically increases your daily battlestaff supply and makes crafting battlestaves far more profitable than buying staves off the GE.

Level 50+: Dragonhide Armor

At level 50, you can craft green dragonhide bodies (from green dragonhides, which you obtain by killing green dragons — covered in our separate guide). Green d'hide bodies are in constant demand as mid-level ranged armor. In 2026, each green dragonhide body sells for approximately 3,000-4,000 GP, and the cost of tanned green dragonhide is about 1,500-2,000 GP per hide.

With Crafting 50-60, you can make approximately 200-250 green d'hide bodies per hour, generating about 200-500K gold per hour in profit. As your Crafting level increases, you can move to blue dragonhide at level 60+ (even more profitable) and red dragonhide at level 70+.

Profit Summary

  • Bowstrings (level 1): 110-200K gp/hr (best low-level method)
  • Leather gloves/boots (1-7): 5-15K gp/hr (training method)
  • Jewelry (8-27): 30-60K gp/hr
  • Glassblowing (20-38): 10-15K gp/hr (vials)
  • Battlestaves (38+): 200-500K gp/hr
  • Green d'hide bodies (50+): 200-500K gp/hr

Conclusion

Low-level Crafting in OSRS offers surprising profitability in 2026. The standout method is bowstring spinning at level 1, which can generate over 100K gold per hour with zero additional requirements. As you level up, battlestaff crafting at level 38 and dragonhide armor at level 50 become even more lucrative. The beauty of crafting is that you're training a useful skill while making money — a rare combination in OSRS. Start with bowstrings, build up your Crafting level, and transition to higher-value items as you progress.
