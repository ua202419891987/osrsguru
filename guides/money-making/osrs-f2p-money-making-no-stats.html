OSRS F2P Money Making No Stats Required – Start Earning Today

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, F2P, Beginner

Just created a brand new account in Old School RuneScape and wondering how to make your first gold? The good news is that OSRS f2p money making with no stats required is absolutely possible in 2026. You don't need any combat levels, skill levels, or quest completions to start earning. In this guide, I'll show you the exact methods that work on a completely fresh level 3 account, with step-by-step instructions and realistic profit expectations.

Method 1: Grabbing Free Items from Spawns

One of the most underrated ways to make money with zero stats is collecting item spawns around Gielinor. Certain items respawn on the ground in fixed locations, and you can pick them up, bank them, and sell them on the Grand Exchange. The best spawns for F2P players include:

  • Steel arrows: Spawn in the Varrock archery shop area and near the Lumbridge combat tutor. Each steel arrow sells for about 15-25 GP. Grabbing a full inventory (28) every few minutes adds up over time.
  • Wine of Zamorak: Spawns at the Chaos Altar north of Varrock. Each wine sells for approximately 500-700 GP on the Grand Exchange. You need to avoid the level 32 monks, but at level 3, you can simply grab the wine and teleport out using the Varrock teleport (requires 25 Magic — but you can use home teleport instead and walk back).
  • Bucket of sand: Spawns in various locations including the Entrana church and Yanille. Each bucket sells for about 60-80 GP in 2026.
  • Free sample items: The Grand Exchange has several NPCs that give free sample items — use them to collect and sell materials.

Profit estimate: Item spawn collecting can generate approximately 10-30K gold per hour, depending on how efficient you are at running between spawn points and banking. It's not a fortune, but it's something to do while you plan your next move.

Method 2: Chicken Killing for Feathers

Chickens are scattered across the F2P map, with the most popular farming spots at the Lumbridge chicken coop and the farm north of Port Sarim. Chickens are only level 1, meaning even a level 3 account can kill them without any gear. They drop raw chicken, bones, and most importantly, feathers.

In 2026, feathers sell for approximately 3-5 GP each on the Grand Exchange. While that sounds low, chickens drop 5-15 feathers per kill, and you can kill them incredibly fast even with bare fists. At level 3, you can generate roughly 20-40K gold per hour from feathers alone, plus a small amount from raw chicken and bones.

Pro Tip: The Lumbridge chicken coop is the most popular spot and often crowded. Try the chickens at the farm north-east of Port Sarim — they're less contested and only a short walk from a bank deposit box at Port Sarim docks. In 2026, Jagex added a deposit box near the chicken farm, making this method even more efficient than before.

Method 3: Mining Copper and Tin Ore

Mining requires zero stats — just a pickaxe, which you can get for free from the mining tutor south of Lumbridge. Copper and tin ore can be mined at level 1 Mining, and both are always in demand on the Grand Exchange because players need them for bronze bar smithing (a common early-game item).

In 2026, copper ore sells for approximately 10-15 GP each and tin ore for about 8-12 GP each. The Varrock east mine is the classic spot, and with close banking at the Varrock east bank, you can fill an inventory (28 ores) in about 2-3 minutes. At a realistic pace for a new player, expect roughly 5-10K gold per hour.

Honest assessment: Copper and tin mining is slow money. The real value here is that you're also training your Mining skill, which opens up better money-making methods as you level up. Think of this as an investment in your account's future rather than a primary income source.

Method 4: Cowhide Collection and Tanning

I've mentioned cowhides in other guides, and they deserve a spot here too because you can kill cows with zero combat stats. The Lumbridge cow field is right next to a bank deposit box (added in 2025 and still one of the best QoL updates for new players). Each cowhide sells for about 80-120 GP on the Grand Exchange in 2026.

At level 3, your kill speed will be slow — maybe 15-20 kills per hour. With 1 cowhide per kill on average, that's about 1.5-2.5K gold per hour just from cowhides. However, as you naturally gain combat levels from fighting cows, your kill speed increases dramatically. By level 10-15, you can easily hit 20-40K gold per hour.

Bonus tip: Pick up raw meat and bones too. Raw meat sells for a small amount, and bones can be buried for Prayer XP (slow, but free). Some players also use bones at the Chaos Altar in the Wilderness for Prayer XP, but that's risky for a level 3 account.

Method 5: Exploring and Collecting Nature Runes from Spawns

Nature runes occasionally spawn on the ground in the Wilderness (in areas like the Hobgoblin mine and near the Chaos Altar). While entering the Wilderness at level 3 is risky, nature runes sell for 80-100 GP each in 2026, making even a small collection worthwhile. I only recommend this if you're willing to risk being killed by other players, but since you have nothing valuable to lose at level 3, the risk-reward is actually decent.

Method 6: Completing F2P Quests for Gold Rewards

Several F2P quests reward you with gold, items, or access to profitable activities. Even at level 3, you can complete many quests. The gold rewards aren't huge, but they add up:

  • Cook's Assistant: Rewards 300 gold coins
  • Sheep Shearer: Rewards 60 gold coins (plus Crafting XP)
  • Rune Mysteries: Unlocks access to the Rune Essence mine
  • Imp Catcher: Rewards an amulet of accuracy (sells for 150-300 GP)
  • Prince Ali Rescue: Rewards 700 gold coins
  • The Knight's Sword: Rewards Smithing XP (saves money on training)

In total, completing all easy F2P quests nets you around 1,000-2,000 gold plus useful items and XP. More importantly, quests unlock access to areas and activities that are far more profitable than anything available at level 3.

Method 7: Rune Essence Mining

Completing the "Rune Mysteries" quest (no stat requirements) gives you access to rune essence mining. You teleport to the essence mine via Aubury in Varrock or Sedridor in the Wizards' Tower. Mining rune essence requires level 1 Mining and is extremely AFK-friendly. In 2026, rune essence sells for approximately 10-15 GP each.

At level 1, you can mine roughly 500-700 essence per hour, generating about 5-10K gold per hour. As your Mining level increases, your mining speed improves slightly, but rune essence is always a low-level rock. The advantage here is that you're also training Mining toward level 15 (for iron ore) or level 30 (for coal), which opens up much better money-making methods.

What I wish I knew: In 2026, you can sell rune essence to the Varrock Rune Shop for slightly more than the Grand Exchange price during certain market conditions. Always check both prices before deciding where to sell. Also, if you're planning to train Runecrafting later, stockpile your rune essence — buying it back later at a markup is a common beginner mistake.

Method 8: Flax Picking in Seers' Village

While Seers' Village is technically a members' area, flax picking requires zero skills. If you're on a free-to-play account, this method isn't available. However, if you have membership (even on a bond-bought new account), flax picking is one of the absolute best zero-stat money makers in the entire game.

Flax fields in Seers' Village respawn very quickly. Each flax sells for about 3-5 GP on the Grand Exchange in 2026 — but the real money comes from spinning flax into bowstrings (requires level 1 Crafting and a spinning wheel in Seers' Village or Lumbridge Castle). Bowstrings sell for approximately 100-150 GP each in 2026.

Picking and spinning bowstrings can generate 80-120K gold per hour even at level 1 Crafting. This is the single most profitable zero-stat method available to members, and I strongly recommend doing it first if you have membership.

The Fastest Path from Zero to Your First 100K

If your goal is to earn your first 100K gold as quickly as possible on a fresh F2P account, here's the optimal route I recommend in 2026:

  1. Step 1: Complete "Rune Mysteries" quest (~5 minutes). Mine rune essence until you have about 10-15K gold worth (~1-2 hours).
  2. Step 2: Switch to chicken killing at the Port Sarim farm. Collect feathers until you have 30-40K total gold (~1-2 hours).
  3. Step 3: Move to cow killing at Lumbridge. You'll naturally gain combat levels, and cowhide collection will accelerate your earnings (~2-3 hours to reach 100K total).
  4. Step 4: Complete easy F2P quests for bonus gold and item rewards (~1 hour).

Total time: approximately 5-8 hours of gameplay to reach 100K gold from scratch. That's not bad for a completely fresh account with no stats, no gear, and no experience.

2026-Specific Tips for New Players

A few things have changed in 2026 that specifically help brand new F2P players make money:

  • New Lumbridge bank deposit box: Added near the cow field, making cowhide farming significantly faster for new players.
  • Improved tutorial: The 2026 updated tutorial now awards 1,000 gold coins upon completion, giving you a small head start.
  • GE search improvements: You can now search for items and see their price history directly from the Grand Exchange interface without needing a separate website.
  • Stackable noted drops: Certain low-level monsters now drop noted items, reducing the number of banking trips needed.

Conclusion

Making money on a brand new OSRS F2P account with no stats in 2026 is entirely doable — it just requires patience and a willingness to grind low-value activities. The best zero-stat methods are chicken feather collecting, rune essence mining, and cowhide farming, with flax picking being the ultimate choice if you have membership. Your first 100K might take 5-8 hours of focused effort, but once you have that starting capital and some basic levels, the game opens up dramatically with much more profitable opportunities.
