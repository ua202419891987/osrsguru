OSRS Chambers of Xeric Loot Profit Guide – 2026

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, PvM, Raids

The Chambers of Xeric (commonly called "CoX" or "Raids 1") is the premier team bossing content in Old School RuneScape, and in 2026 it remains one of the most profitable PvM activities in the game. Understanding Chambers of Xeric loot profit requires knowing how the unique point-based loot system works, what items drop and their current market values, and which team sizes and raid configurations give the best return on your time investment. This guide covers everything you need to know.

How CoX Loot Works

Unlike most boss content in OSRS, the Chambers of Xeric uses a point-based loot system. When you complete a raid, you earn personal points based on your performance across several rooms (combat rooms, puzzle rooms, and the Great Olm boss fight). Points determine your share of the loot chest at the end. Higher personal points mean more items from the chest and a higher chance of receiving unique drops.

Point Factors

Your personal points are affected by:

  • Damage dealt: More damage = more points, especially during the Great Olm fight
  • Puzzle completion: Solving puzzles quickly grants bonus points
  • Team size: Smaller teams give more points per player due to less point splitting
  • Survival: Dying during the raid reduces your point total
  • Resources consumed: Using your own supplies (rather than team-shared) slightly increases points
  • Raid level: Higher raid levels (more rooms) grant more total points

Valuable Drops and Their 2026 Prices

The Chambers of Xeric has one of the most lucrative drop tables in the game. Here are the key unique drops and their approximate 2026 Grand Exchange values:

  • Draconic visage: 3,000,000-5,000,000 GP (dropped from Great Olm)
  • Twisted bow: 800,000,000-1,200,000,000 GP (the ultimate ranged weapon)
  • Elder maul: 15,000,000-25,000,000 GP
  • Dragon claws: 25,000,000-40,000,000 GP
  • Ancestral hat: 5,000,000-8,000,000 GP
  • Ancestral robe top: 8,000,000-12,000,000 GP
  • Ancestral robe bottom: 6,000,000-10,000,000 GP
  • Arcane prayer scroll: 2,000,000-4,000,000 GP
  • Dragon hunter crossbow: 8,000,000-12,000,000 GP
  • Dinhs bulwark: 6,000,000-10,000,000 GP
  • Tumeken's shadow: 300,000,000-500,000,000 GP (from Challenge Mode)

Non-unique drops from the loot chest include runes, food, potions, and skilling supplies. While individually low-value, the sheer quantity of non-unique drops from a full raid can add 50K-200K to your total per raid.

Team Size and Profit Analysis

Solo CoX (1 Player)

Solo Chambers of Xeric is the most skill-intensive and highest-profit-per-kill way to raid. In a solo raid, you keep 100% of the loot. The average solo raid takes 30-60 minutes depending on skill and gear. The average loot value per solo raid in 2026 is approximately 500K-2M gold, with occasional unique drops worth tens or hundreds of millions.

Profit per hour: 500K-3M gold per hour on average, but with massive variance. A solo player who goes 50 raids without a unique drop might average only 500K/hr, while someone who gets a twisted bow in a solo raid just made 1B+ GP in 45 minutes.

Requirements: Solo CoX requires excellent gear (typically Bandos, Void, or better), 90+ combat stats, and significant raiding experience. This is not a beginner method.

Small Teams (2-4 Players)

Small teams are the sweet spot for most experienced raiders. With 2-4 players, each player gets a larger share of the loot compared to larger teams, and raids are typically faster than solo (20-40 minutes per raid). Average loot per player per raid is approximately 300K-1.5M gold.

Profit per hour: Approximately 500K-3M gold per hour per player. Small teams benefit from faster clear times and the ability to split roles effectively (one person tanks, one does damage, one solves puzzles).

Large Teams (5-8 Players)

Large teams clear raids fastest (15-25 minutes per raid) but each player gets a smaller share of the loot. Average loot per player per raid is approximately 100K-500K gold.

Profit per hour: Approximately 300K-1.2M gold per hour per player. Large teams are ideal for learning raids, but the profit per person is lower than small teams or solo.

Cost of Raiding

It's important to account for the cost of supplies when calculating your CoX profit:

  • Food: 50-200K per raid (scales with team size and deaths)
  • Potions: 30-100K per raid (prayer, stamina, antifire)
  • Equipment degradation: Minimal in OSRS, but bring backup gear in case of death
  • Death costs: If you die, reclaiming items from Death's office costs a small fee

Net profit = Total loot value - Supply costs. Always track your expenses to get an accurate picture of your actual profit.

Challenge Mode (CM) CoX

Challenge Mode is a harder version of CoX with modified mechanics and exclusive drops. The most notable CM drop is Tumeken's shadow, one of the most valuable items in the game. CM requires a minimum team size of 3 and significantly better gear and coordination.

In 2026, CM CoX average loot per player is approximately 1-5M gold per raid, with Tumeken's shadow drops pushing average profit significantly higher when they occur. CM raids take 40-90 minutes depending on team skill.

Tips for Maximizing Profit

  • Learn to solo: Solo CoX has the highest profit potential per hour. Invest time in learning solo mechanics — YouTube guides and practice raids are invaluable.
  • Use Discord LFG servers: Finding consistent raid teams through Discord is faster and more reliable than using the in-game Party Finder.
  • Don't die: Deaths reduce your point total significantly. Play conservatively and focus on survival over damage output.
  • Bring your own supplies: Using your own food and potions instead of relying on shared supplies gives a small but meaningful point boost.
  • Raid level matters: Higher raid levels (more rooms before Great Olm) give more total points and better loot. A full raid (level 4-5) is more profitable than a short raid (level 1-2).
  • Track your raids: Use a raid tracker tool to monitor your average loot value, unique drop rate, and profit per hour over time.

What I Wish I Knew Starting CoX

  • Learning CoX is frustrating at first. Your first 20-30 raids will be messy and unprofitable. That's normal — it's an investment in future earnings.
  • The Olm fight mechanics (especially the claw attacks and magic/ranged portals) take practice to learn. Watch YouTube guides before attempting.
  • Don't waste points on the cheap loot options. Save up for purple items (uniques) — the point cost is higher but the potential payoff is massive.
  • CoX prices fluctuate with game updates. In 2026, the twisted bow remains the most valuable unique, but watch for changes that might shift demand to other items.

Conclusion

The Chambers of Xeric is one of the most rewarding PvM activities in OSRS, offering average profits of 500K-3M gold per hour for experienced players. Solo raids offer the highest potential returns, while small teams (2-4 players) provide the best balance of speed and profit. The key to maximizing your CoX earnings is learning the mechanics thoroughly, avoiding deaths, and gradually moving toward solo or small-team content. The unique drop system means there's always the chance of a massive payout, making every raid exciting. Invest the time to learn CoX, and it will pay dividends for the rest of your OSRS career.
