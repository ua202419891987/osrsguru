OSRS Wintertodt Money Making Per Hour – 2026 Breakdown

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Wintertodt, Firemaking

Wintertodt has been one of the most popular money-making activities in Old School RuneScape since its release, and in 2026, it remains a top choice for players looking to earn gold while training Firemaking. If you're wondering about Wintertodt money making per hour, the answer depends heavily on your Firemaking level, your point total, and how efficiently you play the activity. This guide breaks down exactly what you can expect to earn at different levels and point brackets.

What is Wintertodt?

Wintertodt is a boss fight located in the Great Kourend region, accessible via the Games Necklace teleport to "Wintertodt Camp." The Wintertodt is a fire-based entity that you don't fight directly — instead, you maintain a massive brazier by feeding it bruma roots (chopped from nearby trees), fletching the roots into kindling, repairing the brazier when it breaks, and healing the pyromancers when they get damaged.

After each successful subdue (or failed attempt), you earn points based on your performance. These points can be spent on supply crates, which contain the actual loot that makes Wintertodt profitable.

Requirements and Setup

Minimum Requirements

  • 50 Firemaking (recommended 60+ for better success rates)
  • Completion of the "Client of Kourend" quest (for easier travel)
  • A hammer (to repair the brazier)
  • A knife (to fletch bruma roots into kindling)
  • Warm clothing (protection against cold damage)

Recommended Gear (2026)

In 2026, the warm clothing requirement is slightly more lenient than in previous years. You need at least two pieces of warm clothing to avoid taking constant damage. Good options include:

  • Staff of fire or fire battlestaff (provides warm bonus and has some utility)
  • Bruma torch (obtained from Wintertodt itself — warm bonus)
  • Pyromancer outfit pieces (warm bonus, purchased with Wintertodt points)
  • Adventurer's helm or any warm-colored headpiece

Understanding Points and Supply Crates

The money from Wintertodt comes almost entirely from supply crates. Each supply crate costs 500 points to purchase from the supply crate table near the brazier. The loot inside varies based on the type of crate:

  • Simple supply crate: Costs 500 points. Contains basic loot — logs, herbs, seeds, and occasional ore.
  • Sample supply crate: Costs 1,000 points. Better loot table with improved seed drops.
  • Medium supply crate: Costs 2,500 points. Good chance for valuable seeds and resources.
  • Large supply crate: Costs 5,000 points. Best loot table, including rare seeds and items.
  • Master supply crate: Costs 10,000 points. Highest chance for extremely valuable drops.

2026 update: Jagex added a new "Grand supply crate" option for players with over 1,000 total Wintertodt subdues. This crate costs 20,000 points but has a significantly improved rare drop table, including guaranteed magic seed fragments for every 10 crates opened.

Profit Per Hour Breakdown

Your hourly profit from Wintertodt depends on several factors:

At Level 50 Firemaking

At level 50, your brazier lighting success rate is approximately 30%. You'll spend more time relighting the brazier than actually feeding it, which reduces your points per hour. Expect to earn roughly 100-150 points per hour, which translates to about 50-100K gold per hour from supply crates (simple crates only at this point).

At Level 60 Firemaking

Level 60 is where Wintertodt starts becoming genuinely profitable. Your success rate jumps to about 50%, and you can earn roughly 200-300 points per hour. This allows you to open simple or sample crates, generating approximately 150-250K gold per hour.

At Level 75 Firemaking

At level 75+, your success rate approaches 70-80%. You can earn 400-600 points per hour, enabling medium and even large crates. Profit ranges from 250-400K gold per hour, with occasional spikes when you get valuable seed drops like magic seeds (50K-150K each) or snapdragon seeds (30K-60K each).

At Level 90+ Firemaking

Max efficiency at level 90+ Firemaking allows you to earn 700-1,000 points per hour. At this rate, you can open large and master supply crates consistently, generating approximately 350-600K gold per hour on average. The variance is high because rare drops are, well, rare — but over many hours, this is your expected rate.

At Level 99 Firemaking (Max Efficiency)

With the Firemaking cape equipped (+5 invisible FM level boost) and optimal play, a level 99 firemaker can earn approximately 1,000-1,200 points per hour. At this rate, you're looking at 400-700K gold per hour from Wintertodt crates. If you have the new Grand supply crate unlocked (1,000+ subdues), the hourly rate can spike to 500K-1M gold per hour during lucky runs with rare seed drops.

The Loot Table: What Makes Wintertodt Valuable

The bulk of Wintertodt's value comes from seeds. Here are the most valuable drops from supply crates in 2026:

  • Magic seed: 50,000-150,000 GP
  • Yew seed: 20,000-40,000 GP
  • Palm tree seed: 30,000-60,000 GP
  • Spirit seed: 200,000-400,000 GP (extremely rare)
  • Dragonfruit tree seed: 100,000-200,000 GP (rare)
  • Snape grass seed: 5,000-15,000 GP
  • Watermelon seed: 1,000-3,000 GP
  • Torstol seed: 40,000-80,000 GP
  • Ranarr seed: 30,000-60,000 GP
  • Snapdragon seed: 30,000-60,000 GP

In addition to seeds, supply crates contain logs (which can be burned for more FM XP or sold), ores, herbs, and occasionally the Phoenix pet. The pet itself doesn't have monetary value, but it's a prestigious cosmetic that many players grind Wintertodt specifically to obtain.

Pro Tips for Maximizing Profit

  • Always fletch your roots: Fletching bruma roots into kindling gives bonus points per subdue. A full inventory of fletched kindling can significantly increase your point earnings.
  • Stay in the safe zone: The Wintertodt's attacks can damage you. Stay behind the broken wall sections where you're protected from snowfall damage.
  • Don't waste food: If you're low on HP, eat at the camp bank. Don't waste inventory space on food — you need every slot for roots and kindling.
  • World hop for less competition: Wintertodt worlds fill up quickly, especially during peak hours. Find a world with 20-30 players for optimal activity without too much competition for roots.
  • Save points for larger crates: Instead of opening many simple crates, save your points for large or master crates. The improved loot table per point spent is significantly better.
  • Use the "hot vs cold" mechanic: In 2026, the brazier temperature indicator has been made more visible. Keep the brazier in the "warm" zone (not too hot, not too cold) for maximum point gain per subdue.

Wintertodt vs. Other Money Making Methods

How does Wintertodt compare to other AFK money-making methods in 2026?

  • vs. Fishing (sharks): Wintertodt at level 75+ (250-400K/hr) outperforms shark fishing (85-115K/hr) by a significant margin. However, Wintertodt requires more active attention.
  • vs. Woodcutting (magic trees): Wintertodt at level 75+ is roughly 2-3x more profitable than magic tree woodcutting (100-140K/hr).
  • vs. Herbiboar: Herbiboar at level 80+ (100-400K/hr) is comparable to Wintertodt, but requires 80 Hunter vs. 50 Firemaking. Herbiboar is more interactive while Wintertodt is more AFK.

Conclusion

Wintertodt remains one of the best semi-AFK money-making methods in OSRS in 2026. At level 50 Firemaking, expect roughly 100-200K gold per hour. At level 75+, profits increase to 250-400K per hour, and at level 99 with optimal play and high point savings for master crates, you can hit 500K-700K per hour on average. The combination of Firemaking XP, valuable seed drops, and the low attention requirement makes Wintertodt an excellent activity for players who want to earn gold while watching shows, listening to podcasts, or multitasking. Start at level 50, be patient with your early crates, and watch your profits grow as your Firemaking level increases.
