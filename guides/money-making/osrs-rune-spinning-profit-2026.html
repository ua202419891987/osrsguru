OSRS How to Rune Spinning Profit 2026 – Complete Guide

Published: May 2026 | Category: Money Making, Crafting, Spinning

Spinning is one of the most underrated money-making methods in Old School RuneScape. Whether you're spinning flax into bowstrings, wool into balls of wool, or hair into ropes, the spinning wheel is a consistent gold generator. In 2026, rune spinning profit remains stable and accessible, especially for low-level accounts. This guide covers every spinning method in the game, with updated profit calculations and the best locations for maximum efficiency.

Understanding Spinning in OSRS

Spinning is a Crafting sub-skill that uses spinning wheels located throughout Gielinor. You place raw materials (flax, wool, tree roots, etc.) into the wheel and spin them into usable products. The resulting items have trade value on the Grand Exchange, making spinning a viable money-making method. Most spinning activities require only level 1 Crafting, making them accessible to everyone.

Method 1: Flax to Bowstrings (The Gold Standard)

I've covered bowstring spinning in other guides, but it deserves a deep dive here because it's the most profitable spinning method in OSRS. Flax costs approximately 3-5 GP each on the Grand Exchange in 2026, and bowstrings sell for 100-150 GP each. That's a profit margin of 95-147 GP per bowstring.

Spinning Speed

Spinning one flax takes approximately 2.5-3 seconds. With banking time included, you can spin roughly 1,300-1,500 bowstrings per hour. This is consistent across all Crafting levels because the spinning speed doesn't increase with your level (unlike many other Crafting activities).

Profit Calculation

At 1,400 bowstrings per hour with a 120 GP margin per string:

  • Flax cost: 1,400 x 4 GP = 5,600 GP
  • Revenue: 1,400 x 124 GP = 173,600 GP
  • Net profit: ~168,000 GP per hour

In 2026, bowstring prices have been stable at 100-150 GP, making this one of the most reliable money-making methods in the game. The 1% GE tax on selling reduces your profit by approximately 1,200-1,500 GP per hour, which is negligible.

Best Spinning Wheel Locations

  • Seers' Village (top recommendation): The flax field is directly south of the bank. A spinning wheel is located on the second floor of the building just south of the flax field. Round trip: 10-15 seconds.
  • Lumbridge Castle: Spinning wheel on the second floor. Bank on the top floor. Slightly longer round trip but decent alternative.
  • Neitiznot: After "Fremennik Trials," spin at the Neitiznot wheel. Good when Seers' Village is crowded.
  • Prifddinas (maxed players): If you've completed "Song of the Elves," the Prifddinas loom area has everything you need in close proximity.

Method 2: Wool to Balls of Wool

Spinning wool into balls of wool is a classic low-level method. Wool is obtained by shearing sheep (level 1 Crafting to spin). In 2026, wool sells for about 1-3 GP each, and balls of wool sell for 10-20 GP each. The profit per ball is roughly 7-17 GP.

At a spinning speed of about 1,000-1,200 balls per hour, you're looking at approximately 7-20K gold per hour. The profit is low, but this method requires zero starting capital — you shear your own wool for free.

Best location: The Lumbridge spinning wheel (ground floor of the castle) is close to sheep pens. Shear, spin, bank, repeat.

Method 3: Tree Roots to Crossbow Strings

When you chop a tree and clear it (click on the stump), there's a chance to receive tree roots. These roots can be spun into crossbow strings on a spinning wheel. In 2026, crossbow strings sell for approximately 50-100 GP each, and the tree roots themselves have minimal value.

The most profitable root types are:

  • Magic roots (75 Woodcutting): Magic root crossbow strings sell for about 300-500 GP each. At 2-4 magic roots per tree, the profit per tree clearing can be 600-2,000 GP.
  • Yew roots (60 Woodcutting): Yew root crossbow strings sell for about 100-200 GP each.
  • Maple roots (45 Woodcutting): Maple root crossbow strings sell for about 50-100 GP each.

Profit: Because roots are a byproduct of woodcutting rather than a primary resource, this method doesn't have a clean "per hour" calculation. Factor the value of roots into your overall woodcutting profit. For magic tree woodcutters, crossbow strings add roughly 20-40K gold per hour in additional profit.

Method 4: Sinew to Crossbow String

Sinew is obtained from cooking raw beef (from killing cows). When cooked on a range (not a fire), raw beef has a chance to produce sinew instead of cooked meat. Sinew can be spun into crossbow strings on a spinning wheel at level 10 Crafting.

In 2026, crossbow strings made from sinew are the same as those made from tree roots — there's no difference in the final product. The advantage of sinew is that you obtain it passively while training combat and cooking. The disadvantage is that the sinew drop rate is low (approximately 1 in 3-4 raw beef cooked), making it an unreliable primary income source.

Method 5: Jungle Spiders to Rope

Jungle spider silk can be obtained from killing jungle spiders on Karamja (near the Tai Bwo Wannai village). The silk is then spun into rope on a spinning wheel. Rope is used in several quests and construction projects. In 2026, rope sells for approximately 15-25 GP each on the Grand Exchange.

This method is niche and not particularly profitable (~10-15K per hour), but it's worth knowing about if you're already training combat in the Karamja area.

2026 Updates Affecting Spinning

Several 2026 changes affect spinning profitability:

  • Bowstring demand increased: The 2026 Fletching meta has shifted toward broad bolts and dragon arrows, both of which use more bowstrings. Demand for bowstrings has increased by roughly 10-15% compared to 2025.
  • New spinning wheel locations: Jagex added a spinning wheel in the new Farming Guild extension, making it slightly more convenient for players who frequent that area.
  • Flax field respawn speed: The Seers' Village flax field respawn timer has been slightly reduced in 2026, benefiting players who pick their own flax rather than buying it.

Pro Tips for Maximizing Spinning Profit

  • Buy flax, don't pick it: Picking flax yourself generates maybe 200-300 per hour worth of free materials. Buying flax on the GE lets you spin 1,400 per hour for 5-7K GP in material costs. The time saved is worth 100K+ per hour in opportunity cost.
  • Use the "make X" interface: When spinning, always use "make X" and set the quantity to 28 (a full inventory). This saves one click per inventory, adding up to 50+ extra bowstrings per hour.
  • Minimize banking distance: At Seers' Village, optimize your movement path between the flax source (if picking), spinning wheel, and bank. Every second saved is an extra bowstring per hour.
  • Check prices before committing: Bowstring prices fluctuate between 80 and 180 GP. If the price drops below 80 GP, the margin becomes too thin — switch to another activity and come back when prices recover.
  • Combine with Fletching: Once you have bowstrings, you can attach them to unstrung bows for Fletching XP and sell the completed bows. This adds even more value to your spinning session.

Profit Summary

  • Bowstrings: 110-200K gp/hr (best method, level 1)
  • Balls of wool: 7-20K gp/hr (free materials, level 1)
  • Crossbow strings (magic roots): 20-40K gp/hr (bonus to woodcutting, level 75 WC)
  • Sinew crossbow strings: 5-15K gp/hr (passive income from combat, level 10)
  • Rope from spider silk: 10-15K gp/hr (niche method)

Conclusion

Spinning is one of the most accessible and consistently profitable skills in OSRS. Bowstring spinning at level 1 Crafting stands out as the best method, generating 110-200K gold per hour with minimal requirements. The method is AFK-friendly, requires no quests or gear, and has been profitable consistently for years. In 2026, increased bowstring demand from the shifting Fletching meta has only made this method better. Start spinning at Seers' Village, buy your flax in bulk, and enjoy steady gold income while you watch your favorite content.
