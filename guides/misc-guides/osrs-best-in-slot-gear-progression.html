OSRS Best in Slot Gear Progression 2026 — Every Style, Every Budget

Gear progression in OSRS is a puzzle. You've got 50M in the bank and you're staring at a dozen possible upgrades — do you buy Bandos, or a Torture amulet, or save for a Rapier? Buy the wrong upgrade and you've just wasted millions on a marginal DPS increase when a cheaper item would have delivered 3x the value. This guide maps out the entire gear progression path for Melee, Ranged, and Magic at three budget tiers — Budget (under 20M), Mid-Game (20-100M), and Endgame (100M+) — with specific gear grids showing exactly what to buy in every slot at every stage. Use this as your upgrade roadmap and never waste GP on the wrong item again.

Melee Gear Progression

Melee is the backbone of OSRS combat. It's the style you'll use for Slayer, most bossing, and general-purpose PvM. The progression is well-defined and rewards specific milestone upgrades at key price points.

The budget melee setup centers on the Fighter Torso + Obsidian Legs combination, which provides the best strength bonus per GP in the game. The Torso is free from Barbarian Assault (takes roughly 4-6 hours to earn with a decent team) and provides +4 strength bonus — equal to Bandos Chestplate at 0 GP cost. The Neitiznot Helm is a quest reward from The Fremennik Isles and offers +3 strength and +3 prayer — better than a Berserker Helm and entirely free. The Fire Cape from the Fight Caves is your first major "free" upgrade milestone. At 75 Ranged with a blowpipe, the Fight Caves take about 60-90 minutes. If you don't have a Fire Cape, this should be your first priority — it provides +4 strength and +1 prayer over an Ardougne Cloak.

Budget Upgrade Path: 1) Berserker Ring (i) — best strength-per-GP ring in the game, 2) Amulet of Fury — all-around upgrade with prayer, defence, and accuracy, 3) Abyssal Whip — your main weapon upgrade from the Dragon Scimitar, 4) Obsidian Platelegs — +1 strength over Rune/Dragon legs. Skip buying a Dragon Platebody — it's 1.8M for no strength bonus when the Fighter Torso is free.

In the mid-game, your priority is accumulating strength bonus. The Osmumten's Fang from Tombs of Amascut is the single best value upgrade in this bracket — at ~15M, it outperforms the Rapier (~110M) against high-defence targets because of its passive double-accuracy roll. The Torture amulet replaces the Fury as your primary melee amulet (keep the Fury for hybrid content like raids where you switch styles). The Neitiznot Faceguard is crafted from a Basilisk Jaw (1/1,000 from Basilisk Knights at 60 Slayer) and a Neitiznot Helm — it provides +6 strength, making it the second-best melee helmet in the game after the Torva Full Helm.

The Avernic Defender upgrade from the Dragon Defender is a permanent account upgrade (untradeable once used) that provides +2 accuracy and +2 strength. It's typically the first TOB purchase for most accounts after the Rapier. Ferocious Gloves replace Barrows Gloves for melee — they give +2 more strength and melee accuracy but lack the ranged and magic bonuses, so they're only worn when meleeing.

Torva armor from Nex is the final upgrade tier for melee. Each piece provides +2 strength over the Bandos equivalent and significantly more defence. However, upgrading from Bandos to Torva costs roughly 1.1B GP for a total of +6 strength bonus (+2 per piece). This is the worst GP-to-strength ratio in the game, and Torva should be your absolute last melee upgrade — buy everything else (Scythe, Ultor Ring, Infernal Cape, Voidwaker) first. The Scythe of Vitur from Theatre of Blood is the BIS melee weapon against any NPC with a size of 3x3 or larger, dealing up to 84 damage per hit (three hitsplats). Against smaller targets, the Rapier or Fang are better.

Ranged Gear Progression

Ranged is the dominant combat style for endgame PvM in OSRS. The Bow of Faerdhinen (Bowfa) and Toxic Blowpipe cover nearly every boss in the game, and Masori armor with the Twisted Bow pushes ranged DPS to levels that melee can't match against most content.

The Toxic Blowpipe dominates the budget ranged setup. At 75 Ranged with amethyst darts, it delivers the highest DPS of any ranged weapon in the game against low to medium defence targets. The Blessed D'hide set (Saradomin, Guthix, or Zamorak) is preferred over Black D'hide because it provides +1 prayer bonus per piece — a small but meaningful upgrade for bossing. The Magic Shortbow (i) is your budget alternative for content where the blowpipe's short range is a liability (Fight Caves, some bosses). Imbue it via the PvP Arena or LMS for 50% spec cost (2x Dragon Darts specs for 60+ damage).

The Bowfa + Crystal Armor setup is the single best PvM investment in OSRS. The Bowfa has no ammo cost (unlike the blowpipe which burns through scales and darts), has significantly longer range than the blowpipe, and with the full Crystal Armor set, gains 15% accuracy and 7.5% damage — bringing it close to Twisted Bow DPS against non-magic-weak targets. This setup alone unlocks efficient soloing at God Wars Dungeon (Zilyana, Bandos, Kree'arra), Chambers of Xeric, and Tombs of Amascut. The full set (Bowfa + 3 Crystal Armor pieces + 6 Crystal Armor Seeds to charge) costs approximately 200M total and is worth every GP before upgrading to Masori or a Twisted Bow.

Crystal Armor Corruption Warning: Crystal armor pieces degrade and must be recharged with Crystal Shards. The helm, body, and legs each consume 1 shard per 100 hits taken. At corrupted Gauntlet, you earn shards naturally. But if you use Crystal Armor extensively outside Prifddinas content, you'll need to farm shards via Zalcano, Agility courses in Prif, or skilling. Corrupting each piece (costs extra shards but makes it never degrade) is an investment of roughly 400-500 Crystal Shards per piece — worth it if you plan to use Bowfa for hundreds of hours. Don't corrupt prematurely; wait until you're sure Bowfa is a long-term part of your setup.

The Twisted Bow is the single most powerful item in OSRS — its passive scales damage with the target's Magic level, making it absurdly strong against bosses with high Magic (Zulrah, Chambers of Xeric, Inferno, Muspah). The Masori armor set from Tombs of Amascut provides the best ranged accuracy in the game. The fortified (f) variant adds defence comparable to Armadyl while maintaining the offensive bonuses. Zaryte Vambraces replace Barrows Gloves for ranged with +2 ranged strength and accuracy. The Venator Ring is the ranged equivalent of the Ultor Ring, providing +2 ranged strength.

Magic Gear Progression

Magic is the most situational combat style in OSRS. Its primary role is not DPS but utility — freezing, teleporting, and exploiting specific boss weaknesses where magic is the only viable style (Kraken, Nightmare, Olm Mage Hand). The progression focuses on maximizing magic damage bonus, which is the rarest of the three combat bonuses.

The Occult Necklace is the single most broken item in OSRS — it provides a 10% magic damage bonus for 500K GP. For comparison, the Amulet of Torture provides +2 strength (roughly 2-4% damage) for 15M. Buy the Occult Necklace as soon as you hit 70 Magic. It's the best value magic upgrade in the game by an enormous margin. The Trident of the Swamp (75 Magic, 75 Attack to wield) is your main magic weapon for bossing. It has a built-in spell that scales with your Magic level, hitting up to 31 at 99 Magic (34 with an Occult Necklace). The charge cost (~500 GP per cast) is trivial compared to the DPS increase over casting spells manually. The Mage Arena 2 Cape (god cape imbued from Mage Arena 2 at 75 Magic) provides 2% magic damage bonus and is entirely free — just requires killing the three MA2 bosses, which is straightforward with ice barrage or a blowpipe.

In the mid-game, magic upgrades are about stacking percentage-based damage bonuses. The Tormented Bracelet provides 5% magic damage — the second-biggest single-slot magic damage increase after the Occult. The Elidinis' Ward (fortified with an Arcane Sigil from Corporeal Beast) provides 3% magic damage and better accuracy than the Arcane Spirit Shield. The Sanguinesti Staff (82 Magic) is a direct upgrade to the Trident of the Swamp — it hits one max hit higher, has a passive that heals the wielder for 1/6 of damage dealt (up to 6 HP per hit), and has a venom chance on hit. The self-healing makes it a massive quality-of-life upgrade for sustained PvM, particularly at raids and bosses where you take unavoidable chip damage. However, it costs roughly 80M and charges with blood runes — factor in the 800 GP per cast operating cost.

Ancestral Robes from Chambers of Xeric are the BIS magic armor. Each piece provides 2% magic damage bonus (total 6% for the set), stacking with the Occult and Tormented Bracelet to reach a base of 21% magic damage before weapon bonuses. The Tumeken's Shadow (Shadow) from Tombs of Amascut is the magic equivalent of the Twisted Bow — it triples the magic attack and damage bonuses from your worn equipment, making the total magic damage bonus reach absurd levels (21% base bonus multiplied by 3 = 63% effective damage increase). With a Shadow, magic becomes viable DPS against anything that isn't explicitly magic-immune. The Virtus Robes from the DT2 bosses (Whisperer, Duke, Vardorvis, Leviathan) serve as a bridge between Ahrim's and Ancestral, providing 1% magic damage per piece at roughly half the price.

Ironman Gear Progression Path

Ironmen can't buy gear, so the progression path is fundamentally different. The general route through the game's content is:

  1. Early Game: Complete Recipe for Disaster for Barrows Gloves. Grind Fight Caves for Fire Cape at 75 Ranged with Rune Crossbow + Ruby Bolts (e) and Black D'hide (crafted). Complete Dragon Slayer 2 for Ava's Assembler. Grind the Corrupted Gauntlet for Bowfa + Crystal Armor Seeds. This single grind replaces dozens of individual boss grinds because Bowfa unlocks efficient GWD soloing.
  2. Mid Game (Barrows & GWD): After Bowfa, camp Barrows for Ahrim's set and Karil's set (for magic defence). Camp GWD with Bowfa: Zilyana for Armadyl Crossbow (not essential if you have Bowfa), Bandos for Bandos armor, Kree'arra for Armadyl armor (Masori fortification component). Zamorak for the Staff of the Dead (for Muspah and general use) and the Zamorakian Hasta (for Corp and dragons).
  3. Raids Phase: With Bandos, Ahrim's, Bowfa, and a Trident of the Swamp, you can learn Chambers of Xeric for Rigour, Augury, and Ancestral pieces. Then Theatre of Blood for the Rapier, Sang Staff, Avernic, and eventually Scythe. Tombs of Amascut for the Osmumten's Fang, Lightbearer, and Masori pieces. Rigour is the single biggest DPS upgrade in the game — prioritize getting it before any other raid unique.
  4. Endgame (Nex & DT2): With raid gear, start Nex in teams for Torva pieces. DT2 bosses (Duke, Vardorvis, Whisperer, Leviathan) for the Virtus robes, the Venator/Ultor/Magus rings (vestige upgrade path), and eventually the Soulreaper Axe.
Ironman Upgrade Philosophy: As an ironman, you don't control when gear drops. The Bowfa grind at Corrupted Gauntlet can take 400+ kc (roughly 60-80 hours). The key is to not bank-stand waiting for one item. Have multiple parallel goals: do your farm runs, birdhouse runs, and herb runs daily. Send a few CG runs, then switch to Slayer, then do a ToA run, then back to CG. Bouncing between content prevents burnout and keeps your supplies and account progression moving on all fronts simultaneously.

Ring Progression Summary

Rings are slot-specific upgrades that follow a clear path for each combat style. The Lightbearer (1/36 from Tombs of Amascut, ~2M) deserves special mention as a budget ring for all styles. It doubles your special attack regeneration rate (from 10% per 30 seconds to 10% per 15 seconds), meaning you can spec twice as often. For content where special attacks are important (Dragon Warhammer at Corp/raids, Voidwaker at Nex, Bone Dagger at dragons), the Lightbearer often provides more practical value than a stat ring, especially at budget and mid-game levels where the stat rings provide marginal bonuses.

Don't Rush Torture or Anguish Before Bowfa: The Necklace of Anguish (+5 ranged strength, ~15M) is a nice upgrade, but it provides significantly less DPS per GP than saving for the Bowfa + Crystal Armor setup. If you have 100M and you're deciding between Anguish + Pegasians + Arma or Bowfa + Crystal (armor only, recharging with shards), take Bowfa every time. The DPS increase from Blowpipe + Blessed D'hide to Bowfa + Crystal is roughly 30-40% against medium-to-high defence targets. The DPS increase from Fury to Anguish is roughly 5-8%. Bowfa is the single best GP-to-DPS upgrade in OSRS for ranged. Don't let small upgrades delay the big one.
