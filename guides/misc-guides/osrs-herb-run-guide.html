OSRS Herb Run Guide 2026 — Optimal Routes for Profit & Ironman

Herb runs are the closest thing OSRS has to passive income. You spend 5-7 minutes running between patches, plant herbs, and come back 80 minutes later to harvest 100K-300K profit depending on your Farming level and herb choice. When you do 5-6 herb runs a day between other activities, you're generating 500K to 1.8M daily — for less than an hour of total playtime. For ironmen, herb runs are even more critical. They are the primary source of Herblore XP via potion-making, and without them, training Herblore past 70 becomes a soul-crushing grind. This guide covers the optimal herb run route, the best herbs at every Farming level, teleport methods for every patch, and the tools you need to maximize your yield.

The cost of ultracompost (~1K per bucket) is trivial compared to the value of a single saved seed.

Disease-Free Patches and Why They Matter

Three herb patches are permanently disease-free — herbs planted here will never die:

  • Trollheim (My Arm's Patch): Unlocked after My Arm's Big Adventure. This is the single most important patch for profit and ironmen. Plant your most valuable seed here because it's guaranteed to survive. If you're planting torstols, always put one here.
  • Hosidius: Becomes disease-free at 50% Hosidius favour. Since the Kourend favour rework, this is automatically unlocked as you progress through Kourend quests. Plant your second-most-valuable herb here.
  • Weiss: Unlocked after Making Friends with My Arm. Becomes disease-free once you've neutralized the poison with the salt from the Weiss mine (the quest reward covers this). Your third priority patch for valuable seeds.

With three guaranteed patches, the optimal strategy for profit-focused players is: Torstol (or your best herb) at Trollheim, Weiss, and Hosidius. Second-best herb at the remaining five patches with ultracompost.

Tools and Boosters That Maximize Yield

Magic Secateurs

Obtained from the quest Fairy Tale I - Growing Pains. When worn (in your inventory or equipped), magic secateurs increase herb harvest yield by 10%. This is the single most important tool for herb runs and is non-negotiable. Every herb patch yields a base of 3 herbs minimum (with compost). Magic secateurs increase the average yield from ~7.5 herbs per patch (with ultracompost) to ~8.5 — a 13% profit increase that adds up to millions over the life of an account.

Farming Cape Perk

At 99 Farming, the Farming Skillcape provides a 5% chance to harvest extra crops from all patches. This stacks with magic secateurs. The cape also acts as an unlimited teleport to the Farming Guild, which is convenient but not essential — a Skills Necklace does the same thing.

Attas Anima Seed

Obtained from the Hespori boss in the Farming Guild (requires 65 Farming). When planted in the Anima patch in the Farming Guild's high-tier section, the Attas plant increases the yield of all farming patches globally by 3-5% while it's active (lasts 3.5 days). This applies to herbs, allotments, hops, everything. In the long run, Attas is always the best anima seed for herb runs — the other options (Iasor for disease reduction, Kronos for growth speed) don't provide as much value since herb patches already have a fast 80-minute cycle and ultracompost handles disease adequately.

Ultracompost vs Supercompost

Ultracompost is always worth it over supercompost for herb runs. The math:

  • Supercompost: minimum 4 herbs per patch, average of ~6.5 herbs from a living patch, roughly a 10% disease rate.
  • Ultracompost: minimum 5 herbs per patch, average of ~7.5 herbs from a living patch, roughly a 7.5% disease rate.

At current ranarr prices (~6K per grimy ranarr weed), that one extra herb per patch from ultracompost is worth ~48K per run across 8 patches — far exceeding the ~1K cost per bucket of ultracompost. Ultracompost is made by adding 2 volcanic ash to a bucket of supercompost. Volcanic ash is mined on Fossil Island (22 Mining) and costs ~100 GP each on the GE.

Herb Yield Calculator Logic

If you want to estimate your expected profit per herb run, the formula is:

  1. Expected Herbs Per Patch = (Base Max Herbs - 1) * (1 + Compost Bonus + Secateurs Bonus + Attas Bonus) / 2
  2. A living patch with ultracompost, magic secateurs, and Attas yields roughly 8.3 herbs on average.
  3. Expected Living Patches = Number of Planted Patches * (1 - Disease Rate)
  4. With ultracompost and 3 disease-free patches (Trollheim, Weiss, Hosidius), you expect ~4.75 of the 5 non-disease-free patches to survive. Total: ~7.75 living patches out of 8.
  5. Total Herbs = 7.75 patches * 8.3 herbs = ~64 herbs per run.
  6. Profit = 64 * (Herb Sell Price - Seed Cost / Avg Herbs Per Seed). For ranarrs: 64 * (6,000 - 25,000/8.3) = 64 * (6,000 - 3,012) = ~191K profit per run.

In practice, yields vary by 10-20% due to RNG. Over 100 runs, you'll average very close to these numbers.

Ironman Herb Gathering Strategies

Ironmen can't buy herb seeds from the Grand Exchange, so generating a steady seed supply is a core part of the game mode. Here are the best sources:

  1. Managing Miscellania (Kingdom of Miscellania): After completing Throne of Miscellania and Royal Trouble, assign 10 workers to herbs and 5 to maple logs (for bird nests and seeds). At maximum approval and 750K GP in the coffers, the kingdom generates roughly 60 grimy herbs and a significant number of herb seeds per day. This is the most passive and scalable source of herbs in the game. The herb seed output is random but averages about 3-4 ranarr seeds per day with full herb allocation. Maintain 100% approval daily by raking the weeds at the herb patch on Miscellania (takes 30 seconds).
  2. Farming Contracts (Farming Guild): Starting at 45 Farming, you can accept contracts from Jane in the Farming Guild. She asks you to grow and harvest a specific crop. Completing a contract rewards you with a seed pack that contains herb seeds (including ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol at higher tiers). Completing a hard contract (85+ Farming required) gives the best seed packs. Always have a contract active. Pre-plant common contract crops (potatoes, onions, cabbages at low tier; watermelon, snape grass, and limpwurt roots at high tier) so you can instantly complete contracts when they're assigned. "Pre-planting" is the single biggest efficiency gain for ironman farming.
  3. Master Farmers (Thieving): At 38+ Thieving, you can pickpocket Master Farmers for herb seeds. The seed yield scales with your Farming level — at 71+ Farming, Master Farmers drop ranarr seeds at a rate of roughly 1/300 pickpockets. At 94 Thieving with the Ardougne Hard Diary (10% pickpocket success bonus), you stop failing entirely. With the Rogue's Outfit (double loot), you average about 20-25 ranarr seeds per hour — enough to sustain 3-4 herb runs daily indefinitely. Master Farmers are also the primary source of hop seeds for birdhouse runs, making them doubly efficient.
  4. Slayer: Many Slayer monsters drop herb seeds as part of their regular loot table. Aberrant Spectres (60 Slayer) are notable for dropping large quantities of ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol seeds along with noted herbs. Nechryaels (80 Slayer) drop herb seeds at a high rate in the Catacombs of Kourend. Slayer is your "organic" source of seeds — you'll accumulate thousands passively while training combat.
  5. Birdhouse Runs (Fossil Island): Bird nests from birdhouse runs contain tree and fruit tree seeds, but occasionally herb seeds as well. Not a primary source, but a nice passive supplement if you're already doing birdhouse runs for Hunter XP.
Ironman Herb Seed Priority: Ranarr seeds are the bottleneck. You need prayer potions for all endgame PvM (God Wars, Zulrah, Vorkath, raids), and there's no substitute. Kingdom + Contracts + Master Farmers together generate enough ranarr seeds to sustain 3-4 herb runs per day. If you're running low on ranarr seeds, prioritize Master Farmer pickpocketing with the Rogue's Outfit — 20-25 ranarr seeds per hour is the most reliable source. For other herbs (snapdragon, torstol), Slayer will provide more than you can use.

Quick Checklist: Per-Run Inventory

  • 1 seed per patch (8 seeds for a full run)
  • 1 ultracompost per patch (note: disease-free patches still benefit from the yield boost, so ultracompost all 8)
  • Magic secateurs (equipped or in inventory)
  • Seed dibber (tool leprechaun can note your herbs, or bring it)
  • Spade (tool leprechaun can note, or bring it)
  • Rake (most patches can be auto-raked at higher Farming levels, but bring one just in case)
  • Bottomless Compost Bucket (optional but recommended — stores 10,000 compost uses and doubles the compost amount applied. From Hespori at 65 Farming)
  • Ectophial (Morytania patch)
  • Explorer's Ring (Falador patch)
  • Ardougne Cloak (Ardougne patch)
  • Xeric's Talisman (Hosidius patch, mount in POH if possible)
  • Trollheim/Icy Basalt tablets (Trollheim and Weiss patches)
  • Skills Necklace (Farming Guild patch)
  • Catherby Teleport runes or Camelot Teleport runes
