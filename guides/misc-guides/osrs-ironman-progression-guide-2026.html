OSRS Ironman Guide 2026 — Complete Early, Mid & Late Game Progression

Starting an ironman account in Old School RuneScape is one of the most rewarding ways to experience the game. Every item, every level, and every achievement is earned entirely through your own effort — no Grand Exchange shortcuts, no trading. This comprehensive guide walks you through the entire progression path from tutorial island to endgame PVM, covering the essential quests, gear milestones, and strategic decisions that define each stage of the ironman journey.

Progression Overview

The ironman journey can be broken into three distinct phases. The early game (roughly levels 1-70) focuses on rapid questing to skip early grinds and establish account infrastructure. The mid game (combat 80-100, base 70s) is about unlocking core untradeable gear and building resource supplies. The late game (combat 100+) sees you tackling bosses for best-in-slot items and entering raids.

Phase Approximate Stats Key Goals Duration

Phase 1: Early Game — Quest Rush & Account Foundations

The early game of an ironman is all about questing. Quests provide massive XP rewards that let you skip dozens of hours of low-level grinding. The goal is to complete a sequence of quests that establish your account's core infrastructure: transportation, protection prayers, and essential untradeable items.

Priority Quest Order (In Sequence)

  1. Waterfall Quest — The most important early quest. Requires no combat and rewards 13,750 Attack and Strength XP, taking you from level 3 to 30 in both stats instantly. Complete immediately after tutorial island.
  2. Witch's House — Rewards 6,325 Hitpoints XP. Be careful of the final boss (level 53), but safespotting with magic or ranging makes it manageable. This plus Waterfall gives you solid combat stats to start.
  3. Fight Arena — 12,175 Attack XP plus 2,175 Thieving XP. The bosses can be safespotted. This gets you well into the 40s for Attack.
  4. Tree Gnome Village & The Grand Tree — Unlocks spirit trees and the gnome glider network. The Grand Tree gives 18,400 Attack XP. These quests are essential for early transportation and progressing toward Monkey Madness.
  5. Holy Grail — 11,000 Prayer XP and 15,300 Defence XP. This is the most efficient way to get early Prayer XP without dragon bones on a chaos altar.
  6. Dragon Slayer I — Required to wear the rune platebody. Complete this once you have 40+ in all melee stats. Unlocks the rune platebody purchase from Oziach.

Essential Early Unlocks

Alongside questing, target these critical account upgrades as early as possible:

  • 43 Prayer (Priority 1): Collect big bones from hill giants, or train slayer for ensouled heads. Use chaos altar in the wilderness (level 38) for 700% XP — bring one inventory at a time with burning amulet teleport. Protect from Melee/Magic/Missiles are non-negotiable for all PVM content.
  • Ardougne Cloak 1 (Priority 2): Complete the Ardougne Easy Diary. The cloak provides unlimited teleports to the Ardougne Monastery, which is near a spirit tree, fairy ring, and an altar. It's the single best early-game teleport item.
  • Graceful Outfit (Priority 3): Train Agility at rooftop courses. Start at Draynor (10), then Al Kharid (20), Varrock (30), Canifis (40) for maximum marks of grace. The full graceful set reduces weight by 25kg and increases run energy restoration by 30%. Canifis rooftop (40-60 Agility) gives the best mark rates. This will take roughly 12-15 hours but is worth every minute.
  • Fairy Rings (Priority 4): Complete Fairy Tale Part I and start Fairy Tale Part II — you only need to start the quest (no stat requirements after Part I) to unlock fairy rings. This opens up the entire game map for transportation.
Tip: Unlock the Ardougne Cloak and fairy rings as soon as possible. The Ardougne Monastery teleport places you next to a fairy ring (code DJP), giving you rapid access to the entire fairy ring network without needing a Dramen staff once you complete Fairy Tale Part II to the point of using rings without one.

Phase 2: Mid Game — Core Gear & Slayer Grind

The mid game is where ironman accounts take shape. Your goal is to unlock all major untradeable combat gear, achieve base 70s in key skills, and begin the Slayer grind that will carry you into the late game. This phase typically spans combat levels 70 to 100.

Priority Mid-Game Unlocks (In Order)

  1. Barrows Gloves (175 QP): Complete Recipe for Disaster to earn the best-in-slot gloves for all combat styles. This quest requires 175 quest points and is the definitive milestone for the mid game. The gloves provide +12 strength bonus, +12 to all attack styles, and defensive bonuses that remain relevant through endgame. Aim for this before any serious Slayer training.
  2. Dragon Defender: Kill cyclopes in the Warrior's Guild basement for the dragon defender. Start with the rune defender drop and work up. The dragon defender provides +6 strength bonus and is the best melee offhand until the avernic defender. Expect 2-4 hours for the full upgrade.
  3. Fighter Torso: Complete Barbarian Assault for the fighter torso, providing +4 strength bonus. This requires roughly 4-6 hours in a team. Combined with dragon defender and barrows gloves, this is your core melee setup for most of the mid game.
  4. Fire Cape: Complete the TzHaar Fight Caves for the fire cape. Recommend 75+ Range, 70+ Defence, and 60+ Prayer. Use a rune crossbow with broad bolts or a magic shortbow (i) with rune arrows. Bring 8-10 brews and the rest super restores. The +4 strength bonus and +1 prayer bonus make this a permanent melee upgrade.
  5. Quest Cape: Complete all quests. Key late quests include Dragon Slayer II (Ava's assembler, Vorkath access), Monkey Madness II (demonic gorillas, maniacal monkeys), and Song of the Elves (Prifddinas, corrupted gauntlet). Each quest provides critical content unlocks.

Base 70s & Resource Infrastructure

Before diving into heavy PVM, establish your resource supply lines:

  • Herb Runs (38+ Farming): Toadflax (38), avantoe (50), kwuarm (56), snapdragon (62), ranarr (32). Complete farming contracts at the Farming Guild (45 Farming) for consistent high-level seed packs. Ultracompost is non-negotiable for maximum yield.
  • Birdhouse Runs: Start at 5 Hunter on Fossil Island. Yew birdhouses (60 Hunter) give the best value. Bird nests provide crushed nests for Saradomin brews and tree seeds for Farming XP. Run birdhouses on cooldown (50 minutes) for passive Hunter XP and nests.
  • Kingdom of Miscellania: Complete Throne of Miscellania and Royal Trouble. Allocate 10 workers to herbs (9 herbs nests) and 5 to maples (bird nests + logs for fletching). Keep 750K+ gp in the coffers for maximum daily yield. This is the backbone of passive Herblore XP.
  • Seaweed Runs: Farm giant seaweed at the underwater patch on Fossil Island. Combine with sandstone mining for buckets of sand, then use Superglass Make (77 Magic) for molten glass. This is the primary source of Crafting XP (see the dedicated Ironman Crafting guide).

Slayer Priority for Ironmen

Slayer is the engine that drives mid-game ironman progression. Prioritize these PVM unlocks through Slayer:

  • Level 65: Dust devils (burst for Magic XP, herblore secondaries, alchables)
  • Level 75: Gargoyles (consistent gp from alchables)
  • Level 80: Nechryaels (burst for seeds, alchables, and gp)
  • Level 85: Abyssal demons — abyssal whip, your primary melee weapon until ghrazi rapier or blade of saeldor
  • Level 87: Kraken — trident of the seas (essential for Zulrah and raids)
  • Level 93: Smoke devils — occult necklace (10% magic damage increase, best-in-slot amulet)
Critical: Do not skip the Magic training alongside Slayer. Bursting dust devils, nechryaels, and smoke devils is essential for training Magic on an ironman. You need 77 Magic for Superglass Make and 87-94 for barrage spells. Collect chaos and death runes from Barrows and Slayer drops, or buy from Mage Bank with the gp from Slayer alchables.

Phase 3: Late Game — PVM Bossing & Endgame Gear

The late game begins once you have barrows gloves, fire cape, trident of the seas, and base 85+ combat stats. Your goal now is acquiring the best-in-slot gear that transforms your account's PVM capabilities.

Priority Late-Game Progression

  1. Corrupted Gauntlet (Song of the Elves): Complete Song of the Elves and learn the Corrupted Gauntlet. The bow of Faerdhinen (Bowfa) combined with crystal armor is the single biggest gear upgrade for an ironman. Bowfa replaces your ranged weapon for nearly all content. The corrupted version never degrades and uses no ammo. Completion rate is roughly 200-400 KC for the enhanced crystal weapon seed (1/400 drop rate). Expect 2-4 months of dedicated grinding but the payoff is enormous.
  2. Zulrah (Regicide completion): After acquiring trident and Bowfa (or rune crossbow with ruby/diamond bolts as backup), start Zulrah. Priority drops: blowpipe (1/512), magic fang (1/512, for toxic trident), serpentine visage (1/512). The blowpipe with amethyst darts is essential for many PVM encounters. Zulrah also drops massive quantities of resources: dragon bones, battlestaves, and noted resources.
  3. Zenytes (Monkey Madness II): Kill demonic gorillas for zenyte shards. Crafting the zenyte jewelry requires high Crafting (87-93 depending on boost). Priority order: ring of suffering (84 Crafting), anguish (90), tormented bracelet (93), torture (93). The anguish and tormented bracelet are massive damage boosts for ranged and magic respectively.
  4. God Wars Dungeon:
    • Zamorak (K'ril): Zamorakian hasta (1/127 on task) — required to create the dragon hunter lance at Hydra.
    • Bandos (Graardor): Bandos chestplate, tassets, and boots. Melee upgrades for most content.
    • Armadyl (Kree'arra): Armadyl armor pieces. Good ranged armor but often skipped in favor of crystal armor + Bowfa.
  5. Slayer Bosses (91-95 Slayer): Cerberus (91) for primordial crystals (helm/boots). Alchemical Hydra (95) for hydra leather (ferocious gloves) and hydra claw (dragon hunter lance). The lance is best-in-slot melee against dragons, including Vorkath and Olm in Chambers of Xeric.
  6. Raids:
    • Tombs of Amascut (Entry Mode): The most accessible raid for mid-late game ironmen. Entry mode drops include the osmumten's fang (best-in-slot stab weapon) and lightbearer ring (double special attack regen). Both are game-changing at relatively common drop rates.
    • Chambers of Xeric: Once you have Bowfa, trident, and dragon hunter lance, start Chambers of Xeric. Key drops: Twisted Bow, Dexterous/Augury prayer scrolls, ancestral robes, dragon claws.

Progression Flowchart Logic

The ironman progression follows a clear dependency chain. Each milestone unlocks the next:

    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

    ↓

Pro Tip: The most common ironman mistake is rushing into PVM without proper support stats. A maxed POH (83 Construction, boostable from 75 with crystal saw and tea) is arguably the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade. Prioritize Construction before committing to heavy bossing — the ornate pool, portal nexus, and jewelry box save hundreds of hours in banking and transportation.

Common Pitfalls & Efficiency Rules

  • Don't skip Farming and Herblore: Saradomin brews, super restores, and super combat potions are all untradeable. Starting herb runs late means farming herbs when you should be bossing. Plant every seed you get.
  • Don't rush GWD without Bowfa: The bow of Faerdhinen revolutionizes GWD by allowing 6:0 and 7:0 methods that minimize damage taken. Bowfa is worth the CG grind.
  • Birdhouse runs are not optional: You need thousands of bird nests for Saradomin brews. Start running birdhouses at 5 Hunter on Fossil Island and never stop.
  • Kingdom of Miscellania is mandatory: 10 on herbs, 5 on maples (or teaks/mahogany if you need Construction planks). This is passive income you cannot afford to skip.
