OSRS How to Get House Teleport Tablet: Every Method Explained

The house teleport tablet is arguably the single most useful item in Old School RuneScape. If you've invested even moderately into your Player-Owned House — a mounted glory, portal chambers, an ornate rejuvenation pool, a jewelry box — then having instant, one-click access to your house is a game-changer. It turns your POH into a teleport hub, a restoration point, and a safe haven all in one. But how do you actually get these tablets? The answer depends on whether you're a main account, an ironman, or somewhere in between. Let's cover every method for stocking up on house teleport tablets in 2026.

Method 1: Buy from the Grand Exchange (Mains Only)

This is the simplest and most common method. House teleport tablets trade on the Grand Exchange for roughly 700-900 GP each as of 2026 pricing. At that rate, a stack of 100 tablets costs about 80K GP — essentially pocket change for any account past the early game. Search for "Teleport to house" on the GE and buy as many as you need. There's no buy limit on tablets, so you can stock up on thousands at once.

This method requires no skills, no quests, and no effort. The only requirement is having GP and having the Magic level to use the tablet (which is level 40 Magic, same as the spell itself). If you're a main account just looking for convenience, buy tablets from the GE and never think about it again.

Method 2: Craft Your Own (Ironman & Self-Sufficient Players)

For ironmen — or mains who enjoy self-sufficiency — crafting your own house teleport tablets is the way to go. Here's exactly what you need and how to do it efficiently.

Requirements

  • Level 40 Magic — to cast the Teleport to House spell
  • Level 40 Construction — required to have a house (and enough rooms for portals later)
  • A Player-Owned House — purchased from an Estate Agent for 1,000 GP (any location works, but Rimmington is popular for tablet-making since the house portal is right next to Phials, who un-notes items for 5 GP apiece)
  • Soft clay — the base material for tablets
  • Law runes (1), Air runes (1), Earth runes (1) — the rune cost for casting Teleport to House once
  • A Mahogany Eagle Lectern or better — built in your POH study room (requires 67 Construction for Mahogany Eagle or 47 for Oak Eagle, though Mahogany lets you make ALL tablet types)

Step-by-Step Tablet Crafting

  1. Gather soft clay. Mine clay (plenty at the Rimmington mine or Crafting Guild), use a bucket of water on it at a sink or water source, and you'll have soft clay. Alternatively, mine clay with a bracelet of clay equipped — this automatically softens clay as you mine it, saving the water step.
  2. Go to your POH study room where the eagle lectern is built.
  3. Bring your soft clay and runes. Each tablet requires 1 soft clay + the rune cost of the spell (1 law + 1 air + 1 earth for house teleport).
  4. Right-click the lectern and select "Make House Tablet" (or navigate the lectern interface to Teleport to House).
  5. Click craft. Your character will automatically craft all tablets in your inventory as long as you have soft clay and runes. Each tablet takes about 2 ticks (1.2 seconds), so you can craft a full inventory in about 30 seconds.
Ironman Efficiency Boost: Use a Rimmington house portal. Why Rimmington? Because Phials, the NPC just south of the portal (in the general store building), will un-note items for 5 GP each. This means you can bring noted soft clay and noted runes to your house, run to Phials, un-note everything instantly, run back to your house, and craft tablets. No banking, no running. A single trip with 1,000 noted clay + 1,000 of each rune costs 5,015 GP at Phials and yields 1,000 house tablets. That's the most efficient ironman tablet-crafting method in the game. Period.

Method 3: Soft Clay Gathering Strategies

The bottleneck for ironmen is always soft clay. Here are the best sources ranked from easiest to most efficient:

Option A: Mine Clay + Water (No Requirements)

Mine clay at the Rimmington mine (or Crafting Guild if 40 Crafting), bank it, withdraw 14 buckets of water with 14 clay, and use them on each other. Each clay + water = 1 soft clay. This is tedious but accessible at level 3. About 500-600 soft clay per hour at low levels with a rune pickaxe.

Option B: Bracelet of Clay (23 Crafting, 7 Magic)

Craft a Bracelet of Clay (gold bar + sapphire + bracelet mold, enchant with Level 1 Enchant). While wearing it, any clay you mine is automatically softened. This doubles your soft clay per hour because you skip the water step entirely. Mine at the Crafting Guild for close bank access. About 1,000-1,200 soft clay per hour.

Option C: Prifddinas Soft Clay Rocks (Requires Song of the Elves)

After completing Song of the Elves, the Prifddinas mine has soft clay rocks that are mined as soft clay directly — no water, no bracelet needed. Combined with the close bank in Prifddinas, this yields roughly 2,000-2,500 soft clay per hour. This is endgame ironman soft clay gathering and absolutely worth unlocking if you plan to craft thousands of tablets for long-term use.

Method 4: Nightmare Zone Reward Shop (Alternative)

If you've played Nightmare Zone at all, you'll accumulate NMZ points. While you can't buy house teleport tablets directly, NMZ points can be spent on redirect scrolls that modify house tabs to teleport to specific locations (like your house in Rimmington, Taverley, Pollnivneach, etc.). This is a niche method — most players don't need redirected tabs — but it's available for specific use cases where you want a house tablet AND a specific teleport location combined into one item.

More practically, NMZ points can buy herb boxes (worth 15K each) that you can sell for GP, then use that GP to buy more house tablets on the GE. This is a roundabout method but useful if you're NMZ training anyway and have excess points.

Method 5: Clue Scroll Rewards

All tiers of clue scrolls can drop house teleport tablets as part of the "teleport scroll" reward table. Easy and medium clues drop 5-10 tablets at once fairly commonly. If you're doing clue scrolls for other rewards anyway (ranger boots, god pages, etc.), the house tablets are a nice bonus. However, this is not a reliable primary source — you'll get maybe 50-100 tabs from 100 clue scrolls, which isn't enough for sustained use. Treat clue scroll tablets as freebies, not your main supply.

Don't make my mistake: On my first ironman, I crafted exactly 50 house teleport tablets and thought, "That should last me a while." They were gone in two days. Between farm runs, birdhouse runs, clue scroll steps, and random teleporting for quests, I was using 20-30 tablets a day. Now I craft 500 at a time and restock when I drop below 100. Craft in bulk. The time investment of gathering soft clay and runes is the same whether you make 50 or 500 — the only difference is how long you sit at the lectern. Make at least 300 tablets in one session and save yourself the repeated gathering trips.

Should You Upgrade to a Portal Chamber Instead?

If you're using house teleport tablets primarily to access your POH, consider building portal chambers that teleport you directly to your house without consuming a tablet. Inside your house, you can build portals (50 Construction) to other locations. Combined with a mounted glory (47 Construction) and eventually a jewelry box (81-91 Construction), your house becomes a self-contained teleport network.

The question of tablets vs. portals depends on your playstyle:

  • Use tablets if: You're an ironman with limited Construction level, you need the flexibility of one-click teleporting from anywhere (not just from within your house), or you frequently need to get to your POH from deep Wilderness (tablets work up to level 20 Wilderness).
  • Use portals if: You have 50+ Construction, you want to save inventory space (portals don't take up slots), and you're tired of refilling your tablet stack every week.

Most experienced players do both: portals for their most-used teleport destinations (Varrock, Falador, Camelot, Ardougne) and a stack of house tablets in the bank for when they're out in the world and need a quick home teleport. The two methods complement each other nicely.
