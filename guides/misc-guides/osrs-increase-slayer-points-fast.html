OSRS How to Increase Slayer Points Fast: The Complete Point Boosting Guide

Slayer points are the currency that makes Slayer training efficient in OSRS. Without a proper block list, extended tasks, and the ability to skip bad assignments, you're going to waste dozens of hours killing tasks that give terrible XP or no profit. The problem is, slayer points come slowly if you just train Slayer normally — you get 0 points for the first four tasks at any given slayer master, and even after that it's only 10-15 points per task. If you need 500 or 1,000 points for an unlock, that feels like an eternity. Here's how to farm slayer points fast using the Turael boosting method and smart point management strategies that actually work in 2026.

How Slayer Points Actually Work

Before we talk about boosting, you need to understand how the point system works, because most players get this wrong:

  • 0 points per task: Tasks 1-4 at any given slayer master. Everyone gets zero points for their first few tasks.
  • 10 points per task: Tasks 5-9 at low-to-mid slayer masters (Turael, Mazchna, Vannaka).
  • 15-25 points per task: Tasks at higher slayer masters (Duradel, Nieve/Steve, Konar).
  • Every 10th task completion: You get a bonus 5x the normal point rate. So with Duradel, task #10 gives you 75 points instead of 15.
  • Every 50th task completion: You get a bonus 15x the normal point rate. Task #50 at Duradel gives 225 points.
  • Every 100th task: You get a bonus 25x the normal point rate. Task #100 at Duradel gives 375 points.
  • Every 250th task: 35x bonus.
  • Every 1,000th task: 50x bonus.

The key insight: points are heavily back-loaded. The 10th, 50th, 100th, and milestone tasks are where you make the bulk of your points. A normal slayer task at Duradel gives 15 points, but every 10th task effectively gives you 75 points averaged over 10 tasks.

The Turael Boosting Method (Fastest Points Per Hour)

This is the method that every high-level player uses when they need quick slayer points (for skipping tasks during a boss grind, or buying out the slayer shop). Here's exactly how it works:

Step 1: Set Your Task Counter

Go to Turael (in Burthorpe, just north of the games room) and get 9 quick tasks from him. Turael assigns the easiest tasks in the game — think 15-50 kills of creatures like cows, chickens, goblins, skeletons, and cave crawlers. These tasks can each be completed in 2-5 minutes because the monsters are all level 2-20. Do 9 of them. That's your setup.

Step 2: The 10th Task at Your Best Master

On your 10th task, go to your best slayer master (Duradel if you have 100+ combat and 50+ Slayer; Nieve if you're 85+ combat; Konar if you want the brimstone key bonus). Get your 10th task from them. Complete that task. Because it's your 10th task overall, you get the big point bonus.

Step 3: Repeat the 1-9 Cycle at Turael

Go back to Turael. Do 9 more fast tasks. Then return to your main slayer master for the 10th (which is actually your 20th overall). Keep repeating this cycle. At a rate of 2-3 minutes per Turael task, you complete all 9 in about 20-25 minutes. The 10th task might take 10-15 minutes depending on what your main master assigns. So every 30-40 minutes, you earn a milestone bonus.

The Math: At Duradel, a normal 10th-task bonus is 75 points. If you do 9 tasks at Turael (0 points, but 25 minutes total) + 1 task at Duradel (75 points, 10-15 minutes), you're earning about 75 points every 35-40 minutes. That's roughly 110-130 points per hour. For comparison, normal Slayer training at Duradel gives about 25-30 points per hour. The Turael boosting method is roughly 4x faster for raw points.
One Critical Warning: Turael can RESET your task streak if you're not careful. When you get a task from Turael while ALREADY having an active task from another master, he'll offer to replace it. Accepting this replacement resets your streak counter to 0. To avoid this, always COMPLETE or CANCEL (with points) your current task at your main master before going back to Turael for the 1-9 cycle. Never have an active task from your main master when talking to Turael.

Konar Point Boosting (Alternative Method)

If you're at least 75 combat and would rather not do the Turael back-and-forth, Konar's tasks pay out more points at the cost of being assigned in specific locations. Konar gives 18 points per normal task (compared to Duradel's 15) and 90 points per 10th task (compared to 75). Additionally, Konar's tasks drop brimstone keys (1/50 to 1/75 drop rate depending on the monster), and those keys open a chest worth about 100K average. If you value slayer XP alongside points, Konar is slightly better than the Turael method because you're actually gaining XP while boosting.

The Konar Math

At Konar, 10 tasks give you: 9 x 18 = 162 + 90 (10th bonus) = 252 points per 10 tasks, with brimstone keys adding about 200K-300K GP per 10-task cycle. The downside: Konar assigns specific locations, and some are terrible (e.g., Iron Dragons in the Brimhaven Dungeon requiring an axe to chop vines). You'll need to skip more tasks with Konar, which uses up points.

Wilderness Slayer: High Risk, High Reward

The Wilderness slayer master (Krystilia) gives 25 points per task and 125 points per 10th task — the highest per-task rate in the game. She also awards Larran's Keys, which open Larran's Chest for 150K average loot. The catch is obvious: you're in the Wilderness, and PKers will find you. For experienced players, Wilderness Slayer is the fastest raw point rate in the game if you're willing to risk minimal gear and use quick escapes (Royal Seed Pod, Glory teleport). For most players, the annoyance of dying to PKers and losing supplies outweighs the point bonus. But if you're a confident PVP navigator, Krystilia is the point queen.

Point Spending Priority: What to Buy First

Slayer points are valuable, and spending them wrong sets you back hours. Here's the optimal spending order for efficiency:

  1. Bigger and Badder (150 points) — unlocks the ability to receive Superior Slayer Monster spawns. These drop the imbued heart (40M+) and eternal gem (9M). This is the single best slayer unlock in the game and should be your first purchase. Period.
  2. Block Slots 1-2 (100 + 150 points) — block your worst tasks. The universally-blocked tasks are: Hellhounds (unless you do Cerberus), Spiritual Creatures, Black Demons (unless you do Demonic Gorillas), and Suqahs. Blocking the right tasks increases your overall XP/hr more than any other unlock.
  3. Slayer Helmet (400 points) — required to craft the slayer helmet, which gives a 16.67% damage and accuracy bonus on-task. Combine with a black mask (or imbue it at Nightmare Zone for Ranged and Magic bonuses too).
  4. Task Extensions — extend Dust Devils, Nechryaels, and Smoke Devils (the burst/barrage tasks). These give the best XP per hour in the game. Each extension costs 100 points.
  5. Broader Fletching (300 points) — allows fletching broad bolts and arrows. Important for ironmen but optional for mains who can buy on the GE.
  6. Additional Block Slots (200+ points each) — once you have the core tasks blocked, add more to refine your task list. Good candidates: Gargoyles (slow kills at low stats), Aberrant Spectres (annoying prayer drain), and Drakes (terrible XP and terrible money).
Don't make my mistake: I bought the Slayer Helmet first because it looked cool and everyone said it was essential. Then I learned about Superior monsters and realized I'd missed out on potential imbued heart drops for 30+ tasks. The slayer helmet is great, but a black mask (i) gives the same damage bonus. Get Bigger and Badder first, the helmet second. You'll thank yourself later.

How to Skip Tasks Without Wasting Points

Task skipping is where most players hemorrhage points. At 30 points to skip a Duradel task, skipping 3-4 tasks eats up an entire block slot's worth of points. Here's how to minimize point waste:

  • Use Turael to cancel tasks for free: Instead of spending 30 points to skip a bad task from Duradel, go to Turael and have him replace your task with one of his easy ones. This resets your streak counter, so ONLY do this if your next milestone (10th, 50th, 100th task) is far away. If you're at task 8/10, just eat the 30-point skip — a reset would lose you the 10th-task bonus.
  • Set up your block list before your point grind: Don't grind 1,000 points and THEN block tasks. Block immediately. Every bad task you complete when you could have blocked it is both time lost on a bad XP task AND points you didn't earn from a good task.
  • Skip aggressively during slayer point boosting: When you're doing the Turael boosting method, the value of skipping is different. Since Turael tasks are 2-3 minutes each and give 0 points, every task you complete at Turael helps cycle toward the next milestone. The Turael method is about speed, not task quality.

That's a good problem to have — it means you can freely skip tasks at Duradel without ever worrying about running out of points. The initial grind to get there is the only tough part.
